# New Player Primer — Cold Start Syndicate

Welcome to the table. This document gives you everything you need to make a character and jump into our campaign. Read the first two sections before Session 0; skim the rest at your own pace.

---

## The Elevator Pitch

**Nova Praxis** is a tabletop RPG set in a transhuman, post-scarcity future. Humanity survived the Singularity — barely — and now lives scattered across the solar system under the control of six corporate Houses. Your mind can be backed up, copied, and downloaded into new bodies. Money barely exists; reputation is currency. And underneath the utopian surface, the Houses wage a secret Shadow War for dominance.

**Our campaign — Cold Start Syndicate — is this:**

> You woke up 100 years late in this future, pulled from cryo by an unknown patron. Now you run deniable jobs in the House shadow wars while trying to stay human — and stay yourselves.

Every job has the obvious contract and the factional agenda underneath. Your choices about identity, loyalty, and methods have real consequences that carry forward.

---

## What You Need to Know About the Setting

### The Big Picture
- **Earth is gone.** The Singularity wiped it out. Humanity lives on stations, colonies, and terraformed worlds across the solar system and a handful of exoplanets.
- **Six Houses run everything.** They replaced governments. Each controls territory, industry, and ideology. They cooperate publicly and sabotage each other constantly.
- **The Coalition** is the thin democratic veneer over the Houses — a United Nations that the Houses mostly ignore when it's inconvenient.
- **Apostates** are people who live outside House/Coalition control. Black marketeers, rebels, refugees. Your crew has ties to this world.

### Transhumanity (The Cool/Terrifying Part)
- **Apotheosis:** A procedure that digitizes your mind. After Apotheosis, your consciousness can be backed up, transferred, and even copied.
- **Sleeves:** Synthetic or biological bodies you can download into. Think of them as hardware for your mind. You can upgrade, swap, or lose them.
- **SIMs:** People who exist purely as software — no body at all. They interact with the physical world through drones and mesh networks.
- **Pure:** People who never underwent Apotheosis. Still in their original bodies. Increasingly rare and viewed as either principled or stubborn.
- **Forking:** Copying a mind. The copy is a "fork" — a separate person with your memories up to the split point. This is legally and ethically... complicated.
- **Mnemonic Cores:** Your mind's backup drive. If your body dies but your core survives, you can be restored. If it doesn't survive, you're gone.

### Why This Matters at the Table
Identity isn't abstract here. "Who am I?" is an operational question. Characters deal with memory editing, forks of themselves, dying and being restored in new bodies, and the question of whether a copy of you *is* you. The campaign leans into this hard.

## The Game System: FATE (Quick Version)

Nova Praxis runs on a modified version of FATE. If you've never played FATE, here's the core loop:

### Rolling Dice
- Roll **4 Fudge dice** (4dF) — each die shows +1, -1, or 0
- Add your **Skill rank** + any modifiers
- That total is your **Effort**
- Beat the **Difficulty** set by the GM and the margin is your **Shifts**
- **Spin** (3+ Shifts): Something extra-good happens
- **Stall** (fail by 3+): Something extra-bad happens

### Aspects — The Heart of FATE
Aspects are short phrases that describe something true about your character, the scene, or the world. Examples: *"Cryo-Survivor With Trust Issues"*, *"This Hallway Is On Fire"*, *"Owes a Debt to House Kimura"*.

- **Invoke** an Aspect: Spend 1 Fate Point for +2 to a roll (or a reroll) when the Aspect is relevant
- **Get Compelled:** The GM uses your Aspect to make your life harder — you gain 1 Fate Point
- Aspects are the GM's main tool for creating drama and your main tool for bending the odds

### Fate Points
- You start each session with your **Refresh** (usually 5, higher for Pure characters)
- Spend them to invoke Aspects or power certain Stunts
- Earn them when your Aspects get compelled against you
- **Use them or lose them** — they don't carry between sessions

### Stress & Consequences
When you get hit (physically, mentally, or digitally):
- **Stress boxes** absorb minor hits — they clear after the scene
- **Consequences** absorb bigger hits by giving you a negative Aspect (Mild -2, Moderate -4, Severe -6, Extreme -8)
- If you can't absorb the hit, you're **Taken Out** — the attacker decides what happens to you
- You can **Concede** before that happens to keep narrative control over your exit

### Actions You Can Take
| Action | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Test** | Roll vs. a fixed difficulty |
| **Contest** | Roll vs. another character's roll |
| **Maneuver** | Place a temporary Aspect on the scene or target |
| **Assessment** | Discover a hidden Aspect |
| **Declaration** | Introduce a new fact into the story |
| **Attack** | Deal stress to a target |
| **Block** | Prevent a specific action |

## Building Your Character

### Step 1: Pick Your State
This is the biggest choice. It determines your relationship with your body.

| State | What It Means | Flavor | Mechanical Perks |
|-------|--------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Pure** | Original body, no Apotheosis | Principled, old-school, unpredictable | +2 Refresh, free Carpe Diem stunt, +1 to one skill, 8 physical skill ranks |
| **SIM** | No body — pure software | Digital ghost, drone operator, hacker | Free SIM State + choice stunt, SINC bonus, live in virtualities |
| **Sleeved** | Digitized mind in a purchased body | Most common, flexible, upgradeable | Physical stats from sleeve, augmentation points, sleeve Aspects |

### Step 2: Choose a Skill Distribution
You build your skill list using one of three pyramids:

| Style | Shape | Best For |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Specialist** | 1 at +5, 2 at +4, 2 at +3, 3 at +2, 6 at +1 | One standout talent |
| **Expert** | 3 at +4, 3 at +3, 4 at +2, 6 at +1 | Well-rounded competence |
| **Generalist** | 7 at +3, 8 at +2, 3 at +1 | Broad, no weaknesses |

### Step 3: Pick Your Skills
Here's the full list with one-line descriptions:

**Standard Skills:**
| Skill | Does |
|-------|------|
| Assets | Wealth and black market purchasing power |
| Cohesion | Mental stability, sense of self, resisting psychological trauma |
| Demolitions | Explosives — combat and structural |
| Diplomacy | Friendly persuasion, negotiation, making good impressions |
| Education | Book learning — science, history, academic knowledge |
| H. Engineering | Hardware — build, repair, operate machines |
| S. Engineering | Software — code, operate CISTs, digital systems |
| Firearms | Ranged weapons — railguns, coil guns, heavy weapons |
| Guile | Lying, conning, seduction, bluffing, haggling |
| Insight | Mental reflexes, gut feelings, reading emotions |
| Intimidation | Threats, coercion, interrogation |
| Melee | Hand-to-hand and melee weapons |
| Mnemonics | Mind editing, resleeving, forking, AI interaction |
| House Networking | Contacts among the Houses; starting Rep-Rating |
| Pilot | Vehicles — ground, air, space |
| Research | Finding information on the Extranet |
| Resolve | Willpower, courage, resisting temptation |
| Strategy | Tactical planning, initiative in conflict, reading enemy plans |
| Stealth | Hiding, pickpocketing, moving unseen |

**Physical Skills** (ranks come from your State/Sleeve):
| Skill | Does |
|-------|------|
| Athletics | Movement, coordination, lifting — adds to Physical Stress |
| Endurance | Toughness, resisting toxins/disease — adds to Physical Stress |
| Perception | Senses, awareness, noticing details |

**Special:**
| Skill | Does |
|-------|------|
| SINC | Savant hacking — requires Savant stunt |
| Apostate Networking | Underground/black market contacts |

### Step 4: Pick Stunts
Stunts give you special abilities or bonuses. You get a few for free based on your State, then pick more. Each non-free stunt costs 1 Refresh (lowering your Fate Point budget). Key options:

- **Savant** — Unlocks SINC skill and hacking programs (requires Apotheosis)
- **Drone-Jock** — Cheaper, better drones
- **Licensed** — Buy restricted gear legally
- **Battlefield Command** — Move allies tactically
- **Fame** — Celebrity leverage
- **Specialty Aspect** — Free invoke once/scene on a specific skill use
- **Skilled** — +1 to any non-physical skill
- **Refreshed** — +1 Refresh (can take up to 3 times)
- **Transhumanist** — More augmentation capacity on sleeves

### Step 5: Write Your Aspects (5 Total)
1. **High Concept** — Who you are in one phrase (*"Disgraced Coalition Intelligence Analyst"*)
2. **Trouble** — What keeps getting you into problems (*"Can't Leave Well Enough Alone"*)
3. **Background** — Where you came from (*"Grew Up Running Cargo on the Belt"*)
4. **State Aspect** — Related to Pure/SIM/Sleeved (*"This Body Still Feels Wrong"*)
5. **Free Aspect** — Anything else important (*"I Know People Who Know People"*)

Good Aspects are **double-edged** — useful when invoked, interesting when compelled.

### Step 6: If Sleeved, Pick a Sleeve
Sleeves come in tiers. Your starting sleeve can't cost more than MAX(Assets, Rep-Rating) + 4.

| Sleeve | Cost | Phys. Ranks | Aug. Points | Notes |
|--------|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Alpha Bio | 6 | 8 | 2 | Budget; Unattractive aspect |
| Beta Bio | 7 | 9 | 3 | Standard; neutral appearance |
| Gamma Bio | 9 | 10 | 4 | Enhanced; cosmetic options |
| Delta Bio | 11 | 11 | 6 | Premium; exceptional genegineering |
| Epsilon Bio | 14 | 12 | 8 | Top tier |

Cybersleeves exist too — talk to Sean about options. They trade biological flexibility for raw power and modularity.

## What's Happened So Far (Spoiler-Lite)

The crew woke up from cryo owing their existence to a mysterious patron. Since then:

- They've survived a crisis on Europa Station, a rogue AI siege, and a corporate cover-up
- They've discovered that a faction called **Chimera** is orchestrating events behind the scenes
- A woman named **Valare** holds critical information — her backup "fork" is alive but traumatized and refusing to cooperate
- An operative named **Kestrel** has been sent to clean up all evidence — including the crew
- The crew currently has a battle cruiser and a lot of enemies

**Your characters don't need to know any of this going in.** Session 0 will establish how you connect to the crew and the story. Sean will work with you on that.

## Session 0 Checklist

- [ ] Read this primer (you're almost done)
- [ ] Think about what kind of character sounds fun — combat? social? hacking? weird?
- [ ] Pick a State (Pure / SIM / Sleeved) — or come with 2-3 ideas and we'll narrow it down
- [ ] Think about a High Concept and Trouble aspect — even rough drafts help
- [ ] We'll build the rest together at the table

Don't stress about getting everything perfect. Session 0 is for exactly this — we'll walk through it together and make sure your character fits the world and the crew.
