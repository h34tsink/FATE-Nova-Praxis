SWADE → FATE

Migration Guide & Table Reference
NOVA PRAXIS — COLD START SYNDICATE

Why We Switched

FATE handles Nova Praxis's core themes better than SWADE. Identity, reputation, social manipulation, and narrative consequences are first-class mechanical citizens in FATE, not bolted-on subsystems.

SWADE Approach

  • Pick action from menu, then describe it
  • Characters built from mechanical blocks that imply story
  • Failure = nothing happens or take damage
  • GM describes the world, players react

FATE Approach

  • Describe what you do, table determines the mechanic
  • Characters built from story statements that imply mechanics
  • Failure = story goes somewhere unexpected
  • Players co-author the scene via Declarations and Maneuvers
Bottom Line
Less time counting modifiers. More time describing what your character actually does.

3Edges & Hindrances → Stunts & Aspects

SWADE

  • Edges — specific mechanical bonuses
  • Hindrances — flaws that gave build points at creation
  • Hindrances are "always on" negatives
  • Took them once, mostly forgot about them

FATE

  • Stunts — similar to Edges; start with 3, gain more at Milestones
  • Aspects (especially Disadvantage) — flaws that earn Fate Points when compelled
  • Aspects are double-edged — help AND hinder
  • Your Disadvantage is a live wire every session
Muscle Memory Trap
In SWADE, you took Hindrances at creation and mostly forgot them. In FATE, your Disadvantage Aspect fires every session. The GM will compel it. You want this — it is how you get Fate Points.

"I invoke Cryo-Survivor With Trust Issues because I have learned to read when someone is lying" — valid.
"I spend a point to reroll" — not valid.

6Action Economy

SWADE

  • 3 actions with Multi-Action Penalty (−2 per extra)
  • Support = +1 to ally's next roll
  • Draw cards for initiative

FATE

  • 1 Simple Action + 1 Free Action per turn
  • Maneuver → place Aspect → ally invokes for +2
  • Strategy skill sets turn order

FATE Actions

ActionPurposeOn Spin (3+ Shifts)
OvercomeRemove obstacle, resist effectExtra narrative benefit
Create AdvantagePlace or discover an Aspect2 free invokes instead of 1
AttackDeal stress to targetReduce Shifts by 1 to create Effect Aspect
DefendBlock attack or advantage— (reactive, does not cost your action)
Muscle Memory Trap
There is no Multi-Action Penalty because there are no multi-actions. The FATE way to "do more" is to set up Aspects that allies invoke. Teamwork happens across turns, not within one.

Scope Rule

One Aspect per scope per roll: one Character, one Scene, one Zone, one Equipment, one Consequence. Persistent Aspects are exempt.

The "Therefore" Test

"I have [Aspect], therefore it helps me [do this]."
If you need two logical leaps, it is a stretch.

Refresh
Start each session at your Refresh rating (default 5, Pure = 7). Fate Points never carry over. Use them.

9Nova Praxis: Character States

Pure

Physical: 8 ranks (you assign)

Refresh: 7 (+2 bonus)

Perk: Carpe Diem stunt (free scene Aspect), +1 to one skill

Cost: Lose all benefits if you ever Apothesize

SIM

Physical: 7 ranks (virtual only)

Refresh: 5 (standard)

Perk: +1 SINC, +1 S.Eng, choice stunt

Cost: No body — needs drones, suffers lag penalties

Sleeved

Physical: Set by sleeve

Refresh: 5 (standard)

Perk: Augmentations, swappable bodies

Cost: Resleeving = mental attack (Edge 8)

Three Stress Tracks
Unlike SWADE's single Wound track: Physical (Athletics + Endurance), Mental (Cohesion + Resolve), System (SINC + S. Engineering, Savants only).

Sleeves & Augmentations

  • Sleeves set Physical skill ranks and provide Augmentation Points
  • Starting sleeve cost: MAX(Assets, Rep-Rating) + 4
  • Augmentation Limit = Endurance rank × 2
  • More expensive sleeve = better physical stats + more augmentation capacity

11Quick Mindset Resets

When you instinctively want to...In FATE, instead...
"I attack twice with Multi-Action"Attack once, or Maneuver first → invoke the Aspect next turn
"I spend a Bennie to Soak"Take a Consequence (it becomes an Aspect enemies can invoke)
"I roll Vigor to stay up"Absorb with stress boxes + Consequences, or Concede
"What is my Toughness?"Your AR subtracts from incoming stress. That is it
"I roll Notice"Perception (senses) or Insight (gut / reading people)
"What is my Parry?"You roll a defense when attacked — no passive number
"I roll Spirit to resist"Resolve (willpower) or Cohesion (sense of self)
"Can I Aid my ally?"Maneuver → place Aspect → ally invokes for +2
"I use my Edge for a bonus"Check Stunts, or invoke a relevant Aspect
Remember
Good Aspects are double-edged: useful when invoked, interesting when compelled. Lean into compels — they are fuel, not punishment.

12Gone & New

Does Not Exist in FATE

  • Attribute rolls
  • Shaken / Unshaken
  • Soak rolls
  • Bennies for damage reduction
  • Multi-Action Penalty
  • Wild Die
  • Exploding dice
  • Incapacitation table
  • Healing rolls (Consequences heal on a timer)

New Tools to Learn

  • Aspects — invoke for +2, get compelled for +1 FP
  • Declarations — spend FP to add a fact to the scene
  • Create Advantage — place a temporary Aspect anyone can invoke
  • Concession — surrender on your terms
  • Persistent Aspects — free to invoke/compel
  • Scope Rule — one Aspect per category per roll
  • Spin — 3+ Shifts = bonus Effect Aspect
