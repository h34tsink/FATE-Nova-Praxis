# SWADE to FATE Migration Guide

**Summary:** A read-once guide for players transitioning from Savage Worlds Adventure Edition to the FATE-based Nova Praxis system. Covers the conceptual shifts, mechanical translations, and muscle-memory traps.

---

## Why We Switched

FATE handles Nova Praxis's core themes better than SWADE. Identity, reputation, social manipulation, and narrative consequences are first-class mechanical citizens in FATE, not bolted-on subsystems. The fiction drives the mechanics instead of the other way around.

What this means at the table: **you'll spend less time counting modifiers and more time describing what your character actually does.**

---

## The Big Conceptual Shifts

### 1. Fiction First, Then Mechanics

In SWADE, you pick an action from a menu and describe what happens. In FATE, you describe what your character does, and the table figures out which mechanic applies. The narrative isn't flavor text on top of a roll — it determines *which* roll you make and *what* Aspects are relevant.

### 2. Characters Are Defined by Story, Not Stats

SWADE characters are built from Attributes, Skills, Edges, and Hindrances — mechanical building blocks that imply a story. FATE characters are built from [[Glossary/Aspect|Aspects]] — story statements that imply mechanics, using the **ABCDE method** (Ambition, Belief, Connection, Disadvantage, Expertise) plus a High Concept. Your Aspects do more to define your character than any skill rank.

### 3. Failure Is Interesting, Not Punishing

In SWADE, failure usually means "nothing happens" or "you take damage." In FATE, failure means the story goes somewhere you didn't plan. The GM might offer you success at a cost, or a compel that makes things dramatically worse but earns you a [[Glossary/Fate Point|Fate Point]]. Failure fuels the economy that makes you powerful later.

### 4. You Shape the World

SWADE players react to what the GM describes. FATE players actively introduce facts through Declarations, Maneuvers, and Aspect creation. If you say "I bet this warehouse has a back exit" and spend a Fate Point, it does. You're co-authoring the scene, not just moving through it.

## Mechanic-by-Mechanic Translation

### Dice

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| Trait die (d4-d12) + Wild Die (d6), take highest | 4 Fudge dice (4dF): each shows +1, 0, or -1 |
| Acing (exploding dice) | No equivalent — the range is -4 to +4 |
| Raise = beat TN by 4+ | **Spin** = beat Difficulty by 3+ (creates an Effect Aspect) |
| Critical Failure (snake eyes) | **Stall** = fail by 3+ (spectacular failure) |

**Muscle memory trap:** You'll instinctively expect bigger swings. FATE's range is narrow (-4 to +4 on dice alone). A +4 skill is *dominant* — don't underestimate it.

### Attributes and Skills

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| 5 Attributes (Agility, Smarts, Spirit, Strength, Vigor) | **No attributes.** Skills stand alone |
| Skills are linked to Attributes | Skills are independent, ranked +1 to +5 on the [[Glossary/FATE Ladder\|FATE Ladder]] |
| Untrained penalty (-2) | Rolling at +0 (Mediocre) — no penalty, just no bonus |

FATE skills are broader. SWADE's Fighting + Shooting collapse into [[Rules and Mechanics/Skills\|Melee]] and [[Rules and Mechanics/Skills\|Firearms]]. SWADE's Notice becomes [[Rules and Mechanics/Skills\|Perception]] + [[Rules and Mechanics/Skills\|Insight]]. Don't look for a 1:1 map — the categories shifted.

**Physical skills** (Athletics, Endurance, Perception) work differently: their ranks come from your [[Rules and Mechanics/Character States\|State]] or sleeve, not from your skill distribution.

### Edges & Hindrances vs. Stunts & Aspects

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| **Edges** — specific mechanical bonuses | **[[Rules and Mechanics/Stunts\|Stunts]]** — similar; you start with 3, gain more at Milestones |
| **Hindrances** — flaws that gave you build points | **Aspects** (especially Disadvantage) — flaws that earn you Fate Points when compelled |
| Hindrances are "always on" negatives | Aspects are double-edged — they help AND hinder depending on context |

**Key difference:** In SWADE, you took Hindrances at character creation and mostly forgot about them. In FATE, your Disadvantage Aspect is a live wire every session. The GM will compel it. You *want* this — it's how you get Fate Points.

### Bennies vs. Fate Points

| SWADE Bennies | FATE Points |
|---------------|-------------|
| Soak damage | No soak — use Consequences instead |
| Reroll any Trait | Invoke an Aspect: +2 or reroll (must be narratively relevant) |
| Recover from Shaken | No Shaken state exists |
| Earned from Jokers, GM reward | Earned from **compels** on your Aspects |
| Carry over between sessions (some tables) | **Never carry over.** Reset to Refresh each session |

**Muscle memory trap:** You can't just spend a Fate Point for a generic reroll. You must tie it to a specific Aspect and explain *why* that Aspect helps. "I invoke *Cryo-Survivor With Trust Issues* because I've learned to read when someone's lying" is valid. "I spend a point to reroll" is not.

### Damage: Wounds vs. Stress & Consequences

This is the biggest mechanical shift.

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| **Toughness** (passive defense) | **Armor Rating (AR)** subtracts from incoming stress |
| **Shaken** (stunned, must recover) | No equivalent — there's no "stunned" state |
| **Wounds** (3 before Incapacitated) | **Stress boxes** absorb minor hits, clear after scene |
| **Soak rolls** (spend Bennie + Vigor) | **Consequences** absorb big hits by taking a negative Aspect |
| **Incapacitated** (down, Vigor roll to survive) | **Taken Out** (attacker narrates what happens to you) |
| No equivalent | **Concede** — surrender before Taken Out, keep narrative control, gain 1 FP |

**How it works in practice:**
1. Attack hits. Stress = Shifts + Weapon Rating - Armor Rating.
2. Mark stress boxes to absorb it. If boxes aren't enough, take a Consequence (Mild absorbs 2, Moderate absorbs 4, Severe absorbs 6, Extreme absorbs 8).
3. Consequences are negative Aspects — enemies can invoke them against you for free once.
4. Can't absorb the hit? You're Taken Out. Unless you **Concede** first.

**Muscle memory trap:** There's no Soak roll. No "spend a Bennie to shake it off." Once stress lands, you absorb it or you don't. But Concession is powerful — SWADE has nothing like it. Use it.

### Initiative

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| Draw cards, act in order | [[Rules and Mechanics/Skills\|Strategy]] skill determines order |
| Joker = +2 and act first | No equivalent |
| Hold action | GM can shift turn order based on fiction |

### Actions Per Turn

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| 3 actions with Multi-Action Penalty (-2 per extra) | **1 Simple Action + 1 Free Action** per turn |
| Test (opposed Skill vs. Smarts/Spirit) | **Maneuver** (place a temporary Aspect) |
| Support (+1 to ally's next roll) | No direct equivalent — Maneuver to create an Aspect, ally invokes it |

**Muscle memory trap:** You can't multi-action. Instead of doing three mediocre things, you do one thing well. The FATE equivalent of "support + attack" is: one character Maneuvers to place an Aspect ("*Flanked*"), then another character invokes it for +2 on their Attack. It takes two turns but is more reliable.

### Economy and Gear

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| Gold/credits, buy from price lists | **[[Glossary/Rep-Rating\|Rep-Rating]]** (Coalition) or **Assets** skill (black market) |
| Gear has specific stats | Gear has Cost, sometimes WR/AR, and narrative permissions |
| Encumbrance/weight limits | No tracking — if it's narratively reasonable, you have it |

### Powers / Magic

| SWADE | FATE |
|-------|------|
| Power Points, recharge over time | No Power Points — Stunts are always-on or cost Fate Points |
| Arcane Background (Edge) | **[[Rules and Mechanics/Stunts\|Savant]]** stunt (unlocks SINC skill + programs) |
| Spell list with PP costs | **Savant Programs** — each is a SINC skill test vs. a Difficulty |
| Backlash on critical failure | Failed program = System Stress; Stall = catastrophic failure |

## Things That Don't Exist in FATE

These SWADE mechanics have no equivalent. Stop looking for them:

- **Attribute rolls** — Skills cover everything
- **Shaken/Unshaken** — No stunned state
- **Soak rolls** — Consequences absorb automatically
- **Bennies for damage reduction** — Fate Points only boost rolls via Aspects
- **Multi-Action Penalty** — One action per turn, period
- **Wild Die** — All PCs roll 4dF, same as NPCs
- **Exploding dice** — The ceiling is +4 on dice
- **Incapacitation table** — Taken Out means the attacker decides (or you Concede)
- **Healing rolls** — Consequences downgrade on a timer (Mild: 1 hour, Moderate: 1 day, Severe: 1 week)

---

## Things FATE Has That SWADE Doesn't

These are new tools. Learn to use them:

- **[[Glossary/Aspect\|Aspects]]** — Story-true phrases that you invoke (+2) or get compelled against (+1 FP). The entire economy runs on these.
- **Declarations** — Spend a FP to introduce a fact into the scene ("There's a fire escape on this building").
- **Maneuvers** — Place a temporary Aspect on a target or scene that anyone can invoke.
- **Concession** — Surrender on your terms before getting Taken Out. You lose the fight but keep narrative control.
- **Persistent Aspects (P)** — Major facts (Severe/Extreme Consequences, environmental truths) that can be invoked or compelled for free. They don't cost FP to use.
- **The Scope Rule** — You can only invoke one Aspect per "scope" per roll (one Character Aspect, one Scene Aspect, one Equipment Aspect, etc.). This prevents stacking.
- **Spin** — Beat the difficulty by 3+ and you create a free Effect Aspect in addition to succeeding.

## Quick Mindset Resets

| When you instinctively want to... | In FATE, instead... |
|---|---|
| "I attack twice with Multi-Action" | Attack once, or Maneuver first to set up a free invoke for next turn |
| "I spend a Bennie to Soak" | Take a Consequence — it's an Aspect now, enemies will invoke it |
| "I roll Vigor to stay up" | Check if you can absorb the stress with boxes + Consequences. If not, Concede or get Taken Out |
| "What's my Toughness?" | Your AR (from armor/sleeve) subtracts from incoming stress. That's it |
| "I roll Notice" | Roll Perception (senses) or Insight (gut feeling/read emotions) |
| "I use my Edge" | Check if you have a relevant Stunt. Otherwise, invoke an Aspect |
| "What's my Parry?" | You roll a defense when attacked — there's no passive defense number |
| "I roll Spirit to resist fear" | Roll Resolve (willpower) or Cohesion (mental stability/sense of self) |
| "Can I Aid my ally?" | Maneuver to place an Aspect, then your ally invokes it on their turn |
