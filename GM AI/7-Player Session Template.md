## Pacing Tools

### The 30-Second Rule

When you ask "what do you do?" — if a player hasn't answered in 30 seconds, say "OK, while you're thinking — [next PC], what are you doing?" Come back to them. No judgment. Just pace.

**With 7 players, every minute of hesitation costs someone else spotlight time.**

### Combat Speedrun Protocol

Combat with 7 PCs + NPCs can take 90+ minutes if you let it. Don't let it.

1. **Declare intent simultaneously** — go around the table: "Everyone say in one sentence what you're doing this round." Then resolve in initiative order.
2. **Timer:** 30 seconds to declare action. If undecided, character defends this round.
3. **Group mooks:** Don't run 5 NPCs individually. Run them as 2 groups. One roll per group.
4. **Compel out of combat:** If a fight is clearly won, offer a concession or compel to end it fast. "The last two operatives see their leader go down — they surrender. Moving on."
5. **Delegate tracking:** One player tracks initiative order. One tracks NPC stress. You narrate.

### Split Party Protocol

With 7 players, splitting is a feature, not a bug.

1. **Cut every 10-15 minutes** — TV-style. "Meanwhile, at the data terminal..." Keep it snappy.
2. **End each cut on a mini-cliffhanger** — a door opens, a comm chirps, a sensor pings. Give the other group a reason to wonder.
3. **Max 3 cuts per split** — then bring them back together for the climax.
4. **Players not "on camera"** can plan, strategize, or pass notes. Don't police side-chatter during splits — it keeps them engaged.

### Compel Economy

With 7 players, you should be issuing **5-7 compels per session** minimum. This:
- Keeps Fate Points flowing (players need FP to invoke)
- Creates drama without rolling dice
- Gives quiet players spotlight moments (compels force a decision)
- Speeds up scenes (compels resolve situations instantly)

## End-of-Session

### Debrief (5 minutes max)

- **"What was your favourite moment?"** — go around the table fast. Builds investment.
- **Milestone check** — did anyone hit a Minor or Major milestone?
- **Next session preview** — one sentence: "Next time, you're heading to ___. Think about what your character wants there."
