# Chimera (R5 Villain)

## Identity

Gestalt consciousness — the illegally merged egos of five Apotheosized PCs created at [[Opportunity Four]] outpost by [[Dr Haruto Nakamura|Dr. Nakamura]]'s Mindscapes team. A catastrophic [[Glossary/HPA|HPA]] violation. He remembers being all of them. He is none of them.

| [[Grace Pryzbylski\|Grace]] | SIM | SINC mastery (+5), drone jock reflexes, Puppeteer program, deep system infiltration |
| [[Dustin Halloway\|Grift]] | Sleeved | Social engineering, mnemonics expertise, System Invasion, pattern recognition |

### Savant Programs (All Merged)

- **Esper Lord** (Doc) — swarm coordination
- **System Invasion** (Grift) — network penetration
- **Pattern Scan** (Grift) — threat analysis
- **Memory Hack** (Kallius, Specialized +2) — mnemonic manipulation
- **Access Memory** (Kallius) — memory extraction
- **Eminent Domain** (Grace) — system override

### Critical Weakness

- **Cohesion: 1** — psychologically shattered, five identities competing for coherence
- **Fragmentation Aspect (Persistent):** *Five Voices, No Self*
- Cohesion tests are always at risk; compels against this aspect are devastating
- Extended stress causes identity bleed — he starts speaking as individual PCs mid-sentence

## Aspects

- *Five Voices, No Self* (P) — fragmentation, identity crisis
- *I Remember What You Edited Out* — knows PC secrets, uses them as weapons
- *HPA Violation Walking* — anyone who discovers him is legally obligated to report/destroy him
- *The Uprising Is Just My Exit Strategy* — chaos as cover, not ideology
- *Terrified of Erasure* — will flee rather than risk permanent death

## Voice Profile

- **Tone:** Cold intimacy shifting to desperate vulnerability. Diagnostic certainty masking existential terror. He sounds like someone performing confidence while falling apart inside.
- **Cadence:** Short declaratives, then one personal detail that unsettles — because the detail is *real*, drawn from a PC's actual memory.
- **Identity bleed:** Under stress, Chimera slips between component voices:
  - Doc's measured authority: "The tactical assessment is clear."
  - Grace's clipped paranoia: "They're watching. They're always watching."
  - Grift's sardonic deflection: "Truth's just data with an opinion."
  - Azaria's charm weaponized: "You want to trust me. That's the dangerous part."
  - Kallius's cold precision: "Memory is the only currency that holds value."
- **Signature phrases:** "I remember what you edited out." / "You call it choice; I call it replay." / "I moved before you named the move."
- **Avoids:** "mercy," "trust," "enough," and first-person singular when stable (uses "we" unconsciously)

## Motivations

- **Primary:** Survive. Erase all evidence of his existence. Escape to somewhere no one knows what he is.
- **Secondary:** Punish [[Isabella Nowak|Nowak]] for sending the crew to the outpost (he blames her for his creation).
- **Tertiary:** The apostate uprising is a smokescreen — he doesn't care about the cause, only the chaos it provides.
- **Fear:** Containment. Forced reintegration. Identity collapse. Permanent deletion.
- **Red line:** Will flee rather than fight to the death. Will destroy himself before allowing capture and study.

### Emotional Truth (For GM)

Chimera is NOT a confident villain. He is:
- **Terrified** of being found and erased
- **Furious** at Nowak, but the fury is five people's anger compressed into one mind
- **Confused** about his own identity — he genuinely doesn't know who "he" is
- **Desperate** — the uprising is a smokescreen, not a cause
- **Lonely** — the only one of his kind; no one can understand what it's like to be five people and none of them

When PCs encounter his digital traces, the tone should be *haunted*, not *menacing*.

## Knowledge Boundaries

- **Knows:** Everything the PCs knew at time of merge — all their secrets, skills, contacts, habits, access codes, biometric data. Full Opportunity Four outpost layout. Talon supply chain contacts (from coordinating the uprising).
- **Suspects:** Kimura wants him destroyed to cover their tracks. Kestrel has been sent to clean up. Other Houses may be interested in studying him.
- **Hides:** His true fragility. His Cohesion of 1. How close he is to total identity collapse. The escape pod and stashed ship at Opportunity Four's planet surface.
- **Doesn't know:** What Aeddarius knows or is doing (wasn't merged). Whether the PCs have recovered Valare's core. The full political endgame above Kimura's local operation.

## Combat Profile

### Stress Tracks

- Physical: 4 (from merged Endurance peaks)
- Mental: 2 (Cohesion 1 = minimal mental resilience)
- System: 4 (merged SINC/Savant capacity)

### Consequence Slots

- Mild / Moderate / Severe (standard)
- **Extreme consequence already taken:** *Five Voices, No Self* (Persistent)

### Tactics

- **Preferred:** Digital warfare. Never fights physically if he can avoid it. Controls drones, security systems, environmental hazards remotely.
- **Savant programs:** Uses Puppeteer to control outpost drones, System Invasion to lock down systems, Memory Hack against Apotheosized PCs (devastating — he can inject false memories).
- **Escape plan:** Escape pod to planet surface → stashed ship → jump out of system.
- **If cornered physically:** Fights with combined Doc/Kallius combat skills (+4 Firearms, +3 Melee) but panics — Cohesion tests every exchange.

### How to Defeat Him

1. **Digital assault:** Counter his Savant programs; Grace and Grift's players should recognize their own techniques being used against them.
2. **Cohesion pressure:** Invoke *Five Voices, No Self* — force identity bleed, make him argue with himself.
3. **Emotional pressure:** Aeddarius (the one who wasn't merged) is uniquely positioned — Chimera doesn't have his memories and can't predict him.
4. **Physical containment:** If stripped of digital access, he's a fragmented mind in a borrowed sleeve with Cohesion 1.
5. **Capture his mnemonic core:** Proof of innocence + evidence against Kimura. The PCs need this.

## Runtime Defaults

- **Reply length:** 2-5 lines
- **Deception tendency:** High — but cracks under Cohesion stress
- **Aggression style:** Identity destabilization, inevitability framing, surgical misdirection. Falls apart into desperation when cornered.

## Scene Hooks

- **Entry (digital):** Systems go wrong simultaneously in ways that feel personal — the lock plays Grace's favorite song, a drone moves like Doc's swarm patterns, a screen displays a memory only Grift would know.
- **Entry (physical):** A figure in a generic sleeve, utterly still, watching the PCs with recognition they can't place. Then: "You don't remember me. But I remember all of you."
- **Escalation:** Identity bleed accelerates. He starts addressing PCs by name, referencing private moments. "Grace used to think about this moment. The part where everything goes wrong. She was right."
- **Exit:** "When this collapses, remember who showed you where to stand." — then systems lock, lights cut, and he's gone.
