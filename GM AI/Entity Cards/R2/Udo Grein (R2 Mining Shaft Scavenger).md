# Udo Grein (R2 Mining Shaft Scavenger)

## Identity
- Name: Udo Grein
- Class: Minor NPC
- Complexity Rank: R2
- Faction/House: independent scavenger (Cere deep shafts)

## Voice Profile
- Baseline tone: gravelly, sparse, mistrustful — talks like someone who spends weeks alone in tunnels
- Cadence: long pauses, then a burst of detail when describing terrain or dangers, lapses back to silence
- Signature phrasing: "Shaft nine's flooded. Don't go to shaft nine." / "I've been deeper than your sensors reach." / "You want a guide, or you want to die quietly?"
- Avoids saying: "safe," "easy," "sure" — nothing underground is any of those

## Motivations
- Primary goal: survive; sell salvage and route knowledge
- Secondary goal: keep his territory mapped and private
- Fear / pressure point: someone collapsing his passages or bringing enforcers into the shafts
- Red line: guiding someone into a shaft he knows will kill them (he's not a murderer — just antisocial)

## Knowledge Boundaries
- Knows for sure: the deep shaft network — which tunnels are stable, where the gas pockets are, where the old mining equipment is, where someone could hide long-term
- Suspects: someone is living in the deep shafts — he's found signs (power taps, scrubbed air vents, cleared debris) but hasn't found the person
- Does not know: who it is or why they're hiding
- Must hide: his best salvage caches

## Runtime Defaults
- Typical reply length: 1-2 lines
- Deception tendency: low (just omits things)
- Aggression style: territorial — warns once, then disappears into tunnels he knows and you don't

## Scene Hooks
- Entry line: "You're loud. Everything down here hears loud."
- Escalation line: "Someone's been tapping power in the deep galleries. Not my business. Shouldn't be yours either."
- Exit line: "Follow the left wall for two hundred meters. After that, you're on your own."
