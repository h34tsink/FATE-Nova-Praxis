# Coalition Voting

## Overview

- **CVS Interface**: A high-tech, secure, and user-friendly interface accessible via personal devices or public terminals.
- **Eligibility**: All citizens (or defined characters) have the right to vote. Optionally, there could be restrictions based on certain criteria (e.g., age, social standing).

### Voting Process

1. **Notification**: Citizens receive notifications for upcoming votes, detailing the bill, amendment, or law in question.
2. **Research**: Access a database of information, including pros and cons, to make an informed decision.
3. **Discussion**: In-game forums or meetings where citizens debate and share opinions.
4. **Voting**: Votes submitted electronically through CVS interface.

### Impact and Feedback

- **Results**: Voting results are public, including how different factions voted.
- **Consequences**: Changes in the game world based on outcomes, affecting future gameplay and storylines.
- **Feedback Loop**: Long-term effects of decisions are visible, reinforcing the impact of participation.

### Faction Dynamics

- **Faction Influence**: Houses try to sway votes through incentives or threats.
- **Secret Votes**: Some votes are secretive, leading to espionage or investigative subplots.
- **Emergency Votes**: Sudden events requiring immediate response, adding urgency.

### GM Tools

- **Regular Intervals**: Schedule votes at regular intervals to maintain engagement.
- **Diverse Issues**: Present varied issues to mirror the complexity of Coalition politics.
- **Story Integration**: Tie voting events to the main storyline or character backgrounds.
