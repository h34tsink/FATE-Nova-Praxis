# Coalition Houses

A comprehensive reference for all Coalition Houses in Nova Praxis.

## How Houses Work

The "free states" known as the Houses act as a combination of nation, employer, and family.

### Citizenship in a House

- **Birthright** — Every citizen of the Coalition is born into a House.
- **Contractual relationship** — Citizens receive a comfortable minimum standard of living in exchange for furthering their House's interests.
- **Rep-Rating** — As citizens contribute to their House, their [[Glossary/Rep-Rating]] increases, granting access to a more luxurious lifestyle.

### Governance & Autonomy

- **Self-governance** — Each House has its own laws and largely governs itself outside of [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] jurisdiction.
- **Consolidation Concord** — Grants Houses dominion over cultural standards laws, especially areas like morality, religion, and augmentation.
- **Internal affairs** — The House determines its own rank system and manages internal disputes, unless they break Coalition law.

### Law Enforcement

- **Protectorate's role** — Enforces House laws similarly to how it enforces Coalition laws.
- **Jurisdiction** — Only House members are subject to House laws, and only a Centurion who is also a House member can enforce them.
- **Priority** — House law enforcement is always secondary to Coalition law enforcement.

## House Cipriani

**Primary Influence:** Mercury  
**Secondary Influence:** Cygnus Alpha  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist

### Market Discounts
- Atmospheric Flying Vehicles
- Energy (Broadcast Power Generators, etc.)
- CISTs
- Transportation

### Overview
House Cipriani was a **founding force** behind the Coalition, leveraging its immense assets to gain a **near-monopoly on energy production** from Mercury. They are known for their **ruthless business tactics** and strong **Purist ideology**. Cipriani maintains a long-standing **feud with House Dalianis** and is increasingly competing with House Kimura in the virtual vacation industry. They often show **preferential treatment to Purist apostates** for off-the-books operations.

### Detailed Description
Formally known as The Cipriani Group, House Cipriani was instrumental in the Coalition's formation, stemming from a partnership of three major corporations: Italian vehicle manufacturer Giordino Automotive, Inc., Egyptian clothing company Abbasi Textiles, and Saudi Arabian energy giant Tahan Power. They hold a **near-monopoly on energy production** through extensive antimatter facilities on Mercury, operated by subsidiaries like Specular Spread and Infinity Squared. Additionally, they are leading manufacturers of compilers, personal vehicles, CIST technology, public transportation, and clothing. Their subsidiary Icarus Interplanetary manages all interplanetary import and export.

Cipriani is characterized by its **ruthless and often self-serving business practices**, rarely entering contracts that don't favor them and always including escape clauses. Their dominance in the energy market allows them to influence deals involving power-consuming tech. While they produce both utilitarian and highly luxurious products, they have little involvement in military manufacturing, contracting out armor plating and transplasidium production to House Tsarya, with whom they share a tight alliance.

Ideologically, Cipriani is largely **Purist**, which can strain relationships with transhuman-leaning Houses. They have a long-standing, ego-driven feud with House Dalianis over Cygnus Alpha's resources, despite mutual reliance for starship components and transport. They share some business ventures and territory with House Silva, acting as "partners in crime" due to common enemies like Dalianis. However, they are in aggressive competition with House Kimura in the virtual vacation industry, leading to virtual sabotage campaigns. For covert operations, Cipriani often recruits **Purist apostates** due to shared bio-conservative ideals and a perceived lack of ties to other Houses, offering a relatively easy path to membership once patronage is secured.

## House Dalianis

**Primary Influence:** Olympus  
**Secondary Influence:** Luna  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist

### Market Discounts
- Starships
- Rail/Coil Guns
- Combat Drones

### Overview
House Dalianis, once the most powerful corporate partnership on Earth, is now the **foremost manufacturer of starships** and operates the galaxy's largest shipyard, Ansari Station. They are aggressively **expansionist**, focusing on jump drive technology and claiming new territories. While predominantly **Purist**, their pragmatic leadership prioritizes results. Dalianis maintains strong alliances with House Kimura and Jinzhan, but is in constant **feud with Cipriani and Tsarya**.

### Detailed Description
House Dalianis was formed from leading European defense contractors and Greek R&D firms (German Keppler Shipyards, British Macrotech Robotics, French Habarcq Armaments, and Greek Promethic Technologies). They are the **foremost manufacturer of starships** and operate Ansari Station, the largest space-based shipyard in the galaxy, located at E-M Lagrange Point 3 near Luna. Dalianis also specializes in rail/coil guns and combat drones.

Their primary agenda is **expansion**, actively dedicated to improving jump drive technology and expanding the jump ring network. They aggressively claim new territories under the guise of "exploratory R&D," often meeting resistance with force through their security subsidiary, Lighthouse. Dalianis seeks to gain public and legitimate power, particularly by increasing influence in Olympus, the seat of Coalition government. While predominantly **Purist**, their leadership is pragmatic and results-oriented, making them open to working with posthumans if effective.

Dalianis has strong alliances with House Kimura, trading computer systems for control of the civilian drone market, and with House Jinzhan, cooperating on Earth quarantine efforts and shared development on Mars. However, they have significant disputes with House Cipriani over Cygnus Alpha's iron supplies and Cipriani's energy monopoly. They also feud with House Tsarya over volatile materials in the Ceres ice fields. Notably, Thetis Revival Labs, a Silva-run resleeving facility on Phobos, offers bonuses to Dalianis members for switching sleeves for specialized jobs.

## House Jinzhan

**Primary Influence:** Shouye  
**Secondary Influence:** Earth Quarantine  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist

### Market Discounts
- Construction
- Security Technology & Services
- Food
- Explosives
- Ship & Vehicle Weaponry

### Overview
House Jinzhan, originating from a Chinese government megacorporation, specializes in **construction, security technology, and weaponry**. They are a primary force enforcing the **Earth quarantine**. Jinzhan society is deeply **Purist, isolationist, and technophobic**, exhibiting a rise in traditional religions. They conduct extensive research into **planet-destroying weapons** and prefer to hire experienced mercenaries, often avoiding those with apostate members.

### Detailed Description
House Jinzhan emerged from the former Chinese government's megacorporation, focusing on **construction, security technology, food, explosives, and ship/vehicle weaponry**. They play a crucial role in enforcing the **Earth quarantine**, operating facilities like Primatech Labs at E-M Lagrange Point 4, which manufactures ordinance and armaments for quarantine enforcement ships and kill-sats.

Jinzhan society is characterized by strong **Purist ideals**, a resurgence of traditional religions like Buddhism, and a general **isolationist and technophobic** stance. They rarely officially acknowledge apostates and deal swiftly and violently with any interference. A significant portion of the House's resources is dedicated to researching methods and weapons capable of **destroying entire planets** to eradicate the technophage on Earth. While the Vulcan swarms (nanomachine technology) were crucial for rapid habitat creation during the Exodus, Jinzhan rarely uses them now, making them difficult for other Houses to acquire. For targeted operations, Jinzhan typically hires **experienced mercenaries**, often screening out crews with apostate members.

They maintain an alliance with House Dalianis, particularly concerning the Earth quarantine and the expansion and maintenance of Martian settlements like Olympus and Helios. Jinzhan was also paid with a city on Sagitta for Dalianis's extensive use of their Vulcan swarms.

## House Kimura

**Primary Influence:** Chengdan  
**Secondary Influence:** Olympus  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Transhuman

### Market Discounts
- Agents
- Computers & Computer Software
- Cybersleeves
- Cybernetic Augmentations
- Virtuality Software
- Recon Warframes

### Overview
House Kimura is the **leading manufacturer of computer hardware/software, cybersleeves, and cyber-augmentations**, formed from formerly rival tech giants. They dominate the **virtual environment market** through VirtuVac and employ a **massive spy network of SIMs**. Politically **liberal**, Kimura actively opposes the Humanity Preservation Act. For covert operations, they frequently utilize their subsidiary Crimson & Sons, which has ties to the Red Sun (former Yakuza), often preferring **transhuman or Red Sun-affiliated operatives**.

### Detailed Description
House Kimura was formed from the merger of two formerly fierce competitors in computer hardware (Japanese Hiske Electronics) and software (South Korean Aria Softworks), realizing that combining strengths would lead to market dominance. This led to a boom in innovation, allowing them to produce agents, augmentations, sleeves, and drones more cheaply and efficiently without sacrificing quality. Kimura is the **leading manufacturer of computer equipment, software, cybersleeves, and cyber-augmentations**. They also maintain a strong position in the warframe market, specializing in lighter, more agile **recon warframes** with superior sensor systems, having originated warframe technology through Aria Softworks.

Their subsidiary, VirtuVac, is the **number one creator of persistent and temporary virtual environments**, including illicit ones. Kimura aggressively defends this market, engaging in virtual sabotage campaigns against encroaching competitors like House Cipriani, often using Savants and CIST agents. Due to the high market saturation of their equipment and software, Kimura standardizes **backdoors for remote technical access** and extensively employs **SIMs** for on-site assistance, software development, testing, and a **massive, unseen spy network** operating within meshes and the Extranet.

Politically, Kimura is **extremely liberal** and strongly opposes the Humanity Preservation Act, generally preferring to avoid violence. Many Kimurans adhere to the Astralist faith, and their Astralists often arbitrate contracts and peace treaties. They have significant alliances with House Dalianis (trading computer systems for civilian drone market control) and House Silva (providing electronics). Kimura has also been quietly trying to increase its starship production to compete more effectively with Dalianis. For their more violent or covert operations, Kimura works through its subsidiary Crimson & Sons, which is connected to the Red Sun (former Yakuza), and often recruits **transhumans or Red Sun affiliates**.

## House Silva

**Primary Influence:** Luna  
**Secondary Influence:** Phobos  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Transhuman

### Market Discounts
- Biosleeves
- Biotech Augmentations
- PAc Weaponry

### Overview
House Silva originated from non-military Alliance elements, becoming experts in **biosleeves, biotech augmentations, and PAc weapons**. They openly disregard the **Humanity Preservation Act**, conducting genetic and bio-experimentation on indentured servants. Silva operates strictly as a corporation, seeing citizens as employees, and is known for its "reset" clauses in mercenary contracts. They hold alliances with Houses Tsarya, Cipriani, and Kimura, but share a mutual enemy in Dalianis.

### Detailed Description
Silva Corp was formed from non-military elements of the Alliance, specifically American weapons manufacturer Valkyrie Industrial and Canadian biotech research firm Biogenics United. They became masters of **biosleeves, biotech augmentations, and PAc weapons**. Their subsidiary, Valkyrie Interplanetary, holds most of the Coalition's arms contracts and covertly supplies apostates to ensure continued demand for weaponry.

Silva has little regard for the **Humanity Preservation Act** and has faced sanctions for its genetic and bio-experimentation, often using indentured servants or "administrative leave" employees as test subjects. They operate as a strict corporation, viewing citizens primarily as employees, and are known for including "reset" clauses in mercenary contracts, allowing them to withhold mission information from resleeved operatives. Silva is also involved in the Genesis Project on Mars, focusing on creating self-sustaining ecosystems, and operates Thetis Revival Labs on Phobos, considered the galaxy's best resleeving facility.

Silva maintains alliances with House Tsarya, leveraging their illegal contacts for smuggling biotech materials, weapons to apostates, or test subjects. They are also an ally to House Cipriani, sharing business ventures and territory, particularly in the city of Rho on Luna, and finding ways to destabilize their mutual enemy, House Dalianis, with whom Silva has a rivalry over superior particle accelerator weapons. They also ally with House Kimura for electronics. Silva prefers to hire mercenary crews with a reputation for quick, efficient, and discreet mission completion, strictly avoiding anyone who has exposed House secrets. Unofficially, House Silva uses the apostate enclave in the Naesock Processing Hub near Neptune as a black market contact point.

## House Tsarya

**Primary Influence:** Proch  
**Secondary Influence:** Luna  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist

### Market Discounts
- Vehicular & Personal Armor
- Ground Vehicles
- Assault Warframes

### Overview
House Tsarya was founded by corporations known for **war profiteering**, specializing in **vehicular and personal armor, ground vehicles, and assault warframes**. They actively **instigate or fabricate conflicts** to boost demand for their military goods. While traditionally **Purist** with strong religious members, their leadership has pragmatically embraced hiring the **Apotheosized** for dangerous, expendable roles, causing internal friction. Tsarya has strong ties with Cipriani and Silva, but competes fiercely with Kimura and Dalianis.

### Detailed Description
House Tsarya was formed from two pre-Exodus corporations known for **war profiteering**: Russian armor fabrication company Risel Armory (guilty) and Indian vehicle manufacturer Adani Motors (compensated for silence). From its inception, Tsarya has been the **top manufacturer of vehicular and personal armor, ground vehicles, and assault warframes**. They reverse-engineered Kimuran warframe designs to create their own "Assault Class" warframes.

Tsarya leadership **profits directly from conflict**, often instigating or fabricating attacks from apostate or remnant forces to create demand for their military products. They sometimes assist House Silva in staging real attacks using their contacts within rebel forces. While they appear lenient in their citizenship application process for apostates, actual acceptance is very difficult. Historically, Tsarya leans towards **Purist ideals** with many members adhering to traditional religions that disapprove of augmentation. However, their leadership has recently shifted towards accepting and hiring the **Apotheosized** for dangerous, expendable jobs, leading to internal friction with traditionalists.

They maintain trade agreements with House Cipriani and a less legitimate relationship with House Silva, using Silva's black market contacts to smuggle apostates. Tsarya's main competitor in the warframe market is House Kimura, with Tsarya excelling at heavy assault frames and Kimura at recon frames. They also compete with House Dalianis over volatile materials in the Ceres ice fields, though Dalianis aids Tsarya in developing PAc-resistant armors to counter Silva's market position. Tsarya's largest manufacturing facility in the Sol system is located in Asola on Luna, a city known for its poor blue-collar workers and strong Purist tradition.

## Apostate

**Primary Influence:** Independent/Non-Coalition  
**Secondary Influence:** Underground Networks & Black Markets  
**Evolutionary Agenda:** To live outside Coalition control and maintain independence from the corporate Houses, forging their own path.

### Market Discounts
- Black Market Goods
- Underground Services
- Untraceable Equipment
- Apostate Networks
- Independent Technology

### Overview
You are a ghost. Little record of your life exists. You do not show up on most Coalition records and live mostly as an apostate. You have found a way to make it in this world without being tracked by the Coalition.

### Detailed Description
Apostates are individuals who live completely outside the Coalition system, maintaining no official ties to any of the major Houses. They exist as ghosts in the corporate-dominated galaxy, with minimal records and maximum freedom. Living in the shadows of civilized space, apostates have developed extensive underground networks, black market connections, and alternative economies that operate beyond Coalition oversight.

While apostates lack the resources and protection of House membership, they compensate with unparalleled freedom of movement and action. They are not beholden to corporate interests, political agendas, or House feuds, allowing them to work with anyone willing to pay. This independence comes at a cost - apostates must be entirely self-reliant, cannot access many Coalition services, and live with the constant risk of discovery by House security forces.

Apostates often serve as intermediaries between hostile Houses, conduct operations too sensitive for House operatives, or provide services in territories where House presence would be politically problematic. Their networks include everything from information brokers and black market dealers to independent tech specialists and underground transport services.

### Special Rules
- **Free Stunt:** Non-Citizen
- **No Rep Rating:** Apostates do not have a Rep Rating
- **Apostate Favor Bonus:** +2 Assets tests for Favor requests from fellow apostates
