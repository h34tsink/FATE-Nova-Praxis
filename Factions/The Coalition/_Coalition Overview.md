## Summary

The **Coalition of Free States** is the primary governmental body of humanity in the post-Exodus era, formed from the consolidation of seven major corporate Houses following the technophage catastrophe on Earth. The Coalition maintains authority through a combination of democratic institutions, corporate influence, and military enforcement via the Protectorate.

## Governance Structure

### The Consolidation Concord

The foundation of Coalition authority, establishing the framework for government operations and defining the relationship between the Coalition Authority and member Houses.

### Coalition Authority

The Coalition is the ultimate governing force in key domains as established by the **Consolidation Concord**.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Protectorate Stewardship** - Wields the Protectorate as an expression of the populace's desires
- **Infrastructure Oversight** - Maintains jump ring network and critical infrastructure
- **Citizen Welfare** - Guarantees all citizens a baseline quality of life
- **Healthcare Management** - Ensures medical services and enforces the Humanity Preservation Act (HPA)
- **Equal Representation** - Certifies no group is silenced regardless of race, sex, religion, or creed
- **Economic Oversight** - Supervised by InterRep, they oversee the Coalition economy
- **Inter-House Diplomacy** - Resolves disputes between Houses and can dissolve them if required
- **Ministry Management** - Runs Ministries ensuring daily governance proceeds smoothly

### [[Coalition Voting|Coalition Voting System (CVS)]]

The Coalition employs direct democracy through the Coalition Voting System, allowing all citizens to vote on legislation, amendments, and major policy decisions through secure personal devices or public terminals.

**Process:**
1. **Notification** - Citizens receive detailed information on upcoming votes
2. **Research** - Access to comprehensive databases with pros/cons analysis
3. **Discussion** - Public forums and meetings for debate
4. **Voting** - Electronic submission of votes
5. **Implementation** - Changes enacted based on voting outcomes

**Influences:**
- Faction interests may sway votes through incentives or pressure
- Some votes remain secret, creating espionage opportunities
- Emergency votes require immediate response during crises

### The Protectorate

The Coalition's military and law enforcement branch, responsible for:
- Internal security and law enforcement
- External defense against threats
- Earth quarantine enforcement
- Hunting transhuman extremists and HPA violators
- Maintaining order in contested territories

See: [[The Protectorate/]] for detailed structure

## Member Houses

The Coalition is built on seven major corporate Houses, each wielding enormous economic and political power:

### [[Houses/House Cipriani|House Cipriani]]
- **Primary Influence:** Mercury
- **Specialization:** Energy monopoly (antimatter), compilers, vehicles, CIST technology
- **Ideology:** Strongly Purist
- **Key Assets:** Specular Spread (Mercury operations), Infinity Squared
- **Alliances:** House Tsarya (strong), House Silva (partners)
- **Rivalries:** House Dalianis (bitter feud), House Kimura (virtual vacation competition)

### [[Houses/House Dalianis|House Dalianis]]
- **Primary Influence:** Olympus, Ansari Station
- **Specialization:** Starships, rail/coil guns, combat drones
- **Ideology:** Purist, but pragmatic results-oriented
- **Key Assets:** Ansari Station (largest shipyard), Promethic Collective
- **Alliances:** House Kimura (strong), House Jinzhan (Earth quarantine cooperation)
- **Rivalries:** House Cipriani (Cygnus Alpha dispute), House Tsarya (Ceres resources)

### [[Houses/House Jinzhan|House Jinzhan]]
- **Primary Influence:** Shouye (food production)
- **Specialization:** Construction, security technology, weaponry, explosives
- **Ideology:** Strongly Purist, isolationist, technophobic
- **Key Assets:** Primatech Labs (E-M L4), Earth quarantine enforcement
- **Focus:** Planet-destroying weapons research to eradicate technophage
- **Alliances:** House Dalianis (Earth quarantine, Mars development)

### [[Houses/House Kimura|House Kimura]]
- **Primary Influence:** Chengdan
- **Specialization:** Computer hardware/software, cybersleeves, cyber-augmentations, recon warframes
- **Ideology:** Transhuman, politically liberal, opposes HPA
- **Key Assets:** VirtuVac (virtual environments), massive SIM spy network
- **Connections:** Red Sun (Yakuza) through Crimson & Sons subsidiary
- **Alliances:** House Dalianis (computer systems/drones), House Silva (electronics)
- **Rivalries:** House Cipriani (virtual vacation sabotage), House Tsarya (warframe market)

### [[Houses/House Silva|House Silva]]
- **Primary Influence:** Luna (Serro)
- **Specialization:** Biosleeves, biotech augmentations, PAc weapons
- **Ideology:** Transhuman, openly disregards HPA
- **Key Assets:** Valkyrie Interplanetary (arms), Thetis Revival Labs (best resleeving), Genesis Project (Mars)
- **Practices:** Genetic experimentation on indentured servants, "reset" contracts for mercenaries
- **Alliances:** House Tsarya (smuggling), House Cipriani (mutual enemy Dalianis), House Kimura (electronics)

### [[Houses/House Tsarya|House Tsarya]]
- **Primary Influence:** Proch, Luna (Asola)
- **Specialization:** Armor (vehicular/personal), ground vehicles, assault warframes
- **Ideology:** Traditionally Purist, increasingly accepting Apotheosized for expendable roles
- **Practices:** War profiteering, fabricating conflicts to boost military sales
- **Key Assets:** Asola manufacturing (Luna), assault warframe production
- **Alliances:** House Cipriani (strong), House Silva (black market contacts)
- **Rivalries:** House Kimura (warframe market), House Dalianis (Ceres resources)

### [[Hidden Agendas/House Turin|House Turin]]
- **Primary Influence:** New Turin (Mars)
- **Specialization:** Criminal operations, smuggling, black market
- **Ideology:** Profit above all, Russian Mafia (Bratva) heritage
- **Status:** Criminal House operating with unofficial tolerance
- **Operations:** Smuggling, assassination, information brokering, vice operations

## Key Policies and Laws

### Human Personhood Accord (HPA)

The most controversial Coalition law, restricting augmentation and transhuman development:
- Limits on consciousness uploading and modification
- Restrictions on AI development and integration
- Defines boundaries of "human" personhood
- Enforced by Protectorate with severe penalties
- Strongly supported by Purist Houses (Cipriani, Jinzhan, Tsarya)
- Opposed by Transhuman Houses (Kimura, Silva)

### Coalition Identification (CID)

Required for all Coalition citizens:
- Tracks reputation (Rep) and citizenship status
- Enables access to Coalition services and infrastructure
- Monitored through mesh networks and checkpoints
- Those without CIDs are classified as Apostates (~20% of humanity)

### Reputation Economy

The primary social currency of the Coalition:
- Rep determines social standing, job opportunities, and privileges
- Earned through contributions to House or Coalition
- Lost through violations, failures, or social transgressions
- Different Rep levels grant access to different resources and opportunities

## Major Territories

### Sol System
- **Mars (Olympus)** - Coalition capital and government seat
- **Mercury** - Cipriani energy production monopoly
- **Luna** - Major industrial centers (Serro, Asola, Rho)
- **Earth Lagrange Points** - Strategic stations (Vantage, Ansari, Primatech, Sentinel)
- **Outer System** - Resource extraction, research stations, apostate havens

### Exoplanet Colonies
- **Proch** - First colony, Tsarya influence
- **Shouye** - Jinzhan food monopoly
- **Chengdan** - Kimura development, posthuman emergence concerns
- **Sanctuary** - Apostate-controlled, growing independence movement
- **Cygnus Alpha** - Mining, Cipriani/Dalianis competition
- **Sagitta** - Expansion colony, mysterious magnetic phenomena

## Ideological Divisions

### [[Ideologies/Purists|Purists]] (~53% of Coalition)
- Prefer minimal augmentation
- Support HPA restrictions
- Cultural default in Coalition society
- Dominant in Houses: Cipriani, Dalianis, Jinzhan, Tsarya

### [[Ideologies/Transhumans|Transhumans]]
- Advocate augmentation and enhancement
- Oppose HPA limitations
- Push boundaries of human modification
- Dominant in Houses: Kimura, Silva

### [[Ideologies/Apostates|Apostates]] (~20% of humanity)
- Live outside Coalition system
- No CID or official citizenship
- Black market economy and trade networks
- Independent settlements throughout space

### [[Ideologies/Purifiers|Purifiers]]
- Violent anti-augmentation extremists
- Pursue terrorist attacks on augmented individuals
- Hunted by Protectorate
- Small but dangerous radical faction

## Threats and Challenges

### The Technophage
- Destroyed Earth civilization
- Remains active on Earth's surface
- Coalition maintains strict quarantine
- Jinzhan researches planet-destroying weapons as solution
- Fear of spread drives security paranoia

### Apostate Independence Movements
- Sanctuary pushing for formal independence
- Uranus Colony operates independently
- Venus Gondolin enclave threatens energy markets
- Growing apostate networks challenge Coalition monopoly

### House Shadow Wars
- Covert conflicts between Houses
- Rho on Luna as major flashpoint
- Corporate espionage and sabotage
- Olympus political intrigue
- Use of deniable mercenaries and black ops

### Transhuman Emergence
- Chengdan posthumans (The Swift, The Builders, The Seers)
- Seraphim collective at Elysium (ultimate transhumans)
- HPA violations becoming more common
- Technology outpacing legal frameworks

### Hidden Agendas
Multiple secret organizations operate within and against Coalition:
- [[Hidden Agendas/The Fallen|The Fallen]] - Mad Savants spreading chaos
- [[Hidden Agendas/Talons of Freedom|Talons of Freedom]] - US military remnants
- [[Hidden Agendas/Children of Dionysus|Children of Dionysus]] - Anarchist psychedelics
- [[Hidden Agendas/Red Sun|Red Sun]] - Modern Yakuza serving Kimura
- [[Hidden Agendas/Rings of Olympus|Rings of Olympus]] - Mars criminal syndicate
