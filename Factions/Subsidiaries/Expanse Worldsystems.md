# Expanse Worldsystems

**Summary:** A frontier exploitation and resource-acquisition subsidiary of [[Factions/Houses/House Tsarya|House Tsarya]], headed by [[Characters/Named NPCs/Isabella Nowak|Isabella Nowak]]. Expanse handles the kind of work Tsarya wants done at arm's length — black-zone salvage, off-grid prospecting, and contract operations that need to stay off the official ledger.

## Lore

Expanse Worldsystems is a Tsaryan subsidiary that operates the way most subsidiaries do: it gives the parent House plausible deniability. When Tsarya needs a job done in a place the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] would prefer they not be operating, or against an asset technically belonging to a rival House, the contract goes through Expanse.

The subsidiary's official cover is **deep-frontier resource extraction and outpost development** — believable enough to explain its budget, its ships, and its tendency to charter independent crews for one-off jobs. Inside Tsarya it is understood as a deniable operations arm.

[[Characters/Named NPCs/Isabella Nowak|Isabella Nowak]] runs Expanse from within the Tsaryan executive structure. She is high enough in the parent House to authorize Expanse contracts directly, low enough that her name does not appear in Coalition-facing Tsaryan filings. This is the architecture that lets her commission the [[Characters/Named NPCs/Chimera|Chimera]] contract without leaving Tsaryan fingerprints on the operation.

## Operations Pattern

Expanse runs through brokers like [[Characters/Named NPCs/Kestrel|Kestrel]] — talent acquisition specialists whose entire job is to keep the client's identity firewalled from the contractor. A typical Expanse contract:

1. Broker (e.g. Kestrel) approaches a crew with an offer
2. Crew is told the client is "a Tsaryan subsidiary" but never named
3. Job runs against a target — frequently another House's outpost
4. Payment routes through the broker; the crew never speaks directly to Expanse

When something goes wrong, Expanse vanishes. The broker takes the heat or disappears, and Tsarya disclaims any knowledge of the operation.
