# Ardent

**Summary:** Corporate manufacturer of the [[Characters/NPCs/Drones and Creatures/Ardent Lonestar Armed Responder|Lonestar Armed Responder]] line of humanoid security drones — the workhorse platform behind corporate-lobby and high-security drone deployments across the Coalition.

## Lore

Ardent is the brand stamped on the chestplate of every Lonestar that walks a corporate lobby in the Coalition. The company specialises in humanoid security drones — Gamma-class, restricted-tier — built for the markets where intimidation matters as much as capability. The Lonestar LS-7 platform is their flagship: two metres tall, heavily armoured, equipped with a Heavy Coil Assault Rifle, and bearing whatever corporate sigil the buyer pays to have cosmetic-customized onto its plating.

Ardent field teams handle in-house variants and contingency loadouts directly. The unofficial LS-7R rail variant — a coil-to-rail-rifle swap deployed against [[Centurion|Centurion-class]] threats and armoured boarders — is an Ardent field-team modification, not a separate factory model.

The company itself is a background corporate actor, not a faction with hidden agendas. Its drones are everywhere; the executives behind it are nowhere the PCs are likely to meet. Treat Ardent as the *brand on the threat*, not as an antagonist in its own right.

## Mechanics

**Type:** Corporate manufacturer (drone platforms)
**Tier:** Gamma drones (restricted)
**Flagship product:** [[Characters/NPCs/Drones and Creatures/Ardent Lonestar Armed Responder|Lonestar LS-7 Armed Responder]]
**Variants:** [[Characters/NPCs/Drones and Creatures/Ardent Lonestar Armed Responder Rail Variant|LS-7R Rail Variant]] (field modification)

### Use in Play

- Drop Lonestars (or pairs/squads of them) into corporate lobbies, spaceports, House facilities, and high-security checkpoints.
- Use the LS-7R when the PCs bring power armour or vehicle-class threats to the encounter — the rail rifle exists specifically to punch through them.
- Voice-Stress Analyzer makes Lonestars effective in interrogation and access-control scenes, not just combat.

## Continuity

- **Source:** Vault-original (referenced from drone notes)
- **Status:** Active corporate actor; no known hidden agenda
- **Assumptions:** Ardent's parent House (if any) is unestablished. Treat as an independent or cross-House security contractor unless a session explicitly assigns it to a House.
