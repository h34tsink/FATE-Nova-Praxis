## 1) Birrin Species Sheet (Nova Praxis-ready)

### Snapshot
- **Type:** Sapient alien species (biological morph/sleeve)
- **Homeworld:** **Chriirah** — ocean-heavy hothouse world; superheated equatorial band commonly called **“the Kiln.”**
- **Body plan:** Hexapod (six primary limbs) + **two dorsal “radiator” appendages** (adult non-flight; heat shedding + social display).
- **Mouth:** Beak with **quad-opening** bite (can open in four directions).
- **Senses:** Eyestalk vision + strong “structure sense” (vibration/pressure awareness through surfaces).
- **Theme:** Alien-in-shape, *plausible-in-function*, culture-heavy, uncomfortable to anthropomorphize (perfect for “first true sapients”).

### Play a Birrin as…
- **Offworld Envoy:** first-contact diplomat who is *not* here for vibes.
- **Relic Warden:** trained to contain ancient hazards (Void-Cores, Echo sites, Pneuma bleed).
- **Probe Navigator:** long-range scout lineage (their exploration programs are older than most human polities).
- **Displaced Survivor:** post-catastrophe diaspora running dark across “quiet” routes.

### Pick 2 Aspects (1 High Concept + 1 Trouble)
**High Concept (choose one)**
- *Birrin Diplomatic Scout From the Kiln’s Edge*
- *Birrin Void-Custodian (Relic Warden)*
- *Birrin Probe-Navigator, Raised on Silent Routes*
- *Birrin Ecologist Who Treats Ships Like Biospheres*

**Trouble (choose one)**
- *Human Environments Are Cold, Dry, and Hostile*
- *Your “Direct Speech” Reads Like Threat Display*
- *I Am Responsible for Containment (Even When No One Listens)*
- *The Galaxy Expects “Alien Wisdom” — I Have Trauma and Rules*

### Narrative permissions (things you can simply do)
- **Split attention:** eyestalks can watch multiple angles while acting.
- **Brace & move:** hexapod stance = great stability; hard to knock down.
- **Radiator display:** dorsal appendages can vent heat and broadcast posture-signals.
- **Beak precision:** fine manipulation can be done with the beak (tools, latches, sample work).

### Species Stunts (pick 1–3)
1) **Independent Eyes**  
   +2 to **Notice** when scanning for threats while you’re already engaged in another action.

2) **Radiator Display**  
   Once per scene, you may use **Rapport** or **Provoke** with +2 *without speaking* (line of sight required).

3) **Six-Legged Skirmisher**  
   +2 to **Athletics** to resist knockdown, keep footing, or brace against forced movement.

4) **Quad-Beak Snap**  
   +2 to **Fight** for a close natural-weapon strike when your hands/forelimbs are occupied or restrained.

5) **Kilnwise**  
   +2 to **Survival** in extreme heat / thermal hazards and when planning routes around them.

### Built-in Compels (free Fate Point hooks)
- **Thermal Limits:** sealed, cold, or dry environments exhaust you; overheating is still a risk in suit failures.
- **Ergonomics Hate You:** human gear and architecture don’t fit; everything requires mods or awkward use.
- **Eyestalk Tells:** stress makes your attention readable to sharp observers.
- **Alien Social Math:** your “neutral” posture can accidentally escalate a scene.

## 2) Birrin & “That Weird Layer” (slow-burn reveal; keep it secretive)
You *can* connect the Birrin to Pneuma/Mimir/Echo lore — but don’t hand it to the players on a silver platter.

### What the PCs see early (what Birrin will admit)
- The Concord treats certain anomalies like **biohazards**: quarantine, decon, and “do not touch.”
- They use **resonance instrumentation** (vibration, harmonics, structural tapping) that looks like science, ritual, or both.
- They’re **polite**… and absolutely not transparent about *why*.

### What the Birrin actually know (GM-only truth, reveal later)
Birrin doctrine recognizes a deeper “interaction layer” (call it *Pneuma* if/when the PCs earn that word).  
Birrin can engage it through **trained, stabilized resonance methods**, but they consider untrained human contact **unpredictable and potentially destructive**—physically, mentally, and spiritually.

They will not explain this quickly. Not out of malice—out of **containment culture** and hard-learned fear.

### Reveal Tracks (pick one, or mix)
Use these as *future* discoveries, not Session-1 exposition.

**Track A — The Word Comes Late**
- Early: “anomaly,” “contamination,” “resonance spill,” “threshold event.”
- Later: a trusted Birrin uses the forbidden internal term *Pneuma* only in a sealed room.

**Track B — The PCs Prove They Can Be Safe**
- The Concord shares *one* containment step after the PCs follow protocol under stress.
- Each time the PCs act responsibly, they earn another “piece of the method.”

**Track C — The Echo Teaches**
- The PCs learn the layer exists by experience: dreams, pattern bleed, spiritual consequences.
- The Birrin react with dread: “You touched it. You shouldn’t be able to touch it.”

### Practical GM Rule (optional)
Until the PCs learn Pneuma exists, treat it mechanically as:
- **Unknown Anomaly** (clocks, consequences, weird scene aspects)
- **Resonance Containment** (Birrin can create advantages that reduce clock ticks)

## 3) Birrin Faction Sheet (Fate Fractal)
**Faction Name:** The **Chriirahn Concord** (offworld arm commonly called “The Concord”)  
**Concept:** *Containment-first spacefaring civilization, allergic to reckless curiosity*  
**Scale:** Regional power (quiet routes, relay platforms, deep-archive assets)

### Aspects
- **High Concept:** *The Galaxy’s Most Polite Containment Protocol*
- **Trouble:** *Humanity Is an Uncontrolled Anomaly With Legs*
- **Aspect:** *Resonance Doctrine: Measure Twice, Touch Never*
- **Aspect:** *Silent Routes and Dark-Running Logistics*
- **Aspect:** *Post-Fall Memory: We Don’t Do Collapse Again*

### Faction Approaches / Skills (choose what you use)
If you run Fate Core skills:
- **Great (+4):** Resources, Lore  
- **Good (+3):** Notice, Will  
- **Fair (+2):** Contacts, Rapport, Investigate  
- **Average (+1):** Fight, Athletics, Deceive, Provoke

If you run Approaches:
- **Careful +3, Clever +3, Forceful +2, Sneaky +2, Quick +1, Flashy +1**

### Stress & Consequences (faction-level)
- **Stress:** 3 boxes (1/2/3) for **Influence** and 3 boxes for **Operations**
- **Consequences:** Mild/Moderate/Severe: **Political**, **Material**, **Spiritual** (yes, spiritual is real here)

### Faction Stunts (pick 2–4 to use)
- **Harmonic Quarantine:** Once per session, the Concord may declare a scene aspect **Quarantine Field** with two free invokes.
- **Silent Interdiction:** +2 to overcome when blocking transmissions, masking routes, or forcing “quiet” logistics.
- **Containment Authority:** +2 to Rapport or Provoke when issuing safety directives in a crisis (especially around relics).
- **Archive Recall:** Once per session, ask the GM one question about an ancient site/phenomenon; get a true, useful answer (it may be incomplete).

### Signature Assets
- **Relay Platforms (“Quiet Piers”)** hidden in cold orbits
- **Resonance Cages** (portable containment rig)
- **Warden Teams** trained for Pneuma spill response
- **Probe Lineages** with mapped “silent corridors”

### Clocks (use these to pace the faction)
- **Human Wildfire Index (0–6):** fills when humans interact with relics/Pneuma unsafely
- **Concord Escalation (0–6):** fills when the Concord decides “enough” and enforces containment
- **Mimir Breadcrumb Trail (0–8):** fills as PCs uncover bridge-signatures and Echo coordinates

## 5) Mini-Arc: “The Quiet Pier” (First Sapients Introduction)
**Goal:** Introduce the Birrin as the first living sapient species encountered, tie them to Mimir breadcrumbs, and make Pneuma (later) feel dangerous and *real*.

### Premise
The crew discovers a **derelict platform** in cold orbit — no beacons, no EM chatter, just faint rhythmic impacts through the hull.  
When the PCs approach, the station responds with **structured resonance**: an intentional “hail” that human systems misread as noise.

### What’s actually happening
- The platform is a **Concord Quiet Pier** — a relay/containment waypoint.
- The PCs’ recent actions (or cargo) carry an **anomalous instability signature**.
- The signature overlaps with **old-network residue** the Concord monitors obsessively (they will not explain what that means).

### Core Clocks
- **Anomaly Instability (0–6):** fills when PCs push relic/void tech, take strange mental strain, or ignore warnings.
- **Concord Patience (0–6):** fills when PCs refuse protocol, lie, or escalate violence.
- **Echo Wakefulness (0–8):** fills as the PCs approach the Architects’ Echo site and interact with it.

### Session 1: The Knock You Feel in Your Teeth
**Scenes**
1) **Approach / Docking:** the “hail” is physical resonance.  
   - Scene aspects: *Dead Air*, *Vibration in the Bones*, *Cold Orbit Silence*
2) **First Contact Misread:** PCs answer with radio; the pier answers with louder resonance (not aggression—translation attempt).  
   - Compel: *Human Tools Assume Human Minds*
3) **The Door That Opens Sideways:** first Birrin appear (envoy + warden). Not dramatic. Efficient. Watching everything.

**Revelation:** They already know the PCs’ ship ID pattern (because it’s in an old network’s shadow).  
**Payoff:** The envoy offers “help,” but the warden quietly starts a quarantine routine.

### Session 2: Polite Containment
**Scenes**
1) **The Quarantine Talk:** Concord offers climate rigs + translation + “please don’t touch anything.”  
   - PCs can create advantage: *Shared Protocols*, *Trust Token*, *Proof of Intent*
2) **The Test:** Birrin run a resonance scan. Any Pneuma "spark" spikes the readings.  
   - If PCs push back, clock **Concord Patience** ticks.
3) **Mimir Breadcrumb:** Concord reveals a pattern: the crew’s recent anomalies match an old signature called **Bridge-State Residue**.

**Choice:** accept Concord guidance (safer) or slip out to investigate alone (faster, riskier).

### Session 3: Silent Route Run
**Scenes**
1) **Route Plotting:** Serrik-Tuun offers a “quiet corridor” to the breadcrumb coordinate — for a price.  
2) **Hazard Field:** debris, thermal gradients, or weird “echo weather.”  
   - Scene aspects: *Sensor Lies*, *Pressure Ghosts*, *Your Thoughts Aren’t Private*
3) **Echo Site Perimeter:** the Architects’ Echo is *not* a ruin — it’s a condition. It behaves like a sleeping mind.

**Twist:** The site reacts more strongly to humans than to Birrin (unstable interface).  
Tick **Anomaly Instability** whenever a PC “pushes” a mental/spirits action without grounding.

### Session 4: The Echo Opens One Eye
**Scenes**
1) **Resonance Cage vs. Human Reach:** Birrin containment vs. human improvisation clash (the Birrin call it “safety”; humans call it “control”).  
2) **Mimir’s Message:** a breadcrumb triggers a memory-structure: not a video, not a voice — a *rule*.  
   - Example: “Do not complete the loop.” / “Do not name the bridge.” / “Measure the gap; do not cross it.”
3) **The Price:** someone takes a consequence (physical/mental/spiritual) as the Echo tries to “learn” them.

**Big Decision:**  
- **Contain it (Concord path):** safer, slower, yields reliable data and allies.  
- **Touch it (human path):** faster, yields power/visions, risks corruption and spiritual fracture.

### Session 5: Aftermath & New Normal
**Outcomes (pick one or blend)**
- **Alliance:** Concord becomes a recurring faction ally/overseer (containment politics forever).
- **Cold War:** Concord tags the PCs as a wildfire risk; they shadow you.
- **Fracture:** the Echo wakes partially; anomaly effects become more common and scarier in your sector.
- **Mimir Thread:** the breadcrumb points to a second node… and it’s inside a human-controlled system.

### GM Tools: Easy Compels & Consequences
**Compels**
- *Protocol vs. Compassion* (warden chooses safety over saving someone)
- *Misread Display* (humans interpret Birrin posture as threat)
- *The Shortcut Calls to You* (the anomaly offers a “simple” solution)

**Consequences examples**
- **Mild (Physical):** *Thermal Shock / Suit Desiccation*
- **Mild (Mental):** *Echo Headache / Persistent Resonance*
- **Mild (Spiritual):** *Threaded Soul / Dreams in Someone Else’s Grammar*
- **Moderate:** *You Left Something Behind in the Echo*
- **Severe:** *You Are Now Part of the Bridge*
