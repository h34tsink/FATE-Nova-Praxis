# Birrin Species Rules (FATE)

> *"Six limbs means I can work, watch, and hold a weapon simultaneously. Your species should try it."*  
> — Serrik-Tuun, Probe Navigator

## Character Creation: Playing a Birrin

### Species Overview

**Type:** Biological alien species (compatible with biosleeve system)  
**Origin:** [[Factions/Aliens/Birrin/Birrin Species - Biology and Culture|Chriirah]], hothouse world  
**Body Plan:** Hexapod (six primary limbs) + dorsal radiators  
**Compatibility:** Can be played as PC or major NPC

**Who Should Play a Birrin:**
- Players who enjoy "fish out of water" roleplay
- Those interested in alien perspective on human chaos
- Fans of protocol-driven characters with hidden depths
- Anyone wanting mechanical variety (six limbs = new options)

### Required Aspects

#### High Concept (Choose One or Create Similar)

- *Birrin Diplomatic Scout From the Kiln's Edge*
- *Birrin Void-Custodian (Relic Warden)*
- *Birrin Probe-Navigator, Raised on Silent Routes*
- *Birrin Ecologist Who Treats Ships Like Biospheres*
- *Displaced Birrin Survivor Seeking New Purpose*
- *Birrin Archivist Obsessed With Mimir Connection*
- *Quarantine Marshal — Last Line of Containment*

**High Concept Guidelines:**
- Should reference Birrin identity
- Include role/profession
- Hint at backstory or motivation
- Provide clear invoke opportunities (expertise, connections, alien perspective)
- Create compel hooks (cultural differences, species limitations, trauma)

#### Trouble (Choose One or Create Similar)

- *Human Environments Are Cold, Dry, and Hostile*
- *Your "Direct Speech" Reads Like Threat Display*
- *I Am Responsible for Containment (Even When No One Listens)*
- *The Galaxy Expects "Alien Wisdom" — I Have Trauma and Rules*
- *My People's Fall Haunts Every Reckless Decision I See*
- *Resonance Doctrine Conflicts With Human Improvisation*
- *Ergonomic Nightmare: Nothing Fits Six Limbs*

**Trouble Guidelines:**
- Must create genuine dramatic complications
- Should tie to Birrin nature or culture
- Provides regular compel opportunities
- Can be invoked for benefit when appropriate
- Balances the advantages of playing an alien

#### Other Aspects

Create three additional aspects as normal for Nova Praxis FATE characters. Consider:
- **Cultural:** *Post-Fall Protocol Is Life* / *Raised in the Silent Corridors*
- **Relationships:** *I Trust This Human... Mostly* / *The Warden Who Taught Me Everything*
- **Goals:** *Map Every Quiet Route* / *Prevent the Second Fall*
- **Quirks:** *Radiators Don't Lie* / *Six Hands, Twelve Projects*
- **Background:** *House Khrii Loyalist* / *Concord Exile*

### Skills

Use standard Nova Praxis skill list from [[Glossary/Skills]]. Birrin have no species-restricted skills.

**Recommended for Birrin Concepts:**
- **Envoy:** Rapport, Empathy, Contacts, Will, Investigate
- **Warden:** Lore, Investigate, Will, Notice, [[Glossary/Cohesion|Cohesion]]
- **Navigator:** Pilot (or Athletics), Notice, Survival, Stealth, Contacts
- **Archivist:** Lore, Investigate, Rapport, Empathy, Academics
- **Scientist:** Crafts, Lore, Notice, Investigate, [[Glossary/Cohesion|Cohesion]]

**Cultural Skill Context:**
- Birrin [[Glossary/Cohesion|Cohesion]] trained through resonance methodology (safer, slower than human intuition)
- [[Glossary/Provoke|Provoke]] often unintentional via radiator display
- [[Glossary/Rapport|Rapport]] requires cross-species translation awareness

## Narrative Permissions

These are things Birrin characters can simply do without rolling, by virtue of their biology.

### Physical Capabilities

**Multi-Limb Coordination**
- Can manipulate multiple objects simultaneously
- Hold weapon while operating controls while maintaining grip
- Stable platform: difficult to knock down or push off balance
- Hexapod locomotion: can move in unconventional ways

**Independent Vision**
- Eye stalks track different targets independently
- Can watch door while examining object
- Notice threats from multiple angles
- Read two displays at once

**Radiator Display**
- Broadcast emotional state through dorsal appendage posture
- Vent heat efficiently (useful in combat/exertion)
- Non-verbal communication visible at distance
- Cultural meaning readable by other Birrin

**Quad-Opening Beak**
- Powerful bite force (four-direction jaw)
- Fine manipulation with beak tip
- Can operate tools/controls with mouth when hands full
- Natural weapon always available

**Vibration Sense**
- Detect minute structural vibrations
- "Feel" approaching footsteps through deck
- Assess structural integrity by touch
- Sense mechanical operation through contact

### Social Capabilities

**Alien Perspective**
- Offer genuinely non-human viewpoint
- Recognize patterns humans miss
- Question human assumptions naturally
- Provide cultural translation (when known)

**Concord Authority**
- Call on Concord resources (with strings)
- Cite containment protocols
- Access Birrin-only networks/facilities
- Leverage species reputation

**Species Knowledge**
- Automatically know common Birrin lore
- Recognize Chriirah geography/climate
- Understand resonance technology principles
- Know post-Fall history and cultural trauma

## Species Stunts

Choose 1-3 of these in addition to regular stunts. These represent specialized Birrin capabilities.

### Physical Stunts

#### **Independent Eyes**
+2 to [[Glossary/Notice|Notice]] when scanning for threats while you're already engaged in another action (searching while talking, watching door while working, monitoring multiple screens).

#### **Six-Legged Skirmisher**
+2 to [[Glossary/Athletics|Athletics]] to resist knockdown, keep footing, or brace against forced movement. Your hexapod stance is exceptionally stable.

#### **Quad-Beak Snap**
+2 to [[Glossary/Fight|Fight]] for a close natural-weapon strike when your hands/forelimbs are occupied or restrained. Can use beak as Weapon:2 even when "unarmed."

#### **Kilnwise**
+2 to [[Glossary/Survival|Survival]] in extreme heat, thermal hazards, or when planning routes around temperature dangers. You're adapted to hothouse conditions.

#### **Aquatic Grace**
+2 to [[Glossary/Athletics|Athletics]] when swimming or operating in aquatic/zero-G environments. Your six limbs and natural buoyancy sense give you exceptional control.

#### **Radiator Overload**
Once per session, you may vent heat dramatically to create the scene aspect **Blazing Hot** or **Thermal Distortion** with one free invoke (affects infrared sensors, creates uncomfortable zone).

### Social Stunts

#### **Radiator Display**
Once per scene, you may use [[Glossary/Rapport|Rapport]] or [[Glossary/Provoke|Provoke]] with +2 *without speaking* (line of sight required). Your posture communicates clearly across language barriers.

#### **Containment Authority**
When you have any formal leverage (Concord backing, crisis situation, clear hazard), spend a fate point to create **Stand Down** aspect with a free invoke as a free action (represents invoking containment protocols).

#### **Silent Speech**
+2 to [[Glossary/Rapport|Rapport]] when communicating nonverbally through display posture, resonance taps, or vibration signals. Works best with other Birrin or those trained to read your signals.

#### **Cultural Bridge**
+2 to Empathy or [[Glossary/Rapport|Rapport]] when mediating between human factions. Your alien perspective lets you see past species-specific biases.

### Knowledge Stunts

#### **Harmonic Cage**
+2 to Create Advantage when containing an anomaly spill, relic effect, or Pneuma manifestation. Your resonance training provides practical containment methods.

#### **Archive Recall**
Once per session, ask the GM one question about an ancient site, phenomenon, or Birrin history; get a true, useful answer (it may be incomplete). Represents deep cultural/academic knowledge.

#### **Resonance Reader**
+2 to [[Glossary/Investigate|Investigate]] or [[Glossary/Notice|Notice]] when analyzing harmonic patterns, structural vibrations, or resonance-based technology. Your vibration sense is trained.

#### **Post-Fall Protocol**
+2 to [[Glossary/Lore|Lore]] or [[Glossary/Survival|Survival]] when dealing with civilizational collapse scenarios, resource crisis management, or long-term survival planning. Your culture has learned hard lessons.

#### **Warden's Override**
Once per session, you may "lock" a device/door/system via resonance authority if you have any plausible Concord authority (it becomes a scene obstacle with Fair (+2) opposition to bypass).

### Navigation Stunts

#### **Silent Corridor**
+2 to Pilot or Stealth when plotting routes with minimal sensor footprint or navigating "dark" space lanes. Probe-lineage training.

#### **Structure Sense**
+2 to [[Glossary/Notice|Notice]] or [[Glossary/Investigate|Investigate]] when assessing structural integrity, hidden passages, or environmental hazards through vibration/pressure awareness.

#### **Cut the Lights**
Once per session, create the scene aspect **Blackout** or **Sensor Dead Zone** with one free invoke (you know exactly how to blind human systems temporarily).

## Built-In Compels

These are common situations where being Birrin creates complications. Accept the compel for a fate point, or spend a fate point to refuse.

### Physical Compels

**Thermal Limits**
- Sealed, cold, or dry environments exhaust you quickly
- Overheating is still a risk in suit failures or extreme exertion
- Need climate control modifications for human-standard habitats
- *Example: "The station's life support is set for human comfort. You're getting hypothermic."*

**Ergonomics Hate You**
- Human gear and architecture don't fit your body plan
- Six limbs require custom modifications
- Control layouts assume two arms and hands
- Furniture and spacing inadequate
- *Example: "The pilot couch is built for humans. You'll need to brace awkwardly to reach all the controls."*

**Eyestalk Tells**
- Stress makes your attention readable to sharp observers
- Can't hide what you're looking at
- Emotional state visible in eye movements
- *Example: "Your eyestalks snap to the hidden panel. The guard notices your attention shift."*

### Social Compels

**Alien Social Math**
- Your "neutral" posture can accidentally escalate a scene
- Radiator displays misread by humans
- What's polite for Birrin is threatening to humans (and vice versa)
- *Example: "You raise your radiators to vent stress. The armed guards interpret it as aggression and draw weapons."*

**Protocol vs. Compassion**
- Containment doctrine conflicts with saving people
- Safety rules require abandoning someone
- The containment math is cold
- *Example: "Concord protocol says seal the breach now. There's still a crew member in there."*

**The Weight of the Fall**
- Watching humans repeat pre-Fall mistakes is triggering
- Post-collapse trauma resurfaces during crises
- Compulsion to prevent catastrophe at any cost
- *Example: "They're strip-mining the colony world unsustainably. You've seen this before. Your people died by the billions."*

### Cultural Compels

**Containment Doctrine**
- Must follow safety protocols even when inconvenient
- Cannot ignore potential hazards
- Duty to report anomalies to Concord
- *Example: "You recognize a Pneuma signature. Protocol says you must quarantine the entire section and call a warden... but that would expose the team's mission."*

**Wildfire Assessment**
- Your cultural programming sees humans as dangerous children
- Hard to trust human judgment around relics/Pneuma
- Temptation to take control "for their own safety"
- *Example: "The human pilot wants to take a shortcut through the anomaly field. Every instinct screams 'wildfire risk.'"*

**Concord Obligations**
- Your faction expects regular reports
- Torn between loyalty to crew and species
- Concord may issue orders that conflict with personal goals
- *Example: "The Concord recall code comes through. You're ordered to return for debriefing immediately, but your crew needs you here."*

## Birrin & Pneuma

*This section contains mild spoilers. Discuss with GM.*

### Mechanical Integration

Birrin characters can learn and use [[Rules and Mechanics/Pneuma Rules/Pneuma|Pneuma mechanics]], but with important differences from human users.

**Access Method:**
- Birrin use trained resonance methodology (not intuitive "reach")
- Requires **Harmonic Cage** stunt or similar resonance training
- Cannot learn Pneuma without in-universe justification (warden training, dangerous accident, extended study)
- Safer but more limited than human improvisation

**Mechanical Differences:**
- Birrin Pneuma users get +1 to Overcome when using containment/safety protocols
- Birrin Pneuma users take -1 to Create Advantage (less spontaneous creativity)
- Backlash consequences for Birrin are typically physical/structural, not spiritual
- Cannot use some "wild" Pneuma effects without risking serious consequences

**Cultural Context:**
- Using Pneuma around other Birrin requires justification or secrecy
- Concord sees trained use as acceptable; untrained use as criminal
- Human-style Pneuma use is terrifying to Birrin observers
- Learning human methods is taboo but some do it anyway (PC option)

### Character Options

**Warden-Trained Pneuma User:**
- Has Concord authorization
- Uses resonance-cage methodology
- Focuses on containment and stability
- Respected but watched carefully
- *Add Aspect:* **Warden-Certified Resonance Practitioner**

**Secret Student:**
- Learning human-style Pneuma without authorization
- High risk of discovery and exile
- More powerful but more dangerous
- *Add Aspect:* **I Learned What I Shouldn't Touch**

**Pneuma-Resistant:**
- Deliberately avoids all Pneuma interaction
- Focuses on mundane excellence
- Moral stance or trauma response
- *Add Aspect:* **I Don't Touch the Deep Structure**

**Hybrid Experimenter:**
- Trying to blend Birrin safety with human power
- Research/experimental focus
- Potential breakthrough or catastrophe
- *Add Aspect:* **Bridging Two Methods (Probably Safely)**

## Equipment & Augmentations

### Birrin-Specific Gear

**Climate Suit (Basic)**
- Cost: Low
- Maintains comfortable temperature for Birrin in human environments
- Adds **Thermally Regulated** aspect
- Noticeable bulk; -1 to Stealth in social situations

**Resonance Tap Kit**
- Cost: Average
- Portable harmonic analysis tools
- +1 to Investigate when analyzing structural vibrations or anomalies
- Standard Birrin equipment

**Hexapod Harness**
- Cost: Low
- Properly fitted gear attachment system for six limbs
- Allows carrying human-equivalent load comfortably
- Required for most human-designed equipment

**Radiator Mesh**
- Cost: Low
- Thermal regulation enhancement
- Extends comfortable temperature range
- Can be fashion/display element

**Multi-Limb Tool Rig**
- Cost: Average
- Allows simultaneous operation of multiple tools/weapons
- +1 to Crafts or similar when multitasking
- Requires training to use effectively

### Augmentations

Birrin can use [[Glossary/Augmentations]] with modifications:

**Compatible:**
- Most cybersleeve enhancements (eyes, internal organs)
- Neurological augments
- Medical nanotech
- Communication implants

**Requires Modification:**
- Limb replacements (six limbs = higher cost)
- Skeletal reinforcement (hexapod structure different)
- Armor plating (custom fitting)

**Incompatible/Problematic:**
- Human-standard prosthetics
- Two-arm weapons platforms
- Biped-specific mobility enhancements

**Birrin-Specific Augments:**

**Resonance Implant**
- Cost: High
- Internal harmonic sensor suite
- +2 to Notice when detecting vibrations/anomalies
- Potential Pneuma sensitivity risk

**Radiator Enhancement**
- Cost: Average
- Improved heat management and display control
- +1 to Rapport/Provoke when using radiator display
- Can function in more extreme temperatures

**Hexapod Coordination Suite**
- Cost: High
- Neural enhancement for limb coordination
- +1 to Athletics for complex multi-limb maneuvers
- Allows attempting impossible coordination tasks

## Integration with Nova Praxis Systems

### Sleeve Compatibility

**Biosleeve:**
- Birrin biosleeves available (expensive outside Birrin space)
- Ego transfer between Birrin and human sleeves requires significant adjustment
- Cultural taboo among many Birrin (but not universal)
- Stats remain consistent (sleeve is just physical form)

**Cybersleeve:**
- Birrin-format cybersleeves rare
- Usually custom-built
- Some Birrin adopt human sleeves for infiltration/adaptation
- *Add aspect:* **Sleeved in Enemy Shape** if using human cybersleeve

**Unique Challenges:**
- Birrin egos in human sleeves struggle with: two-limb coordination, limited vision, thermoregulation
- Human egos in Birrin sleeves struggle with: six-limb coordination, eyestalk control, radiator interpretation

### Coalition Citizenship

Most Birrin PCs have **guest status** in Coalition space, not full citizenship.

**Legal Status:**
- Protected under Coalition xeno-sapient rights laws
- Cannot vote or hold Coalition office
- Trade rights respected
- Subject to containment authority in crisis situations

**Path to Citizenship:**
- Service in Coalition military/organizations
- Major contribution to humanity
- Marriage/family bonds (if recognized)
- Special dispensation (rare)

### House Relationships

**House Kimura:**
- Xenobiology division fascinated by Birrin
- Some collaboration; some Birrin discomfort with being "studied"
- Trade in biological/medical tech

**House Silva:**
- Military cooperation on containment operations
- Some wardens embedded with Silva units
- Mutual respect but cultural friction

**House Dalianis:**
- Trade route coordination
- Silent corridor data exchange
- Navigator partnerships

**House Håvik:**
- Entertainment industry interest in "alien perspective"
- Some Birrin find this degrading; others profit

## Advancement

Birrin characters advance as normal FATE characters with these notes:

### Aspect Evolution

As Birrin characters develop, aspects may shift:
- **Early:** Focus on alien-ness, cultural difference, being outsider
- **Mid:** Integration challenges, hybrid identity, building trust
- **Late:** True bridge between species, unique synthesis, new possibilities

### Skill Development

Consider natural progression:
- Learning human social cues (improving Rapport/Empathy)
- Adapting to human equipment (Athletics, technical skills)
- Teaching human crewmates resonance methods (Lore)
- Developing personal style that blends both cultures

### Stunt Progression

Later stunts might reflect:
- Cross-cultural expertise
- Hybrid methodologies (especially re: Pneuma)
- Leadership roles bridging species
- Innovation combining both heritages

### Milestone Considerations

**Minor Milestone:** Adjust aspect to reflect growing human understanding or Concord distance

**Significant Milestone:** Add stunt reflecting cross-cultural synthesis

**Major Milestone:** Transform core aspect from "Birrin outsider" to "Bridge between species" (if appropriate)

## GM Guidance

### Portraying Birrin NPCs

**Voice & Mannerisms:**
- Measured speech patterns (careful word choice)
- Literal interpretation common (cultural translation gaps)
- Physical communication through radiators (describe posture)
- Eyestalk movements show focus/attention
- Clicking/tapping sounds as thinking or emphasis

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Long-term planning (species lives 80-100 years)
- Safety first, profit/speed second
- Preventing another Fall (species trauma)
- Containment protocols not negotiable
- Curiosity exists but carefully managed

**Conflict Sources:**
- Protocol vs. human improvisation
- Containment vs. progress/profit
- Species loyalty vs. personal relationships
- Trauma-driven caution vs. actual current risk

### Balancing Alien-ness

**Too Alien:**
- Players can't relate or connect
- Motivations incomprehensible
- No common ground for cooperation
- Roleplay becomes frustrating

**Too Human:**
- Loses interesting differences
- Just humans with extra limbs
- Cultural depth unexplored
- Misses dramatic potential

**Just Right:**
- Understandable motivations, alien methods
- Common goals, different approaches
- Relatable emotions, unique expressions
- Bridge-able differences with effort

### Story Uses

**Early Campaign:**
- First contact scenarios
- Cultural misunderstanding complications
- "Alien perspective" problem-solving
- Introduce containment philosophy

**Mid Campaign:**
- Deep culture revelation (post-Fall trauma)
- Birrin-human cooperation challenges
- Pneuma knowledge disparity becomes relevant
- Trust-building payoff

**Late Campaign:**
- Full Pneuma truth revealed
- Joint species threats require cooperation
- Birrin methodology proves essential
- True synthesis of both approaches
