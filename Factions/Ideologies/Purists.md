## Overview

Purists represent approximately 53% of Coalition humanity—those who choose to minimize augmentation and maintain relatively unaugmented biological forms. This ideology spans those who simply lack augmentation to those actively opposing technological modification of the human form.

## Core Characteristics

### Biological Baseline
- **Minimal augmentation** - Most maintain baseline biological form or only essential augmentations
- **Natural lifespan extension** - Average life expectancy ~250 years (significantly longer than baseline human but limited compared to heavily augmented)
- **Traditional human identity** - Maintain emphasis on biological humanity as core identity
- **Variety of motivations** - Range from economic (cannot afford augmentation) to ideological (oppose modification)

### Ideological Spectrum
- **Conservative traditionalists** - Value human biological form as sacred or optimal
- **Economic baseline** - Cannot afford augmentation; live unaugmented by necessity rather than choice
- **Cautious technophobes** - Suspicious of technology's long-term effects after technophage
- **Religious opposition** - Many traditional religions oppose Apotheosis and augmentation

## Political Power & Influence

### Coalition Dominance
As clear majority (53%), Purists maintain substantial political influence:
- **Senate representation** - Significant voting bloc in Coalition government
- **House control** - Dominate Cipriani, Jinzhan, Tsarya; significant presence in most Houses
- **Cultural default** - Unaugmented form remains "normal" in Coalition society
- **Legislation** - Primary force behind Humanity Preservation Act

### House Alignment
- **House Cipriani** - Purist-dominated; Purist ideals drive House policies
- **House Jinzhan** - Primarily Purist; isolationist technophobia tied to religious Purist values
- **House Tsarya** - Traditionally Purist; includes large religious membership
- **House Dalianis** - Mostly Purist but increasingly pragmatic
- **Houses Silva & Kimura** - Bio-liberal minority; constant tension with Purist members

## Ideological Tensions

### Primary Conflicts
- **Augmentation acceptance** - Oppose mandatory enhancement or pressure toward modification
- **Apotheosis concerns** - View consciousness uploading as loss of soul (especially religious Purists)
- **Transhumanism** - Ideological opposition to transhuman evolution philosophy
- **Technology trust** - Lingering technophobia from Earth's destruction by technophage

### Religious Connection
Traditional religions concentrate among Purist population:
- **Soul doctrine** - Believe physical form houses essential soul
- **Apotheosis as abomination** - View consciousness uploading as spiritual corruption
- **Natural life** - Value biological continuity over technological persistence

## Economic Reality

### Market Implications
- **Affordable baseline** - Unaugmented or minimally augmented status economically accessible
- **Compilation benefits** - Even Purists access compiled goods and services
- **Technological divide** - Increasing economic gap as augmentation becomes more common
- **Social pressure** - Economic necessity may force augmentation despite ideological opposition

## The Purifiers: Violent Extremism

### Definition
Purifiers represent violent wing of Purist ideology—extremists actively attacking augmentation infrastructure and transhumanist targets.

### Activities
- **Infrastructure attacks** - Bombing resleeve facilities and augmentation centers
- **Violent opposition** - Attacking heavily augmented or posthuman individuals
- **Terrorist tactics** - Using fear and violence to oppose transhuman advancement
- **Coalition response** - Protectorate targets for elimination as extremists

### Relationship to Mainstream Purists
Most mainstream Purists repudiate Purifier violence while maintaining ideological opposition to augmentation. Purifiers represent extremist fringe rather than mainstream Purist position.
