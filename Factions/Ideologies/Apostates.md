## Overview

Apostates represent approximately 20% of humanity—those without Coalition Identification (CID) living scattered throughout known space. They exist outside the official Coalition structure, forming their own settlements, trade networks, and economies based on black market operations and gold currency.

## Key Characteristics

### Definition & Status
- **No CID status** - Lack Coalition Identification, preventing access to official services, compilers, and infrastructure
- **Scattered enclaves** - Organize into distributed settlements rather than centralized territories
- **Black market economy** - Use gold currency for trade instead of Coalition reputation system
- **Second-class treatment** - Generally viewed as problems to be managed rather than citizens

### Major Settlement: New Montreal
**HD413** hosts New Montreal, the largest known apostate settlement. Its size and organization make it the closest apostate faction to a permanent capital, though still subject to Coalition pressure.

### Other Known Communities
- **Santa Maria flotilla** - Mobile apostate population living aboard ships
- **Scattered outposts** - Various small settlements and hidden facilities throughout Coalition space

## Resources & Survival

### Compiled Supplies
Apostates lack direct access to Coalition compilers, forcing reliance on:
- **Black market sources** - Obtaining compiled goods through illegal channels
- **House Turin's trade network** - New Turin serves as stable weapons/supply source
- **Smuggling operations** - Rings of Olympus and other criminal networks
- **Spoilage and decay** - Living with inferior supplies compared to Coalition citizens

### Food & Sustenance
Especially critical given Jinzhan's monopoly on Shouye agricultural production. Apostates struggle to obtain real food, forced to subsist on compiled alternatives or engage with criminal networks.

## Political Dynamics

### Coalition Perspective
- **Jinzhan isolationism** - Almost official refusal to acknowledge apostates; violent response to interference
- **Tsarya exploitation** - Offers apparent citizenship while maintaining near-slavery conditions
- **Silva recruitment** - Offers contracts with high risk of biotech experimentation
- **Cipriani selectivity** - Rarely accepts apostate membership; requires significant patronage investment

### Apostate Agency
Despite marginalization, apostates actively:
- **Form resistance movements** - Talons of Freedom, Remnant Forces
- **Engage Shadow War** - Some House operatives employ apostate crews
- **Build communities** - Create functional societies despite Coalition restrictions
- **Access Extranet** - Maintain independent communication networks beyond Coalition Monitors

## Ideological Diversity

Apostates span wide range of motivations:
- **Political opposition** - Remnant government loyalists and patriots
- **Economic necessity** - Those unable to afford Coalition citizenship
- **Ideological resistance** - Opposition to Apotheosis, Humanity Preservation Act, or Coalition control
- **Criminal enterprise** - Pirates, smugglers, and black market operators
- **Ascension cults** - Extremist transhumanists seeking unauthorized Apotheosis
- **Prophetic movements** - Religious groups operating outside Coalition religious framework
