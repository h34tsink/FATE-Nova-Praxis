## Overview

Transhumans represent humanity's choice to voluntarily evolve beyond baseline biological limitations through augmentation and technology. This ideology encompasses both personal evolution approaches and collective consciousness philosophies, unified by belief that technology enables positive human advancement.

## Core Philosophy

### Two Primary Approaches

**Personal Evolution Path**
Individuals pursue augmentation selectively, enhancing specific capabilities while maintaining individual identity and autonomy. This approach emphasizes personal choice and diverse enhancement strategies.

**Collective Consciousness Path**
Some transhumans pursue merger of minds into collective systems, prioritizing unified consciousness over individual identity. Often associated with utopian visions of merged intelligence and shared thought.

### Shared Values
- **Technology as salvation** - Contrast to Purist technophobia
- **Voluntary augmentation** - Agency in personal evolution decisions
- **Mind uploading potential** - Apotheosis technology as natural endpoint
- **Post-biological freedom** - Belief in transcending biological limitations

## Regulatory Framework

### Humanity Preservation Act (HPA)
The Coalition restricts transhuman development through the HPA, imposing limitations on:
- Extent of augmentation permissible
- Consciousness uploading and Apotheosis technology
- Creation of posthuman entities
- Collective consciousness systems

### Apotheosis Technology
Mimir-designed consciousness uploading system allowing minds to exist as software:
- **Mind as software** - Ego backed up and restored in new substrates
- **Mimir-imposed limits** - Constraints preventing uncontrolled evolution
- **Resurrection mechanism** - Backup/restore procedures for continuity
- **Potential abuse** - Corporate use for forced servitude (Second Chances operation)

### Aberrants & Ascension Cults
Most dangerous transhuman faction—posthuman extremists pursuing unrestricted evolution:
- **HPA violation** - Deliberately exceed Coalition limitations
- **Apotheosis pursuit** - Rapid, uncontrolled consciousness evolution
- **Cult dynamics** - Often organized around charismatic leaders or transcendence promises
- **Coalition response** - Target for Protectorate elimination operations

## House Alignment

### Supportive Houses
- **House Kimura** - Explicitly liberal, opposes HPA limitations; employs numerous transhumans
- **House Silva** - Bio-liberal, engaged in black market enhancement research
- **House Dalianis** - Increasingly willing to employ posthumans for military purposes

### Opposed Houses
- **House Cipriani** - Purist-dominated, suspicious of augmentation; engages in market conflicts with transhuman-leaning houses
- **House Jinzhan** - Technophobic, suspicious of advanced AI and augmentation
- **House Tsarya** - Traditionally conservative but shifting acceptance due to military necessity

## Relationship with Apostates

Transhumans and apostates often intersect:
- **Criminal overlap** - Many apostate enclaves house transhumanist experiments
- **Ideological opposition** - Some apostates are Purist anti-augmentation
- **Coalition pressure** - Both face restrictions but from different directions
- **Opportunity for alliance** - Shared opposition to Coalition controls in some cases

## Society & Community

### Culture
- **Diversity of expression** - Wide range of augmentation choices and enhancement strategies
- **Technical specialization** - Many become experts in their specific enhancements
- **Network communities** - Form groups around shared enhancement goals
- **Conflict with tradition** - Tension between transhumanist ideals and Coalition conservatism
