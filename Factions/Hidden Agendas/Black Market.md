# The Black Market

The unofficial economy operating outside Coalition regulation. Where the [[Glossary/Rep-Rating\|Rep-Rating]] system provides baseline goods to all citizens, the black market provides everything the Coalition restricts: weapons, illegal augmentations, forged CIDs, restricted sleeves, unregistered mnemonic cores, and information.

## How It Works

The Coalition's compiler-based economy means raw materials are cheap but regulated. Black market operators exploit this gap:

- **Compilers** can fabricate most goods, but restricted items (weapons above a certain rating, HPA-violating augmentations, military hardware) require authorization codes. Black market fabricators use stolen or forged codes.
- **Rep laundering** converts legitimate Rep-Rating into untraceable favors and goods.
- **Dead drops and cutouts** prevent direct buyer-seller contact; transactions often happen through intermediary SIMs or apostate brokers.
- **Apostate hubs** like [[Locations/Cere\|Cere]] and other unregistered stations serve as physical marketplaces beyond Coalition surveillance.

## What's Available

- **Weapons:** Restricted firearms, military-grade armor, explosives
- **Augmentations:** HPA-violating mods, unregistered Apotheosis procedures
- **Sleeves:** Black-market biosleeves and cybersleeves without CID registration
- **Information:** Forged CIDs, stolen data, surveillance bypass tools
- **Services:** Unlicensed resleeving, mnemonic editing, ego backup without Coalition records

## Campaign Relevance

PCs interact with the black market through their [[Glossary/Assets\|Assets]] skill. The shadow market on [[Locations/Cere\|Cere]] is a central location in the current campaign arc. Black market contacts are essential for crews operating outside House patronage.
