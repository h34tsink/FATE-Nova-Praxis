## Overview

**Primary Influence:** Luna, Phobos
**Primary Evolutionary Agenda:** Transhuman
**Market Discounts:** Biosleeves, Biotech Augmentations, PAc Weaponry

House Silva formed from non-military elements of the Alliance choosing consolidation over continued conflict: American weapons manufacturer **Valkyrie Industrial** and Canadian biotech research firm **Biogenics United**. During the technophage, both corporations focused on evacuating employees, military contacts, and reputable workers from competing firms (who were contractually obligated in exchange for rescue).

## Current Operations

### Biotech & Augmentation Dominance
- **Best biosleeves and biotech augmentations** on market
- **PAc weapon specialization** - Leading developer with Silva subsidiary Valkyrie Interplanetary providing Coalition's largest weapons contracts
- **Research & development violations** - R&D department regularly breaks Humanity Preservation Act; employees facing sanctions are demoted, fired, or reassigned to "administrative leave" (experimentation)
- **Test subject sourcing** - Black market contacts smuggle apostates; apostate recruitment contracts often lead to biotech lab servitude under guise of "voluntary experimentation"

### Market Competition & Shadow War
**Arms market leadership** - Largest weapons output source via Valkyrie Interplanetary. Silva secretly supplies apostates (particularly Alliance remnants) with weapons to maintain Coalition demand and keep markets balanced.

**PAc weapon vs. kinetic arms race** - In-atmosphere, PAc weapons vastly outclass Dalianis' kinetic weapons. Silva constantly improves PAc technology; Dalianis (partnered with Tsarya) develops PAc-resistant armor. Silva simultaneously expands starship production to force Dalianis resource reallocation.

### Biotech Black Market Operations
Tsarya utilizes Silva's black market contacts for:
- Weapon handoffs to apostates
- Smuggling HPA-violating contraband between labs
- Sourcing test subjects when indentured servants run out

## Political Structure

### Corporate Governance
Unlike other Houses, Silva maintains minimal governance changes from pre-Exodus corporate structure. Citizens are viewed as employees; termination means exile and Rep hit (may default but cannot seek House employment).

### Apostate Integration
- **Lenient application guidelines** (but nearly impossible acceptance) - Requires valuable skills and passing extensive background checks
- **Failed applicants** - Often accepted anyway with contract clauses sending them to biotech R&D labs as "volunteers for experimentation"
- **Test subject exploitation** - Lacking skills or failing background checks results in lab assignment

### Religious Culture
Neo-Christianity remains popular among Silva citizens for its straightforward approach to moral relief with minimal lifestyle interference.

### Mercenary Contracts
Silva requires mercenary crews with reputation for:
- Quick, efficient mission completion
- Absolute discretion
- No prior exposure of House secrets
- "Reset clauses" mandating backup before missions; Silva retains right to withhold previous mission information if operative dies and resurrects from backup

## House Relationships

- **Partnership with Tsarya** - Tsarya handles illegal smuggling/slave-trading contacts; mutual weapons market cooperation
- **Partnership with Cipriani** - Shared business ventures; common enemy in Dalianis
- **Alliance with Kimura** - Reciprocal tech/weapons arrangements (hidden from Tsarya)
- **Market rivalry with Dalianis** - Intense PAc vs. kinetic weapons competition
