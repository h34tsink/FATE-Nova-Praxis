## Overview

**Primary Influence:** Shouye, Earth Quarantine
**Primary Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist
**Market Discounts:** Construction, Security Tech & Services, Food, Explosives, Ship & Vehicle Weaponry

Unlike other Houses, Jinzhan is not comprised of pre-Coalition corporations but evolved directly from the Chinese government's megacorporation. When the technophage devastated China and its military collapsed, Jinzhan assumed control—hiring surviving military personnel and organizing the largest mercenary operation in history for evacuation efforts.

## Current Operations

### Food Production Monopoly
**Shouye** is the only Coalition world capable of reliably producing food naturally and in bulk. Real Shouye food commands premium prices compared to compiled alternatives, making it extremely popular on black markets. Apostates particularly seek Shouye goods since they lack compiler access.

### Security Infrastructure
- **Guardian** security contractor subsidiary - Comprised largely of pre-Exodus mercenaries; reputation for reliability and ethical operations
- **Beta AI Monitors** (vs. higher-security Gamma AIs) supplement mesh security with significantly more human personnel
- **Earth Quarantine operations** - Jinzhan provides majority of quarantine forces, equipment, and Primatech Labs orbital research for containment efforts

### Military Production
- **Upscaled particle acceleration and kinetic weapons** applied to vehicles
- **Explosive weapons** on personal and vehicle scales
- **Vehicle weaponry specialization**

## Political & Ideological Structure

### Religious Turn
Following the technophage, Jinzhan citizens view technology more as a tool of destruction than salvation. This sparked rise of religion within the House, particularly **traditional Buddhism**, popular for its emphasis on rebirth—allowing coping with technophage guilt and tragedy.

### Growing Isolationism & Technophobia
- **Limited apostate acceptance** - Nearly impossible for apostates to attain membership; Jinzhan almost officially refuses to acknowledge apostate existence
- **Technology restrictions** - Distrust of augmentation, advanced AI, nanotech; fear of repeating Earth's mistakes
- **Enforced isolationism** - Minimal unnecessary House interaction; apostate interference met with swift, violent response

### Earth Destruction Initiative
Jinzhan allocates significant resources to researching methods and weapons for planetary destruction—seeking to erase Earth's memory and destroy the technophage entirely.

## Shadow War & Recruitment

Jinzhan engagement in Shadow War typically targets specific acquisition/elimination objectives rather than market competition. The House refuses crews with apostate members, preferring experienced mercenaries. Suspicion exists that Jinzhan involvement precipitated the Odysseus Station incident three months ago.
