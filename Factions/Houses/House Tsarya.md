## Overview

**Primary Influence:** Proch, Luna
**Primary Evolutionary Agenda:** Purist
**Market Discounts:** Vehicular & Personal Armor, Ground Vehicles, Assault Warframes

House Tsarya emerged from two controversial pre-Exodus corporations: Russian **Risel Armory** (investigated for war profiteering; actually guilty) and Indian **Adani Motors** (heavily compensated for silence). Both companies relied heavily on conflict for profit, establishing Tsarya's core philosophy: profit from war and conflict.

## Current Operations

### Armor & Warframe Dominance
- **Top manufacturer** of personal and vehicular armor, ground vehicles
- **Assault Warframe specialty** - Heavy, well-armed, tank-like designs (reverse-engineered from Kimuran recon frames then adapted)
- **Metallurgy security** - Prevents Kimura obtaining Tsaryan armor data; results in inferior Kimuran assault warframes
- **Warframe competition** - Specialization differences prevent either House from market dominance; neither matches other's specialties

### Black Market Operations
Tsarya's greatest strength lies in illegal networks and war profiteering:
- **Slave trader relationships** - Offers apostate "citizenship" under pretense of "saving" them from slavers; actual contracts near-identical to slavery
- **Weapons smuggling** - Hand-off weapons to apostates via smuggler contacts
- **HPA contraband** - Transports smuggling and HPA-violating contraband
- **Artificial conflict creation** - Sometimes pools illegal contacts to instigate (or fabricate) apostate/remnant attacks; occasionally assists other Houses (like Silva) in staging realistic attacks

### Relationship with Apostates
Two-faced approach:
- **Apparent leniency** - Application guidelines appear more lenient than other Houses
- **Actual difficulty** - Acceptance nearly impossible; slightest mistake triggers blacklisting for decades
- **Aggressive response** - Vehement demands for Coalition response to apostate "aggression" (often incited or fabricated by Tsarya themselves)

Some apostate applicants promised House citizenship for aiding attack staging; few who survive resulting conflict actually receive membership.

## Political & Military Doctrine

### War Profiteering Philosophy
Tsarya leadership openly profits from conflict; House leadership has no qualms with war profiteering—there just needs to be a war. When conflict dries up, Tsarya engineers artificial conflicts through black market contacts.

### Religious Composition
House includes large number of traditional religion members disapproving of augmentation/Apotheosis. However, soldiers needing to fight, die, and fight again require extensive gear. Leadership is shifting messaging toward acceptance of Apotheosized to address troop availability needs.

### Transhuman Acceptance Evolution
Growing number of transhumanists/posthumans within House increasingly appreciate Tsaryan tolerance efforts, though friction remains with traditional religious majority.

## House Relationships

- **Alliance with Cipriani** - Strong trade agreement: Cipriani purchases armor/transplasidium for luxury vehicles
- **Relationship with Silva** - Black market connections; Tsarya smuggles apostates to Silva; smuggles HPA-violating biotech materials; sources test subjects
- **General detente with other Houses** - Relatively good terms; oddly peaceful given extensive illegal activities
- **Warframe competition with Kimura** - Neither dominates; specialization prevents clear victor

## Recruitment Practices

- **Mercenary hiring** - Relatively open process; now happily hire transhumans for dangerous jobs despite previous avoidance
- **Apostate integration** - Extremely difficult despite appearing lenient; slightest failures trigger long-term blacklisting
- **Preferred affiliations** - Prefer transhumans associated with Tsarya or Silva
