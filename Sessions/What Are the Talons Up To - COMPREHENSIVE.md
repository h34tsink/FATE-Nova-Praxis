# The Rumors Become Reality: Icarus Hub Mutiny

**Campaign Arc:** Civil Unrest Part 3  
**Session(s):** Session 10+ (or triggered when PCs finish [[Sessions/Valare's Situation|Valare situation]])  
**Primary Focus:** Background chaos while PCs pursue Nowak's location  
**Status:** Ready to activate  
**Scope:** Regional destabilization, Nowak's Talon connection revealed, hub destruction, 400-600 Talon recruitment

---

## Overview

Until now, the PCs have only heard whispers about the Talons of Freedom planning a big move against the Coalition. Those rumors are about to materialize into a **full-scale mutiny operation**.

**The Icarus Hub** has been selected as target. About a year ago, Talon leadership approved "Fracture Protocol"—a coordinated strike against one of the Coalition's critical infrastructure nodes. This hub not only houses a growing, disgruntled working class but also offers immense strategic and material value if seized. Its fall would:

- **Military Advantage:** Provide the Talons with 8-12 ships, 400-600 new recruits, weapons caches for years of independent operations
- **Strategic Blow:** Cripple Proch's logistics as the hub is vital supply link; rerouting would take 2+ months to fully recover
- **Psychological Victory:** Demonstrate Coalition can be defied; inspire mass recruitment across disillusioned populations across multiple stations
- **Resource Gain:** Equipment, ships, docking facilities, control over critical trade routes, industrial equipment
- **Organizational Emergence:** Elevates Talons from ideological movement to military threat with strategic capability

---

## The Operation: "Fracture Protocol"

**Operation Name:** Fracture Protocol  
**Target:** Icarus Hub, Proch Station Complex  
**Objective:** Seize assets, destroy Coalition infrastructure, establish Talon foothold  
**Expected Duration:** 4-6 hours from initiation to escape  
**Timeline:** Triggered either by player completion of Valare situation OR at Session 10 natural escalation

The Talons know they cannot withstand full Coalition military might. This is not a war—it's a **calculated hit-and-run operation** designed to maximize gains while minimizing exposure time and Coalition response capability.

### Preparation Phase (Completed Before Session Start)

**With Chimera's aid**, the Talons have spent months preparing:

**Infrastructure Infiltration:**
- Planted **20-30 agents** throughout the hub in various roles (maintenance, security, logistics, docking control)
- Established **15+ weapon caches** hidden in maintenance shafts, storage areas, and sealed bay sections
- Identified and recruited **sympathetic dock workers** (300+ ready to assist)
- Created false identities and access credentials for all infiltrators
- Mapped every lock, every override code, every security checkpoint

**Mobile Supply Caches:**
- **5 armed merchant ships** positioned at key dock berths, ostensibly for routine supply transfers
- Each carries: weapons (ballistic and energy), armor rigs, explosives, medical supplies, rations
- Ships staffed with Talon operatives disguised as merchant crews
- Positioned to allow rapid loading of personnel and cargo
- Pre-fueled, engines warmed up, departure protocols ready

**Intel & Surveillance:**
- Complete hub layout with access codes (updated weekly via Chimera network)
- Coalition security patrol patterns mapped and verified
- Identification of security override systems and manual controls
- Knowledge of shift changes and personnel rotations
- Layout of structural load-bearing points for demolition planning

**Key Target Selection:**
- **Primary Target:** Docking Ring Alpha (northeast corner)
  - Contains 8 large berths capable of accommodating Legion's troop transports
  - Heavily automated systems (easier for Savant to compromise)
  - Structural position critical to hub stability
  - Destruction forces Coalition to reroute all reinforcements through smaller southern docks (adds 2+ hours to response)
  - Contains logistics hub storing month's worth of replacement parts and supplies

- **Secondary Targets:**
  - Hub's central fusion reactor (not destroyed, but control codes obtained for leverage/shutdown threat)
  - Communications array (to limit Coalition coordination)
  - Personnel quarters section (blocks evacuation routes, accelerates chaos)

### Execution Timeline

**Hour 0 (T+0:00) — Synchronization**
- All Talon agents receive activation signal via encrypted mesh message
- Savant begins penetration of docking control systems
- Armed crews on 5 merchant ships prepare for rapid departure
- Demolition teams move to preset positions with explosive charges

**Hour 0.5 (T+0:30) — Systems Compromise**
- Savant gains administrative access to docking systems (takes 15-30 minutes)
- Once achieved:
  - Can lock/unlock berths individually
  - Control which ships can undock
  - Disable airlocks remotely
  - Override normal traffic patterns
  - Lock down specific sections
  - Reroute emergency broadcasts
  - Feed false sensor data to Coalition observers
- Communications systems partially jammed

**Hour 1 (T+1:00) — Rapid Mobilization**
- Talon forces move from concealment to assault positions
- Weapon caches are opened; agents arm and equip rapidly
- Coalition security responds (too late to prevent major actions)
- Workers in target sectors either evacuate or are "encouraged" to participate
- First explosions trigger at non-critical locations to spread confusion

**Hour 2-3 (T+2:00-3:00) — Active Assault**
- Major combat at Docking Ring Alpha
- Demolition crews plant explosives on critical structural supports
- Talon assault teams secure berth areas
- Transport ships rapidly load personnel, supplies, recruits
- Coalition forces engaged in firefights across multiple hub sections
- Chaos spreads: civilian panic, alarm klaxons, emergency broadcasts

**Hour 4-5 (T+4:00-5:00) — Extraction Phase**
- Explosives on critical structural supports detonate in sequence
- Docking Ring Alpha begins catastrophic failure
- Armed merchant ships break free and begin departure
- Talon forces still aboard fight rear-guard actions
- Coalition reinforcements arriving but delayed

**Hour 6 (T+6:00) — Escape Window**
- Last Talon ships clearing the hub
- Coalition realizes full scope of losses
- Hub in critical structural condition

## The Savant Element

**Critical Asset: Unnamed Savant Operative**

This Savant's role is absolutely essential to the operation's success.

**Capabilities:**
- Can interface directly with Coalition computer systems
- Has pre-installed backdoors throughout hub systems (planted via Chimera months ago)
- Can simultaneously manage: docking systems, communications, security locks, sensor feeds
- Once locked in, becomes stationary—must defend position or abandon interface

**Location & Vulnerability:**
- Will be positioned in central control station (Docking Hub Command Center)
- Well-protected with Talon guards and defensive positions
- Has 3-4 hour window of active interface (after which backup systems kick in)
- If killed/disabled: all compromised systems reset, operation becomes much harder

**Player Opportunity:**
- If PCs discover control center location, they can either:
  - Assault Savant directly (difficult combat encounter)
  - Destroy/damage critical connections (shutdown the advantage)
  - Use the Savant's presence as leverage/bargaining chip
- **Warning:** Talon forces will defend this position desperately

**Talon Contingency:**
- If Savant is killed: operation continues but less efficiently
- Manual override codes distributed to secondary teams
- Operations take longer (hours become + 50% additional time)
- More forces needed to hold gained positions
- Increases Coalition response effectiveness

## Nowak's Role & Connection

**Crucial Detail: Nowak & The Talons Are Connected**

This is NOT a coincidence that PCs are chasing Nowak while this happens.

### The Real Situation

**Nowak's True Identity:**
- Is a Talon of Freedom **high-level coordinator**
- Her "mysterious outpost" location is actually a **Talon staging base**
- The job she hired the PCs for is part of larger Talon preparation

**Why She Hired the PCs:**
- **Needed reliable operators** for independent task (deniable operation)
- **Outside the Talon network** so success doesn't compromise internal operations
- **Her erasure of their Extranet records** was **TEST of Talon capabilities**
- **She was assessing them** for potential recruitment OR elimination

**What She Actually Wanted Them To Do:**
- The original mission details don't matter as much as OUTCOME
- **Success** = Proves PCs are competent (useful to Talons)
- **Failure** = Proves they're not (eliminate or exile)
- **Throughout**, Chimera was monitoring (all actions logged, analyzed)

### The Revelation

**Timing:**
- As PCs search for Nowak during mutiny chaos, they'll eventually find her
- She may be **AT the mutiny** (coordinating logistics)
- OR they find **evidence of her involvement** (Talon communications, operational plans)
- OR an **NPC tells them the truth** (defector, Talon prisoner, Coalition Intel)

**The Moment:**
When PCs locate Nowak, reveal: "The Talons have been watching you. **I** was watching you. You were *useful*. And now... you have a choice."

### Three Possible Nowak Scenarios

**Scenario A: Nowak Is Sympathetic/Ideological**
- Honestly wants to help change the system
- Using Talon resources but doesn't fully align with extreme methods
- Can become ally or informant
- Helps PCs navigate through chaos
- But still has genuine Talon loyalties

**Scenario B: Nowak Is Opportunistic/Pragmatic**
- Was using them as tool
- If they're useful, offers recruitment/partnership
- If they're obstacle, views them as threat
- Depends entirely on PC actions throughout campaign
- Can be negotiated with if PCs have leverage

**Scenario C: Nowak Is Talon Hard-Liner**
- Full committed revolutionary
- PCs were always expendable assets
- Now actively trying to prevent interference
- May attempt to capture or kill them
- Only cooperation if PCs prove immediately useful

### How To Play Nowak In Sessions

**Early Clues (Session 10 Early):**
- PCs notice Talon cells/activity is unusually well-organized
- Chimera records mention her name (if they hack anything)
- Coalition Intel mentions "Talon coordinator Nowak"
- A Talon operative confirms her involvement (under pressure)

**Mid-Point Realization (Session 10 Mid):**
- PCs piece together that Nowak's task and mutiny timing overlap suspiciously
- They realize the erasure of their records was about Chimera access
- NPC reveals Talon organization is more structured
- PCs start questioning everything about their original mission

**Climax (Session 10+):**
- PCs find Nowak (either as goal or as encounter during mutiny)
- Confrontation reveals truth and forces choice
- Her reaction depends on PC competence/actions throughout campaign
- Outcome determines her future role

## Outcomes & Consequences

The mutiny will **likely succeed** unless PCs intervene strategically.

### If Talons Achieve Complete Success (Most Likely)

**Immediate (Days 1-7):**
- Talons depart with: 400-600 new recruits, 8-12 ships, thousands of tons of weapons/supplies
- Docking Ring Alpha destroyed
- Coalition suffers significant casualty losses (200-400 dead)
- Hub placed under martial law

**Short-Term (Weeks 2-4):**
- Talon organization emerges as serious threat
- Recruitment surge across disaffected populations
- Proch faces month+ of supply shortages
- Coalition military stretched thin

**Long-Term (Months+):**
- Talons establish forward bases in deep space
- Coalition's authority visibly weakened
- Other factions emboldened to act
- Campaign landscape shifts

### If PCs Significantly Disrupt (Moderate Success)

**Possible Disruptions:**
- Destroy weapon caches (reduces resources by 30-50%)
- Kill/disable Savant (operation continues but inefficiently; takes 8+ hours)
- Alert Coalition early (response times cut in half)
- Prevent loading of key personnel or supplies
- Sabotage merchant ships (reduces escape vessels)

**Result:**
- Talons still escape but with fewer resources
- Still significant blow to Coalition, but not catastrophic
- Nowak still departs (though possibly angry or impressed)
- Coalition frames it as heroic victory

### If PCs Prevent Mutiny (Unlikely But Possible)

**Requires:**
- Killing Savant AND preventing system override
- Alerting Coalition early for major troop deployment
- Destroying enough caches that operation becomes impossible

**Result:**
- Talon operation completely fails
- Hundreds of Talon operatives killed or captured
- Nowak must flee without resources
- PCs become famous in Coalition
- Creates massive refugee/insurgent problem
- Talon retaliation more likely later

## Running Sessions With The Mutiny

### How To Present The Backdrop

**For Early Sessions (While Searching for Nowak):**
- Mutiny is **BACKGROUND noise**
- News reports mention "growing disturbances at Icarus Hub"
- Coalition increases security across stations
- Talon sympathizers become more visible
- Creates tension building

**For Mid Sessions (As Mutiny Accelerates):**
- Alarms become more frequent
- Supply shortages start appearing
- Coalition military movements visible
- Nowak becomes harder to reach
- Talon activity blocks some routes
- Forces choices

**For Climax Sessions (During Active Mutiny):**
- Chaos is **IMMEDIATE and VISCERAL**
- Explosions, alarms, emergency broadcasts constant
- PCs must navigate through active combat/panic
- Finding Nowak means moving through hot zones
- Moral dilemmas everywhere

### Scene Setups

**Scene Type 1: Hub Transit**
- PCs need to travel through hub to reach Nowak
- Mutiny triggers during transit
- Choose: hide/evade chaos, or push through
- Encounters: Talon checkpoints, Coalition patrols, civilians, structural damage

**Scene Type 2: Intel Gathering**
- PCs searching for Nowak's location
- Systems in lockdown
- Access before lockdown, or navigate during chaos
- Encounters: Chimera networks, Coalition security, Talons

**Scene Type 3: Direct Confrontation**
- PCs locate Nowak's position
- She's at staging base or command center
- Mutiny is active; getting to her means fighting through Talon forces

**Scene Type 4: Moral Dilemma**
- Civilians needing rescue en route to Nowak
- Coalition forces asking for help
- Pursuing Nowak means leaving people behind
- Forces player agency

**Scene Type 5: Unintended Consequence**
- PCs' actions accidentally trigger/accelerate parts of mutiny
- Demonstrates that mutiny is large, complex system
- PCs are one factor among many

### Environmental Details

**The Hub Itself:**
- Space station organized in cubic modules connected by corridors/transit tubes
- Docking Ring Alpha targeted for destruction (northeast quadrant)
- Central fusion reactor (critical system)
- Communications array (eastern spire)
- Multiple population centers
- Multiple independent levels (can move vertically to bypass locked sections)

**During Mutiny:**
- Sections going dark (power cuts)
- Emergency bulkheads sealing (routes blocked)
- Pressure warnings
- Fires breaking out
- Vacuum warnings where hull breaches occur
- Transit systems failing intermittently

**Sound & Sensation:**
- Klaxon alarms (loud, constant)
- Emergency broadcasting
- Explosions (distant and immediate)
- Vibrations (station shaking)
- Crowds (panicked voices)
- Radio traffic (mixed Coalition and civilian chatter)

### NPCs & Encounters

**Coalition Military:**
- Officers trying to restore order
- Soldiers engaged with Talon forces
- Some sympathetic to Talon cause
- Command center desperate for help

**Talon Operatives:**
- Combat teams securing perimeters
- Demolition crews
- Recruitment teams
- Escape coordinators

**Nowak (When Encountered):**
- Cool, composed despite chaos
- Professional and efficient
- Her reaction depends on scenario chosen earlier
- May be busy coordinating mutiny
- May have questions about PC involvement

**Civilians:**
- Workers caught in chaos
- Families trying to evacuate
- Hub security protecting people
- Those sympathetic to Talons
- Injured and dying

## Mechanics & Encounter Types

### Obstacle Types During Mutiny

**Physical Obstacles:**
- **Sealed Bulkheads:** Locked by Savant (Athletics/Crafts to force)
- **Structural Damage:** Collapsed corridors, hull breaches
- **Fires:** Spreading rapidly
- **Crowds:** Panicked civilians blocking routes

**System Obstacles:**
- **Access Lockdowns:** Doors controlled by systems
- **Network Interference:** Chimera jamming
- **Database Blocks:** Information encrypted/inaccessible

**Combat Obstacles:**
- **Talon Checkpoints:** Scattered operatives controlling areas
- **Coalition Patrols:** Military forces responding
- **Crossfire:** Caught between opposing forces

### Challenge Types

**Overcome Obstacles:**
- Force through sealed doors
- Navigate through fire/damage
- Talk your way past Talon checkpoint
- Hack/bypass locked systems

**Create Advantages:**
- Find weapon cache
- Discover Savant's location
- Rally workers to your cause
- Learn Nowak's exact position

**Attack/Defend:**
- Combat with Talon forces
- Coalition military attacking
- Protecting civilians
- Fighting to reach critical infrastructure

**Defend Against Complication:**
- Building falling apart
- Civilians need help
- Time pressure

### How To Use Timeline

**If PCs Are Searching For Nowak:**
- Tell them approximately how much time remains
- Mutiny progresses on schedule regardless
- Every encounter/delay moves time forward
- Creates pressure: finding Nowak before escape

**If PCs Are Trying To Stop Mutiny:**
- Each successful intervention buys time
- Each failure accelerates timeline
- Creates race against clock

**If PCs Are Caught In Middle:**
- Timeline provides structure
- Each scene = 15-30 minutes narrative time
- Helps GM know when major events occur

## Key GM Reminders

✓ **This is backdrop, not main plot.** Nowak is the focus; mutiny is pressure and chaos.

✓ **PCs cannot prevent it entirely.** Give them chances to disrupt, but mutiny should mostly succeed.

✓ **Nowak's Talon connection is campaign game-changer.** Play reveal carefully.

✓ **Moral pressure matters as much as combat.** Civilians needing help create real dilemmas.

✓ **Time pressure is real.** Mutiny operates on schedule; Nowak will leave; hub will collapse.

✓ **Consequences matter long-term.** Mutiny success destabilizes region; ripples extend throughout campaign.

✓ **Player agency is limited but meaningful.** They can't stop it, but they can influence outcome.
