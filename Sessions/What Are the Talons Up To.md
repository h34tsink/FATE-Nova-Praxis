
# The Rumors Become Reality

Until now, the PCs have only heard whispers about the Talons of Freedom planning a big move against the Coalition. Those rumors are about to materialize into a full-scale operation.

About a year ago, a decision was made: the **Icarus Hub servicing Proch** would be an ideal target. This hub not only houses a growing, disgruntled working class but also offers immense strategic and material value if captured. Its fall would:

- Provide the Talons with hundreds of ships, thousands of new recruits, and enough weapons and supplies to last for years.
- Deliver a significant blow to Proch, as the hub is a vital logistical link. Without it, Proch would face supply shortages, and rerouting shipments would take months.
- Bolster the Talons’ reputation by demonstrating their ability to defy the Coalition, inspiring others to join their cause.

### **The Mission**

The Talons know they cannot withstand the full might of the Coalition. Their mutiny is not a war but a **hit-and-run operation** designed to maximize gains while minimizing exposure.

#### **Preparation**

- With Chimera’s aid, the Talons have:
  - Planted **agents and weapon caches** across the hub.
  - Docked several **ships loaded with weapons, armor, and explosives**, using them as mobile distribution centers for their forces.
- The Talons’ first target is one of the hub’s corners, chosen because it contains docking stations capable of accommodating the **Legion’s larger troop transports**. Destroying these docks will:
  - Force the Coalition to rely on smaller transports.
  - Severely slow down the deployment of reinforcements.

#### **Execution**

- A **Savant working for the Talons** will seize control of the hub’s docking systems, granting the Talons the ability to:
  - Decide who can and cannot dock.
  - Control which ships can decouple and depart.
- Once the Talons gain control, they will:
  - Pack transport ships with as many **supplies and recruits** as possible.
  - Dispatch demolition crews to rig explosives around the structures connecting the station’s cubes.
  - Detonate the explosives, tearing the station apart as they escape.

### **Aftermath**

The mutiny will likely succeed unless the PCs intervene, and even then, stopping it entirely is nearly impossible. The Talons are prepared, organized, and determined. If they succeed:

- The Talons leave with ships, supplies, and new recruits, emerging as a formidable threat to the Coalition.
- The event solidifies the Talons as a symbol of rebellion, marking them as a serious adversary moving forward.
- Proch’s infrastructure is severely weakened, causing months of logistical chaos.

### **Integrating This with the PCs’ Objectives**

#### **Steering the PCs**

- The Talons’ mutiny serves as a backdrop to the PCs’ main mission: **tracking down Nowak**. While the mutiny unfolds, the PCs should:
  - **Focus on Nowak**: Finding out why she hired them, why she erased their Extranet records, and where the mysterious outpost is.
  - **Witness Key Events**: Let the PCs see the mutiny’s impact firsthand, but ensure they remain focused on their mission.

#### **Possible Interactions**

- The PCs could uncover **Talons’ caches or plans** while searching for Nowak, presenting opportunities for sabotage or extraction of critical intel.
- Encounters with **Talon forces or sympathetic workers** could lead to tense negotiations or skirmishes.

#### **Player Decisions**

While stopping the mutiny outright is unlikely, the PCs can:

- Disrupt certain objectives, such as sabotaging the Savant’s control of docking systems or preventing key Talon transports from departing.
- Choose whether to aid Coalition forces, the Talons, or remain neutral.

### **Running the Game During the Mutiny**

#### **Dynamic Backdrop**

- Use the mutiny to create tension and urgency. As the PCs move through the station:
  - **Alarms blare**, and station-wide announcements warn of breaches.
  - **Explosions and skirmishes** erupt in the distance, shaking the station.
  - Civilians flee in panic, creating obstacles and moral dilemmas for the PCs.

#### **Encounter Ideas**

- **Sabotage Team**: The PCs stumble upon a Talon demolition crew planting explosives and must decide whether to intervene.
- **Civilian Refugees**: A group of workers begs the PCs for protection or safe passage through a dangerous area.
- **Coalition Checkpoint**: Coalition forces mistake the PCs for Talon agents, forcing a tense standoff or combat encounter.

#### **Reinforcing the Stakes**

- Remind the players of the **larger consequences** of the mutiny:
  - The potential collapse of Proch’s supply chain.
  - The rise of the Talons as a major threat to the Coalition.
