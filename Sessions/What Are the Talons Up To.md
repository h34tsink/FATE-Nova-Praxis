# The Rumors Become Reality: Icarus Hub Mutiny

**Campaign Arc:** Civil Unrest Part 3  
**Session(s):** Session 10+ (or triggered when PCs finish Valare situation)  
**Primary Focus:** Background chaos while PCs pursue [[Nowak connection|Nowak's location]]  
**Status:** Ready to activate

---

## Overview

Until now, the PCs have only heard whispers about the [[Factions/Talons of Freedom|Talons of Freedom]] planning a big move against the Coalition. Those rumors are about to materialize into a **full-scale mutiny operation**.

**The Icarus Hub** has been selected as target. About a year ago, Talon leadership approved "Fracture Protocol"—a coordinated strike against one of the Coalition's critical infrastructure nodes. This hub not only houses a growing, disgruntled working class but also offers immense strategic and material value if seized. Its fall would:

- **Military Advantage:** Provide the Talons with 8-12 ships, 400-600 new recruits, weapons caches for years of independent operations
- **Strategic Blow:** Cripple Proch's logistics as the hub is vital supply link; rerouting would take 2+ months to fully recover
- **Psychological Victory:** Demonstrate Coalition can be defied; inspire mass recruitment across disillusioned populations across multiple stations
- **Resource Gain:** Equipment, ships, docking facilities, control over critical trade routes, industrial equipment
- **Organizational Emergence:** Elevates Talons from ideological movement to military threat with strategic capability

### **The Mission**

The Talons know they cannot withstand the full might of the Coalition. Their mutiny is not a war but a **hit-and-run operation** designed to maximize gains while minimizing exposure.

#### **Operation Name: "Fracture Protocol"**

Expected Duration: 4-6 hours from initiation to escape. Trigger: Either by PC completion of [[Sessions/Valare's Situation|Valare situation]] OR at Session 10 natural escalation.

#### **Preparation Phase (Completed Before Session Start)**

**With [[Characters/NPCs/Chimera|Chimera]]'s aid**, the Talons have spent months preparing:

**Infrastructure Infiltration:**

- Planted **20-30 agents** throughout the hub in various roles (maintenance, security, logistics, docking control)
- Established **15+ weapon caches** hidden in maintenance shafts, storage areas, and sealed bay sections
- Identified and recruited **sympathetic dock workers** (300+ ready to assist)
- Created false identities and access credentials for all infiltrators
- Mapped every lock, every override code, every security checkpoint

**Mobile Supply Caches:**

- **5 armed merchant ships** positioned at key dock berths, ostensibly for routine supply transfers
- Each carries: weapons (ballistic and energy), armor rigs, explosives, medical supplies, rations
- Ships staffed with Talon operatives disguised as merchant crews
- Pre-fueled, engines warmed up, departure protocols ready

**Intel & Surveillance:**

- Complete hub layout with access codes (updated weekly via Chimera network)
- Coalition security patrol patterns mapped and verified
- Identification of security override systems and manual controls
- Knowledge of shift changes and personnel rotations
- Layout of structural load-bearing points for demolition planning

**Key Target Selection:**

- **Primary Target:** Docking Ring Alpha (northeast corner)
  - Contains 8 large berths capable of accommodating Legion's troop transports
  - Heavily automated systems (easier for Savant to compromise)
  - Structural position critical to hub stability
  - Destruction forces Coalition to reroute all reinforcements through smaller southern docks (adds 2+ hours to response)
  - Contains logistics hub storing month's worth of replacement parts and supplies

- **Secondary Targets:**
  - Hub's central fusion reactor (not destroyed, but control codes obtained for leverage)
  - Communications array (to limit Coalition coordination)
  - Personnel quarters section (blocks evacuation routes, accelerates chaos)

#### **Execution Timeline**

**Hour 0 (T+0:00) — Synchronization**

- All Talon agents receive activation signal via encrypted mesh message
- Savant begins penetration of docking control systems
- Armed crews on 5 merchant ships prepare for rapid departure
- Demolition teams move to preset positions with explosive charges

**Hour 0.5 (T+0:30) — Systems Compromise**

- Savant gains administrative access to docking systems (takes 15-30 minutes via pre-planted backdoors)
- Once achieved: can lock/unlock berths, control which ships depart, disable airlocks remotely, override traffic patterns, lock down sections, reroute broadcasts, feed false sensor data
- Communications systems partially jammed (Coalition hears alarms but limited info transfer)

**Hour 1 (T+1:00) — Rapid Mobilization**

- Talon forces move from concealment to assault positions
- Weapon caches are opened; agents arm and equip rapidly (coordinated via Chimera network)
- Coalition security responds (too late to prevent major actions)
- Workers in target sectors either evacuate or are "encouraged" to participate
- First explosions trigger at non-critical locations to spread confusion

**Hour 2-3 (T+2:00-3:00) — Active Assault**

- Major combat at Docking Ring Alpha
- Demolition crews plant explosives on critical structural supports
- Talon assault teams secure berth areas using combined arms tactics
- Transport ships rapidly load personnel, supplies, recruits
- Coalition forces engaged in firefights across multiple hub sections
- Chaos spreads: civilian panic, alarm klaxons, emergency broadcasts

**Hour 4-5 (T+4:00-5:00) — Extraction Phase**

- Explosives on critical structural supports detonate in sequence
- Docking Ring Alpha begins catastrophic failure
- Armed merchant ships break free and begin departure
- Talon forces still aboard fight rear-guard actions
- Coalition reinforcements arriving but delayed by destroyed docks

**Hour 6 (T+6:00) — Escape Window**

- Last Talon ships clearing the hub
- Coalition realizes full scope of losses
- Hub in critical structural condition; partial station failure imminent

### **The Savant Element**

**Critical Asset: Unnamed Savant Operative**

This Savant's role is absolutely essential to the operation's success. Without system control, everything becomes exponentially harder.

**Capabilities:**

- Can interface directly with Coalition computer systems
- Has pre-installed backdoors throughout hub systems (planted via Chimera months ago)
- Can simultaneously manage: docking systems, communications, security locks, sensor feeds, emergency systems
- Can feed false data to Coalition observers (showing reinforcements en route when they're not, etc.)
- Once locked in, becomes stationary—must defend position or abandon interface

**Location & Vulnerability:**

- Will be positioned in central control station (Docking Hub Command Center, level 4)
- Well-protected with Talon guards and defensive positions (8-12 operatives minimum)
- Has 3-4 hour window of active interface (after which backup systems kick in automatically)
- If killed/disabled: all compromised systems reset, operation becomes much harder
- Facility has redundant power; destroying power doesn't help (backup generators activate)

**Player Opportunity:**

- If PCs discover control center location, they can either:
  - **Assault Savant directly** (difficult combat encounter against prepared position)
  - **Destroy/damage critical connections** (shutdown the advantage)
  - **Use the Savant's presence as leverage** (bargaining chip with Talons)
  - **Interface with system themselves** (if they have Savant-like capabilities; Talon presence prevents this)
- **Warning:** Talon forces will defend this position desperately (it's their operation's linchpin)

**Talon Contingency:**

- If Savant is killed: operation continues but less efficiently
- Manual override codes distributed to secondary teams (slower, less flexible)
- Operations take longer (+ 50% additional time)
- More forces needed to hold positions (ties up operatives)
- Increases Coalition response effectiveness

## Nowak's Role & Connection

**Crucial Detail: Nowak & The Talons Are Connected**

This is NOT a coincidence that PCs are chasing Nowak while this happens.

### The Real Situation

**Nowak's True Identity:**

- Not independent operator or simple rogue agent
- Is a [[Factions/Talons of Freedom|Talon of Freedom]] **high-level coordinator**
- Her "mysterious outpost" is actually a **Talon staging base**
- The job she hired the PCs for is part of larger Talon preparation

**Why She Hired the PCs:**

- **Needed reliable operators** for independent task (deniable operation)
- **Outside the Talon network** so success doesn't compromise internal operations
- **Her erasure of their Extranet records** was **TEST of Talon capabilities** (Chimera access level)
- **She was assessing them** for potential recruitment OR elimination
- Task was designed to be completable but challenging (filtering mechanism)

**What She Actually Wanted Them To Do:**

- Original mission details don't matter as much as OUTCOME
- **Success** = Proves PCs are competent, creative, reliable (useful to Talons)
- **Failure** = Proves they're not or they're loose ends (eliminate or exile)
- **Throughout**, Chimera was monitoring (all their actions logged, analyzed, stored)

### The Revelation

**Timing:**

- As PCs search for Nowak during mutiny chaos, they'll eventually find her
- She may be **AT the mutiny** (coordinating logistics or at staging base)
- OR they find **evidence of her involvement** (Talon communications, operational plans)
- OR an **NPC tells them the truth** (defector, Talon prisoner, Coalition Intel, Chimera)

**The Moment:**
When PCs locate Nowak, reveal: "The Talons have been watching you. **I** was watching you. You were *useful*. And now... you have a choice."

### Three Possible Nowak Scenarios

**Scenario A: Nowak Is Sympathetic/Ideological**

- Honestly wants to help change the system
- Using Talon resources but doesn't fully align with extreme methods
- Can become ally or informant over time
- Helps PCs navigate through chaos to reach her staging base
- But still has genuine Talon loyalties (creates ongoing moral tension)

**Scenario B: Nowak Is Opportunistic/Pragmatic**

- Was using them as tool
- If they're useful, offers recruitment/partnership at favorable terms
- If they're obstacle, views them as threat to eliminate (nothing personal)
- Depends entirely on PC actions throughout campaign (competence? trustworthiness? danger level?)
- Can be negotiated with if PCs have leverage or mutual interests

**Scenario C: Nowak Is Talon Hard-Liner**

- Full committed revolutionary; ideologically pure
- PCs were always expendable assets; no personal loyalty
- Now actively trying to prevent interference with mutiny
- May attempt to capture or kill them (unless immediately useful)
- Only cooperation possible if PCs prove more valuable alive than dead

## FATE Sequence: Reaching Nowak

When the PCs arrive at the Icarus Hub and need to reach Nowak during the chaos, use this Sequence to track their progress through the war zone.

### Sequence Mechanics (Assumes 4 PCs)

**Success Track:**

```
☐ | ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐
```

**Goal:** Reach and capture Nowak so she can be interrogated  
**Time Between Rounds:** 10 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Hard (4)

### Scene Aspects

**Established Aspects:**

- **"Icarus Hub in Orbit Around Proch"**
- **"Lighthouse has been called in to put down the threat... with extreme prejudice."**
- **"The mesh has been... flaky, ever since the explosions started."**
- **"Chimera helped instigate this—he's a hero to the apostates and Talons."**
- **"Much of the station is damaged, and the environmental systems have been compromised."**
- **"More forces coming from Proch!"**

### Sample Declarations (Players Can Create)

**Tactical Advantages:**

- "Hanger R-17 Is Clear for Docking"
- "Security Logs Show Nowak in Security Checkpoint B-46"
- "We've got control of the environmental systems."
- "Sgt. Toric owes us a favor."
- "Lighthouse Command Vessel Destroyed"

**Faction Support:**

- "Ramsey and his gang are going to watch our backs."
- "The Talon cell recognizes us as allies."
- "Apostate workers provide distraction."

**Environmental Control:**

- "All Ports on Cube 3 Are Sealed"
- "Hanger Bay B-13 Exposed to Vacuum"
- "We rerouted the power grid."
- "Maintenance shafts provide hidden routes."

### Sample Consequences

| Severity | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Mild** | Mesh Intrusion Detected |
| **Moderate** | Heavy Assault Squad Deployed |
| **Severe** | "Kestrel has our position!" |
| **Extreme** | Nowak's Security Team Is Dead and She's on the Run |

### Running the Sequence

**Each Round (10 minutes):**

1. Describe the current situation and obstacles
2. Players declare actions and roll appropriate skills
3. Successes fill the track; failures may trigger consequences
4. Compel aspects for complications and drama
5. Allow creative solutions using Create Advantage actions

**Time Pressure:**

- Every 3-4 rounds, escalate the situation
- Kestrel gets closer to Nowak's location
- Reinforcements arrive from Proch
- Station damage worsens
- Factions shift positions

**Success:**

- PCs reach Nowak before Kestrel
- She's cornered in security post with few guards
- Can be interrogated for information
- See detailed outcomes in session guide

**Failure:**

- Kestrel reaches Nowak first and kills her
- PCs receive coordinates from Kestrel
- Branded as traitors to the Coalition
- Must proceed to Part 4 without her intel

## Nowak's Talon Connection (MAJOR REVEAL)

### How To Play Nowak In Sessions

**Early Clues (Session 10 Early):**

- PCs notice Talon cells/activity is unusually well-organized (too organized for ragtag rebels)
- Chimera records mention her name (if they hack anything)
- Coalition Intel mentions "Talon coordinator Nowak"
- A Talon operative they interrogate confirms her involvement (under pressure or negotiation)
- Communications intercepts show her directing operations

**Mid-Point Realization (Session 10 Mid):**

- PCs piece together that Nowak's task and mutiny timing overlap suspiciously
- They realize the erasure of their records was about Chimera access, not just personal security
- NPC reveals Talon organization is more structured/dangerous than assumed
- Someone tells them: "That woman isn't a client, she's a handler"
- PCs start questioning everything about their original mission

**Climax (Session 10+):**

- PCs find Nowak (either as goal or as encounter during mutiny)
- Confrontation reveals truth and forces choice: work with her, against her, escape her, kill her
- Her reaction depends on PC competence/actions throughout campaign
- Broader implications: if PCs work with Talons, what does that mean for their future?

## Outcomes & Consequences

The mutiny will **likely succeed** unless PCs intervene strategically. Even then, stopping it entirely is nearly impossible.

### If Talons Achieve Complete Success (Most Likely)

**Immediate (Days 1-7):**

- Talons depart with: 400-600 new recruits, 8-12 ships, thousands of tons of weapons/supplies
- Docking Ring Alpha destroyed; partial station failure spreads
- Coalition suffers significant casualties (200-400 dead, 100+ missing)
- Hub placed under martial law

**Short-Term (Weeks 2-4):**

- Talon organization emerges as serious military threat
- Recruitment surge across disaffected populations
- Proch faces month+ of supply shortages
- Coalition military stretched thin

**Long-Term (Months+):**

- Talons establish forward bases in deep space
- Coalition's authority visibly weakened
- Other factions emboldened to act
- Campaign landscape shifts toward systemic crisis

### If PCs Significantly Disrupt (Moderate Success)

**Possible Disruptions:**

- Destroy weapon caches (reduces resources 30-50%)
- Kill/disable Savant (operation takes 8+ hours)
- Alert Coalition early (response faster)
- Sabotage merchant ships (fewer escape vessels)

**Result:**

- Talons still escape but with fewer resources
- Significant Coalition blow, but not catastrophic
- Nowak still departs (impressed or angry)
- Coalition frames as heroic victory

### If PCs Prevent Mutiny (Unlikely)

**Result:**

- Talon operation fails completely
- Hundreds of operatives killed/captured
- Nowak flees without resources
- PCs become famous in Coalition
- BUT: Creates desperate insurgent problem; retaliation likely

### **Integrating This with the PCs’ Objectives**

#### **Steering the PCs**

- The Talons’ mutiny serves as a backdrop to the PCs’ main mission: **tracking down Nowak**. While the mutiny unfolds, the PCs should:
  - **Focus on Nowak**: Finding out why she hired them, why she erased their Extranet records, and where the mysterious outpost is.
  - **Witness Key Events**: Let the PCs see the mutiny’s impact firsthand, but ensure they remain focused on their mission.

#### **Possible Interactions**

- The PCs could uncover **Talons’ caches or plans** while searching for Nowak, presenting opportunities for sabotage or extraction of critical intel.
- Encounters with **Talon forces or sympathetic workers** could lead to tense negotiations or skirmishes.

#### **Player Decisions**

While stopping the mutiny outright is unlikely, the PCs can:

- Disrupt certain objectives, such as sabotaging the Savant’s control of docking systems or preventing key Talon transports from departing.
- Choose whether to aid Coalition forces, the Talons, or remain neutral.

### **Running the Game During the Mutiny**

#### **Dynamic Backdrop**

- Use the mutiny to create tension and urgency. As the PCs move through the station:
  - **Alarms blare**, and station-wide announcements warn of breaches.
  - **Explosions and skirmishes** erupt in the distance, shaking the station.
  - Civilians flee in panic, creating obstacles and moral dilemmas for the PCs.

#### **Encounter Ideas**

- **Sabotage Team**: The PCs stumble upon a Talon demolition crew planting explosives and must decide whether to intervene.
- **Civilian Refugees**: A group of workers begs the PCs for protection or safe passage through a dangerous area.
- **Coalition Checkpoint**: Coalition forces mistake the PCs for Talon agents, forcing a tense standoff or combat encounter.

#### **Reinforcing the Stakes**

- Remind the players of the **larger consequences** of the mutiny:
  - The potential collapse of Proch’s supply chain.
  - The rise of the Talons as a major threat to the Coalition.

## Running Sessions With The Mutiny

### How To Present

**Early Sessions:** Background noise; news reports; increased security; building tension

**Mid Sessions:** Alarms frequent; shortages appearing; routes locked; forced choices

**Climax Sessions:** Visceral chaos; explosions constant; moral dilemmas everywhere

### Environmental Details

**The Hub:** Cubic modules, Docking Ring Alpha (target), reactor, communications array, multiple levels with alternate routes

**During Mutiny:** Power cuts, sealed bulkheads, fires, vacuum warnings, failed systems, panicked crowds

**Sound/Sensation:** Alarms, broadcasts, explosions, vibrations, screaming, mixed radio traffic, smoke/ozone smells

## Key GM Reminders

✓ **Backdrop, not main plot.** Nowak is the focus.

✓ **PCs cannot prevent it entirely.** Mutiny mostly succeeds.

✓ **Nowak's Talon connection is game-changer.** Play reveal carefully.

✓ **Moral pressure equals combat pressure.** Civilians create real dilemmas.

✓ **Time pressure is real.** Mutiny operates on schedule.

✓ **Consequences ripple long-term.** Destabilization extends throughout campaign.

✓ **Player agency is meaningful.** They can influence outcomes and Talon/Nowak views of them.
