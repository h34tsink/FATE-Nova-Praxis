# Session 0: FATE Migration — Cold Storage

**Purpose:** Teach FATE mechanics through play using the real PCs. Introduce two new players. Plant the first breadcrumb of the [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation|Standard Deviation]] arc.

**Runtime:** 2-3 hours

**Setting:** A decommissioned [[Factions/Houses/House Kimura|House Kimura]] research facility on the dark side of [[Locations/Sol System/Cere|Cere]]. Powered down, supposedly empty, listed for asset reclamation.

**Crew:** All players use their real characters. The two new players create characters before the session (State, Aspects, Skills, Stunts) and are introduced in-fiction during Scene 3.

**When:** Takes place in the campaign timeline between the last SWADE session and the first full FATE session. This is canon.

---

## The Job

**Contractor:** [[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar Blackwood]] (Grift's fixer)

**Brief:** A Kimura research facility on Cere was decommissioned three years ago and flagged for asset reclamation. A data broker has reason to believe the facility's servers still hold proprietary research that Kimura forgot to wipe. Retrieve the data core from the facility's main server before Kimura's reclamation team arrives to strip the site. Payment: 4 Rep bumps each.

**Complication:** The facility isn't as empty as advertised. Kimura automated security is still running on backup power — drones, pressure locks, and a mesh defense grid. And deep in the sublevel, something is drawing power that shouldn't be.

**The Twist:** The facility is a Standard Deviation project site. The sublevel contains cryo pods — two of which are activating. The PCs find two strangers waking up with intake tags they recognize from their own revival. And on the terminal next to the pods: **"Project: Standard Deviation."**

---

## Pre-Session: New Player Character Creation

Before play begins, the two new players create their characters. Walk them through:

1. **State:** Pure, Sleeved, or SIM — explain the tradeoffs
2. **Aspects:** HC + ABCDE (+ Physical Trait if Pure, + Savant Disadvantage if Savant)
3. **Skills:** Expert or Specialist template
4. **Stunts:** 3 base slots (+ state freebies if applicable)
5. **Stress:** Calculate Physical, Mental, System tracks

Give them the [[Campaign Overview/New Player Primer - Cold Start Syndicate]] and the [[Campaign Overview/SWADE to FATE - Table Reference]] as reference.

**Critical:** Do not tell the new players about the Standard Deviation arc, the cryo reveal, or that their characters will wake up during the session. They should think they're joining the crew at the start. Their actual introduction happens in Scene 3.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

**Location:** Grift's usual meeting spot with Briar
**Mechanic Focus:** Aspects, Invokes, Declarations

### Setup

Briar Blackwood briefs the crew — casual as always, like she's asking a favour instead of hiring mercenaries. She explains the job: get in, pull the data core from the main server, get out before Kimura's reclamation crew arrives.

**Read aloud (Briar):**
> "Kimura mothballed a research site on Cere three years ago. Small facility, sublevel layout, nothing fancy. My buyer says the servers were never properly wiped — there's proprietary data still on the drives. Kimura's sending a reclamation team to strip the site in 72 hours. You have a window. Get in, pull the core, get out. Don't break anything you don't have to — I don't need Kimura asking questions about who was there."

### Teaching Moment: Aspects and Invokes

Before they leave, ask each player to **read their Aspects aloud** and explain what each one means in one sentence. This gets everyone reacquainted with their sheets in the new system.

Then — Briar mentions the facility has automated Kimura security still running on backup power.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Dustin (Grift) Halloway/Dustin Halloway - FATE Character|Grift]]:** "You've cracked Kimura systems before. Do any of your Aspects help you understand their security architecture?"

If he invokes a relevant Aspect (*Digital Locks Are Just Suggestions*, *Ghost in the System*), give him +2 on an S. Engineering or Research test (Diff 2) to recall Kimura security protocols. This teaches **invoking an Aspect for a bonus**.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Kallius/Kallius - FATE Character|Kallius]]:** "You work in Kimura's shadow. What do you know about mothballed facilities?"

Same mechanic — invoke an Aspect for +2 on Education or Networking.

### Teaching Moment: Declarations

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Azaria Dawson/Azaria Dawson - FATE Character|Azaria]]:** "Has Silva ever done business with this particular Kimura site?"

If she spends a Fate Point, she can **Declare** a useful fact — maybe Silva contracted biosleeve work here, and she has an old access code. This is now true. Place it as a scene Aspect anyone can invoke later.

This teaches **Declarations** — spending FP to introduce facts.

### End Scene

The crew heads for Cere. Everyone should understand: Aspects are true things, you can invoke them for +2, and you can spend FP to declare new facts.

**New players sit out this scene.** They're watching, learning the rhythm. Tell them: "Your characters enter the story soon. Pay attention to how Aspects work — you'll need yours."

## Scene 2: Facility Breach

**Location:** Kimura Research Facility — Surface Level
**Mechanic Focus:** Tests, Maneuvers, Difficulty numbers

### Setup

The facility is dark. Emergency lighting only. The main entrance is sealed with a Kimura biometric lock. Inside: dusty corridors, stripped labs, the hum of backup generators running something deeper in the building.

**Scene Aspects:** *"Emergency Lighting Only"* (P), *"Kimura Automated Security Active"*

### Teaching Moment: Tests (Basic Roll)

The main door requires bypassing the biometric lock.

**Option A — Tech:** S. Engineering test, Diff 3 (spoof the biometric)
**Option B — Force:** Melee or Demolitions test, Diff 4 (breach the door)
**Option C — Investigate:** Research or Insight test, Diff 2 (find a maintenance access point)

Let the players choose. Walk through the roll: 4dF + Skill = Effort. Beat the Difficulty = success. Count the Shifts. If they get Spin (3+), give them a bonus — they notice the backup generators are drawing more power than a mothballed facility should need. Something deeper is running.

**If they fail:** Don't block progress. They get in, but trip a sensor — place the Aspect *"Security System Alerted"* on the scene. This teaches **failure as complication, not dead end**.

### Teaching Moment: Maneuvers

Inside, the main server room is on the ground floor. The data core is accessible but encrypted.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Dustin (Grift) Halloway/Dustin Halloway - FATE Character|Grift]] or [[Characters/Players/Kallius/Kallius - FATE Character|Kallius]]:** "Can you crack the encryption on the server?"

They roll S. Engineering or SINC (Diff 3). On success, they create the Aspect: *"Kimura Encryption Bypassed"* with one free invoke. On Spin, two free invokes.

This teaches **Maneuvers** — rolling to place an Aspect that helps later.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Grace Pryzbylski/Grace Pryzbylski - FATE Character|Grace]]:** "Can you send a drone deeper into the facility to scout?"

She rolls Pilot or Perception (Diff 2). On success, the drone reports: there's a sublevel. It's powered. There are active systems down there that aren't on the facility's public schematics. And there's a faint biosignal.

### Revelation

The data core is pulling — but something else is happening below. The sublevel is drawing power for a reason. The crew has to decide: grab the core and leave, or investigate.

They'll investigate.

## Scene 3: The Sublevel

**Location:** Kimura Research Facility — Sublevel (Cryo Archive)
**Mechanic Focus:** Assessments, Combined Actions, Supplemental Actions

*This is the scene where the new players enter the game.*

### Setup

The sublevel is a sealed cryo archive. Climate-controlled, humming with power drawn from the facility's backup generators. The corridor opens into a chamber containing:

- A bank of **decommissioned cryo pods** — most dark, most empty
- **Two pods that are active** — frost clearing from the glass, status lights cycling green
- A **research terminal** glowing with a single header: **"Project: Standard Deviation"**
- A second terminal displaying **intake processing logs**

The two active pods are opening. Inside: two people. Confused, disoriented, shivering. They have Standard Deviation intake tags on their wrists — the same tags the existing PCs had when they woke up.

**New players enter the game here.** They roleplay waking up: confusion, cold, strangers standing over them, a world they don't recognize.

**To the existing PCs:** "You recognize the intake tags. They're identical to the ones you had when you were revived."

**Scene Aspects:** *"Cryo Fog Clearing"*, *"Two Strangers Waking Up"*, *"Terminal Displaying 'Project: Standard Deviation'"*
**Zones:** Corridor | Pod Chamber | Terminal Alcove | Storage Annex

### Teaching Moment: Assessments

The terminal is active. Someone should investigate it.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Kallius/Kallius - FATE Character|Kallius]]:** "The terminal header says 'Project: Standard Deviation.' That name mean anything to you?"

It doesn't — yet. But he can **Assess** the terminal to learn more. Roll Research or S. Engineering (Diff 3). On success, reveal:

- The facility was a long-term cryo storage site for a pre-Singularity research project
- Approximately 200 subjects were archived here
- Most pods were decommissioned and subjects relocated years ago
- These two pods were flagged for "delayed activation" — no explanation of why or by whom
- The intake processing logs list subjects by **code and name** — but the terminal glitches before he can read them all

Place the Aspect: *"We Were Part of Something"* — free invoke.

**GM note:** Kallius does NOT see the full manifest yet. He sees the project name, the pod count, and a few corrupted entries. This is just enough to be unsettling. Holt is controlling what the terminal shows — she wants them curious, not overwhelmed.

### Teaching Moment: Combined Actions

The new PCs need medical attention — cryo revival is rough. One person examines them while another monitors the terminal.

**Primary roller:** Whoever has the highest Education or Insight. Roll Education (Diff 3) to assess the new PCs' physical/mental state.

**If a second character assists:** They roll their relevant skill. If higher than the primary, primary gets +1. If lower, -1. Walk through the **Combining Skills** rule.

**If someone stands guard:** They can take a **Supplemental Action** — watch the corridor while doing something else, at -1 to their primary roll. Teach Supplemental Actions here.

### The First Drop

While the crew is dealing with the new arrivals, Holt acts.

**Prompt [[Characters/Players/Dustin (Grift) Halloway/Dustin Halloway - FATE Character|Grift]] or [[Characters/Players/Grace Pryzbylski/Grace Pryzbylski - FATE Character|Grace]]:** "The terminal flickers. New files are appearing in the intake log — data that wasn't there a moment ago. Like something just... unlocked."

The terminal now displays three corrupted interview fragments:

---

> **Intake Interview 09-F — Subject: Callum Voss**
> *Q: "If your memories were altered without your knowledge, how would you know?"*
> *A: [long pause] "I wouldn't. That's the point, isn't it?"*

> **Intake Interview 14-C — Subject: Sarah Donnelly**
> *Q: "How do you respond to environments where social hierarchy is unstable?"*
> *A: "I find the person everyone's watching and I stand next to them."*

> **Intake Interview 07-B — Subject: Adrian Cruz**
> *Q: "Under what circumstances would you accept cognitive augmentation?"*
> *A: "None."*
> *Q: "Even to save your life?"*
> *A: "Especially then."*

---

**GM notes:** Read these aloud, slowly. Don't explain them. Don't comment. If a player asks "who are these people?" — "You don't know." If James (Kallius) reacts to the memory question — give him a look but say nothing. If Dan (Aeddarius) twitches at the augmentation refusal — same. The fragments are just data on a screen. The players can copy them, ignore them, or puzzle over them. All three reactions are correct.

If anyone asks why the files appeared — "The terminal glitched. Could be a cache dump, could be corrupted data surfacing. It's an old system." Don't hint at Holt.

### End Scene

The crew has two new members, a project name they don't understand, and three interview fragments with strangers' names. And the facility's security system just noticed them.

## Scene 4: Lockdown

**Location:** Kimura Research Facility — All Levels
**Mechanic Focus:** Compels, Concession, Zones, Movement, Stress

### Setup

The facility's automated security triggers a lockdown. Pressure doors seal. Kimura security drones activate — four small aerial units sweeping the corridors, plus a locked-down turret covering the main exit.

The crew needs to get from the sublevel back to the surface exit with the data core, the two new PCs, and whatever they downloaded from the terminal.

**Zones (in order):**
1. Sublevel — Pod Chamber
2. Sublevel — Corridor
3. Stairwell (narrow, pressure door at top)
4. Ground Level — Lab Wing
5. Ground Level — Main Corridor
6. Surface Exit

**Scene Aspects:** *"Lockdown — Pressure Doors Sealing"* (P), *"Drone Swarm Sweeping Corridors"*, *"Emergency Lighting Only"*

### Teaching Moment: Compels

As the lockdown begins, **compel specific players.** Hit every player at least once during this scene.

| Character | Aspect to Compel | Trigger |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Aeddarius** | *The Habit Behind the Composure* | Stress of the lockdown — hands shaking, needs a moment |
| **Azaria** | *Celebrity Status At Any Cost* | A security camera is still recording — does she disable it or make sure it catches her good side? |
| **Grift** | *Curiosity Kills More Than Cats* | A sealed lab door on the way out — what's behind it? Stop to hack it? |
| **Grace** | *Thin Skinned and Trauma-Triggered* | The new PCs are panicking — their fear triggers her own cryo-revival trauma |
| **Kallius** | *I Know a Truth the Coalition Would Kill to Bury* | The terminal data — stop to download more, or run? |
| **New Player 1** | *[Disadvantage Aspect]* | Just woke up, disoriented — appropriate trigger based on their sheet |
| **New Player 2** | *[Disadvantage Aspect]* | Same — use whatever they wrote, this is their first compel |

Walk through the full compel flow with the first one:
1. "I'm compelling your Disadvantage. Here's what happens..."
2. "Accept: earn 1 FP, but [consequence]. Refuse: pay 1 FP, keep moving."
3. **Either way, the table learns how compels work.**

**For the new players:** Make their first compel gentle. Accept = earn a Fate Point and something inconvenient but not dangerous happens. This teaches the mechanic without punishing someone who just woke up in a cryo pod ten minutes ago.

### Teaching Moment: Zones and Movement

Walk through movement rules as the escape progresses:
- **Adjacent zone = Free Action** (no penalty)
- **Hustle (2 zones) = Supplemental Action** (-2 to other actions)
- **Sprint = Athletics test** (Diff 2, each Shift = 1 more zone)

Let Grace's drones scout ahead. Let Grift find shortcuts through the security mesh. Let Aeddarius command the group. The new PCs follow — they don't need to lead, just participate.

### Teaching Moment: Stress (Light)

A security drone engages whoever is in the rear. Resolve it as a quick Contest. The drone attacks (Firearms +2, WR 1). The PC defends with Athletics. If they take a hit, walk through **Stress boxes** — mark them, explain they clear after the scene.

**Don't take Consequences here.** Save that for Scene 5.

### Teaching Moment: Concession

If any PC is in serious trouble, offer the option to **Concede**: "You can surrender right now. You'll be pinned down by drone fire, but you narrate what that looks like — and you get 1 FP. Or you can keep running and risk being Taken Out, where *I* narrate what happens."

Even if no one concedes, explain the mechanic.

### End Scene

The crew reaches the ground level. The main exit is ahead — but the turret is online and a Kimura security response team has arrived early. The reclamation crew wasn't 72 hours out. It was already on its way.

## Scene 5: Extraction

**Location:** Kimura Research Facility — Main Exit
**Mechanic Focus:** Full Combat (Attack, Defense, Consequences, Blocks)

### Setup

The surface exit is blocked. A Kimura security turret covers the main corridor. Outside, three Kimura security operatives and a Savant handler are approaching from a landed shuttle. The crew has to fight through, talk through, or find a creative third option.

**Zones:**
1. Main Corridor (cover: lab equipment)
2. Entry Vestibule (turret position, narrow)
3. Surface Platform (open, shuttle visible)
4. Shuttle / Escape Vehicle (objective)

**Scene Aspects:** *"Automated Turret Covering the Vestibule"* (P), *"Kimura Shuttle on the Platform"*, *"Dust Storm Rolling In"*

### NPCs

**Kimura Security Operative (x3)**
Skills: Firearms +3, Athletics +2, Perception +2, Melee +2
Stress: 3 boxes | Mild Consequence
Armor: AR +2 (Tactical Vest) | Weapon: Coil Pistol (WR 2)
Aspect: *"Kimura Corporate Loyalty"*

**Kimura Savant Handler (x1)**
Skills: SINC +4, S. Engineering +3, Insight +3, Firearms +2
Stress: 3 boxes | Mild + Moderate Consequence
System Stress: 4 boxes
Armor: AR +1 | Weapon: Light Coil Pistol (WR 1)
Aspect: *"Full Mesh Authority Over This Facility"*
Programs: Blackout, Security Skim, Stream of Consciousness

### Round-by-Round Teaching

**Round 1 — Initiative and Actions:**
Roll Strategy for turn order. Highest result goes first; ties go simultaneously or in player-preferred order.

Each character's turn has four action types:

- **Simple Action** — the main thing you do. One per turn. This is where your skill roll goes.
  - *Examples:* Attack, Maneuver (place an Aspect), Block (set a potency to stop an enemy action), Full Defense (+2 to all defense tests until your next turn), use a Savant Program, reload, remove a Temporary Aspect.

- **Free Action** — fast, nearly effortless. One per turn, no roll required.
  - *Examples:* Move into an adjacent zone, take cover (grants *Behind Light Cover* Aspect — no test), follow an opponent into a zone you pushed them into, activate a Free Action augmentation.

- **Supplemental Action** — doing something secondary *while* doing your Simple Action. Not a separate slot — it costs −1 to your primary roll (Hustle across 2 zones costs −2).
  - *Examples:* Move through rough terrain while attacking, reload while doing something other than attacking, attempt to remove an Aspect at a lower action cost (Diff 2).

- **Instant Action** — happens any time, even off your turn. Limited to one per turn *except* for defense.
  - **Defense is always free** — rolling to defend is a special Instant Action that never counts against your limit. Being attacked never costs you your turn.
  - *Other Instant examples:* Augmentations that interrupt turn order (e.g., Neura-Surge), responding when a Block is ignored.

**Prompt someone to Maneuver first** rather than attack. Examples:
- Grift hacks the turret (S. Engineering vs Diff 4) to place *"Turret Disabled"*
- Azaria talks to the lead operative (Diplomacy vs Insight +3) to place *"Momentary Hesitation"*
- Grace sends drones to flank (Pilot vs Diff 2) to place *"Drones Behind Enemy Lines"*

The point is demonstrating Maneuvers before Attack.

**Round 2 — Attack and Defense:**
Walk through a full attack exchange:
1. Attacker rolls Firearms + mods
2. Defender rolls Athletics (or relevant defense)
3. Defender's roll becomes the Difficulty
4. Shifts + WR - AR = Stress dealt

**When someone takes a hit bigger than their stress boxes:**
Walk through **Consequences**. "You can take a Mild Consequence to absorb 2 of that stress. What does it look like? Give it a name — *'Grazed Shoulder'*, *'Ringing Ears'*, *'Cracked Visor'*. That's now an Aspect. The enemy can invoke it against you — first time free."

**Round 3+ — Blocks and Savant:**
The Kimura Savant tries to Blackout Grace's drone feed or Stream of Consciousness a non-Savant. This teaches **System Stress** and the Savant mechanic. The target defends with SINC (if Savant) or Resolve (if not).

If a combat-focused PC wants to prevent the Savant from acting, they can set up a **Block**: "I'm laying suppressive fire — Firearms test to set a Block potency. If the Savant wants to use a program, she has to beat my potency first."

**For the new players:** Give them clear, simple options. "You can shoot, you can take cover, you can help someone else. What sounds fun?" Don't overwhelm them with tactical choices on their first combat. Let them contribute and feel useful.

### Resolution Options

**Fight:** Take out the operatives, reach the shuttle or the crew's ship.
**Talk:** Azaria or Aeddarius could negotiate — the security team is corporate, not military. They might accept a deal if the crew can convince them nothing valuable was taken.
**Creative:** Hack the turret and turn it on Kimura. Trigger the dust storm as cover. Use the facility's own lockdown against the security team. Have Grace's drones create a diversion.

### Teaching Moment: Taken Out vs. Concede

If an NPC runs out of stress and consequences, they're **Taken Out** — the PCs decide what happens. "Do you knock him out? Tie him up? Leave him?" The PCs narrate.

If a PC is about to be Taken Out, remind them about **Concession** one more time.

## Wrap-Up

### Mechanic Checklist

Ask the players:

- [ ] "What are Aspects?" (True things about characters, scenes, and situations)
- [ ] "How do you invoke?" (Spend 1 FP, name the Aspect, explain why it helps, get +2 or reroll)
- [ ] "What's a compel?" (GM uses your Aspect against you — accept for 1 FP, or pay 1 FP to refuse)
- [ ] "What's a Maneuver?" (Create a temporary Aspect that anyone can invoke)
- [ ] "How does damage work?" (Shifts + WR - AR = Stress. Absorb with boxes or Consequences)
- [ ] "What's a Concession?" (Surrender on your terms before you're Taken Out)
- [ ] "What's a Declaration?" (Spend FP to introduce a fact — it's now true)

### Debrief: What Just Happened

Let the table talk about:
- **The new PCs:** Who are they? Why were they in cryo? Do they remember anything?
- **"Project: Standard Deviation":** What is it? Should they investigate further?
- **The interview fragments:** Who are Callum Voss, Sarah Donnelly, and Adrian Cruz? Why were they being interviewed?

Don't answer any of these. Let the questions sit. The players will carry them into the next session on their own.

### New Player Welcome

For the two new players:
> "Welcome to the crew. You woke up in a cryo pod in a Kimura facility with strangers standing over you. You have intake tags you don't understand, a project name that means nothing yet, and five people who seem to recognize what happened to you. That's your starting position. Everything else, you'll figure out together."

## GM Notes

### Pacing

- **Pre-session (30 min):** New player character creation (do this first, before play starts)
- **Scene 1 (10 min):** Briefing. Keep it tight.
- **Scene 2 (15-20 min):** Facility breach. Let them explore but keep it moving.
- **Scene 3 (25-30 min):** The sublevel. This is the dramatic core — the cryo reveal, new player introduction, and Drop 1. Give it room to breathe.
- **Scene 4 (15-20 min):** Lockdown chase. Fast and chaotic. Compels should fly.
- **Scene 5 (20-30 min):** Combat. As long as it needs to be.
- **Wrap-up (10 min):** Debrief and questions.

### Difficulty Calibration

This is a teaching session. **Err on the side of player success.** The point is for them to use the mechanics and feel good about it. Set difficulties at 2-3. NPCs should be competent but not lethal.

The new players especially should feel effective, not carried. Give them moments to shine — a door they can kick open, a drone they can shoot, a question only they can answer because of their unique Aspect.

### The Drop 1 Fragments

The three interview fragments are listed in the [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation#Fragment Drop Schedule|Standard Deviation drop schedule]] as Drop 1. Key rules:

- **Read them aloud, slowly, one at a time.** Let each one land.
- **Do not explain them.** If players ask questions, the answer is "You don't know."
- **Do not hint at Holt.** If anyone asks why the files appeared — "Old system, cache dump, corrupted data surfacing. Could be anything."
- **Let the players react however they react.** Some will note the names. Some will move on. Both are fine.

### Holt's Fingerprints

For Grift specifically: the Kimura encryption in Scene 2 should feel **slightly too easy** to crack. Not obviously — just a nagging "that shouldn't have been that simple." This is Holt's first fingerprint. Don't flag it. If Rob notices, reward it with a look. If he doesn't, it'll land later when the pattern becomes clear.

### What If They Don't Investigate the Sublevel?

They will. The biosignal from Grace's drone scout is too compelling to ignore, and every player at the table will want to know what's drawing power. But if somehow they don't — the cryo pods complete their activation cycle and the facility's intercom crackles with confused voices from below. The new PCs announce themselves.

### What If They Try to Download the Full Terminal?

Holt controls what the terminal shows. If they try to pull the full database, give them the three fragments, facility schematics, and a massive encrypted partition they can't crack — labeled only with a project code: **SD-7**. This becomes a future hook. Kallius will spend weeks trying to crack it.

## PC Questions: What to Tell Them

This section covers the most likely questions PCs will ask during Session 0 and how to answer them. The goal is never to shut players down — you want to give them *material to work with* even when the honest answer is "your character doesn't know." Always lean toward opening a door rather than closing one.

---

### The Golden Rule

**"You don't know for certain — but what do *you* think happened?"**

This is your universal redirect. It validates the question, signals that mystery is intentional, and — most importantly — invites the player to propose something. Whatever they suggest becomes a character belief you can later confirm, complicate, or subvert. Write down what they say.

---

### The Standard Deviation Questions

These will come hard and fast in Scene 3. Expect all of them.

**"What is Project: Standard Deviation?"**
> "The name doesn't mean anything to any of you yet. There's no record of it in public Kimura documentation. Whatever it is, it wasn't published — and someone went to significant effort to keep it off the official facility manifest. Your character knows what you see: a project header, some cryo pods, and intake logs. That's it."
>
> *If they push:* "You could spend some time with the terminal. What are you trying to find?"

**"Were we experiments? Were we subjects?"**
> "You don't know. The intake logs don't say what you were there *for* — only that you were processed in, tagged, and put under. What's your character's read on it? That gut feeling is real even if the data isn't there yet."

**"How long were we in cryo?"**
> "The facility was decommissioned three years ago. Your pod's status log shows an active date that predates the decommission by at least six months. So at minimum: over three years. Possibly longer — the full intake timestamp is in the corrupted partition."
>
> *Let this land.* Three years is a long time. Ask the player: "What's your character's first thought when they hear that number?"

**"Why were these two pods different? Why did they activate now?"**
> "The pods were flagged for 'delayed activation' in the facility records — but there's no authorization code attached. Someone set this up. You don't know who. You don't know what triggered the activation cycle."
>
> *Don't hint at Holt. If someone suggests 'the terminal unlocking the data triggered it' — that's a reasonable in-fiction theory. Let it sit.*

**"Who are Callum Voss, Sarah Donnelly, and Adrian Cruz?"**
> "You don't recognize the names. The interview dates in the fragments predate your own intake. They could be earlier subjects. They could be researchers. They could be the names on the pods next to yours — you didn't check all of them."
>
> *If they want to check the pod labels:* "Most of the pods have plates, but the engravings are worn. You can make out a few partial names. You don't see 'Voss,' 'Donnelly,' or 'Cruz' on the ones you can read — but there are 200 pods and most are dark."

---

### Per-Character Questions

#### Grift (Rob)

**"Why did the encryption feel too easy?"**
> "It felt practiced. Like the security architecture had a soft spot that had been carefully maintained — not neglected. Systems degrade unevenly. Maybe someone left that door cracked. Maybe you're overthinking it."
>
> *Don't confirm Holt. Just let him hold the feeling. Write down that he noticed.*

**"Can I pull anything else off the servers before we leave?"**
> "You can try. What are you looking for — you want to just pull everything, or dig for something specific?" — Let him define the question. Then give him a roll. If he succeeds, give him the SD-7 partition label and whatever schematics you decide are interesting. Full database pull gets him noise and an encrypted wall.

**"Do I know Kimura's security architecture well enough to predict the response team's timing?"**
> Roll Networking or Education, Diff 2. On success: "You'd estimate a security contract team deployed from Cere's surface hub takes about 45 minutes from activation. You're maybe 20 minutes in. You've got time — but not a lot." On failure: "You genuinely don't know this specific site's contract arrangement. Your Kimura knowledge is broader than deep."

#### Azaria (Kaleigh)

#### New Player 1 and New Player 2

These characters have *nothing* — no context, no history with the crew, no understanding of where or when they are. That's their power, not a weakness. Here's what to tell them when they ask questions:

**"Where am I?"**
> "A decommissioned research facility on the dark side of Cere. A dwarf planet in the outer Sol system. You were in cryo storage here. These people just opened your pod."

**"When am I?"**
> Tell them the in-game year. Let the weight of that number land. "Your last memory is from [however many years ago their backstory implies]. That's how long you were under."

**"Who are these people?"**
> "Strangers, as far as you know. But they recognized your intake tag — the same way they recognized each other's. Something connects you. You don't know what yet."

**"Do I remember anything from before?"**
> "What do *you* want to remember?" — This is player-controlled. Whatever they say is true. If they don't know yet, tell them: "You have flashes — impressions. You don't have to decide right now. Let it come to you."

**"Why am I so disoriented?"**
> "Cryo revival. Your body's been in suspended animation for years. You're running on adrenaline and muscle memory. Your head will clear, but it'll take hours. Right now: you're cold, confused, and there are drones trying to kill everyone in this building. Focus on that."

**"What can I do? How do I help?"**
> Look at their sheet. Name one Skill they have that's relevant right now. "You've got [Skill X]. There's a sealed door, an unconscious Kimura operative, and a corridor full of drones. Pick one."

### General "I Don't Know Yet" Phrases

Use these when you want to give players a real answer that opens rather than closes:

- *"Your character knows what you see. What does that tell you?"*
- *"You can't verify that — but what do you think is true? That instinct is real."*
- *"That answer exists. You just don't have access to it yet. What would your character do with it if they did?"*
- *"Interesting that you're asking that. What made you wonder?"* (When the question reveals a player theory you want to track)
- *"You've got [Relevant Skill]. Roll and tell me what [Character Name] notices."* (Turn a question into a Test)
- *"You've seen something like this before — what does this remind you of?"* (Invite a Declaration or character history)
- *"Yes — but not all of it. Here's the piece you know..."* (Partial reveals feel more real than full ones)
- *"That's the right question. File it."* (Acknowledges the question is important without answering it)
