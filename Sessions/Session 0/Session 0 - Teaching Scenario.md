# Session 0: Teaching Scenario — Dead Drop on Ansari

**Purpose:** A short, self-contained job that teaches FATE mechanics through play. Each scene introduces a new mechanic so players learn by doing, not reading.

**Runtime:** 90-120 minutes

**Setting:** [[Locations/Sol System/Ansari Station]] — a mid-tier Coalition transit hub orbiting Mars. Neutral ground, busy, lots of cameras.

**Crew:** 5 existing PCs (minus Doc) + 2 new players (pregens provided below)

**When:** This job takes place **before** the current arc. A flashback to the crew's early days running contracts together. Nothing here affects the live campaign.

---

## The Job

**Contractor:** [[Characters/Named NPCs/Lester Lucas|Lester Lucas]] (via the Chickenhawk)

**Brief:** Pick up a data core from a dead drop on Ansari Station. The core contains stolen House Tsarya bioweapons research. Deliver it to a Chickenhawk courier at Docking Bay 7. Payment: 3 Rep bumps each.

**Complication:** A Tsarya security team (4 operatives + 1 Savant handler) is already on station, sweeping for the core. They don't know who has it — yet.

**The Twist:** The dead drop contact is already dead. The core is inside his mnemonic implant. Someone has to extract it from a corpse in a public morgue without drawing attention.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

**Location:** The Chickenhawk's cargo hold
**Mechanic Focus:** Aspects, Invokes, Declarations

### Setup

Lester Lucas briefs the crew via a flickering holo from an undisclosed location. He explains the job: pick up a data core from a dead drop on Ansari Station, deliver it to Docking Bay 7.

**Read aloud:**
> "Simple retrieval. Your contact is a Tsarya lab tech named Daro Voss. He lifted a data core from a bioweapons project and stashed it in a locker on Ansari's commercial level. Locker 1138. Here's the key code. Get the core, bring it to Bay 7, hand it to my courier. Don't open it, don't copy it, don't ask what's on it. Questions?"

### Teaching Moment: Aspects and Invokes

Before they leave, ask each player to **read their Aspects aloud** and explain what each one means in one sentence. This gets everyone familiar with their own sheet and each other's.

Then — Lester mentions the Tsarya security team is on station.

**Prompt Aeddarius (Dan):** "You're House Dalianis. Tsarya is a rival House. Do any of your Aspects help you understand their security protocols?"

If Dan invokes **Aesthetic Aristocrat** or **Newly Minted Lord with Old Debts** to claim knowledge of Tsarya ops, give him +2 on an Education test (Diff 2) to recall details. This teaches **invoking an Aspect for a bonus**.

### Teaching Moment: Declarations

**Prompt Azaria (Christa):** "You're a celebrity. Have you ever been to Ansari Station?"

If Christa spends a Fate Point, she can **Declare** that she did a media appearance there last year and still has a backstage access pass. This is now true. Place the scene Aspect: *"Azaria's Old Backstage Pass"* — anyone can invoke it later.

This teaches **Declarations** — spending FP to introduce facts.

### End Scene

The crew heads for Ansari. Everyone should understand: Aspects are true things, you can invoke them for +2, and you can spend FP to declare new facts.

## Scene 2: Station Arrival

**Location:** Ansari Station — Commercial Level
**Mechanic Focus:** Overcome actions, Create Advantage, Difficulty numbers

### Setup

The crew docks and moves through Ansari's busy commercial concourse toward Locker 1138. The station is crowded — transit hub traffic, vendors, AR advertisements floating everywhere.

**Scene Aspects:** *"Bustling Crowd"* (P), *"ARIS Surveillance Everywhere"*

### Teaching Moment: Overcome (Basic Test)

The locker bay requires a transit pass. The crew doesn't have one.

**Option A — Social:** Diplomacy or Guile test, Diff 2 (talk past the attendant)
**Option B — Tech:** S. Engineering test, Diff 3 (spoof a pass)
**Option C — Stealth:** Stealth test, Diff 3 (slip past during a crowd surge)

Let the players choose. Walk through the roll: 4dF + Skill = Effort. Beat the Difficulty = success. Count the Shifts. If they get Spin (3+), give them a bonus — the attendant mentions a Tsarya team was asking about lockers earlier.

**If they fail:** Don't block progress. The attendant lets them through but gives them a suspicious look — place the Aspect *"That Attendant Remembers Your Face"* on the scene. This teaches **failure as complication, not dead end**.

### Teaching Moment: Create Advantage

They reach Locker 1138. It's empty. Daro Voss never made the drop.

**Prompt Grace (Sara):** "You're a SIM. Can you search the station's mesh for Daro Voss?"

Grace rolls Research or SINC (Diff 3). On success, she creates the Aspect: *"Voss Checked Into Station Medical 6 Hours Ago"* with one free invoke. On Spin, two free invokes.

This teaches **Create Advantage** — rolling to place an Aspect that helps later.

**Prompt Grift (Rob):** "You're a systems infiltrator. Can you check the security feeds?"

Grift rolls S. Engineering or SINC (Diff 3). On success, he discovers Voss was carried to the morgue. He didn't check out of Medical — he died there.

### Revelation

The data core isn't in a locker. It's in a dead man's mnemonic implant. Someone has to extract it. And the Tsarya team is looking for the same thing.

## Scene 3: The Morgue

**Location:** Ansari Station Medical — Sublevel Morgue
**Mechanic Focus:** Maneuvers, Assessments, Combined/Supplemental Actions

### Setup

The station morgue is restricted access. Voss's body is in cold storage. A bored medical attendant monitors the intake desk. Two Tsarya operatives are in the waiting area, flashing credentials and demanding access to "their employee's remains."

**Scene Aspects:** *"Sterile and Quiet"*, *"Tsarya Operatives in the Lobby"*
**Zones:** Lobby | Intake Desk | Storage Corridor | Cold Storage Room

### Teaching Moment: Maneuvers

The crew needs to get past the desk and extract the core before Tsarya does.

**Prompt Azaria (Christa):** "Can you distract the Tsarya operatives?"

Azaria rolls Diplomacy or Guile vs. the operatives' Insight (they roll +3). On success, she places the Aspect *"Tsarya Distracted by Celebrity Encounter"* on them. First invoke is free.

**Prompt Kallius (James):** "You're a mnemonic specialist. What do you need to extract a data core from a mnemonic implant?"

Kallius rolls Education (Diff 2) as an **Assessment** — he's discovering what's true about the implant. On success, reveal: the implant is a standard Kimura model. He knows exactly how to extract it. Place *"Kallius Knows This Implant Model"* — free invoke.

### Teaching Moment: Combined Actions

Extracting the core requires a Mnemonics test (Diff 4). It's delicate work.

**If Rhys (Pregen A) assists:** Rhys rolls Education. If Rhys's Education (+4) is higher than Kallius's Mnemonics (+5), Kallius gets +1 on his roll. If lower, -1. If equal, +0. Walk through the **Combining Skills** rule.

**If Sable (Pregen B) stands guard:** Sable can take a **Supplemental Action** — watch the corridor while doing something else, at -1 to her primary roll. Teach Supplemental Actions here.

### Complication: The Clock

While the extraction happens, the Tsarya team gets suspicious. The attendant is getting nervous. Place a **countdown**: the GM rolls a d6. On a 1-2, Tsarya breaks free of the distraction before extraction finishes.

If Tsarya arrives early, the crew has to talk, bluff, or run. Don't fight here — save that for Scene 5.

### End Scene

The crew has the data core. But Tsarya knows someone got to it first. The chase is on.

---

## Scene 4: The Chase

**Location:** Ansari Station — Commercial Level to Docking Ring
**Mechanic Focus:** Compels, Concession, Movement, Zones, Stress

### Setup

The Tsarya team spots the crew leaving Medical. Two operatives give chase through the commercial level. This is a **Contest** — the crew is trying to reach Docking Bay 7 before Tsarya catches them.

**Zones (in order):**
1. Medical Exit
2. Commercial Concourse (crowded)
3. Transit Hub (open, cameras)
4. Maintenance Corridor (dark, narrow)
5. Docking Ring
6. Bay 7

**Scene Aspects:** *"Panicking Crowd"* (P), *"AR Advertisements Blocking Sightlines"*

### Teaching Moment: Compels

As the chase begins, **compel specific players:**

**Compel Grift (Rob) — "Curiosity Kills More Than Cats":**
> "You pass a Tsarya operative's dropped datapad on the concourse floor. It's clearly got intel on it. Your instinct is to grab it — but stopping means the pursuers gain ground. Do you stop?"

If Rob accepts: he gets 1 FP but falls one zone behind. If he refuses: he pays 1 FP and keeps running. **Either way, the table learns how compels work.**

**Compel Sable (Pregen B) — "Impulsive — Acts First, Thinks During":**
> "There's a maintenance hatch to your left. You could duck in and flank them — but you'd be alone and you don't know what's down there."

If they accept: they split from the group into an unknown zone. Adventure and 1 FP. If they refuse: 1 FP spent, they stay with the group.

**Compel Aeddarius (Dan) — "Always Challenges the Strongest Foe":**
> "The lead Tsarya operative is bigger than you, armored, and gaining. Everything in you says turn and face him."

If Dan accepts: Aeddarius stops and confronts the operative, buying the team time (and earning 1 FP). This naturally leads to the first real physical exchange — a brief Contest or Block. If he refuses: costs 1 FP.

### Teaching Moment: Zones and Movement

Walk through movement rules as the chase progresses:
- **Adjacent zone = Free Action** (no penalty)
- **Hustle (2 zones) = Supplemental Action** (-2 to other actions)
- **Sprint = Athletics test** (Diff 2, each Shift = 1 more zone)

Let Grace use drones to scout ahead. Let Sable use Stealth to find shortcuts. Let Azaria use the *"Backstage Pass"* aspect from Scene 1 to bypass a security checkpoint (free invoke from earlier).

### Teaching Moment: Stress (Light)

If Aeddarius confronted the operative, resolve it as a quick Contest. The operative attacks (Firearms +3, WR 2). Aeddarius defends. If he takes a hit, walk through **Stress boxes** — mark them, explain they clear after the scene.

**Don't take Consequences here.** Save that for Scene 5.

### Teaching Moment: Concession

If any PC is in serious trouble during the chase, offer the option to **Concede**: "You can surrender to Tsarya right now. You'll be captured, but you narrate what that looks like — and you get 1 FP. Or you can keep running and risk being Taken Out, where *I* narrate what happens."

Even if no one concedes, explain the mechanic. It's one of FATE's most important tools and SWADE has nothing like it.

### End Scene

The crew reaches the Docking Ring. But the Tsarya team has called reinforcements. Bay 7 isn't empty.

## Scene 5: Docking Bay 7

**Location:** Ansari Station — Docking Bay 7
**Mechanic Focus:** Full Combat (Attack, Defend, Consequences, Blocks)

### Setup

The courier's shuttle is in Bay 7, engines warming up. But three Tsarya operatives and their Savant handler are between the crew and the shuttle. The two from the chase arrive behind them one round later.

This is a **Conflict**. Fight through, talk through, or find a creative third option.

**Zones:**
1. Bay Entrance (cover: cargo crates)
2. Open Bay Floor (no cover, loading equipment)
3. Cargo Gantry (elevated, overlooking bay)
4. Shuttle Ramp (objective)

**Scene Aspects:** *"Crates Full of Industrial Solvents"* (P), *"Engines Warming Up — Deadline"*, *"Low Gravity Docking Clamps"*

### NPCs

**Tsarya Operative (x3)**
Skills: Firearms +3, Athletics +2, Perception +2, Melee +2
Stress: 3 boxes | Mild Consequence
Armor: AR +2 (Tactical Vest) | Weapon: Coil Pistol (WR 2)
Aspect: *"Tsarya Loyalty Runs Deep"*

**Tsarya Savant Handler (x1)**
Skills: SINC +4, S. Engineering +3, Insight +3, Firearms +2
Stress: 3 boxes | Mild + Moderate Consequence
System Stress: 4 boxes
Armor: AR +1 | Weapon: Light Coil Pistol (WR 1)
Aspect: *"I See Everything on This Mesh"*
Programs: Blackout, Security Skim, Stream of Consciousness

### Round-by-Round Teaching

**Round 1 — Initiative and Actions:**
Roll Strategy for turn order. Walk through: each character gets 1 Simple Action + 1 Free Action. Defense does not cost your action.

**Prompt someone to Create Advantage first** rather than attack. Example: "The crates are full of industrial solvents. Anyone want to do something with that?" If someone Maneuvers to place *"Toxic Fumes Flooding the Bay"* — everyone understands the flow: create Aspect, invoke it on an attack next round.

**Round 2 — Attack and Defend:**
Walk through a full attack exchange:
1. Attacker rolls Firearms + mods
2. Defender rolls Athletics (or relevant defense)
3. Defender's roll becomes the Difficulty
4. Shifts + WR - AR = Stress dealt

**When someone takes a hit bigger than their stress boxes:**
Walk through **Consequences**. "You can take a Mild Consequence to absorb 2 of that stress. What does it look like? Give it a name — *'Grazed Shoulder'*, *'Ringing Ears'*, *'Cracked Visor'*. That's now an Aspect. The enemy can invoke it against you — first time free."

**Round 3+ — Blocks and Savant:**
The Tsarya Savant tries to Blackout Grace's drone feed. This teaches **System Stress** and the Savant mechanic. Grace defends with SINC.

If Aeddarius or another PC wants to prevent the Savant from acting, they can set up a **Block**: "I'm laying suppressive fire — Firearms test to set a Block potency. If the Savant wants to use a program, she has to beat my potency first."

### Resolution Options

**Fight:** Take out the operatives, reach the shuttle.
**Talk:** Azaria or Aeddarius could try to negotiate — the Savant handler might accept a deal if the crew can convince her Tsarya is better off letting this go.
**Creative:** Blow the solvent crates (Demolitions or Firearms, Diff 3) to create cover chaos. Trigger the docking clamps to seal the bay behind them. Grace hacks the bay systems.

### Teaching Moment: Taken Out vs. Concede

If an NPC runs out of stress and consequences, they're **Taken Out** — the PCs decide what happens. "Do you knock him out? Tie him up? Shove him in an airlock?" The PCs narrate.

If a PC is about to be Taken Out, remind them about **Concession** one more time. "You can concede right now. You'll be captured or injured, but you tell me how. Or you can take the hit and I decide."

## Wrap-Up

After the scenario, debrief with the table:

### Mechanic Checklist

Ask the players:

- [ ] "What are Aspects?" (True things about characters, scenes, and situations)
- [ ] "How do you invoke?" (Spend 1 FP, name the Aspect, explain why it helps, get +2 or reroll)
- [ ] "What's a compel?" (GM uses your Aspect against you — accept for 1 FP, or pay 1 FP to refuse)
- [ ] "What's a Maneuver?" (Create a temporary Aspect that anyone can invoke)
- [ ] "How does damage work?" (Shifts + WR - AR = Stress. Absorb with boxes or Consequences)
- [ ] "What's a Concession?" (Surrender on your terms before you're Taken Out)
- [ ] "What's a Declaration?" (Spend FP to introduce a fact — it's now true)

### Transition to Real Characters

For the two new players:

> "Now you've seen how FATE plays. Which parts did you like best? Let's build your real characters around the stuff that was fun. We'll use the ABCDE Aspect method and you'll pick your State, skills, and stunts."

Hand them the [[Campaign Overview/New Player Primer - Cold Start Syndicate]] and the [[Campaign Overview/SWADE to FATE - Table Reference]].

## GM Notes

### Pacing

- Don't let Scene 1 run more than 10 minutes. It's table-setting.
- Scene 2 is 15-20 minutes. Let exploration happen but keep it moving.
- Scene 3 is the longest non-combat scene (20-25 min). The extraction is the dramatic core.
- Scene 4 should be fast and chaotic (15 min). Compels should fly.
- Scene 5 is 20-30 min depending on how creative they get.

### Difficulty Calibration

This is a teaching scenario. **Err on the side of player success.** The point is for them to use the mechanics and feel good about it, not to challenge them. Set difficulties at 2-3. NPCs should be competent but not dangerous.

If a player rolls badly, use it as a teaching moment for interesting failure rather than punishment.

### Compel Targets (Cheat Sheet)

| PC | Best Compel Target | When |
|----|-------------------|------|
| Aeddarius (Dan) | Always Challenges the Strongest Foe | Chase scene — confront the lead pursuer |
| Grace (Sara) | Thin Skinned and Trauma-Triggered | Morgue scene — the corpse, the clinical setting |
| Grift (Rob) | Curiosity Kills More Than Cats | Chase scene — the dropped datapad |
| Azaria (Christa) | The Real Me Isn't Camera-Ready | Distraction scene — what if the act almost fails? |
| Kallius (James) | I Know a Truth the Coalition Would Kill to Bury | Morgue scene — what if he recognizes Tsarya's bioweapons work? |
| Rhys (Pregen A) | Flashbacks from the Elysium Outbreak | Morgue scene — medical setting triggers PTSD |
| Sable (Pregen B) | Impulsive — Acts First, Thinks During | Chase scene — the maintenance hatch |

### What If They Skip a Scene?

The mechanics teaching order matters more than the plot. If they bypass the morgue by hacking Voss's records remotely, pivot — have the Tsarya team intercept the data transfer, and teach Maneuvers/Assessment through a digital confrontation instead. The scenario is a vehicle for the mechanics, not the other way around.
