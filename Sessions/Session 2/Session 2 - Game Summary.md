# Session 2: Recap

**Summary:** The Europa Station threat goes from “incoming” to “already here.” The crew identifies the point of failure inside the station, confronts the Apostate operation, and pays for a hard choice that leaves Europa damaged and everyone with new enemies.

## Key Events

- The crew follows the thread from Parallax’s stolen hardware to [[Europa Station]]’s compromised maintenance and logistics.
- Evidence confirms the station’s internal team replacements weren’t random—this is a coordinated Apostate operation with real access.
- A confrontation escalates into an emergency response in a critical station zone (docks/maintenance/ops), forcing the crew to choose between containment and collateral.
- [[Zephyr Solis]] makes the call that saves lives in the moment—but the station takes lasting damage (the breach seen in Session 3).

## Leads / Clues

- **Confirmed:** The Apostates had legitimate-looking credentials, schedules, and access routes (someone built a real cover, not a quick forgery).
- **Confirmed:** The stolen mech/walker thread is connected to the station’s security assumptions (the simulation Grift flagged wasn’t a coincidence).
- **Unconfirmed:** The operation’s real goal wasn’t just an “attack”—it was to force Europa into chaos long enough to move something (or someone) through.

## NPC / Faction Shifts

- [[Europa Station]] security and command now treat the crew as capable but disruptive—assets with a risk profile.
- The Apostates mark the crew as personal obstacles (not just witnesses).

## Assets / Losses

- **Asset:** Partial identification of the internal access method (how the replacements moved undetected).
- **Loss:** Europa’s physical integrity and public confidence take a hit.

## Open Threads

- Who built the Apostates’ cover package (credentials + scheduling + operational familiarity)?
- What exactly did the Apostates intend to move/steal during the chaos window?
- What is [[Zephyr Solis]]’s personal stake (fear, loyalty, coercion, or necessity)?

## Next Session

- Stabilize the station situation and follow the internal-access trail.
- Decide what to do with any recovered evidence: go public, go quiet, or trade it.

## Continuity

- **Assumptions:** Session 2 contains the on-station escalation that culminates in the airlock/docking-area breach referenced in Session 3.
- **Conflicts to resolve:** If the breach happened in Session 1 or off-screen, adjust the “Key Events” bullets accordingly.
