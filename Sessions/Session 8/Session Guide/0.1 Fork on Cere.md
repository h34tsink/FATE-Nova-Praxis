### **Interaction with Valare’s Fork**
Valare’s fork is rumored to have passed through Cere, possibly seeking refuge in one of its many hidden corners. Leads to track her include:

- **Witnesses**: Refugees in the lower quarters recall someone matching the fork’s description, but their accounts are vague and contradictory.
- **Trade Logs**: Vendors in the Core Markets might have sold supplies to the fork. Searching their records or questioning them could yield clues.
- **The Deepshaft Tavern**: Kal Paddock claims to have seen the fork, but he’ll demand payment or a favor in return for the information.
- **Mining Shafts**: If all else fails, the fork may be hiding in the shafts, requiring the players to explore the dangerous tunnels to locate her.

### **Challenges and Complications**

- **Hostile Environment**: The patchwork systems of Cere make survival challenging. Air quality can drop unexpectedly, and the aging infrastructure is prone to failures.
- **Local Factions**: Rival groups, including rogue miners and smugglers, may interfere with the players’ investigation, either out of suspicion or opportunity.
- **Hidden Dangers**: The mining shafts present physical and environmental hazards, such as collapsing tunnels and gas pockets. Scavenger creatures may also pose a threat.
- **Distrustful Locals**: Cere’s inhabitants are wary of outsiders and Coalition operatives. Gaining their trust may require bribes, favors, or acts of goodwill.

### **GM Notes**
- Use Cere’s harsh and lawless atmosphere to emphasize the stakes and urgency of the players’ mission.
- Kal Paddock can be a key NPC, providing leads or misleading the players depending on their approach.
- The mining shafts can serve as a suspenseful exploration sequence, filled with environmental hazards and unexpected encounters.
- The refugee quarters offer an opportunity for role-playing and moral dilemmas, as the players interact with those displaced by the Coalition or other factions.
