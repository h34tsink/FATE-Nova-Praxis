### Valare - Initial Meeting

When the players discover [Valare](app://obsidian.md/Sessions/Session%207/Valare)'s [mnemonic core](app://obsidian.md/Glossary/Mnemonic%20Core.md)🔗, it seems like they can finally get the information they need to proceed. However, things aren’t that simple. [Valare](app://obsidian.md/Sessions/Session%207/Valare)'s past is full of contingencies, and one such contingency turns into an unexpected hurdle for the PCs.

[Valare](app://obsidian.md/Sessions/Session%207/Valare)’s [sleeve](app://obsidian.md/Glossary/Sleeve.md)🔗 is broken, her body damaged from the explosion that killed her. Her **[mnemonic core](app://obsidian.md/Glossary/Mnemonic%20Core.md)🔗** remains intact but is heavily fragmented. If the players manage to extract the core and repair it, they can interact with [Valare](app://obsidian.md/Sessions/Session%207/Valare) in a **Virtual Reality (VR)** setting. Her memory is unstable, and her personality is fragmented beyond repair.

[Valare](app://obsidian.md/Sessions/Session%207/Valare) knows important information about **Nowak** and her involvement but refuses to share it until the players help her **merge with her [fork](app://obsidian.md/Glossary/Fork.md)🔗**.

---
**Fragmentation**: 1

**Valare**: (Voice shaking, eyes wide) "Who... who are you? Why are you here? Did Nowak send you to finish me off? I... I knew I shouldn't have trusted her."

- _She stares at the players, trying to assess if they’re a threat or not. Her hand twitches, ready to reach for a nearby object in self-defense._

**PCs ask about what happened to her:**

**Valare**: (Pauses, breathing shaky, her voice lowering) "What happened...? It was... it was supposed to be a simple exchange, you know? Just... just a few supplies, some information, all for the cause." (She closes her eyes tightly, swallowing hard) "I... I didn’t expect betrayal. Not from someone I trusted."

- _She curls into herself slightly, as if reliving the memory causes her physical pain._

**Valare**: "One of them—a Talon—turned out to be a Centurion... He was wearing my friend's sleeve." (Her voice cracks) "When I realized what was happening, it was too late. The grenade... I couldn’t stop it. I felt the explosion, then... then nothing but the cold. I was just... floating, and I knew I was done for."

- _She starts rubbing her arms as if to warm herself, her body shivering as she recalls the feeling of suffocating in the vacuum of space._

---

**PCs ask what she knows about her previous job or why she was scrubbing records:**

**Valare**: (Shifts uncomfortably, her eyes narrowing) "You want to know why I was scrubbing records?" (She hesitates, glancing at the ground) "It wasn't my idea, believe me. I was hired by someone... someone with influence. Someone who wanted you off the radar—no questions asked."

- _She lets out a bitter chuckle, shaking her head as if disbelieving her own decisions._

**Valare**: "You don’t ask questions when that kind of money’s on the line. You just do the job, clean the data, and get out. I don’t know the full story—I wasn’t paid for curiosity. But I knew enough to understand it was risky, and I could tell you were tied up in something much bigger."

- _She looks at the players, her expression a mixture of suspicion and resignation._

**Valare**: "Whoever it was, they didn’t want anyone knowing what you’d been up to. They paid a lot to keep it quiet. But now... now I wonder if they really had your best interests in mind. People like that... they don’t usually care who gets caught in the fire."

- _Valare's voice trails off, and her eyes dart around the room nervously, as if she fears someone might be listening._

**Valare**: "All I can say is, there are some powerful interests at play here. I think you know that already, or you wouldn't be here, right?"

---

**PCs ask why she won’t help them right away:**

**Valare**: (Bitter laugh, almost a sob) "You don’t get it, do you?" (She looks up, her eyes brimming with tears) "You want me to merge... to take in the memories of my death. To relive... that." (She clenches her jaw, her entire body tense) "I... I don't want that. I felt myself die, felt the cold, the emptiness. My mind breaking apart."

- _She wraps her arms around herself tightly, her voice becoming almost a whisper._

**Valare**: "You think I want to go through that again? You think it’s easy to just... just take all that pain and make it a part of me? I can’t. I don’t... I don’t want to."

- _Valare glances at the players, her gaze a mixture of fear and hope. Her voice cracks slightly as she continues._

**Valare**: "But if... if I don’t merge, I know what that means for you. And for me. I’ll help, but only if you can make her—make me—see that it's worth it. Otherwise, I’d rather stay as I am. Fragmented... but alive."

---

**If the players fail or choose a more forceful approach:**

**Valare**: (Recoiling, her eyes wide in panic) "No... I won’t do it. You can’t make me!" (She scrambles away, her back against the wall) "I’d rather die than go through that again... You can’t understand! You don’t know what it’s like!"

- _Valare’s breathing becomes rapid, almost hyperventilating. Her fear manifests physically—she’s shaking, her fingers clawing at her own skin as if trying to ground herself._

**Valare**: "You want me to suffer again? You want me to lose myself? I won’t! I’ll fight you... I swear I will!"

### **Notes for the GM**

- **Valare’s Actions**:
    
    - Throughout the conversation, Valare’s emotions should be portrayed as raw and unstable. Her body language is defensive—hunched shoulders, arms wrapped around herself, frequent flinching.
    - She may break into tears at points, her voice cracking as she talks about her death.
    - If reassured, she might try to regain composure, but it’s clear she’s scared and vulnerable.
- **Emphasize Trauma**: Valare’s trauma is at the forefront. She’s haunted by her death—players need to acknowledge and address that fear to make progress. This is a great chance for players to show empathy or cunning.
    
- **Varying Tone**: Depending on how the players approach her, Valare’s tone shifts. If they are empathetic, she softens and becomes vulnerable. If they push too hard, she becomes panicked and defensive, almost animalistic in her desperation.
    

This dialogue is meant to show Valare as a deeply scarred but still capable character—one who has been through something unimaginable and is struggling to find her way. The PCs' actions will determine if she becomes an ally or another lost opportunity.
