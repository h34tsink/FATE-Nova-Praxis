# Data Wipes: Valare vs Chimera

The sources detail two distinct instances of data manipulation related to the players' lost memories: one performed by Valare and the other by Chimera. Here's a breakdown of their actions and motivations:

## Valare's Actions

- **What she did:** Used her Savant Program to erase the players' "digital footprints" from the Extranet, including security logs and local mesh data related to the PCs and their failed mission.
- **Why she did it:** Hired by Nowak (Tsaryan official) to wipe data after someone began purging the players' records. Nowak wanted to protect herself from any connection to the PCs' mission.
- **Scope:** Focused on removing **external** traces—public records, security footage, and mission logs. Goal: make it seem like the players never went on their mission.
- **FATE GM Note:** Treat Valare's actions as a Create Advantage or Overcome action targeting the scene Aspect "Digital Footprints." Success means the PCs' activities are hidden from external investigation.

## Chimera's Actions

- **What he did:** Erased data from the players' personal records and social networks, including private Extranet interactions, passwords, and intimate knowledge.
- **Why he did it:** As an illegal gestalt entity, Chimera acted out of self-preservation, erasing personal data to cover his tracks and avoid discovery.
- **Scope:** Targeted the PCs' **personal** data, aiming to erase evidence of his creation and obstruct investigation into the players' disappearance.
- **FATE GM Note:** Chimera's actions can be modeled as a Create Advantage or Overcome targeting the Aspect "Personal Memories." Success means the PCs have gaps in their own recollections and records.

## Key Differences

- **Motivation:** Valare acted on Nowak's orders to protect Nowak; Chimera acted out of self-preservation to conceal his existence.
- **Focus:** Valare targeted external data (mission traces); Chimera targeted personal data (memories, private records).

## GM Guidance for FATE

- Use Aspects like "Digital Footprints" and "Personal Memories" to represent the effects of these wipes.
- Allow PCs to attempt Overcome rolls to recover lost data or memories, with difficulty based on the thoroughness of the wipe.
- Success may reveal clues about Nowak, Valare, or Chimera; failure may reinforce gaps and confusion.

In [[Sessions/Session 7/Summary|Summary]], Valare's data wipe was a **cover-up** for Nowak, while Chimera's was an act of **self-preservation** to keep him hidden.
