# Session 9: Scenes and Zones

> **System:** Nova Praxis (FATE)  
> **Theme:** Identity, consent, trauma, and consequence  
> **Core Question:** *Do the PCs secure Valare’s fork at the cost of her autonomy—or protect her agency at the cost of their mission?*

---

## Player-Facing Session Summary (Read or Paraphrase)

> **What You Know**  
> Someone connected to Valare is alive—scared, hidden, and running. Powerful factions are looking for her, and every question you ask makes the net tighter. This isn’t just a retrieval job.

> **What’s at Stake**  
> Trust, time, and identity. You can be careful, or you can be fast—but not both forever.

---

## Recap & Transition

The PCs have recovered Valare’s mnemonic core and learned that her backup fork is alive, resleeved, and hiding somewhere beyond the Icarus Hub.

Valare’s fork is terrified of merging due to the trauma of her death and is withholding critical information the PCs need.

Leads point to:

- Siri’s asteroid habitats
- Talon-friendly contacts (Seren)
- Cere’s mining colony
- The Shadow Market

Multiple factions are searching. Time matters.

---

## Escalation Mechanic: Exposure Clock

A visible 4-segment clock.

1. Rumors Spread  
2. Location Narrowed  
3. Agents Move  
4. Intervention (Talon / Coalition / Market)

Tick the clock on failures, delays, or noisy success.

## Extended Action: Convincing Valare’s Fork to Merge

- **Difficulty:** 4  
- **Mark:** 12  
- **Time per Roll:** 10 minutes  

**Key Aspect:** *This Decision Cannot Be Taken Back*

- **Spin:** Reduce the mark by 1  
- **Stall:** Catastrophic failure (fork flees, breaks down, or merge becomes impossible)

Each roll represents a distinct emotional or technical beat.

## Player-Facing Outcome Summary

No matter what happens, this choice echoes forward.  
You may gain answers, lose allies, or change Valare forever—but the galaxy noticed how you treated someone who was afraid.

## GM Notes

- Escalate every 2–3 exchanges  
- Use Compels as invitations, not punishments  
- There are no clean wins—only meaningful ones  

*Identity is not disposable.*
