# Deepshaft Tavern

**Zone Type:** Social hub — information brokerage under surveillance

**Canonical location:** [[Locations/Cere/Deepshaft Tavern]]

## Aspects

- *Everyone's Listening*
- *Cheap Drinks, Expensive Answers*

## Description

The Deepshaft is Cere's closest thing to a public square — a low-ceilinged bar carved into an old mineshaft junction. The light is amber, the drinks are cheap, and every conversation carries to at least two people who weren't invited to hear it. Miners, fixers, dock runners, and people who don't want to explain why they're here all share the same stale air.

## Clue

Someone saw a woman matching Valare's sleeve buying a cheap air canister and burner comm. Available through social rolls (Contacts, Rapport) with locals or [[GM AI/Entity Cards/R2/Kal Paddock (R2 Minor Contact)|Kal Paddock]].

## Complication

A Talon sympathizer tests the PCs: *"Are you here to save her, or own her?"* — this is a loyalty gate, not a fight. How the PCs answer shapes whether Talon-adjacent contacts open up or shut down.

## NPC Introduction — Kal Paddock

Kal is already here — corner booth, drink in hand, watching the room. He doesn't approach. He **interrupts** when the PCs are about to do something stupid.

> **Option A — The Correction:** *"You're at a table, asking the bartender about recent arrivals, when a voice from the next booth says, 'She's not going to tell you anything. And the way you're asking, you just told three people what you're looking for.' The guy doesn't look up from his drink. He's been there the whole time."*
> Kal's motivation: the PCs are making noise in a place where noise gets people hurt — including him. He's not helping; he's managing a problem.

> **Option B — The Toll Gate (if PCs ask around first):** After the PCs talk to a few locals and get nothing, have someone point them: *"The miner shrugs. 'I don't know. But Paddock does. Corner booth. Buy him a drink before you sit down.'"*
> Now the PCs approach Kal, and he's in the power position. He was named as a resource, so he knows his information has value.

> **Option C — The Business Offer (if PCs were quiet at the dock):** Kal recognized them from docking. He comes to their table because they're *potential clients*: *"A guy slides into the seat across from you. You recognize him from the dock — the one who didn't wave you through. 'You handled intake quiet,' he says. 'That's unusual. People who dock quiet usually need something loud kept low. I do that.'"*
> Here Kal's motivation is profit. He's not curious — he's prospecting.

**If PCs ignore him or refuse:** He shrugs and leaves. They lose the easiest lead in the scene. He doesn't chase — but the next time they make noise, he's less willing to help.

## NPC Introduction — Seren

Seren is **not in the tavern** when the PCs arrive. She appears only through a **referral chain** — the PCs have to earn access.

> **Option A — The Dead Drop:** A Talon sympathizer (the one who tested them with *"Save her or own her?"*) decides the PCs passed the test. They don't introduce Seren directly — they leave a signal: *"The sympathizer writes something on a napkin and slides it across. It's a location and a time. 'Be there alone. Don't bring weapons you can't hide. And don't use her name out loud.'"*
> The PCs go to a secondary location (maintenance corridor, closed stall, dead-end shaft) and Seren is waiting.

> **Option B — Seren Finds Them:** If the PCs have been demonstrably non-coercive (empathetic approach, no threats, no loud digital searches), Seren initiates — but on her terms: *"You're heading back from the bar when someone falls into step beside you. She doesn't look at you. She's walking the same direction, same pace. 'You've been asking careful questions,' she says. 'That's the only reason I'm here.'"*
> Seren's motivation: she's assessing risk. She needs to know the PCs aren't a trap before she commits anything.

> **Option C — The Conditional Test:** Seren sends a proxy — a kid, a miner, someone harmless — with a question: *"A kid tugs on your sleeve. 'Lady says: why do you want to find her?' That's it. The kid's waiting for an answer."*
> If the PCs answer well (honest, non-coercive), the kid comes back with a meeting time. If they answer badly, nothing happens. Seren was watching from somewhere in the room.

**If PCs are coercive or loud:** Seren never appears. The Talon network closes. This lead is gone.
