# Core Markets

**Zone Type:** Commercial — vendor stalls, supply trade, information-for-price economy

## Aspects

- *Information Has a Price*
- *Too Many Eyes for a Quiet Conversation*

## Description

The Core Markets fill a wide excavated chamber near Cere's central habitat ring. Stalls and kiosks crowd every surface — equipment vendors, food sellers, comm brokers, and a few operations that don't have signs. The foot traffic is constant. Privacy is a commodity you buy by finding a vendor who'll close a curtain.

## Clue

[[GM AI/Entity Cards/R2/Mira Okafor (R2 Core Markets Vendor)|Mira Okafor]] sold Valare a privacy tarp and a mnemonic dampener patch. Available through Resources or Investigate rolls with Mira.

## Complication

A [[Locations/Shadow Market]] runner offers information for a favor that will come due later. The favor is unspecified — that's the point. Taking the deal gets two clues immediately but creates an obligation aspect (*Owes the Shadow Market a Favor*).

## NPC Introduction — Mira Okafor

Mira doesn't approach. She **reacts** — and her reaction gives her away.

> **Option A — The Freeze:** *"You're working the stalls, asking vendors if anyone sold survival gear to a woman in the last few cycles. Most of them shrug or quote a price for the question. But the woman at the third stall — privacy tarps, signal dampeners, cheap electronics — she doesn't shrug. She freezes. Her hand stops mid-reach for a display item. She doesn't look at you. She looks at the exit."*
> Mira's fear is the introduction. She sold supplies to a fugitive and she knows it. The PCs can read her body language (Notice roll, low difficulty) and decide how to approach — gently, firmly, or by pretending they didn't notice and coming back later.

> **Option B — The Accidental Tell:** The PCs aren't even asking Mira specifically. They're browsing. But something on her stall matches what Valare would need: *"You're scanning the market for mnemonic dampener patches — Grift knows what they look like. You spot one on a stall display, reach for it, and the vendor pulls it back. 'That one's sold,' she says. Too fast. Sold to who?"*
> Now the PCs have a natural reason to press. Mira's mistake (pulling the item back) is what starts the conversation.

**If PCs are gentle:** She talks in whispers. She wants to know the woman is okay. She's scared, not hostile.

**If PCs are aggressive:** She shuts down, closes the stall, disappears into the crowd. The clue is lost unless they follow her — which is loud and ticks Exposure.

## NPC Introduction — Shadow Market Runner

The runner is a **competitor** who wants to turn the competition into a trade.

> **Option A — The Counter-Offer:** *"You're leaving Mira's stall when someone steps into your path. Not blocking — just... present. She's compact, sharp-eyed, no visible faction markers. 'You're looking for the same person I am,' she says. 'I have something you don't. You have something I don't. We can race, or we can deal. Your call — but decide now, because I'm not standing still.'"*
> The runner's motivation is efficiency. She doesn't care about the PCs — she cares about the target. A trade is faster than a competition.

> **Option B — The Shadow:** The PCs notice someone following them between stalls — same face, two zones back, now here. They can confront her or let it play out. If they confront: *"She doesn't flinch. 'I was curious if you were any good,' she says. 'You noticed me. That's a start.'"*
> Her following them is a competence test. She only deals with people who can spot a tail.

**If PCs refuse to deal:** She shrugs and disappears. But she's still in the race — and she might get to Valare first. That becomes time pressure in later scenes.

## Compel Target

- *Information Has a Price* → The vendor who helped Valare wants payment in kind — not credits, a task
- *Celebrity Status At Any Cost* (Azaria) → Someone recognizes her; now the crew's presence is public knowledge
