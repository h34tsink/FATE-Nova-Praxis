### How to Introduce: Kal Paddock

Kal is a **gatekeeper** — he controls docking flow and he's already in the scene. Don't have him walk up; make the PCs walk *through* him.

> **Option A — The Toll Gate:** *"The dock intake clears you through, but the guy working the second console — stocky, coveralls, not customs — hasn't waved you past. He's reading your manifest like he's deciding something. He doesn't look up."*
> The PCs have to decide: wait for him, push past, or engage. Any of those starts the conversation on his terms.

> **Option B — The Reaction:** If the PCs ask anyone about recent arrivals or Valare before reaching Kal, have him be the one who *notices* the question: *"You're asking the dock attendant about recent berth rentals when the guy one console over stops what he's doing. He doesn't turn around, but his hands stop moving. He heard you."*

> **Option C — The Problem Manager:** If the PCs are being loud or sloppy: *"A voice behind you says, 'You're making noise in my intake lane. That's bad for both of us.' He's already leaning against the hatch frame, arms folded, like he was there before you noticed."* Kal's motive here is keeping his operation quiet, not helping them.

**Why it works:** Kal doesn't need a reason to talk — the PCs are *in his space*. He's the guy you deal with to get past this point. His self-interest (quiet dock, paying clients, no faction heat) is what drives the interaction.

### How to Introduce: Shadow Market Runner

The runner is a **mirror** — doing the same thing the PCs are doing, which creates natural friction.

> **Option A — Parallel Arrival:** *"While you're dealing with docking, you notice another ship — smaller, faster, no markings — slotting into a berth two rows over. The pilot doesn't go through intake. Someone waves them through. They're already inside."*
> The PCs see someone who has access they don't. That's suspicious, and it sets up a later encounter.

> **Option B — The Overheard Question:** *"You're not the only ones asking questions. A woman at the next intake window is asking about the same berth Valare used — different angle, same target. She catches you looking and stops talking."*
> Now both parties know the other exists. The runner doesn't approach — she *retreats*, which is more unsettling.

## Read or Paraphrase — Arrival

> Cere doesn't announce itself. No beacon, no welcome signal — just a scrambled transponder ping and a traffic queue of battered haulers and rock-runners circling a habitat cluster that looks like it was bolted together by someone who ran out of both time and material halfway through. The asteroid surface is pocked and dark. The docking arms are functional, probably. The comms are full of crosstalk and locals talking in shorthand you don't have context for. Nobody's hailing you. Nobody's glad you're here. But somebody in that cluster is already watching who just joined the queue.

## Clues Available

- **Comms scan (passive):** Local chatter includes a reference to "the new sleeve" — someone arrived two cycles ago who paid for a private berth in cash-equivalent and hasn't been seen since
- **Docking intake logs (active, risky):** A query for recent arrivals matching Valare's description is possible — but it's the kind of search her savant programs would flag
- **Kal Paddock (social):** He's seen her. He'll confirm she was here — for a price, and only if the PCs don't set off his "these people will cause problems" instinct

---

## Suggested Compels

- *Everyone's Watching the New Arrival* → A local power broker notices the crew's ship and sends someone to assess them before they even dock — costs time and cover
- *Patchwork Comms — Old Signals, New Enemies* → Any digital search the PCs run leaks fragments into the local net; tick Exposure if they query by name

## Transition Hooks

- **Clean:** Kal smooths them through; they hit the Deepshaft Tavern with cover intact → Scene 1 (Echoes in the Noise) opens warm
- **Noisy:** Someone in the queue recognizes the ship or crew; faction runner gets there first; tick Exposure 1 before boots hit the deck
- **Escalation:** Shadow Market runner makes contact — willing to trade intel for cooperation, or to race them if refused

## Opening Line to the Table

> *"You're in the queue. Nobody's hailing you. What are you doing in the last few minutes before you dock — and are you being careful about it?"*
