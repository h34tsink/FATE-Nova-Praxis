# Docking Bay Exterior

**Zone Type:** Physical — first boots on station

## Aspects

- *Failing Infrastructure, Flickering Seals*
- *The Air Smells Like Recycled Desperation*

## Description

The docking bay exterior is where the illusion of a functioning station ends. Seal gaskets leak. Overhead lights cycle between dim yellow and dead. The air hits you the moment the hatch cycles — metallic, recycled, carrying the faint chemical tang of atmosphere scrubbers working past their service life. This is where you commit to being inside [[Locations/Cere's Asteroid Habitats|Cere]].

## Mechanics

- **Transition point:** Crossing into the bay commits the crew to on-station presence; digital footprint begins
- **Docking intake logs (active, risky):** A query for recent arrivals matching Valare's description is possible here — but it's the kind of search her savant programs would flag

## NPC Introduction — Shadow Market Runner

If the runner wasn't introduced in the intake queue, this is the backup zone. She's already ahead of the PCs.

> *"You step off the ramp and the bay opens up — cavernous, poorly lit, three other ships in various states of lockdown. One berth is already cold and empty. Someone docked faster, cleared faster, and is already inside the station. You catch a glimpse of a figure disappearing through the inner hatch — quick steps, no luggage, purpose in the walk."*

**Why now:** The runner has better access than the PCs. She's not a threat yet — she's a *comparison*. Someone else is doing what you're doing, and they're faster. That creates urgency without a conversation.
