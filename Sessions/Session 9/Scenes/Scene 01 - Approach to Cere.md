## Read or Paraphrase — Arrival

> Cere doesn't announce itself. No beacon, no welcome signal — just a scrambled transponder ping and a traffic queue of battered haulers and rock-runners circling a habitat cluster that looks like it was bolted together by someone who ran out of both time and material halfway through. The asteroid surface is pocked and dark. The docking arms are functional, probably. The comms are full of crosstalk and locals talking in shorthand you don't have context for. Nobody's hailing you. Nobody's glad you're here. But somebody in that cluster is already watching who just joined the queue.

---

## Clues Available

- **Comms scan (passive):** Local chatter includes a reference to "the new sleeve" — someone arrived two cycles ago who paid for a private berth in cash-equivalent and hasn't been seen since
- **Docking intake logs (active, risky):** A query for recent arrivals matching Valare's description is possible — but it's the kind of search her savant programs would flag
- **Kal Paddock (social):** He's seen her. He'll confirm she was here — for a price, and only if the PCs don't set off his "these people will cause problems" instinct

---

## Suggested Compels

- *Everyone's Watching the New Arrival* → A local power broker notices the crew's ship and sends someone to assess them before they even dock — costs time and cover
- *Patchwork Comms — Old Signals, New Enemies* → Any digital search the PCs run leaks fragments into the local net; tick Exposure if they query by name

---

## Transition Hooks

- **Clean:** Kal smooths them through; they hit the Deepshaft Tavern with cover intact → Scene 1 (Echoes in the Noise) opens warm
- **Noisy:** Someone in the queue recognizes the ship or crew; faction runner gets there first; tick Exposure 1 before boots hit the deck
- **Escalation:** Shadow Market runner makes contact — willing to trade intel for cooperation, or to race them if refused

---

## Opening Line to the Table

> *"You're in the queue. Nobody's hailing you. What are you doing in the last few minutes before you dock — and are you being careful about it?"*
