# Session 9: Guide

Use this as the **runbook** for the session. The scene/zone structure lives in [[Session 9 - Scenes and Zones]].

## Recap & Intent

- The PCs recovered Valare’s mnemonic core.
- A **backup fork of Valare** is alive, resleeved, and running.
- **Valare is a Savant** with active savant programs—she detected when the core came back online and immediately went dark.
- She refuses to merge because merging means **reliving her death** and risking loss of autonomy/identity.
- She's not just hiding physically—**she's digitally aware and monitoring threats**.
- The PCs need her cooperation (or at least her information) while multiple factions close in.

## Key Objectives

### 1) Find Valare’s fork

- Use leads: [[Cere|Cere's mining colony]], [[Shadow Market]], [[Talons of Freedom|Talon-friendly contacts]] (e.g., [[Seren]]), and local fixers (e.g., [[Kal Paddock]]).

### 2) Keep the search quiet

- The table should feel time pressure without railroading: progress is possible, but every loud move costs.

### 3) Resolve the merge question

- The core question is consent vs coercion.
- If the PCs push hard, play the cost forward (reputation, enemies, Valare’s trust, or future complications).

## Escalation Mechanic: Exposure Clock (How to Run)

Tick the clock on failures, delays, noisy success, or **digital carelessness**.

- **When to tick:** failed rolls, taking too long in one zone, obvious leverage (bribes/threats), contacting known factions, leaving witnesses/camera evidence, **obvious data queries about Valare**.
- **When not to tick:** quiet success, good cover stories, or paying a cost that keeps attention contained (e.g., a favor owed to the [[Shadow Market]]), **careful digital tradecraft**.

### Clock Effects by Segment

1. **Rumors Spread** — People connect "Valare" to "you." Someone starts asking what you're asking. **Valare's savant programs also detect the investigation pattern.**
2. **Location Narrowed** — A pattern emerges: a district, shift schedule, or habitat block. **Valare shifts location based on detection.**
3. **Agents Move** — A team is en route ([[Talons of Freedom]] / [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] / [[Shadow Market]]). They are not here to negotiate. **Valare may attempt a digital countermeasure to throw everyone off the scent.**
4. **Intervention** — The fork is cornered (or extracted). The PCs arrive mid-crisis.

**Escalation rule of thumb:** If the table energy dips, tick once and introduce a new pressure (tail, comms ping, checkpoint, rival team, **digital alert**). If the PCs are being particularly loud with data searches, she'll know sooner.

## How to Use the “GM Inline Compels”

The italicized lines under each scene in [[Session 9 - Scenes and Zones]] are **ready-made compel prompts**. Use them to introduce pressure and consequences without taking away player agency.

- **Mechanically (Fate):** Offer a complication. If accepted, the player gains a Fate Point (or you pay the table pool, if that’s your table style). If refused, the player pays a Fate Point or proposes an alternate cost.
- **Fiction-first:** Tie the compel to an aspect (character aspect, situation aspect, or consequence). If you don’t have one, create one quickly: *Eyes Everywhere*, *Leaking Questions*, *Panic Spiral*, *Market Witnesses*.
- **Keep it fair:** A compel should create a playable problem (not a dead end) and should move the story.

### Quick examples

- **Scene 1** — *“You feel eyes on you.”* → a tail forms, a camera tracks them; tick Exposure or add *Marked Faces*.
- **Scene 2** — *“Helping each other costs time.”* → save someone now, but tick Exposure.
- **Scene 3** — *“Urgency feels like a threat.”* → the fork shuts down, demands terms, or prepares to flee.
- **Scene 4** — *“Someone is already reframing what happened.”* → a faction narrative starts; introduce fallout.

## Scene-by-Scene Runbook

### Scene 1: Investigation under pressure

**Opening beat (read/paraphrase):**

The air tastes like recycled metal and old breath. Everyone is tired, and tired people talk. The moment you say Valare's name—even as a question—you feel the room lean in. And somewhere in the network, a Savant's programs ping with a pattern match.

#### Ask the table

- What are you offering: money, protection, a story, or a threat?
- Are you trying to find her **to help**, **to force**, or **to bargain**? (This matters in Scene 3.)
- **Are you being obvious with your questions, or careful?** (This matters for when Valare detects them.)

#### Information model

Each stop should yield one of: (a) **location**, (b) **handler/contact**, or (c) **state of mind**. Give one on success; give two on success-with-cost.

Use the Scene 1 zone notes in [[Session 9 - Scenes and Zones]] to seed clues and complications.

#### Savant Awareness Detail

- Valare is monitoring networks. If the PCs make obvious queries, data searches, or comms about "Valare," her programs will flag the pattern.
- She doesn't know *who* they are yet, but she knows *someone is looking*.
- This can be a complication or twist: the PCs might realize they've been detected, or Valare might try to contact them (as a threat or test).
- If the PCs are careful and don't leave digital traces, this doesn't trigger—but if they're sloppy, she's aware.

### Scene 2: Hazards and pursuit

**Tone note:** Make Cere feel harsh and lawless—failing infrastructure, suspicious locals, dangerous tunnels.

**Pacing:** One hazard beat per zone; if Exposure is 2+, add a human threat beat (patrol, rival searchers, or a deal).

**Principle:** Gentle choices improve the lead quality; reckless choices tick Exposure and leave visible damage.

### Scene 3: Consent conversation

**Opening beat (read/paraphrase):**

You find her in a place that isn’t really a home—just a corner that hasn’t killed her yet. When she realizes you’ve found her, her first reaction isn’t anger.

It’s panic.

#### What she wants

- To stay separate.
- To not relive death.
- To not be coerced into becoming someone else.

#### What she fears (say it plainly in play)

- “If I merge, I’ll remember the vacuum. I’ll feel it again.”
- “If I merge, I might not be me anymore.”

**Hard line:** If the PCs “pitch” fast or treat urgency like leverage, she reads it as coercion.

### Extended Action: Convincing the fork to merge

Use the mechanics in [[Session 9 - Scenes and Zones]]. Each roll should be a distinct emotional/technical beat.

Suggested beats (one per roll):

- Prove safety (*Safe Route*, *Quiet Comms*, *Clean Exit*).
- Define consent terms (*Shared Definitions*).
- Ground a trauma flashback.
- Explain the merge process honestly.
- Make the ask as a choice, not a demand.
- Apply external pressure (knock/comms ping/agents arrive): tick Exposure or offer a compel.

### Scene 4: Fallout

Bring consequences forward immediately:

- Who noticed (Talons / Coalition / Market)?
- What story is being told about the PCs?
- What obligation, heat, or opportunity follows?

If you need a clean end-of-night landing:

- **Consent merge:** PCs gain info + an ally, but inherit a protection/relocation obligation.
- **Forced merge:** PCs gain info fast, but trigger lasting fallout (reputation, enemies, Valare’s trust broken).
- **No merge:** PCs gain partial info, but must chase the next lead while factions close in.

## Default reveals (if the table stalls)

Use these if the players get stuck or rolls go cold, to keep momentum without invalidating choices.

- **Name (who to talk to):** [[Seren]] can put them on the right trail—if the PCs can convince him they aren’t here to coerce a merge.
- **Location (where she hid):** the fork is sheltering in a Hidden Refuge reached via an Abandoned Mining Access route in [[Cere]].
- **Timing (when it goes hot):** once Exposure reaches 3 (**Agents Move**), the first team arrives within one segment (≈10–20 minutes of in-fiction time). If you need a hard deadline: “before the next two meaningful actions finish.”
