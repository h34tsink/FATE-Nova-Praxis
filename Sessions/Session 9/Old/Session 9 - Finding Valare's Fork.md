# Session 9: Finding Valare's Fork

> [!warning] Deprecated
> This note is kept for history. Use [[Session 9 - Scenes and Zones]] (scene/zone structure) and [[Session 9 - Guide]] (runbook).

## Recap & Transition

- The PCs have recovered Valare’s mnemonic core and learned that her backup fork is alive, resleeved, and hiding somewhere beyond the Icarus Hub.
- Valare’s fork is terrified of merging due to the trauma of her death and is withholding critical information the PCs need.
- The team has leads: [[Cere's Asteroid Habitats|Siri’s asteroid habitats]], Talon-friendly contacts ([[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Seren|Seren]]), [[Cere|Cere’s mining colony]], and the [[Shadow Market]].

## Key Objectives

1. **Track Down Valare’s Fork**
   - Investigate habitats aligned with the Talons.
   - Seek out [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Seren|Seren]] (Talon contact) and [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kal Paddock|Kal Paddock]] ([[Locations/Cere/Deepshaft Tavern|Deepshaft Tavern]], Cere).
   - Search trade logs, question refugees, and explore mining shafts for clues.
   - Possible Aspects: *Fork on the Run*, *Shadow Market Connections*, *Distrustful Locals*, *Hidden Refuge*.

2. **Convince the Fork to Merge**
   - Prepare for tense, emotional negotiations.
   - Use empathy, logic, manipulation, or leverage—each approach affects Talon relations and Valare’s trust.
   - Address the fork’s fear of traumatic memories; offer support or technical solutions ([[Rules and Mechanics/Mnemonic Editing|mnemonics editing]], therapy).
   - Possible Aspects: *Traumatized Survivor*, *Fear of Merging*, *Fragmented Memories*, *Coalition Regulations*.

## Scene Guidance

### 1. Arrival at Cere or Target Habitat

- Describe Cere’s harsh, lawless environment: failing infrastructure, suspicious locals, dangerous mining tunnels.
- Emphasize urgency—Valare’s fork is hiding, and time is running out before Talon agents or Coalition operatives intervene.
- Possible Aspects: *Hostile Environment*, *Patchwork Systems*, *Unstable Infrastructure*.
- Example Dialog: "You don't look like miners. What brings you to the Deepshaft?" ([[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kal Paddock|Kal Paddock]])

### 2. Investigation & Social Encounters

- Refugees and vendors may provide leads, but require bribes, favors, or acts of goodwill.
- [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kal Paddock|Kal Paddock]] is a key NPC—his information may be valuable or misleading.
- The [[Locations/Cere/Deepshaft Tavern|Deepshaft Tavern]] and Core Markets are prime locations for gathering intel.
- Example Dialog: "I saw someone matching your description, but information isn't free here. What are you offering?"
- Possible Aspects: *Information Has a Price*, *Distrustful Locals*, *Web of Favors*.

### 3. Exploration & Hazards

- Mining shafts present environmental dangers: collapses, gas pockets, scavenger creatures.
- PCs must balance risk and reward, possibly splitting up or using specialized skills to navigate hazards.
- Example Challenge: Overcome roll to avoid a cave-in, Create Advantage to find a safe path.
- Possible Aspects: *Collapsing Tunnels*, *Toxic Air*, *Scavenger Threats*.

### 4. Emotional & Ethical Challenges

- Convincing Valare’s fork to merge can be run as an **Extended Action** (see [[Rules and Mechanics/Extended Actions|FATE rules]]):
  - Set a difficulty (e.g., 4 for a traumatized, resistant fork).
  - The mark is difficulty × 3 (e.g., 12).
  - Each test (empathy, logic, technical, etc.) takes a set amount of time (e.g., 10 minutes per roll).
  - Add up Shifts from each successful roll; reach the mark to succeed.
  - **Spin** reduces the mark by 1 (the fork is more receptive, or a breakthrough occurs).
  - **Stall** means a catastrophic failure—Valare flees, breaks down, or the merge becomes impossible.

#### Example Extended Action: Convincing Valare's Fork to Merge

- **Difficulty:** 4 (traumatized, resistant)
- **Mark:** 12
- **Time per roll:** 10 minutes
- **Skills:** Empathy, Rapport, [[Rules and Mechanics/Mnemonic Editing|Mnemonic Editing]], Deceive, Will, etc.
- **Aspects:** PCs and GM can invoke Aspects like *Supportive Presence*, *Bond of Trust*, *Therapeutic Support*, *Fear of Death*, *Unresolved Trauma*.
- **Narrative:** Each roll represents a different approach or emotional beat (comfort, logic, technical solution, appeal to history).

#### Example Scene Flow

1. PCs begin with empathy, offering comfort and support.
2. They try logic, explaining the importance of merging for the mission.
3. They offer technical solutions to ease the trauma.
4. They appeal to shared history and trust.
5. Each successful roll adds Shifts; Spin reduces the mark; Stall ends the attempt.
6. If the mark is reached, Valare agrees to merge—roleplay the emotional aftermath and consequences.

#### Example Dialog for Each Step

- Empathy: "We know you're scared, but we're here for you. Let us help you heal."
- Logic: "Without your memories, we can't protect anyone. Merging is the only way."
- Technical: "We can buffer the worst memories, so you don't have to relive everything at once."
- Relationships: "You trusted us before. Trust us now. We'll stay with you, no matter what."

#### GM Tips for Extended Actions

- Use Extended Actions for any complex, multi-step negotiation, therapy, or technical process.
- Let PCs use teamwork, creative approaches, and Aspect invocations to generate more Shifts.
- Track time and consequences—delays may mean Talon agents or Coalition operatives intervene.
- If the PCs fail, narrate the consequences: Valare flees, breaks down, or leaves behind clues for future attempts.

### 5. Consequences & Next Steps

- **Success:** Valare merges, providing critical intel and future support.
- **Failure:** The fork flees or is lost, limiting the PCs’ options and Talon reputation.
- The PCs’ actions shape future Coalition and Talon relations.
- Example Dialog (NPC): "If you help us, maybe the Talons will look the other way next time. If not, you're on your own."

## FATE RPG GM Tips

- Use Aspects like "Fork on the Run," "Traumatized Survivor," "Hostile Environment," and "Distrustful Locals" for scenes and NPCs.
- Allow Create Advantage and Overcome rolls for investigation, negotiation, and technical challenges.
- Track time and use countdowns for urgency—NPCs may escape, die, or turn on the PCs if delayed.
- Let player choices drive the narrative, especially in social and ethical dilemmas.
- Encourage players to invoke Aspects for bonuses or compel them for drama and complications.
- Use dialog and NPC motivations to reinforce the stakes and emotional weight of the scenario.
