## 1. Setting the Scene

- **Arrival at Icarus Hub**:
  - The players are tasked with capturing [[Isabella Nowak]], traveling to the Icarus Hub in orbit around [[Proch]]. Emphasize urgency and travel time (20 hours by jump-capable ship, 30 hours public).
  - Describe the Hub: massive cubic structures, bridges, passageways, and a sense of tension.

- **Explosion and Initial Chaos**:
  - Just before docking, an explosion rocks a cube—beginning the uprising.
  - Workers (apostates, House Tsarya laborers) revolt; chaos spreads.

## 2. Establishing the Objectives

- **Primary Goal**: Reach and capture Nowak for interrogation.
  - Complexity: 8 (use FATE challenges/contests for infiltration, negotiation, or combat).
  - Each Segment: 10 minutes. Track time—delays risk Nowak escaping or being killed.

- **Important Locations**:
  - **Nowak’s Location**: Unknown (Difficulty: 4). Offices in lockdown—require Overcome rolls (Hacking, Security, Stealth) to bypass.
  - **Lighthouse Security**: Brutal crackdown. Use FATE Aspects ("Brutal Security Sweep," "Mesh Unreliable") to create scene advantages/complications.

## 3. Moving Through the Hub

- **Navigating the Environment**:
  - Interconnected cubes, some damaged. Mesh network unreliable (−2 to SINC/network rolls; create FATE Aspect: "Mesh Down").
  - Encourage creative approaches: Stealth, Athletics, Hacking, or social skills to avoid patrols.

- **Encounters**:
  - **Dock Patrols**: Light (Difficulty: 4), Heavy (Difficulty: 6). Use FATE opposition rolls; allow players to Create Advantage ("Distracted Patrols," "Hidden Route").
  - **Kestrel & Crimson Sons**: Arrive to clean up and eliminate PCs/Nowak. Use FATE Aspects ("Assassin on the Hunt") and invoke for tension.

## 4. Finding and Confronting Nowak

- **Locating Nowak**:
  - Office sealed. Bodyguards (Difficulty: 8). Overcome/Contest rolls for infiltration, negotiation, or trickery.
  - Time pressure: If slow, Kestrel kills Nowak (use FATE countdown clock or compel time-based Aspects).
  - If captured, Nowak may offer intel for safe passage—roleplay her fear and leverage.

## 5. Dealing with Other Factions

- **Apostate & Talon Forces**:
  - Apostates (instigated by Chimera) rebel. Use FATE Aspects ("Uprising in Progress") for scene complications.
  - Talons of Freedom: May assist or hinder PCs. Allow PCs to Create Advantage by aiding rebellion or negotiating.

- **Lighthouse Forces**:
  - Some restore order, others shoot on sight. Use FATE Aspects ("Trigger-Happy Security") and compel for danger.
  - PCs can negotiate, sneak, or fight—let their approach shape future faction relations.

## 6. Complications & Environmental Hazards

- **Damaged Station**:
  - Depressurized areas, fires, collapsing infrastructure. Use FATE Aspects ("Flickering Lights," "Hull Breach") and invoke/compel for obstacles.
  - Alternate routes may require Athletics, Engineering, or Hacking rolls.

- **Get Nowak Before She Gets Away (Difficulty: 4)**:
  - Lighthouse lockdown restricts movement. Use FATE countdowns and compel urgency.

## 7. Possible Outcomes

- **Successful Capture**: PCs secure Nowak, dash to ship, avoid assassins. Gain valuable intel and possible future allies.
- **Failure**: Kestrel eliminates Nowak; PCs get coordinates but lose deeper intel.

## GM Tips for FATE

- Use Aspects liberally for scenes, locations, and NPCs. Encourage players to Create Advantage and invoke/compel for drama.
- Track time and use countdowns for urgency.
- Let player choices (stealth, negotiation, aiding factions) shape the story and future consequences.
- Stress the chaos and danger—every encounter can escalate or de-escalate based on actions and Fate Points spent.

## Summary

- Set the scene with chaos and uprising.
- Guide players through dangerous, dynamic environments using FATE mechanics.
- Make every faction encounter meaningful and reactive.
- Stress urgency to reach Nowak and highlight consequences of success or failure.
