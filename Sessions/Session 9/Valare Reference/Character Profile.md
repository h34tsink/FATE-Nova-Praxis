# Valare: Character Profile (GM Reference)

**Purpose:** Understand Valare's psychology, motivations, and boundaries so you can play her authentically and react truthfully to the PCs' choices.

**Core Tension:** She is a person forced to choose between two impossible options—relive trauma through merging, or remain fragmentary and alone.

---

## THE SITUATION AT A GLANCE

### What Happened
1. **Original Valare died** under circumstances the PCs may not fully understand yet
2. **A private resurrection policy triggered automatically**, resleeving her from a backup almost immediately after death
3. **This fork has been living in hiding** ever since the resleeve (her new beginning)
4. **The PCs recovered and repaired Valare's mnemonic core** from the death scene (or elsewhere)
5. **Now two versions of Valare exist:** the fork (who has lived continuously since resleeve) and the core (the "official" backup the PCs recovered)
6. **The fork refuses to merge** back into the original consciousness stored in the core

### The Puzzle the PCs Face
- They have Valare's "official" consciousness (the core) but need the fork's cooperation or information
- The fork is terrified, informed, and **refuses to be treated as an object to be recovered**
- Multiple factions (Talons, Coalition, Market) are also hunting her, creating time pressure
- The PCs can't simply "force" a merge without destroying something essential about Valare

### What "Doing Things Right" Means
**Playing this storyline correctly is about:**
- Respecting Valare's agency as a person, not a quest item
- Making the fork's refusal feel **real and justified**, not an arbitrary obstacle
- Allowing the PCs' approach (coercion vs. respect) to have **real consequences**
- Treating identity and consent as **the actual story**, not backdrop

## WHO IS VALARE'S FORK?

### Background: Savant
- **Valare is a Savant** — she has Synthetic Intelligence Neural Control and runs advanced savant programs
- Her programs allow her to **manipulate and monitor data systems**
- This is crucial: she's not just a survivor; she's someone with active technical capabilities and awareness
- She uses her programs for survival monitoring, information gathering, and detecting threats

### The Moment She Became Aware
**The fork became aware of her fork status through technical means:**
- The fork has been alive since the immediate resurrection (after Original Valare's death)
- For [time since death], she was unaware there was a separate "original" consciousness in storage
- **Recently** (when the PCs recovered and repaired the core), her savant programs **detected the core coming back online**
- Her systems sent alerts: consciousness signature reactivating, mnemonic core restored, thought patterns resuming
- **That's when she realized:** *"There's another me. And someone fixed her."*
- She immediately went into hiding, assuming whoever brought the core online would want to force a merge

### Current State
**Physical:**
- Resleeved in a cheap biosleeve from her resurrection policy (not her original body, nothing comfortable or high-end)
- In hiding on Cere mining colony (harsh, unforgiving environment)
- Running on supplies, living in a safehouse, constantly watching for threats
- She has been hiding and surviving for [time since death]—long enough to establish a network, find a safehouse, understand the landscape
- Sleep-deprived, stressed, but **functional and smart**

**Technical/Mental:**
- **Her savant programs are active** — she's monitoring networks, data flows, consciousness signatures
- She likely detected the PCs' investigation (questions about Valare, searches, data queries)
- She knows *approximately* when they recovered the core and got it repaired
- She may have technical information about who was involved in her death (if that data was logged somewhere)
- She can analyze threats, trace connections, and monitor her pursuers digitally

**Psychological:**
- **Terrified** but not broken; fear is her baseline now
- **Alert and observant**; she's survived this long because she's careful
- **Resourceful**; she bought her own supplies, found her own hideout, created her own network
- **Guarded but capable of trust** if given reason to believe the PCs aren't here to coerce her
- **Angry** underneath—at her original self (the core), at the PCs for showing up, at the unfairness of existing as a "backup"

### Identity Question
**This is the core of her being:**

She is NOT:
- A copy waiting to be restored
- A backup that failed to activate
- An alternate version that can be "fixed" by merging
- An object or insurance policy

She IS:
- A continuous consciousness with [time since death] of lived experience since the resurrection
- The "successful" activation of a resurrection policy—she *was supposed to be brought back this way*
- A person who has experienced trauma (waking up after death, isolation, fear, survival, hiding, loss)
- Someone who has made choices that the original core (which was damaged/corrupted and had to be repaired by the PCs) doesn't contain
- Someone whose continued existence depends on **not being absorbed back into something else**

**The Core Difference:**
- **The Fork:** Has lived continuously since resurrection. Every moment of her survival is real to her. She woke up, realized she was alive, and had to *survive and hide*.
- **The Core:** Was damaged/corrupted at the moment of death, recovered and repaired by the PCs. It contains the moment of death and everything up to that point, but is essentially "paused"—it doesn't have the fork's experience.
- **Why merging terrifies her:** She would have to absorb the core's death experience AND the core would have [time] worth of her survival experience integrated into it. She fears she'll lose herself in that integration.

**What she knows about the fork:**
1. **Reliving death:** The original core contains the moment of Original Valare's death—all the trauma, pain, and fear. She will experience it as if it just happened to her.
2. **Identity dissolution:** She's been the "survivor" for [time since death]. If she merges, she becomes just another version of something that died. Her entire post-resurrection existence becomes absorbed into a history that ended in death.
3. **Loss of autonomy:** Everything she's done since resurrection—the choices, the survival, the growth—might become irrelevant in the merged consciousness. The core will have the authority of "being original" and she'll lose her voice.
4. **Technical concern:** A merge would integrate two divergent consciousness streams + death trauma. The instability could be severe. She's run models; the scenarios are bad.
5. **Worst case:** She merges, the merged consciousness is traumatized and unstable, and the merged Valare becomes dangerous or suicidal.
6. **Unspoken fear:** What if the resurrection policy was a mistake? What if she was never supposed to exist as a separate person? What if the merged version erases her retroactively?

**What she needs to hear (not necessarily to believe, but to hear):**
- *"You're a person, and we recognize that."*
- *"You get to choose what happens to your own consciousness."*
- *"We're not here to take something from you. We came to ask."*

## HER MOTIVATIONS (LAYERED)

### Primary: Survival
- She is literally running for her life
- Multiple factions want her (they want the fork, the core, or information from either version)
- She's been surviving in hiding for [time since death]—every day matters
- She fears that if factions catch her, they'll force the merge to consolidate information
- **Recent escalation:** The core came back online [X time ago]. Her savant programs detected it. Now she's even more hunted.

### Secondary: Autonomy
- She does NOT want to be treated as a missing object to be recovered (she's not lost—she's *alive*)
- She does NOT want to be forced into a merge by pressure, threat, or coercion
- She wants recognition that her resurrection was legitimate—that she's supposed to exist
- She wants agency over her own consciousness and timeline
- This matters to her **more than survival** in some moments (she may take risks to protect her autonomy)

### Tertiary: Understanding
- She wants someone to acknowledge that her existence matters
- She wants to know that she isn't a "mistake" or a "problem to be solved"
- She wants the PCs (or someone) to understand why she's refusing

### What She's NOT Motivated By
- Revenge (not inherently; though coercion might create this)
- Glory or connection to her original self's mission (she may have different priorities)
- Bargains or leverage plays (these feel like threats to her)
- Pity or rescue fantasies (these are infantilizing)

## HER FEARS (SPECIFIC & EXPLOITABLE)

### Real Fears (Use These Carefully)
1. **Death (again):** The actual sensation of vacuum, the memory in the core
2. **Dissolution:** Ceasing to exist as a consciousness
3. **Being used:** Becoming a tool in someone else's game
4. **Being alone:** She's been isolated and scared for a long time
5. **Being forced:** Any hint that her choice is being taken away

### How to Play These
- **Never use her fears as simple compel fodder.** These are deep.
- A compel touching her fear should open roleplay, not close it
- Example: *"She hears the knock and her hands start shaking"* → this is an invitation for the PCs to show care, not an automatic surrender
- If she feels genuinely unsafe, **she will run**. Make this real—if the PCs push hard, she flees into the mining tunnels, and they have to decide whether to pursue or let her go

## WHAT SHE KNOWS (Information Value)

### What She Knows (Information Value)

**She has two types of information:**

**Type 1: What she's personally experienced**
- How and why she died (Original Valare's death)
- Who wanted her dead
- What she was doing when she died
- Who resleeved her and why

**Type 2: What her savant programs have detected**
- System signatures and consciousness patterns
- Data flows and network activity
- Faction movements (detected through traffic analysis, alerts, etc.)
- Possibly who recovered the core and when
- Technical details about the resurrection and current state of the core
- Data corruption or damage to the original core (and how it was repaired)

### The Catch
- She will **not** volunteer this information if she feels coerced
- Under duress, she may give false information or partial truths
- If treated with respect and given genuine choice, she becomes a full information partner

### Information Tiers (GM Guidance)
**Tier 1 (Free if she agrees to talk):**
- She's been in hiding for X time
- She was resleeved by [contact/method]
- She's been supported by [network/faction/individual]
- Her immediate fears about the merge

**Tier 2 (Requires trust or negotiation):**
- Details of her death (the circumstances)
- Who was involved in creating the backup
- What the original Valare was working on
- Her concerns about the merged consciousness's stability

**Tier 3 (Only if truly trusting, or if forced):**
- Names, dates, operational details
- Conspiracy or conspiracy-adjacent details
- Information that would change the PCs' understanding of the campaign

## HOW TO PLAY HER IN SCENES

### Scene 1: Investigation (She's Not Here Yet)
- Her presence is **felt** through clues, messages, traces
- The burst transmission *"I won't merge. Don't make me."* is her voice—someone found her; she's warning them off
- Play her as **smart and resourceful** through the evidence (she's set up dead drops, coded messages, networks)
- **She's also monitoring digitally:** Her savant programs are likely detecting PCs' investigation (queries about Valare, data searches, comms activity, network patterns)
- Establish that she *knows* they're looking for her, understands what they want (the core + merge), and is actively resisting
- The clues show a person who has been surviving and planning for [time since death], not someone who just woke up
- **She may have detected:** When the core came back online, who accessed it, how it was repaired, where the PCs are now

### Scene 2: The Approach (She Still Doesn't Know Their Intent)
- More traces of her presence
- Possible contact (she might observe them from hiding, testing them)
- If she makes contact before they reach the safehouse, she's:
  - Guarded and ready to flee
  - Testing to see if they're here to help or capture
  - **Listening to tone more than words**
- Environmental description reflects her state: harsh, isolated, barely functional

### Scene 3: First Contact (The Real Conversation)
- **Opening position:** She has a weapon (or access to one), she's cornered but not helpless
- **Read the room:** Is she angry? Terrified? Resigned?
- **Test the PCs:** Her first questions are about their intent:
  - *"Why are you here? To take me back? To force the merge?"*
  - *"Did Siren send you? The market? The Talons?"*
  - *"Are you going to hurt me?"*

#### If the PCs Approach with Force / Speed / Threat
- She reads it as coercion immediately
- **She will flee** unless truly trapped
- If cornered and forced, she agrees to merge but is now **broken** (a consequence that echoes forward)
- Post-merge, the merged consciousness is damaged (more suicidal ideation, fragmentation, instability)

#### If the PCs Approach with Respect / Patience / Listening
- She becomes cautious but willing to talk
- She explains her position (see "What She Fears" section above)
- She tests them with harder truths (*"If I merge, I'll remember dying"*)
- She becomes increasingly open if they pass the tests
- She may agree to merge with conditions, or she may refuse—but the refusal will be heard

#### If the PCs Negotiate / Offer Alternatives
- **Partial merge:** She agrees to merge specific memories only, compartmentalizing death/trauma
- **Delayed merge:** She agrees to merge, but prepares for it, gets support, sets a timeline
- **No merge:** She agrees to share information and continue existing as a separate fork
- **Conditional merge:** She merges only if PCs agree to certain protections/support for the merged consciousness

### Scene 3: The Merge Question (The Heart)
- **Her core statement:**
  > *"I know what you want. You want me to merge back with the core and pretend none of this happened. But I've lived this. I've run. I've been scared. I've made choices that aren't in that core. If I merge, do all of that disappear? Does she get to erase what I've been? And what if I merge and realize that 'I' stop existing? What if there's no room for both of us?"*

- **Play this as genuine**, not performative
- **Let it sit** if the table goes quiet
- **Don't rush her** to a decision
- If the PCs push, she becomes more entrenched
- If the PCs listen, she becomes more vulnerable

### Scene 4: The Aftermath (Her Role Changes)
- **If merged:** She becomes an allied NPC or PC resource, carrying new aspects and potentially fragmentation
- **If refusing:** She becomes someone the PCs are protecting, creating new obligations
- **If coerced:** She becomes a liability (may flee, may sabotage, may commit suicide)

## COST OF DIFFERENT APPROACHES

### Approach 1: Coercion / Force
**What Happens:**
- She agrees under duress
- She merges (or the PCs force the merge)
- The merged consciousness is unstable

**Mechanical Cost:**
- **Consequence:** *Coerced Merge*, *Fragmented Consciousness*, *Resentment Seeds*
- **Reputation:** Word spreads (especially among Talons) that the PCs will force a merge. This affects future NPC trust.
- **Narrative:** The merged Valare is less cooperative, more self-destructive, potentially dangerous

**What You Play Forward:**
- Valare (merged) has moments of dissociation or suicidal ideation
- She may withhold information or provide it reluctantly
- She may later confront the PCs about the coercion
- She becomes an example of what the PCs will do under pressure

### Approach 2: Negotiation / Respect
**What Happens:**
- She listens and engages
- Outcomes vary based on how well negotiation goes
- She may agree to merge, partially merge, delay merge, or refuse

**Mechanical Cost:**
- **Consequence:** Depends on outcome—*Willing Partnership*, *Delayed Reintegration*, *Separate Paths*, etc.
- **Reputation:** Talons and other factions note that the PCs respected her agency. This improves future faction relations and trust.
- **Narrative:** Valare becomes a full ally and information source, or she remains an asset the PCs are protecting

**What You Play Forward:**
- Valare (if merged) is integrated and stable
- She (merged or separate) becomes a resource and confidante
- She may bring her own objectives into play
- The campaign gains a new dynamic based on her choices

### Approach 3: Manipulation / Leverage
**What Happens:**
- She sees through it (she's smart and paranoid)
- She treats it as a threat, even if masked
- Outcomes similar to coercion, slightly better if subtle

**Mechanical Cost:**
- Similar to coercion, but slightly mitigated
- **Consequence:** *Tested Loyalty*, *Conditional Trust*
- She will eventually learn the full truth and will resent being played
- Fallout comes later, often at a critical moment

## RED FLAGS: WHAT NOT TO DO

### ❌ Don't Treat Her as a Puzzle to Solve
- She's not a quest object with a "right answer"
- Her refusal is not a stat to overcome with the right roll
- The Extended Action for convincing her to merge is a **framework for conversation**, not a lock-pick minigame

### ❌ Don't Minimize Her Trauma
- Death is real to her. The fear is real.
- Don't have her cry and then immediately agree to merge
- Don't frame her fear as weakness
- Don't suggest the PCs can "logic" her into merging

### ❌ Don't Make Her a Damsel or Martyr
- She's not waiting to be saved
- She's not noble for sacrificing herself
- She's not broken or in need of fixing
- She's a person making hard choices under impossible circumstances

### ❌ Don't Use Urgency as a Shortcut
- *"The Talons are closing in, so you have to decide NOW"* is coercion
- Let the urgency be real, but don't use it to bypass negotiation
- If she needs more time to decide, **give her a way to get it** (the PCs buy time, create a distraction, etc.)

### ❌ Don't Contradict Her Agency
- If she refuses, she refuses. Don't have her magically agree after drinks or a pep talk
- If she sets a condition, honor it
- If she changes her mind, show the reasoning—don't retcon her personality

## VALARE'S LIKELY POSITIONS (By Conversation Type)

### If the PCs Say: *"We need you to merge so we can get the information the original core has"*
**Her Response:**
> *"So it's about what you need, not what I need. That's what I thought. You're not here to ask me. You're here to use me."*

**Play:** She becomes defensive and less cooperative

### If the PCs Say: *"We know this is terrifying. We want to understand your side before asking anything of you"*
**Her Response:**
> *"Okay. Okay. I... I didn't expect that. Do you really want to know? Or is this some kind of trick?"*

**Play:** She becomes cautious but opens up. This is a turning point.

### If the PCs Say: *"You don't have to merge. But we need information. Can we find another way?"*
**Her Response:**
> *"You'd do that? You'd let me stay separate, knowing it costs you something?"*
> *[Pause]*
> *"Maybe I can help. I'm not sure yet. But... yes. I think I can."*

**Play:** She becomes an ally. The merge becomes one option among many, not the only goal.

### If the PCs Say: *"Tell us where you are, and we'll keep you safe"* (without first meeting her)
**Her Response:** (via comms or message)
> *"Why should I believe you? Everyone says that."*

**Play:** Words alone aren't enough. She needs to meet them in person and test them.

## MERGE MECHANICS (If It Comes to That)

### The Extended Action Framework
- **Difficulty:** 4 (Moderate)
- **Mark:** 12 total marks needed to complete
- **Time per Roll:** ~10 minutes of in-fiction time
- **Key Aspect:** *This Decision Cannot Be Taken Back*

### What Each Roll Represents
1. **Roll 1:** Prove Safety — *"Will this hurt me?"*
2. **Roll 2:** Define Consent Terms — *"Do I get to choose what happens to my memories?"*
3. **Roll 3:** Manage Trauma Flashback — *"The fear is overwhelming. Can you help me through it?"*
4. **Roll 4:** Explain Merge Process Honestly — *"What actually happens when we merge?"*
5. **Roll 5:** Make the Ask as a Choice — *"So... will you do this? Can I really say no?"*
6. **Optional Roll 6:** External Pressure — *Talons are knocking. Timers are ticking. Does this change anything?*

### Success vs. Failure
- **Success:** One mark toward merge completion. Valare agrees on that specific point.
- **Success + Cost:** Two marks, but something breaks (promise is made, resource is burned, time is wasted)
- **Failure:** No progress. Valare pushes back or needs a different approach. Consider ticking Exposure or introducing external pressure.
- **Critical Failure:** She refuses entirely and flees OR merges trauma without proper support (consequence: *Fractured Merge*, *Lost to Herself*)

### Remember
- Each roll should be a **distinct scene**, not a checklist
- **Let the PCs roleplay between rolls** — don't rush
- If they fumble one, they can try a different approach (differently framed persuasion, bringing in a neutral third party, etc.)
- **Valare can change her mind** after agreeing if circumstances shift (external threat, better option, realization of what merge means)

## COMPANION NOTES: HOW EACH FACTION SEES HER

### The Talons
- **What they want:** Her information (what she knows about her death)
- **How they'd treat her:** As a valuable asset, but also as a potential threat
- **If they find her first:** Extraction with minimal casualties (they'd prefer cooperation, but can force merge)
- **Valare's feelings:** She may have sympathies with Talons if she knows them, but won't trust them with her autonomy

### The Coalition
- **What they want:** Likely the same information, or leverage against Talons
- **How they'd treat her:** As a security issue; would likely want to contain/isolate her
- **If they find her first:** Detention, interrogation, potential forced merge
- **Valare's feelings:** She fears the Coalition more than most; they're corporate, efficient, cold

### The Shadow Market
- **What they want:** To sell her information, or to sell her (resleeved, intact consciousness) as an asset
- **How they'd treat her:** As a commodity
- **If they find her first:** Negotiation, but with implied threat
- **Valare's feelings:** She may have market contacts, but doesn't trust the market's motives

## QUICK REFERENCE: VALARE'S LINES IN THE SAND

### What Will Definitely Anger Her
- Treating her as an object or property
- Lying to her (she'll find out, and it breaks trust permanently)
- Threatening her, or people she cares about
- Dismissing her refusal to merge as "just fear you can overcome"

### What Might Work
- Genuine listening
- Honoring her conditions
- Offering her real choices
- Treating her death/trauma as significant
- Acknowledging that she's a person, not a backup

### What Absolutely Won't Work
- Logic arguments ("Merging makes sense because X")
- Urgency plays ("You have to decide now")
- Pity ("You poor thing")
- Loyalty demands ("Original Valare would want this")
- Threats or blackmail

## ENDNOTE: THE BIG PICTURE

Valare's fork is not a problem to solve. She's a person who has been forced into an impossible situation: she can relive her death, or she can remain fragmentary and alone.

**Your job is to play her truth.** If the PCs treat her well, she blossoms into an ally and information source. If they coerce her, she becomes a broken asset and a mirror reflecting their choices back at them.

Either way, the galaxy is watching how they treat someone who is afraid and asking for autonomy.

*That's the story.*
