## Key Changes to Valare's Situation

### The Timeline
- **Original Valare died** (under circumstances TBD)
- **Her resurrection policy triggered immediately** → A backup was automatically resleeved (this is the fork you're hunting)
- **The fork has been alive and surviving for [time since death]** → This is her only continuity of experience
- **The PCs later recovered and repaired Valare's mnemonic core** → This is what they have now
- **Result:** Two separate Valares exist, with completely different lived experiences

### What This Means for Running Her

**Valare is a Savant:**
- She has Synthetic Intelligence Neural Control and runs savant programs
- Her programs actively monitor data systems, consciousness signatures, network activity
- She's not just hiding physically—she's digitally aware and tracking threats
- This makes her **much more dangerous and informed** than a typical survivor

**How She Became Aware:**
- She's been alive for [time since death]
- Her savant programs detected when the original core came back online
- System alerts: consciousness signature reactivating, mnemonic core restored, thought patterns resuming
- **That's when she realized:** *"There's another me. And someone fixed her."*
- She immediately went dark, knowing whoever brought the core online would want to force a merge

**Valare's Fork:**
- Is not a "copy" or "failure" — she's the **successful** activation of a resurrection policy
- Has [time since death] of lived experience (survival, hiding, choices, growth)
- Is as real and continuous as any consciousness
- Woke up after death and had to *survive*—everything since is her actual life
- **Is also technically aware** — she can monitor investigations, detect when the PCs are looking for her, track faction movements digitally

**The Core (in the PCs' possession):**
- Is technically "earlier" in timeline (captured at moment of/before death)
- Was damaged/corrupted and had to be repaired by the PCs
- Doesn't have the fork's [time since death] of experience
- Is essentially "paused" at the moment of death
- The fork knows its condition because she detected the repair process

### Why She Refuses to Merge

**It's not arbitrary. She has legitimate reasons:**

1. **She'll relive death** — The core contains the moment of Original Valare's death. Full integration means re-experiencing that trauma.

2. **She IS the survivor** — Everything she's done since resurrection is real to her. Merging means being absorbed into a history that *ended in death*. Her post-resurrection existence would become just a footnote.

3. **Identity threat** — The core would have "authority" as the "original." She fears she'll lose her voice in the merged consciousness.

4. **Practical concern** — A merge integrating two timelines + death trauma = potentially unstable/suicidal merged consciousness.

5. **Legitimacy question** — Deep fear: *"What if the resurrection policy was a mistake? What if I'm not supposed to exist?"* Merging might erase her retroactively.

## Play Her As

**Smart, resourceful, AND technically aware** — She's been surviving alone for [time since death]. She:
- Has established networks
- Understands the threat landscape
- Knows why factions are hunting her
- Has set up contingencies and escape routes
- **Is monitoring digital activity** — she will detect sloppy investigations
- **Knows when the core came back online** — she can tell the PCs approximately when and who was involved
- **Can analyze faction movements** through traffic analysis and system signatures
- **Has prepared digital countermeasures** — tracing her through networks is extremely difficult

**Terrified, but not broken** — She's scared of:
- Being caught and forced to merge
- Reliving her original's death
- Ceasing to exist
- Being treated as an object

But she's been functioning in that fear for [time since death], so fear is her baseline, not her breaking point.

**Capable of trust** — If the PCs treat her with respect:
- She becomes an information partner
- She can negotiate conditions (partial merge, delayed merge, alternative arrangements)
- She becomes a resource and ally

**But unforgiving of coercion** — If pushed hard:
- She reads it as a threat immediately
- Under duress, she may agree, but she'll be broken (consequences: *Coerced Merge*, *Resentful*, *Compromised*)
- She'll look for opportunities to escape or sabotage later

## Updated Scene Guidance

### Scene 1: Clues Show Survival (Physical AND Digital)
The evidence of her presence should feel like someone who's been **hiding and planning for [time since death]**, not someone who just woke up.

**Physical evidence:**
- Dead drops, coded messages, networks
- Professional-level OpSec
- Evidence of resource gathering and preparation
- Signs of trauma but also resilience

**Digital evidence (if the PCs dig):**
- Her savant programs are actively monitoring networks
- If the PCs are sloppy with queries about Valare, **she detects them**
- She may have left traces intentionally to track who's looking for her
- Data signatures that only a Savant would recognize (traps, false leads, digital breadcrumbs)

### Scene 2: Environmental Hazards Mirror Her State
The harsh Cere environment reflects her situation:
- Inhospitable, barely functional
- Requires constant survival decisions
- Dangerous but navigable if careful
- She's adapted to it; PCs have to learn it

### Scene 3: The Real Negotiation
The merge question becomes:
- *"Will you treat me as a person with legitimate reasons for refusing?"*
- *"Will you honor my consent, or override it?"*
- *"Can I trust that my existence matters?"*

**This is where PC choices have the most weight.**

## GM Reminders

- **She has been alive for [time since death]** — every moment counts to her
- **The core is not her "original self returning"** — it's a backup from before her experience
- **Merging is legitimately traumatic for her** — not a mechanical obstacle to overcome
- **Coercion has consequences** — not just for her, but for the campaign and PC reputation
- **Respect changes the story** — if the PCs honor her agency, she becomes a true ally

## What To Fill In

Before the session, specify:
- **[time since death]:** How long has the fork been alive? (weeks? months? years?)
- **Who resleeved her:** Who paid for the resurrection? Why?
- **Why she's in hiding:** Is it factions hunting her? Fear of merging? Both?
- **How the PCs found out about her:** Who told them the backup survived?

These details anchor her reality and make her feel less abstract.
