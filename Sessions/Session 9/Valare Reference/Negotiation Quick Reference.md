# Valare Negotiation Quick Reference (GM Cheat Sheet)

**Use this during the session to track how Valare responds to different PC approaches.**

---

## VALARE'S EMOTIONAL STATE (When PCs Arrive)

**Default:** Terrified but functional. She's been surviving in isolation for [time since death]. She's alert, suspicious, and ready to run or fight if needed.

**Her Assumptions About the PCs:**
- They want to force a merge
- They're either House operatives or technically skilled mercenaries
- They may already know about the mutiny (depending on how they found her)
- They represent a threat to her autonomy

**Her Goals:**
1. **First:** Understand what the PCs want
2. **Second:** Assess if they're trustworthy or threatening
3. **Third:** Secure her own freedom/survival
4. **Fourth:** (If possible) prevent them from forcing the merge

---

## THE NEGOTIATION TREE

### Branch 1: RESPECTFUL APPROACH → PC asks for her consent/agency

**Examples:**
- "We came to ask, not to take. You get to decide."
- "You're a person, not an object. What do you want to do?"
- "Help us understand why you're refusing to merge."
- "Can we talk about what you need?"

**Valare's Initial Response:** Suspicious relief (she wasn't expecting this approach)

**What Happens:**
- She'll **open up gradually**
- Starts sharing Tier 1 information (what she's experienced, why she's afraid)
- Tests the PCs' sincerity (listens more than talks, watches for signs of coercion)
- Makes it clear: *"I'm not going to merge unless I choose to."*

**PC Succeeds If:** They continue respecting her agency, listen to her fears, don't pressure her, and treat her refusal as legitimate

**Result:** 
- Tier 1 + Tier 2 information available
- Possible willing merge (if they convince her over time)
- Possible alliance without merge (she helps them, stays separate)

**PC Fails If:** They slip into coercion language, treat the merge as inevitable, or show impatience

**Result:** 
- She closes down
- Attempts escape
- No merge, limited information, possible hostility

---

### Branch 2: COERCIVE APPROACH → PC attempts to force merge or control her

**Examples:**
- "We're not leaving without you or the merge happening."
- "You don't have a choice here."
- "We'll hold you until you agree."
- Threats of violence, capture, or faction-turning
- Technical coercion (blocking escape routes, cutting comms, etc.)

**Valare's Immediate Response:** Pure fear → fight or flight

**What Happens:**
- She treats the PCs as an enemy
- She will **resist with everything she has**
- Options she considers:
  - Fighting (uses ranged combat, tries to create distance)
  - Fleeing (into the mining tunnels, through ventilation, via her own transport)
  - Sabotage (wipes her systems, destroys evidence, alerts other factions)
  - Capitulation (agrees to merge under duress, but unstable)

**If Merge Happens Under Coercion:**
- Merged Valare is **deeply resentful and unstable**
- She has Tier 3 information but is unreliable
- She will sabotage or betray the PCs at critical moments
- Creates an ongoing complication for Part 3
- May require "fixing" her broken consciousness mid-campaign

**PC Achieves:** Forced merge, Tier 3 information, hostile asset

---

### Branch 3: MANIPULATIVE APPROACH → PC uses bargaining, leverage, or deception

**Examples:**
- "If you merge, we'll protect you."
- "The Talons are hunting you; we can help."
- "We have something you want—agree to merge and you'll get it."
- Lying about their intentions or misrepresenting the situation

**Valare's Response:** Cautious interest mixed with suspicion

**What Happens:**
- She'll **listen**, but she's evaluating for manipulation
- She's lived alone for [time since death]; she's learned to spot lies
- If PCs are honest about their offer: she may negotiate (possible partnership without merge)
- If PCs are lying: she detects it and becomes hostile (treated as coercion)

**PC Succeeds If:**
- The offer is genuine ("We can help you escape" + proving capability)
- The PCs are honest about their limitations ("We can't force you, but we need your help")
- They offer something she actually needs (resources, escape plan, information about threats)

**Result:** Negotiated alliance, possible willing merge, Tier 2 information

**PC Fails If:**
- The offer is a lie
- They misrepresent what they can provide
- She sees through the manipulation

**Result:** 
- She attacks/flees
- She becomes hostile to any further negotiation
- No merge, limited information, possible betrayal

---

### Branch 4: NEUTRAL/FORMAL APPROACH → PC treats this as a tactical negotiation

**Examples:**
- "The core is valuable. What are your terms?"
- "We have the core. What will you trade for cooperation?"
- "This is a business arrangement. What's your price?"

**Valare's Response:** Guarded, but potentially workable

**What Happens:**
- She understands this language (professional, transactional)
- She sees the PCs as business partners, not saviors or threats (yet)
- She'll negotiate from a position of knowing she has value
- She can be bought, but it's expensive (favors, resources, escape, protection)

**PC Succeeds If:**
- They offer something valuable (money, resources, protected passage, information)
- They keep the tone professional (no threats, no false emotion)
- They're honest about the transaction

**Result:** Alliance without merge, Tier 1-2 information, professional working relationship

**PC Fails If:**
- They low-ball the offer
- They slip into coercion language after starting neutral
- They can't deliver on what they promise

**Result:** 
- She turns down the offer
- She may attempt escape to find better terms elsewhere
- Limited cooperation, possible hostility

## QUICK RESPONSE MATRIX

| PC Approach | Valare's Reaction | Information Available | Merge Likely? | Complications? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Respectful + Listening** | Opens up, tests sincerity | Tier 1-2, possible Tier 3 | Possible (if PCs win trust) | Low (unless PC pressure later) |
| **Coercive** | Hostile, fight/flight | Tier 3 (forced), unreliable | Forced, unstable | High (resentful asset, sabotage) |
| **Manipulative (honest)** | Cautious, evaluates offer | Tier 1-2 | Unlikely (but possible) | Medium (depends on offer quality) |
| **Manipulative (lying)** | Detects lie, becomes hostile | Minimal | No | High (active opposition) |
| **Neutral/Transactional** | Professional, negotiates | Tier 1-2 | Unlikely | Low (businesslike, predictable) |
| **Disrespectful** | Angry, defensive | Minimal | No | High (active sabotage/betrayal) |

## DURING NEGOTIATION: VALARE'S KEY PHRASES

**Use these to indicate her emotional state and what she'll do next:**

**Opening (before trust is established):**
- *"Why should I trust you? You're here because someone wanted me found."*
- *"I've survived this long because I don't trust people."*
- *"If you're here for the core, you should know: I'm not going anywhere quietly."*

**Testing Sincerity (after respectful approach):**
- *"You say that now. But what happens when someone offers you enough to force me?"*
- *"How do I know you won't change your mind?"*
- *"Prove it. Show me you respect that I'm a person."*

**Expressing Her Fear:**
- *"You want me to merge, to combine my consciousness with a backup that died. Do you understand what that means? I'll experience her death. All of it. The vacuum, the pain, the moment everything went wrong."*
- *"I've been alive for [time since death]. That's real. That's my life. If I merge, it becomes irrelevant. I become a footnote in someone else's story."*
- *"I'm not a backup. I'm not a failure. I'm what I was supposed to be. And you want to erase that."*

**If Pushed Hard:**
- *"I will not let you take this from me."* [Becomes confrontational]
- *"If you force me, I'll make sure neither version survives the merge intact."* [Implies sabotage/self-destruction]
- *"Get out of my way, or we do this the hard way."* [Prepared to fight]

**If She Agrees (Willing Merge):**
- *"Okay. But understand what I'm doing. I'm letting you kill the person I've become. I'm trusting that the merged consciousness will be worth it."*
- [After merge, she needs a moment to integrate] *"I remember. Both timelines. Both lives. The death and the survival. It's... a lot."*

**If She Escapes:**
- *"Tell whoever sent you: I'm not property. Not for the core, not for the Talons, not for anyone."*
- *"Maybe we'll meet again when you understand that people have rights."*

## RED FLAGS FOR VALARE

**Things that will instantly make her flee or fight:**

1. **Physical Restraint:** Any attempt to hold her, restrain her, or block her escape
2. **Network Lockdown:** Cutting her comms or digital access (she will fight this)
3. **Core Threat:** Threatening to wipe/destroy the core ("Merge or I destroy it")
4. **Faction Offers:** Suggesting she work for Coalition, House, or Talon without consent
5. **Identity Dismissal:** Treating her as "just a copy" or "backup that failed"
6. **Authority Language:** Speaking as if they have the right to make decisions for her

**Any one of these will shift her from "testing sincerity" to "escape/fight mode."**

---

## SUCCESS CONDITION: WHAT MAKES VALARE SAY YES TO MERGE

**Valare will agree to willing merge if PCs:**

1. **Demonstrated Genuine Respect** for her autonomy and personhood
2. **Addressed Her Specific Fears** (acknowledged the death trauma, acknowledged her [time since death] of living)
3. **Gave Her Control** over the timing and conditions ("When you're ready, not when we force you")
4. **Provided Real Reassurance** (not false promises, but genuine understanding of what she's risking)
5. **Showed Personal Investment** in her wellbeing, not just in getting the merge done

**Example Success Path:**
- PC: *"I understand why you're refusing. You're terrified and you've been surviving alone. That's legitimate."*
- Valare: *"You actually get it."*
- PC: *"What would you need to feel safe enough to consider it?"*
- Valare: *[Lists conditions, fears, needs]*
- PC: *[Respects those conditions, works to address them]*
- *[Over time/scenes, Valare sees the PCs are genuine]*
- Valare: *"Okay. I'm ready. Let's do this."*

**The merge then becomes a moment of trust and vulnerability, not violation.**

---

## FAILURE CONDITION: WHEN VALARE SAYS NO (AND MEANS IT)

**Valare will refuse merge if PCs:**

1. **Used Coercion** (threats, physical force, restriction)
2. **Showed Impatience** (treated merge as inevitable/required)
3. **Dismissed Her Agency** (spoke as if her opinion didn't matter)
4. **Failed to Address Real Fears** (ignored her concerns as obstacles to bypass)
5. **Proved Untrustworthy** (lied, broke promises, misrepresented themselves)

**Result:** 
- She escapes (or fights if cornered)
- PCs don't get merger
- Part 3 proceeds without her as ally
- She may become active opposition if she feels threatened

---

## PART 3 INTEGRATION (After Resolution)

**If Merge Succeeds:**
- Merged Valare is now a **full PC ally**
- She provides digital support, investigation assistance, and insider knowledge
- She helps locating Nowak, countering frameup, identifying Chimera
- She becomes emotional investment (the PCs respect her, she respects their respect)

**If Merge Fails (Willing Refusal):**
- Valare **may still help** (if PCs ended on respectful terms)
- She provides limited cooperation, possibly from a distance
- She's focused on her own survival, but not hostile
- She may reveal herself to PCs later if they need her help

**If Merge Fails (PC Escape):**
- Valare **goes dark**
- PCs must discover Part 3 information through their own investigation
- She may reappear mid-campaign if circumstances allow
- She's not hostile, but not allied either

**If Merge is Forced:**
- Merged Valare is **unstable and potentially hostile**
- She provides information but unreliably
- She may sabotage PCs at critical moments
- She's a complication the PCs need to manage
- May require "fixing" her consciousness before she becomes fully useful
