## Setup Snapshot (Read/Paraphrase)

- The crew must reach **Icarus Hub** orbiting Proch.
- Contacting Nowak remotely fails; direct contact is required.
- On approach, a cube section detonates: mutiny is active.
- Hub battlespace includes Lighthouse forces, Talons/apostates, and cleanup teams.

## Active Pressures

- **Mutiny Chaos:** route instability, crowds, fighting fronts.
- **Frame-Up:** forged digital traces tie PCs to the uprising.
- **Kestrel Hunt:** Kestrel is moving to erase liabilities.
- **System Degradation:** flaky mesh, unstable life support, sealed segments.

Use and invoke scene aspects aggressively:

- *Icarus Hub in Orbit Around Proch*
- *Lighthouse Is Here with Extreme Prejudice*
- *Mesh Instability During Explosions*
- *Much of the Station Is Damaged*
- *Reinforcements Are Coming from Proch*

## Runtime Procedure (Per Sequence Round)

1. **Present route pressure:** what changed this round?
2. **Offer approach choices:** stealth, social, technical, direct force.
3. **Resolve roll and effect:** progress, cost, or setback.
4. **Apply environmental/faction response:** show consequences fast.
5. **Advance track + fiction:** keep Nowak objective visible.

### GM Rule

Failure never means "nothing happens." It means progress at a cost, lost position, or sharper pursuit.

## Encounter Palette (Drop-in)

Use one pressure from each category when needed:

### Security / Combat Pressure

- Lighthouse checkpoint sweep
- Heavy assault team redeploy
- Emergency lockout of transit corridors

### Environmental Pressure

- Partial decompression route
- Fire suppression failure
- Power-loss in lift/tube segment

### Political / Narrative Pressure

- Talon cell demands proof of alignment
- Apostate workers ask for immediate aid
- Dock records show forged PC signatures

## Finding Nowak

### Location Logic

- Nowak is in a fallback security post after offices are compromised.
- Core discovery methods: surveillance logs, contact leverage, local guides, emergency routing traces.

### Time Rule

If PCs lose too much momentum, Kestrel reaches her first.

## Interrogation Payload (If PCs Reach Nowak)

Nowak initially resists, then bargains from fear.

### What she can provide

- Opportunity Four outpost coordinates
- Context on why the outpost drew her attention
- Why she commissioned scrub operations around the mission
- Offer to clear names in exchange for extraction

### Deal structure

- **PC ask:** location + name-clearing + context
- **Nowak ask:** immediate extraction from hub conflict

## Failure Pivot (If Kestrel Reaches First)

When PCs arrive too late:

- Nowak is dead (GM selects responsible force; Kestrel default)
- PCs receive short extranet message from Kestrel with outpost coordinates
- PCs lose direct interrogation leverage but retain campaign momentum

### Immediate Fallout

- Frame-up narrative hardens
- Political pressure escalates
- Transition to Opportunity Four becomes mandatory and urgent

## Success/Failure Transition Pack

### Success (Nowak alive)

- Gain high-value intel directly
- Secure negotiable political leverage
- Still under active pursuit during exfil

### Failure (Nowak dead)

- Keep objective continuity through coordinates
- Increase paranoia and traitor pressure
- Move to next arc with less certainty, more heat

## Practical GM Prompts

- "Do you burn time to stay quiet, or push and risk exposure?"
- "Which matters right now: getting to Nowak first, or preserving your ability to extract?"
- "What evidence do you erase, and what evidence do you leave behind?"

## Chimera Clue Plants (Drop During Sequence)

Weave these into the sequence to build Chimera's unseen presence:

1. **Digital forensics** (Grift/Savant check): Security feeds show impossible access — someone bypassed hub authentication using *multiple CID signatures simultaneously*. No single person has those codes. A merged ego does.

2. **Propaganda intercept**: Talon broadcast names the PCs as "heroes who sparked the revolution." Language patterns match no known Talon operative. Merged Valare (if present) recognizes Chimera's speech patterns immediately.

3. **False biometrics**: Lighthouse flags show PCs' biometric data at weapons cache locations they never visited. Forgeries are technically perfect — made by someone who *has their actual biometric data from the inside*.

4. **Mesh ghost** (Savant/SINC check): Hub mesh shows a ghost user — access patterns moving too fast for any single consciousness. Multiple subsystems accessed simultaneously from different nodes. This is SINC behavior at a scale no single Savant could manage.

5. **Nowak's testimony** (if reached alive): "There's something in the system. It knows everything — your access codes, your habits, your weaknesses. It's not a program. It's *someone*. Someone who used to be you."
