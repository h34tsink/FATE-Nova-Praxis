# Part 3: Civil Unrest – Step-by-Step GM Guide (FATE RPG)

## 1. Setting the Scene

### Travel to Icarus Hub

**Attempting Contact:**

- Attempts to contact [[Isabella Nowak|Nowak]] through the Extranet prove **useless**
- Even if PCs get a personal message to her, she **won't acknowledge them**
- Their only option is to **approach her in person**

**Journey Details:**

- Must travel to the **Icarus Hub orbiting [[Proch]]**
- **In the Chickenhawk** or another jump-capable ship: **~20 hours**
- **Using public jump-ring network**: **~30 hours**
- Emphasize the travel time for dramatic buildup

### The Icarus Hub

**Physical Description:**

- Like most Icarus Hubs: a cluster of **large cubical structures**
- Each cube edge is about **a kilometer long**
- Arranged into a **larger cube formation**
- Each smaller cube hooks onto adjacent cubes with **dozens of connecting structures**
- Allows passage of both **shipping containers and people**
- Cubes spaced apart enough for **most mid-sized craft to pass between**
- Every surface of every cube is **exposed**
- Each surface features **a few dozen docking stations**

**Ownership & Staffing:**

- Owned and operated by **[[Factions/Houses/House Cipriani|House Cipriani]]**
- This hub is staffed mostly by **[[Factions/Houses/House Tsarya|House Tsarya]]**
- Dockworkers and manual laborers are **apostates and Tsaryans with very low Rep**

**Background Tensions:**

- For years the hub-workers have been **treated unfairly by House Tsarya**
- **Cipriani has largely turned a blind eye** to the growing problem
- Cipriani considered it **Tsarya's problem**
- Tsarya **hasn't seen it as a problem at all**
- **Until today...**

### Arrival - The Explosion

**The Moment:**

- When crew arrives at the hub, **before docking**
- They see the **corner of a cube explode and break away**
- **The mutiny has begun**

**Chimera's Role:**

- [[Characters/NPCs/Chimera|Chimera]] played a **large role in instigating** the Talon's attack
- While it would have happened eventually, **Chimera saw an opportunity** to shorten the timeline
- Knowing the attack could help **cover his tracks**, he took it
- Not only does Chimera know everything the PCs do, but he can **collate it all**
- He understands how to **best take advantage of the PCs' combined skills, contacts, and assets**

## 2. The Sequence: Reaching Nowak

### Sequence Mechanics (Assumes 4 PCs)

**Success Track:**

```
☐ | ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐
```

**Goal:** Reach and capture Nowak so she can be interrogated  
**Time Between Rounds:** 10 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Hard (4)

### Scene Aspects

**Established Aspects:**

- **"Icarus Hub in Orbit Around Proch"**
- **"Lighthouse has been called in to put down the threat... with extreme prejudice."**
- **"The mesh has been... flaky, ever since the explosions started."**
- **"Chimera helped instigate this—he's a hero to the apostates and Talons."**
- **"Much of the station is damaged, and the environmental systems have been compromised."**
- **"More forces coming from Proch!"**

### Sample Declarations (Players Can Create)

**Tactical Advantages:**

- "Hanger R-17 Is Clear for Docking"
- "Security Logs Show Nowak in Security Checkpoint B-46"
- "We've got control of the environmental systems."
- "Sgt. Toric owes us a favor."
- "Lighthouse Command Vessel Destroyed"

**Faction Support:**

- "Ramsey and his gang are going to watch our backs."
- "The Talon cell recognizes us as allies."
- "Apostate workers provide distraction."

**Environmental Control:**

- "All Ports on Cube 3 Are Sealed"
- "Hanger Bay B-13 Exposed to Vacuum"
- "We rerouted the power grid."
- "Maintenance shafts provide hidden routes."

### Sample Consequences

| Severity | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Mild** | Mesh Intrusion Detected |
| **Moderate** | Heavy Assault Squad Deployed |
| **Severe** | "Kestrel has our position!" |

### Digital Fingerprints & Frame-Up

**Chimera's Trap:**

- Chimera and **House Kimura used the hub's systems heavily** in attempts to aid the Talons
- Chimera **intentionally left behind digital fingerprints**
- Knowing what he does about the PCs, Chimera **left evidence implicating the PCs in the mutiny**
- This was another attempt to **obstruct their investigations**
- Hoping to direct retribution back at them **instead of just the Talons**

**Nowak's Response:**

- Nowak **unleashes Lighthouse** on the apostates
- Declares the PCs to be **enemies of House Tsarya**
- Sends Lighthouse after them in the chaos
- The docks have become a **war zone**
- Instigated by Chimera to **get revenge against Nowak**

**Important Note:**

- PCs may hear about how Chimera helped **arm the Talons and apostate workers**
- But **the PCs never meet him** or anyone who has during Part 3
- He remains a shadow, pulling strings from behind the scenes

## 3. Moving Through the Hub

- **Navigating the Environment**:
  - Interconnected cubes, some damaged. Mesh network unreliable (−2 to SINC/network rolls; create FATE Aspect: "Mesh Down").
  - Encourage creative approaches: Stealth, Athletics, Hacking, or social skills to avoid patrols.

- **Encounters**:
  - **Dock Patrols**: Light (Difficulty: 4), Heavy (Difficulty: 6). Use FATE opposition rolls; allow players to Create Advantage ("Distracted Patrols," "Hidden Route").
  - **Kestrel & Crimson Sons**: Arrive to clean up and eliminate PCs/Nowak. Use FATE Aspects ("Assassin on the Hunt") and invoke for tension.

## 4. Finding and Confronting Nowak

### Nowak's Location

**Security Fallback:**

- PCs need to get to Nowak so they can interrogate her
- They'll find her in one of the **local security posts**
- This has become a **fallback position** since Nowak's offices were taken by apostate and Talon forces
- She's in a **back room**
- Only a few **tired and wounded Lighthouse operatives** to protect her

**Finding Her:**

- Office originally sealed with bodyguards (Difficulty: 8)
- Overcome/Contest rolls for infiltration, negotiation, or trickery
- **Time pressure**: If slow, [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kestrel|Kestrel]] kills Nowak
- Use FATE countdown clock or compel time-based Aspects
- Security systems may be compromised by the uprising
- Mesh network unreliability affects tracking and communication

## 5. Dealing with Other Factions

### Apostate & Talon Forces

**The Uprising:**

- Apostates (instigated by [[Characters/NPCs/Chimera|Chimera]]) rebel against House Tsarya
- **[[Factions/Talons of Freedom|Talons of Freedom]]** are backing the operation
- Use FATE Aspects ("Uprising in Progress," "Talon-Armed Workers") for scene complications
- May assist or hinder PCs depending on PC actions and reputation
- Allow PCs to Create Advantage by aiding rebellion or negotiating
- Some may recognize PCs as being framed by Chimera's digital evidence

### Lighthouse Forces

**Brutal Crackdown:**

- Nowak has **unleashed Lighthouse with extreme prejudice**
- They're putting down the threat with lethal force
- Some work to restore order, others shoot on sight
- Use FATE Aspects ("Trigger-Happy Security," "No Quarter Given") and compel for danger
- Lighthouse also hunting PCs as **declared enemies of House Tsarya**
- PCs can negotiate, sneak, or fight—let their approach shape future faction relations

### Kestrel & the Red Suns

**House Kimura's Cleanup Crew:**

- [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kestrel|Kestrel]] arrives with a **group of Red Sun enforcers**
- Ordered by **House Kimura** to clean up this mess
- Their job: **Kill the PCs, Nowak, and anyone else involved**
- Purpose: **Cut ties between Kimura and recent events**

**Tactical Approach:**

- Red Suns try to **avoid the main conflict** between Lighthouse, Talons, and apostates
- Instead **focus their efforts on finding and eliminating the PCs**
- Use FATE Aspects ("Assassin on the Hunt," "Professional Killers") and invoke for tension
- **Kestrel knows the PCs** from being their former patron/fixer
- Can predict their tactics and likely hiding spots
- Creates moral complexity - fighting someone who once helped them

**The Race to Nowak:**

- If PCs are slow, **Kestrel reaches Nowak first and kills her**
- If PCs succeed, they can secure her for interrogation
- Either way, this sets up the confrontation dynamics

## 6. Complications & Environmental Hazards

### Damaged Station

**Physical Hazards:**

- **Depressurized areas**: Require suits or quick passage
- **Fires**: Spreading through oxygen-rich sections
- **Collapsing infrastructure**: Structural damage from explosions
- Use FATE Aspects ("Flickering Lights," "Hull Breach," "Explosive Decompression")
- Invoke/compel for obstacles and dramatic moments
- Alternate routes may require Athletics, Engineering, or Hacking rolls

**System Failures:**

- **Environmental systems compromised**: Temperature fluctuations, oxygen levels
- **Mesh network flaky**: −2 to SINC/network rolls
- **Power grid unstable**: Lighting, doors, lifts unreliable
- **Life support critical**: Some sections evacuated
- Create FATE Aspect: "Systems Failing"

### Movement Restrictions

**Lighthouse Lockdown:**

- **Security checkpoints** activated throughout hub
- **Biometric scanners** checking identities
- **Blast doors** sealing off sections
- **Difficulty: 4** to bypass security measures
- Use FATE countdowns and compel urgency
- Each delay risks Kestrel reaching Nowak first

**Navigation Challenges:**

- **Interconnected cubes**: Complex 3D navigation
- **Some cubes damaged**: Routes blocked or dangerous
- **Combat zones**: Active fighting between factions
- **Crowd panic**: Civilians fleeing, creating obstacles
- Encourage creative approaches: Stealth, Athletics, Hacking, or social skills

## 7. Possible Outcomes

### Success: PCs Reach Nowak First

**Initial Confrontation:**

- At first, Nowak **claims she doesn't know** where the PCs' cores are
- She's defensive, frightened, desperate to survive
- Lighthouse operatives may surrender or fight to the death (GM choice)

**What She Knows (Under Interrogation):**

In truth, she knows the following:

1. **About the Outpost:**
   - Nowak received word that the **Kimuran outpost** orbiting the new planet had **gone dark for over two months**
   - She wanted the outpost checked for anything of interest on **Opportunity Four**
   - She **didn't know the outpost was host to illegal mnemonics research**
   - **Being a Purist**, she ordered it **destroyed** when she found out

2. **About the PCs' Mission:**
   - The PCs were **killed during an attempt** to take the outpost by force
   - She had ordered them to **sabotage the environmental systems**
   - She declared them dead and moved on

3. **About the Data Purge:**
   - She was going to **ignore the dead PCs**
   - But when one of her aides told her that they had apparently begun **wiping much of their own personal data**, she feared what they were up to
   - **Of course, it was [[Characters/NPCs/Chimera|Chimera]] wiping the data**, but Nowak didn't know this
   - So she **contacted [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Valare|Valare]]** and tasked her to **scrub the mission** to cover the PCs' association with Nowak

**The Deal:**

- If captured, Nowak offers them a deal:
  - **Get her off the hub safely**
  - In exchange, she'll:
    - Tell them the **location of the outpost** *[Secret: 7]*
    - **Clear their names** of the mutiny charges
- Roleplay her fear, desperation, and leverage
- She has valuable intel but needs the PCs' help to survive
- PCs must decide: trust her, force cooperation, or abandon her

**Escaping the Hub:**

- PCs must secure Nowak and dash to their ship
- Avoid Kestrel's Red Suns who are actively hunting them
- Navigate through war zone of Lighthouse vs Talons/Apostates
- Potential chase scene through damaged station
- Environmental hazards from explosion damage
- Time pressure from reinforcements arriving from Proch

**Consequences of Success:**

- Gain valuable intel about Opportunity Four
- Learn about Chimera's involvement (partially)
- Understand Nowak's motivations and the cover-up
- Potential future ally (if they save her)
- Names cleared within House Tsarya (if she survives to testify)
- Deep understanding of the conspiracy

### Failure: Kestrel Reaches Nowak First

**The Grim Discovery:**

- PCs find Nowak in the security post, but **it's too late**
- She's been **killed already** by one of:
  - The apostates
  - The Talons
  - **Kestrel** (most likely)
- Scene shows evidence of professional execution if Kestrel did it
- Lighthouse operatives also dead
- Any intel Nowak could have provided is lost

**Kestrel's Message:**

- Shortly after, PCs get a **message over the Extranet**
- It's from [[Characters/NPCs/Named NPCs/Kestrel|Kestrel]]
- Contains only the **coordinates of the outpost**
- No explanation, no gloating
- Just: "Your answers are there, not here"
- Tactical purpose: Get PCs to leave the hub voluntarily
- Strategic purpose: Leads them into Part 4 confrontation

**Consequences of Failure:**

- PCs are **branded traitors** and enemies of the Coalition
- They are now effectively **apostates** until they can prove their innocence
- Lost opportunity for detailed intel from Nowak
- Must piece together the conspiracy from limited clues
- Kestrel eliminated House Kimura's connection to events
- Sets up more desperate situation for Part 4
- Potential for PC capture/status loss
- No one left to clear their names of mutiny charges

**Either Way:**

- PCs are directed toward Opportunity Four
- The answers they seek are at the outpost
- Part 4 awaits with confrontations with both Chimera and Kestrel

## GM Tips for FATE

- Use Aspects liberally for scenes, locations, and NPCs. Encourage players to Create Advantage and invoke/compel for drama.
- Track time and use countdowns for urgency.
- Let player choices (stealth, negotiation, aiding factions) shape the story and future consequences.
- Stress the chaos and danger—every encounter can escalate or de-escalate based on actions and Fate Points spent.

## Summary

- Set the scene with chaos and uprising.
- Guide players through dangerous, dynamic environments using FATE mechanics.
- Make every faction encounter meaningful and reactive.
- Stress urgency to reach Nowak and highlight consequences of success or failure.
