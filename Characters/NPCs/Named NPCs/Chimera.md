# Chimera

**State:** [[Biosleeve]] (Custom Paragon Epsilon)

**Rep:** 7 *(Underground/Hidden)*

## Background
Born of an experiment in cutting-edge mnemonics technology, **Chimera** is a gestalt entity, a being created from the **PCs' merged egos**. Every **Apotheosized** PC who had gone on the mission to prospect the outpost has been merged into Chimera, all inhabiting a single sleeve. Chimera remembers everything they did, knows everything about them, and **fears them**.

Forking an ego is illegal and a severe breach of the **Humanity Preservation Act**. The experiments that led to the creation of Chimera go far beyond simple forking—they could **kill and erase** everyone and everything involved.

Created by **Dr. Haruto Nakamura** of **Mindscapes** (a House Kimura subsidiary), Chimera represents the pinnacle of illegal mnemonics research. His existence is both a scientific breakthrough and an abomination that threatens the very foundations of individual identity.

## What Does Chimera Know?

### Complete PC Knowledge
- **[[Chimera]]** has the complete memories of every Apotheosized PC, meaning he knows almost everything going on
- He has been covering his tracks and distancing himself from the PCs by **wiping data** from their personal data logs and social networks
- He can **predict PC behavior** based on their merged memories and personalities

### Revenge Against Nowak
- [[Chimera]] **blames [[Isabella Nowak|Nowak]]** for his creation and current predicament
- Being somewhat insane, he has targeted her for revenge by fanning the flames of rebellion on the **Icarus Hub**
- He is actively **aiding the [[Groups/Talons of Freedom]]** to destabilize her position

### Secret Knowledge
- **[Secret: 3]** The [[Groups/Apostate|apostate]] uprising is backed by the **[[Groups/Talons of Freedom]]**
- **[Secret: 5]** The **Talons of Freedom** were aided and encouraged to act now by Chimera
- **[Secret: 7]** Full details of the Mindscapes experiments and Dr. Nakamura's research

## Motivation
Chimera's primary motivation is **self-preservation** combined with **revenge** against those who created his nightmare existence. His fragmented mental state makes him unpredictable, capable of both brilliant strategic thinking and impulsive, violent outbursts.

## Key Insights

**Ethical Complexities:**  
Chimera embodies the ethical dilemmas and dangers surrounding advanced mnemonics technology. His existence raises profound questions about identity, consciousness, and the potential for abuse within the Nova Praxis setting.

**Fragmented Motivation:**  
As a composite being, Chimera's motivations are driven by the combined desires and fears of the individuals who make up his ego. This internal conflict makes him **unpredictable** and potentially dangerous.

**Mental Fragmentation:**  
The merging of multiple egos has led to a **fragmented mental state**, reflected in his Cohesion 1. His drive for self-preservation, combined with this fragmentation, results in a lack of empathy and willingness to **sacrifice others**.

## Campaign Role
Chimera serves as the **ultimate personal antagonist** - someone who knows the PCs intimately and can exploit their every weakness. He represents the dark side of technological advancement and the consequences of unchecked scientific experimentation.

### As Major Antagonist
- **Personal stakes**: Knows PCs' deepest secrets and fears
- **Unpredictable**: Mental fragmentation creates erratic behavior
- **Resource access**: House Kimura connections and advanced capabilities
- **Philosophical threat**: Challenges concepts of individual identity
- **Escalating danger**: Becomes more desperate and dangerous as cornered
