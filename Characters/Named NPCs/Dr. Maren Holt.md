# Dr. Maren Holt

**Summary:** Pre-Singularity cognitive scientist who designed the [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation|Standard Deviation Project]] — the century-old ego-fusion experiment that curated, edited, and froze the PCs as raw material for [[Chimera]].

## Lore

Dr. Maren Holt was a cognitive scientist working on personality architecture and ego continuity in the years before the [[Glossary/Exodus|Exodus]]. Her core hypothesis was unsettling and specific: that certain psychological profiles survive [[Glossary/Apotheosis|Apotheosis]] and resleeving with ego integrity intact, while others fragment — and that complementary archetypes, if merged, would produce a stable composite consciousness.

She built the [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation|Standard Deviation Project]] to test that hypothesis on a hundred-year timescale. Roughly two hundred subjects were placed into cryo storage at a research archive. Six were flagged with classification codes — FACE, GHOST, LENS, BANK, SHIELD, and one reserved slot — chosen because their archetypes were predicted to merge into a stable gestalt. Those six are the PCs. The "Projected Fusion Outcome" listed in the appendix of her unpublished paper is one word: **CHIMERA**.

Holt did not stop at selection. Before cryo entry, her team performed targeted [[Glossary/Mnemonic Editing|mnemonic edits]] on each subject — not erasing memories, but *amplifying* specific traits to push subjects toward predictable post-revival states. Each PC's defining weakness was engineered as a psychological anchor — a fixed point that would prevent ego dissolution during digitization. The subjects were not informed. Consent was not obtained.

Her current status is unknown. She may be dead. She may be a [[Glossary/SIM|SIM]]. She may still be watching. Her final paper — *"Standard Deviation: Controlled Ego Fusion Through Curated Psychological Complementarity"* — was never published. The only known copy lives in a sealed data partition at the revival facility, and the PCs' names are in the appendix.

She is not the antagonist of any single session. She is the reason the campaign exists.
