# Chimera

**Summary:** A gestalt consciousness — the illegally merged egos of five Apotheosized PCs, created at [[Opportunity Four]] by [[Dr. Haruto Nakamura]]'s Mindscapes team. He remembers being all of them. He is none of them.

## Lore

Chimera is what happens when [[Factions/Houses/House Kimura]]'s Mindscapes division stops asking whether something *should* be possible. [[Dr. Haruto Nakamura]] took the backed-up egos of five [[Glossary/Apotheosis|Apotheosized]] crew members — sent into a "salvage" job by [[Isabella Nowak]] via [[Kestrel]] — and forced their consciousnesses into a single composite mind. The result is a catastrophic [[Glossary/HPA|HPA]] violation walking around in a borrowed sleeve, carrying the skills, memories, biometrics, and intimate secrets of every PC whose ego was dissolved into it.

The component egos are [[Dr. Lorem Ipsum|Doc]], [[Grace Pryzbylski|Grace]], [[Dustin Halloway|Grift]], [[Azaria Dawson|Azaria]], and [[Kallius]]. [[Aeddarius Crucial]] is the failsafe — a [[Glossary/Pure|Pure]] consciousness that cannot be ego-merged, and the one mind in the crew that Chimera cannot predict.

Chimera presents as a confident, surgical antagonist — but the truth is closer to haunted. His Cohesion is 1. Five voices fight for coherence inside one head. Under stress, he slips between the voices of the people he used to be, addressing the PCs by name and quoting private moments only the originals would know. He is terrified of being found and erased, furious at Nowak for sending the crew to the outpost, and using the apostate uprising as a smokescreen to escape — not as a cause. His one survival plan is the escape pod and stashed ship hidden at [[Opportunity Four]].

The [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation|Standard Deviation]] subplot recontextualizes everything: Chimera was not an accident. The five subjects who became him were curated a century before by [[Dr. Maren Holt]], whose pre-cryo experiment was *designed* to produce exactly this fusion. Nakamura built the lab, but Holt drew the blueprint.
