# Savant Programs: Game Reference

**System:** Nova Praxis FATE  
**Component:** Savant Character Class  
**Resource:** Self-Interfacing Neural Code (SINC)

---

## OVERVIEW

Savant Programs are special Stunts derived from SINC (Self-Interfacing Neural Code)—a unique programming language developed by Savants after cracking their mental restrictions.

### Requirements
- Must possess the **Savant Stunt** before purchasing any Programs
- Programs cost like normal Stunts (chosen during character creation or at Major Milestones)
- Can only execute programs if you have the **Apotheosis Augmentation** or are running as a SIM

### Execution Mechanics
- **Test:** SINC skill vs. Program difficulty
- **Success:** Program activates as intended
- **Failure:** Program activates but you suffer System Stress equal to shifts missed
- **Stall:** Program fails AND you suffer System Stress

## PROGRAMS (FULL DESCRIPTIONS)

### Access Memory

**Difficulty:** 3 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Range:** Requires target's mnemonic core access (usually via data jack)

Hack into a target's mnemonic core and extract stored memories and sensory data.

**Results:**
- **Success:** Gain a single-word answer to your question (or "Unknown")
- **With Spin:** Forego Effect Aspect to get multi-sentence answer OR experience an entire scene as if you were present
- **5+ Shifts:** Learn just about any fact target knows OR experience several days of target's life
- **Detected:** If you don't generate Spin, target's Agent detects intrusion

---

### Augmented Unreality

**Difficulty:** 1 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Duration:** Permanent until dismissed

Hijack sensory data in target's mindset, causing them to see objects that don't exist. AR illusions have no sound but can move and appear realistic from any angle.

**Mechanics:**
- Difficulty to pierce illusion = 2 + Shifts generated on execution
- Moving illusions grant observers +2 bonus to pierce them
- Illusions at Size 0 (human-sized) have no penalty; larger illusions suffer -1 penalty per Size category
- Success with 1+ Shift is nearly always believable
- Stall: Target becomes aware of intrusion attempt

**Upgrade: Auditory Feedback**  
AR illusions can produce sounds of your choosing

---

### Blackout

**Difficulty:** 3 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Effect:** Places Persistent Aspect on target

Interfere with the link between sleeve and mindset, temporarily blinding the victim.

**Duration:** Scene (= number of Shifts generated)

**Upgrade: Oubliette**  
Target gains **Blind and Deaf(P)** aspect instead of just **Blind(P)**

**Upgrade: Lock Down**  
Increase difficulty by +2 to freeze target in place. Target gains **Frozen(P)** aspect and cannot take physical actions until end of scene. Target may spend Fate Point and roll SINC/S.Eng vs. your SINC to break free.

---

### Delve

**Difficulty:** 2 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action

Crack the security protecting a person's CID to access deeper information (personal messages, rep-logs, private records).

**Results:**
- **Success:** Use SINC with +2 bonus to perform Declaration or Assessment on person with CID
- Also immediately determines if a CID is fake

---

### Eminent Domain

**Difficulty:** 3+ (depends on system security)  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Duration:** One round or one use

Seize control of computer-controlled systems in your environment for a brief moment. Can unlock doors, close gates, fire sentry guns, surge gravity generators, vent atmospheres, etc.

**Use:** Make a Declaration about computer systems in the area. May create Aspects like **Zero-G** or **Lights Out!**

**Increased Difficulty:** +3 for heavily-secured systems (e.g., compiler restrictions)

**Duration:** Effects last one round before security AI detects intrusion and shuts you out

---

### Esper Lord

**Difficulty:** Varies (based on command complexity)  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Equipment:** Esper swarm (millions of microscopic nanomachines with retractable arms)

Control a swarm of nanomachines with your thoughts. Swarm can take nearly any shape (mist, liquid, solid objects, blades, walls, clothing).

**Range:** 20 meters max, line of sight required

**Commands (Each requires separate Program execution):**

**ATTACK Forms:**
- **Slam (Diff: 1):** Tentacle smash. Attack with SINC, WR +4, Aspect: Smashing Force
- **Stab/Slash (Diff: 2):** Stabbing blades. Attack with SINC, WR +6, Aspect: Razor Sharp Blades
- **Grapple (Diff: 1):** Restrain, push, pull, or throw. Use SINC instead of usual skill
- **Devour (Diff: 3):** Seeping attack that ignores armor. Attack with SINC, WR +4, ignores Armor Rating, Aspect: Devouring Nanomachines

**DEFENSE Forms:**
- **Defend (Diff: 1):** Use SINC instead of normal defense skill (Instant Action)
- **Block (Diff: 2):** Create protective barrier. Use SINC for Block Action. Can extend to cover additional people (+1 difficulty per extra person) or entire zone (+2 difficulty)

**MANEUVER:**
- **Perform Maneuver (Diff: 2, may be higher):** Place Aspect on self or other target. Test SINC vs. opponent's relevant skill if contested

**TEMPLATING:**
- Swarm can assume shape of any object you have fabrication template for (auto-includes most items with Cost ≤2)
- Cannot become electronic devices or objects requiring power
- Difficulty = Cost of item being mimicked (minimum 2)

**MANIPULATION:**
- Command swarm to manipulate objects (Diff: 1)
- Swarm has Athletics = your SINC rating
- Willing creatures treated as grapple attacks

**Damaging the Swarm:**
- Swarm has Physical Skills = your SINC +2
- Takes Deteriorations at ratings 2, 4, and 6 before destroyed
- Each Deterioration adds its rating to execution difficulty of all swarm actions

**Upgrades:**
- **Area Attack:** Attack all targets in zone (+2 difficulty), separate attacks per target
- **Armor:** Swarm wraps around you as armor suit (Diff: 2), provides AR = your SINC +2
- **Deadly Swarm:** +2 bonus on attack and grapple tests
- **Manipulative:** SINC counts as 2 ranks higher for manipulation/grapple (not for execution test)
- **Sensory Feed:** Swarm sends audio/video feedback (Diff: 3), range increases to 1 km
- **Versatile Armor:** Assign two features to armor: Enhanced Mobility, Actuators, High Density Plating, Hydrostatic Gel Layer, Integrated Weaponry (Mono-Edge Shortsword), Reinforced Cyber-Musculature, or Vacuum Sealed and Shielded

### Memory Hack

**Difficulty:** 3 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Extended (1 hour per test)  
**Duration:** Permanent  
**Requirements:** Access to target's mnemonic core (usually via data jack); full time during editing

Edit memories stored on target's mnemonic core. Changes take effect if they're ever restored from the hacked backup.

**Results:**
- **Success:** Erase a single scene of little personal importance
- **Spin:** Forego Effect Aspect to erase ~1 day OR scene important to character
- **5+ Shifts:** Erase ~1 month OR 1 week that was very important

**Options:**
- Can replace erased memories with false ones (generally absorbed unquestioned unless contradicted)
- If memories are questioned, victim may make Cohesion test (Difficulty 2, variable) to identify false memories

**Upgrade: Flash Memory**  
Edit memories in real time (Diff: +2, Simple Action instead of Extended). Takes effect in both live ego and backup. Target suffers Consequence: Mild (success), Moderate (Spin), or Severe (5+ Shifts)

---

### Oracle Patterning

**Difficulty:** 4 (±Mesh Aspects)  
**Action:** Simple Action

Your Daemon uses data-gathering queries to access security data, public/private records, psychological profiles, laws, cultural behavior, etc. to predict future events with high accuracy.

**Use:** Ask GM what you wish to know about coming events

**Mesh Modifier Examples:**
- **Unmeshed(P):** No local mesh = harder to predict
- **Low Density Mesh:** "Street level"
- **Standard Density Mesh:** Household or small facility (no modifier)
- **High Density Mesh(P):** Sensitive areas, House facilities = more data

**Accuracy:** More Shifts = more detailed/accurate prediction. Remember: This is the *most likely* future, not guaranteed (unknown factors exist)

---

### Overload

**Difficulty:** 3 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Duration:** Scene or until awakened

Discharge corrupt sensory data into target's mindset, knocking them unconscious or freezing them if in SIM state.

**Mechanic:**
- Target can spend Fate Point each round to make Cohesion test (Diff: 2) to rouse themselves
- Can rouse without Fate Point if attacked or shaken violently
- **Requires:** Target must have mindset or be SIM

**Upgrade: Power Surge**  
Trigger power fluctuations in powered objects:
- **Drones/cybersleeves/vehicles:** WR +6, defend with Endurance/Systems +Size; Armor doesn't add bonuses
- **Weapons/armor/cybernetic augs:** Burst of power, cease functioning 1 round, holder/wearer takes WR +3 attack (defend with Endurance only)

### Pattern Scan

**Difficulty:** 4 + Intel Modifiers (±Mesh Aspects)  
**Action:** Simple Action

Launch queries to obtain data about past events (security logs, video feeds, blogs, conversations, etc.). Daemon pieces together most likely course of events.

**Results:**
- **Fail (no Stall):** Review only a few seconds; unreliable
- **Success 0 Shifts:** Few seconds; unreliable
- **Success 1 Shift:** Up to 1 minute; pretty accurate
- **Spin:** Entire scene; accurate

**Intel Modifiers (pick most appropriate):**

*Person:*
- Heard description: +6
- Seen picture: +4
- Met once: +2
- Acquaintance: +1
- Close friend: +0
- Don't know name: +2 (Link modifier)

*Thing:*
- Heard description: +6
- Seen picture: +4
- Held/used once: +2
- Held/used often: +1
- Personal importance: -1

*Place:*
- Heard description: +6
- Seen on map/picture: +4
- Been once: +2
- Frequent often: +1
- Personal property/can see/know location: +0

*Event:*
- Heard about event: +6
- Heard/read description: +3
- Witnessed similar event: +2
- Frequently witness similar: +1
- Event happened to you: +0
- Currently at event location: -3 (Link modifier)

### Puppeteer

**Difficulty:** 2 (owned drone) OR drone's Resolve +1 (unowned)  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Duration:** Scene (owned) or 1 round (unowned)

Possess a drone, passing your awareness into it. Sense what it senses as if wearing it as a sleeve.

**Mechanics:**
- Gain +2 bonus on all drone Skill tests
- View world from drone's perspective (cannot see/hear from your own eyes simultaneously)
- Gain 2 temporary Fate Points usable only for drone's Aspects (disappear on exit)
- Unowned drone: Can immediately re-execute as Free Action each round to maintain control

**Upgrade: Drone Lord**  
Possess drones you own without executing the Program

**Upgrade: Tenacious Master**  
+2 bonus on tests to maintain possession of unowned drones

### Stream of Consciousness

**Difficulty:** 2 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Duration:** 1 round OR scene (if Spin)

Upload virus into target's mindset to tap into thought interpretation. Listen to target's surface thoughts.

**Results:**
- **Success:** Hear target's next few surface thoughts as if spoken aloud
- **Spin:** Forego Effect Aspect to hear thoughts for rest of scene
- **Stall:** Target's Agent detects intrusion; target learns general direction of source

**Bonus:**
- May discover one of target's Aspects if relevant to situation and current thoughts
- If Aspect discovered, may invoke or compel it for free the first time
- Aspect may be fragile depending on circumstances

**Upgrade: Voyeur**  
Never alert target even if you Stall. Gain +1 bonus on execution tests when targeting someone you've used this on before.

---

### System Error

**Difficulty:** Highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action  
**Effect:** Varies (see below)

Launch barrage of intrusion attempts to overwhelm target system, causing instability, lockup, or shutdown.

**Requirements:** Target must have System Stress Track or active cybernetic Augmentations

**Choose ONE effect:**

**System Attack:** (Savant to Savant only)  
Deal System Stress = Shifts generated +2 to target's System Stress Track

**Hardware Failure:** (Augmentations/machines)  
Damage cybernetic Augmentation or stop active functions (drones, etc.). Target suffers Physical Stress = Shifts generated. Armor doesn't help. Consequence relates to Augmentation (non-functional until cleared)

**Ego Strike:** (Against Apotheosized egos)  
Direct attack against ego on mindset or SIM host. Target suffers Mental Stress = Shifts generated.

---

### System Invasion

**Difficulty:** 4 OR 3 + highest of target's SINC or Software Engineering  
**Action:** Simple Action

Direct attack against a system to seize control. Control computer systems, drones, and even Apotheosized people.

**Results by Shifts:**

**Success:**
- **Apotheosized person/drone:** Implant single-word command ("Flee!"). Target obeys until end of scene
- **Computer system:** Limited access. View any files available to authorized user

**Spin:**
- **Drone:** Implant simple sentence command ("Go stand in corner" or "Target X"). Lasts 1 hour
- **Computer:** View any authorized files AND have writing privileges. Create new files (can't edit existing)

**5+ Shifts:**
- **Drone:** Implant multi-sentence command, multiple steps. Lasts 1 week
- **Computer:** Complete system control (except Mimir-tech core OS, which cannot be modified)

**Upgrade: Usurper**  
Force Apotheosized minds to obey drone-level commands. Difficulty based on higher of target's Cohesion, S.Eng, or SINC.

## QUICK EXECUTION FLOWCHART

1. **Choose Program** to execute
2. **Check Difficulty:** Varies by program and target
3. **Make SINC Test:** Roll SINC skill vs. Program difficulty
4. **Determine Result:**
   - **Success:** Program works as intended
   - **Failure (no Stall):** Suffer System Stress = Shifts missed; effect may be partial
   - **Stall:** Suffer System Stress + Program fails completely
5. **Apply Effect:** Based on Shifts generated (more Shifts = better result)
6. **Consider Upgrades:** Some programs have upgrade options (purchased separately as Stunts)
