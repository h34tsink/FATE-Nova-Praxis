Rolling Dice & Actions

"The mesh tracks everything. Fortunately, so do we."

The Core Roll

4 FUDGE Dice + Skill Rank + Modifiers = Effort

FUDGE Dice

4d6 marked +1 / 0 / −1. Range: −4 to +4. Average result: 0.

Alt: Roll d6 − d6 (range −5 to +5, more random). All players use same method.

Bonuses Stack

Almost everything stacks. +2 from one source + +1 from another = +3 total. Unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Action Types

Test

Skill vs. GM-set difficulty. Succeed if Effort ≥ Diff. Excess = Shifts.

Contest

Both parties roll; highest Effort wins. Tie: both succeed (or re-roll, per situation).

Conflict

Multi-round opposition. Attacker rolls vs. defender's Effort as difficulty. Generates stress.

Extended Action

Set difficulty. Mark = Diff × 3. Accumulate Shifts across multiple rolls until Mark reached. Spin: Mark −1. Stall: fail completely.

Supplemental Action

Doing something extra during a Simple Action. Imposes −1 penalty on the primary test. The supplemental part is the lower-difficulty component.

Combined Skills

Two skills both required simultaneously. Primary skill tested; secondary modifies:

Secondary higher than primary+1
Secondary lower than primary−1
Same rank±0
Not the same as Supplemental — both parts would need their own test.

Action Economy (Per Turn in Conflict)

Actions per Turn

Simple Action × 1 — attacks, skill tests, most actions

Free Action × 1 — move adjacent zone, draw weapon, cover

Instant Action × 1 — anytime; defense never counts against this limit

Stunts/augmentations may grant additional actions.

Spin

Effort exceeds difficulty by 3+ Shifts.

Creates a free Effect Aspect — very temporary; goes away after first invoke/compel.

On attack tests: spend 1 Shift to place the Effect Aspect instead of adding it to stress.

Max one Effect Aspect per round. In a contest, Spin overrides Stall.

Stall

Effort misses difficulty by 3+. Opposite of Spin.

GM may create an Effect Aspect reflecting the catastrophic failure. Can result in Extended Action failing completely.

Aspect Types & Scope

What Are Aspects

Core Rule

Aspects are phrases or quotes describing something important. They can be invoked for benefits or compelled for complications.

Characters, objects, zones, and scenes can all have Aspects.

Aspects should be double-edged — useful AND a source of interesting trouble.

Aspect Scope Rule

You may invoke or compel only one Aspect per scope per test.

  • Character Aspects
  • Consequences
  • Stunt Aspects
  • Scene Aspects
  • Augmentation Aspects
  • Zone Aspects
  • Another character's Aspects
  • Equipment Aspects
  • Temporary Aspects
Persistent Aspects do NOT count against scope limits.

Aspect Durations

Temporary

Created by Maneuvers, Spin, etc. Last until end of scene or until invoked/compelled (if 0-Shift creation). Remove with a Simple Action or Supplemental Action + Skill test (Diff: 2).

Lasting

Longer-term temporary Aspects (debts, reputations, network status). Recorded on character sheet. Last until situation resolves.

Persistent (P)

Major, hard-to-ignore traits. Free invoke/compel for bonus/penalty or reroll (no FP cost). Still costs FP for Block or Declaration uses.

You do not earn FP when a Persistent Aspect is compelled. Declining costs 1 FP. Never soft compel a Persistent Aspect.

Fate Points & Invoking

Fate Point Economy

Refresh

At start of each session, reset FPs to your Refresh Rating. FPs do not carry over.

Default Refresh Rating: 5. Pure characters: +2. Refreshed Stunt: +1 per purchase (max 3×).

Free Invocations

Any Aspect intentionally created in play (Maneuver, Consequence, Spin) gets one free invoke/compel — the first person to use it pays no FP.

Spin: grants two free invocations instead of one.

Invoking (Spend 1 FP)

Invoke Benefits — Choose One

  • +2 bonus on your test
  • Reroll your dice
  • Create a Block (usually Potency 2)
  • Make a Declaration (narrative control)
The Therefore Rule: you must be able to connect the Aspect to your intent using only one "therefore" — no inferred steps allowed.

Invoking External Aspects

Players can invoke Aspects on enemies, zones, equipment, or the scene itself. Requires slightly more justification than your own Aspects.

The same Aspect may only be invoked or compelled once per turn by the same character.

FP paid by the invoker leaves play (goes to no one) unless invoking an enemy's Aspect as a compel.

Chapter 4 — Gameplay

Compelling Aspects

Compel Mechanics

Compel Effects — One of:

  • −2 penalty on a test
  • Forced reroll
  • Suffer a Block
  • Suffer a Declaration

Accept → earn 1 FP. Decline → pay 1 FP.

GM has an endless FP pool for distributing compels. If another player compels and it's accepted, they give their FP to the recipient.

Active vs. Passive Compels

Active: GM or player explicitly offers compel with an FP token. Player decides.

Passive: Aspect causes trouble naturally during play. GM hands over FP retroactively.

Soft Compel: Minor consequence — player can reject for free. Don't use for Persistent Aspects.

No Pain, No Gain

Don't award the FP until the compel actually impacts the character. If it didn't really hurt, it didn't count.

Maneuvers, Assessments & Declarations

Maneuvers (Creating Aspects)

How It Works

Simple Action — test or contest. Success places a Temporary Aspect on target, scene, or zone.

If Diff: 0, GM may allow as Supplemental Action.

0 Shifts: Aspect is weak — goes away on first invoke/compel.

1+ Shifts: Lasts scene or until actively removed.

Spin: Aspect can be invoked or compelled for free twice.

Remove with Simple Action, or Supplemental + Skill test (Diff: 2).

First Use Free

The first person to invoke or compel any Aspect created in play does so for free — even if they didn't create it.

This enables teamwork: one character sets up an Aspect, others exploit it.

Assessments (Revealing Aspects)

How It Works

Observe a target for at least 1 round, then test a relevant Skill. On success, GM reveals an existing (or invented) Aspect about the target. First use is free.

Skills: Perception (most common), Insight, Strategy, Engineering (for relevant subjects).

Vs. people: usually Insight vs. Guile contest.

In conflict: Normally 3 rounds minimum. Accept −1 penalty per round removed (1 round minimum).

Outside conflict: +2 bonus for 1 hour; +3 for 4 hours.

Spin: May reveal an additional Aspect or more potent detail.

Declarations (Asserting Story Facts)

By Invoking an Aspect

Instead of +2 or reroll, spend 1 FP to declare a fact or place an Aspect on a person, place, or thing. Subject to GM veto.

Best used to move the story forward, add drama, or resolve a conflict. Avoid mixing in game mechanics (no "+4 AR" — that's a red flag).

By Skill Test

Use a knowledge Skill to declare a fact about the world. Default Diff: 2. Creative/entertaining Aspects should be easier; boring or disruptive ones harder or vetoed.

Keep Declarations narrative. Any mention of numbers is a red flag that you're doing it wrong.

Stress Tracks

Track Sizes

Each track starts at 2 boxes, plus bonuses from relevant Skills:

TrackRelevant Skills
PhysicalAthletics + Endurance + Size
MentalCohesion + Resolve

Skills 1–2: +1 box. Skills 3–4: +2 boxes. Skill 5+: +3 boxes.

Taking Stress

Physical attacks → Physical stress. Mental attacks → Mental stress.

Stress = Shifts generated + WR − AR.

Mental stress = Shifts generated + Edge − Determination.

Check boxes left to right. All filled → Taken Out.

All stress clears after a few minutes of rest at scene end.

Extreme Consequences

Never auto-downgrade. After allotted time, become a permanent (E) Aspect. Can only be removed after next Milestone, or by replacing an existing character Aspect.

Taken Out

The person who defeated you describes what happens — death, incapacitation, etc.

Cash in all FPs (must have at least 1) to force them to describe it differently. Always results in removal from conflict and some loss.

Concession: Offer before being taken out. Absorbs all stress. Accept or negotiate. If rejected (and GM deems it reasonable), offerer gains 1 FP, refuser loses 1 FP.

Running Conflicts

5-Step Setup

Steps Before First Exchange

StepAction
1Frame the Scene — describe sights, smells, sounds. Reveal threats last so players absorb the environment first.
2Reveal Aspects — announce obvious scene/zone Aspects. Call Perception rolls for subtle ones. Anyone can invoke/compel them.
3Determine Zones — break the area into meaningful components. Draw a map for complex layouts.
4Establish Initiative — each character rolls Strategy (physical) or Insight (mental) + modifiers. Act highest to lowest. Ties: higher Perception wins; further ties favor player nearest GM's right or the PC over NPC.
5Begin Exchanges — proceed in initiative order, repeating each round.

Not every social exchange needs a mental conflict. Use for critical moments that matter to the story.

Edge & Determination

Edge = "weapon" for mental attacks. Bonus (0–5+) added to attack roll, not just stress. Based on quality of roleplay, clever arguments, or tactical advantage (e.g., loved ones at gunpoint).

Determination = mental armor bonus (0–5) added to defense roll. Reflects commitment to a position or fear of consequences for yielding.

Unlike WR, Edge is added to the attack test itself — before checking if the attack hits.

Horror & Trauma

Contact with horrors, resleeving trauma, torture — all cause mental attacks with only an Edge bonus (no attacking Skill).

Edge reflects incident severity and character experience. First posthuman encounter: ~+8. Veteran of dozens: possibly 0.

Torture: ~Edge 5 per session or per 8 hours.

System Conflict

System Stress

When It Occurs

Savant-vs-Savant cyber attacks, or when a Savant fails an execution test for a Savant Program. Destabilizes the Savant's mindset/sleeve systems.

Non-Savant characters can be targeted by Savant Programs but suffer localized effects rather than System Stress.

Savant Program Execution

Each program has an execution difficulty. Roll SINC vs. difficulty.

Fail: suffer system stress equal to amount failed by, but program may still execute.

System Stress Track size: 2 + bonuses from Engineering (Software) + SINC.

Sample System Consequences

TypeExamples
MildMinor Lag, Memory Access Error, Running Hot
ModerateMemory Leak, Data Corruption, Damaged Actuator
Severe(P)Random Reboots, Connections Severed, Hard Locked
Extreme(P)System Crash, Catastrophic Data Loss, Unresponsive
Taken OutScrapped
System Consequences recover at same rate as Physical. Emergency aid uses Hardware or Software Engineering.

SIMs & Host Systems

SIMs are self-contained software. System stress from attacks or failed programs affects only the SIM — not the host system it's running on.

Virtuality & Dominion

Virtual Environments

Virtuality Rules

  • Physical Skills replicate normally in virtual space
  • Real-world stress/Consequences don't carry into virtuality
  • Physical stress taken in virtuality doesn't affect real body
  • Most virtualities eject you if you suffer a Consequence
  • On forced exit: mental attack with Edge based on worst Consequence suffered inside (+2 Mild / +4 Mod / +6 Severe / +8 Extreme / +10 Taken Out)
  • Characters normally have Guest access — can interact physically but not alter reality

Dominion (Admin Access)

The admin of a virtuality is effectively a god. She can reshape the environment at will.

Simple Action — Software Engineering or SINC test (Diff: 2) → make a Declaration about the virtual environment.

Direct attacks (turning air to acid, ripping avatar code): use Software Engineering or SINC. WR = attacking Skill Rank × 2. Defend with relevant Skill, or Cohesion for code-based attacks.

SIMs have admin access to their personal home environments.

Combat Actions

Combat Rules

Ambushing

Target unaware of threat → no Skill on defense. Dice only. Armor Rating still applies.

GM should still roll defense for mooks in this case.

Full Defense

Simple Action spent only defending → +2 on all defense tests until next turn.

If you haven't acted when first attacked, declare Full Defense and forfeit your Simple Action that turn.

Hold Your Action

Delay your turn to act after another character. Hold through the entire round → go first next round. Multiple holders: Strategy contest for order.

Taking Aim

Simple Action + Perception (Diff: 2) → place In My Sights Aspect on target. Invoke free on next attack. Fragile — may need refresh if target moves erratically.

Taking Cover

Free Action in your zone.

TypeAspect
Light CoverBehind Light Cover
Hard CoverBehind Hard Cover(P) — spend FP to declare "can't be hit"

Push / Pull / Throw

Contested Athletics + Size. Win → target moves to adjacent zone of your choice. If larger: push extra zones up to Size difference. Can follow as Free Action.

Help from Others

Helper rolls same Skill vs. difficulty = primary's Skill rank.

Success+1 bonus to primary
Spin+2 bonus to primary
FailNo effect
Stall−1 penalty to primary

Block Actions

Declare what you block + Skill → roll = Block Potency. Anyone doing the blocked action must beat Potency (Simple Action). Overcoming = Supplemental penalty on follow-up.

Sample BlockSkill
Suppression FireFirearms
RestrainMelee or Athletics
DistractionGuile or Diplomacy
Ignoring a Block may grant Blocker a free Instant Action response.

Zones

Zone Rules

Same zone = hand-to-hand range. Adjacent = thrown/short range. 5–10 zones = weapon range limit.

Zones represent spatial relationships, not exact distances. Rooms, corridors, and open areas make natural zone boundaries.

Use rough sketches — not grids — unless area is featureless. Assign Aspects to each zone.

Chapter 5 — Characters

States: Pure, SIM & Sleeved

Starting States

Pure

Born body, no Apotheosis. May still have bio/cyberware Augmentations.

  • 8 Skill Ranks for Athletics, Endurance, Perception (max 5 each)
  • Extra Aspect describing a physical trait
  • Refresh Rating +2
  • Free Carpe Diem Stunt
  • +1 to any one non-Physical Skill (max 5)
Lose all Pure benefits permanently if you take the Apotheosis Augmentation.

SIM

Apotheosized; exists purely as software on a host system.

  • 7 Skill Ranks for Athletics, Endurance, Perception (virtual interaction)
  • Free SIM State Stunt + one of: Digitally Evolved, Digital Spider, Drone Jock, Infusion, Skilled (SINC), or System Slip
  • SIM State(P) Aspect

Lag Penalties: <1000 km = none · same system = −2 · different system = −4

Change hosts: 10 min (local) / 1 hr (same system) / 4 hrs (out-of-system).

Sleeved

Apotheosized; inhabits a biosleeve or cybersleeve.

  • Begin with a sleeve with Cost ≤ higher of Assets or Rep-Rating +4
  • Physical Skills (Athletics, Endurance, Perception) determined by sleeve
Can switch between Sleeved and SIM via resurrection chamber after Apotheosis. Can never return to Pure.
