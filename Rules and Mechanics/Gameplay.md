This summary of Chapter 4, "Gameplay," provides the core mechanics and rules for playing **Nova Praxis**, a game centered on the **Shadow War** between the corporate Houses.

### **The Core Mechanic**

The game uses four FUDGE dice (4dF), which are marked with "+", "-", or "0".

- **The Formula:** 4dF + Skill Rank + Modifiers = **Effort**.
- **Success:** If Effort meets or exceeds the Difficulty (Diff), the action succeeds. The difference is measured in **Shifts**.
- **Degrees of Success:**
    - **Spin:** Generating **3 or more Shifts** results in a Significant Success. You may **elect** to create an **Effect Aspect** (a short-lived Temporary Aspect). Effect Aspects disappear once invoked or compelled. On **attack tests**, you must reduce Shifts by 1 to create an Effect Aspect. Limit: **one Effect Aspect per round**.
    - **Stall:** Failing by **3 or more** results in a spectacular failure (GM may also create an Effect Aspect).

### **Aspects and Fate Points**

Aspects are phrases that define people, places, or things.

- **Invoking:** Spend 1 **Fate Point (FP)** to gain a **+2 bonus**, a **reroll**, create a **Block**, or make a **Declaration**.
- **Compelling:** Gain 1 FP when an Aspect creates a complication, penalty, or forced narrative event.
- **Free Invocations:** Aspects created via Maneuvers, Spin, or Consequences can be invoked **once for free**.
- **Persistent Aspects (P):** Major environmental or physical traits. Invoking/compelling for a **+2 bonus or reroll is free**. Blocks and Declarations still **cost an FP** as normal. You do **not** gain FP from compels on Persistent Aspects. Declining a compel on a Persistent Aspect **always costs an FP** (never a soft compel). Persistent Aspects do not count against scope limits.

### **Taking Action**

- **Tests:** Roll against a fixed difficulty set by the Game Master (GM).
- **Contests:** Roll against another character's Effort.
- **Maneuvers:** A Simple Action used to place a **Temporary Aspect** on a target or the scene.
- **Assessments:** Using Skills (like Perception) to **discover** an existing hidden Aspect.
- **Declarations:** Using an FP or a Skill test to introduce a **new fact or element** into the story.

### **Conflict and Health**

Conflicts (Physical, Mental, or System) are divided into rounds where each character gets a **Simple Action** and a **Free Action**.

- **Stress:** On a hit, stress = Shifts + **Weapon Rating (WR)** − **Armor Rating (AR)**. Note: **Explosive Rating (ExR)** is added to the **attack roll itself** (not to stress after a hit) — mechanically different from WR.
- **Consequences:** To avoid being **Taken Out** when stress boxes fill, players can take Consequences (Mild -2, Moderate -4, Severe -6, Extreme -8).
- **Concessions:** Offered any time you take a Consequence; absorbs all stress from the attack. You do **not** automatically gain an FP for conceding. You gain 1 FP only if the opponent **refuses** a reasonable concession (and they lose 1 FP).
- **Mental Conflict:** Uses **Edge** and **Determination**. Edge is added to the **attack roll** (not to stress like WR — you don't need to hit first). Determination is added to the **defense roll**.
- **System Conflict:** Savants and CIST users suffer System Stress when failing to execute programs or being cyber-attacked.

### **Special Gameplay Rules**

- **Movement:**
    - **Standard Move:** Move to an adjacent zone as a **Free Action** (or Supplemental Action in rough terrain).
    - **Hustle:** Move through one zone into the next as a **Supplemental Action**. Imposes **-2 penalty** on your other action, **additive** with other Supplemental Action penalties.
    - **Run:** Like Hustle but **-3 penalty** plus an Athletics test (Diff: 2); each Shift = one additional zone of movement.
- **Blocks:** An action taken to prevent a specific result (e.g., Suppression Fire or Restraining). Foes must roll against the **Block Potency** to proceed.
- **Economy:**
    - **Rep-Rating:** Used in Coalition space to acquire items; failing a test causes a **Rep Hit**.
    - **Assets:** Used for black market or apostate trading via a direct test vs. Cost.
- **Resleeving:** Moving an ego to a new body is a **mental attack (Edge 8)** vs. Cohesion. Modifiers: **+2 Edge** if first time resleeving; **+2 Edge** if you remember dying. **+2 defense bonus** if resleeving into a clone or same-model sleeve.

### **Extended Actions**

- Set a **Difficulty** as normal, then **Mark = Difficulty × 3**.
- Make repeated Skill tests; accumulate Shifts until total Shifts ≥ Mark.
- **Spin** on a test reduces the Mark by 1. **Stall** = catastrophic failure; the Extended Action cannot continue.

### **Aspect Scope**

- A character may invoke or compel only **one Aspect per scope per test** (e.g., one Character Aspect, one Scene Aspect, one Equipment Aspect, etc.).
- **Persistent Aspects** do not count against scope limits.

### **Refresh & Fate Points**

- **Refresh Rating:** 5 (Pure characters and those with the Refreshed Stunt have higher).
- FPs reset to Refresh Rating at the start of each session (or mid-session at GM discretion).
- **FPs do not carry over between sessions.** Use them or lose them.

### **Consequence Recovery**

Consequences **downgrade** rather than disappear outright. At the end of the recovery period, a Consequence becomes the next lesser type (Severe → Moderate → Mild → gone). If the lesser slot is already occupied, it cascades down.

| Consequence | Physical/System | Mental |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mild | 1 hour | 1 day |
| Moderate | 1 day | 1 week |
| Severe(P) | 1 week | 1 month |
| Extreme(P) | Special | Becomes a permanent Aspect marked **(E)** |

- Recovery facilities (hospitals, psychiatrists) reduce these times significantly.
- Emergency aid (Diff: 2 Skill test, 10 min) can remove a Mild Consequence in the field.

---

**Analogy for Aspects:** Think of **Aspects** as the "camera focus" in a movie. The world is full of details, but when you **Invoke** an Aspect, you are zooming the camera in to show why that specific detail—like a _Slippery Floor_ or your _Legionnaire Training_—is the exact reason you succeed in that moment. When the GM **Compels** an Aspect, they are zooming in to show how that same detail is the exact reason you are now in trouble.
