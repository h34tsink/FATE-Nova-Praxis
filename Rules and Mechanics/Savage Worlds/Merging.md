Merging is the process of combining multiple instances of a character's **ego** into a single, cohesive whole. This is often necessary if the character has been **forked** or if they are restored from backup and later find their intact mnemonic core.

## Process of Merging

- **Resurrection Chamber Required**: Merging requires the use of a **resurrection chamber** to facilitate the merging process. This technology is essential to integrate the various versions of the ego without catastrophic data loss.
    
- **Memory Integration**: Once the merging process is completed, the newly formed ego can remember everything that has happened to all of the merged instances. The merged character gains the collective experience, memories, and insights of all previous versions.
    
- **Risk of Fragmentation**: Merging multiple instances of an ego can be **psychologically damaging**. After the merge, the character must make a **Spirit roll**, modified by **Cohesion** and the **total Fragmentation penalties** suffered by both the character and their fork(s).
    
    - **Failure**: If the roll fails, the character suffers **Fragmentation** as described above after merging.
        

## Traits, Skills, and Advances

- **Merging Traits and Skills**: When merging characters who have been apart for a long time, they may have developed differently—gaining new **Advances**, **Skills**, **Edges**, etc. The player may choose which **Traits** the newly merged character retains, but the total number of points in **Attributes** and **Skills** cannot exceed that of the fork with the highest values.
    
- **Advances**: The newly merged character has the same number of **Advances** as the fork with the most.
    
- **Edges and Hindrances**: Players can select which **Edges** and **Hindrances** the newly merged character retains.
    
    - **Edges**: The number of **Edges** cannot exceed that of the contributing fork who had the most.
        
    - **Hindrances**: The number of **Hindrances** cannot be fewer than the fork with the most.
        

### Additional Details

- **Psychological Impact**: Merging often results in psychological challenges, especially if the forks have experienced vastly different events. The merged ego must reconcile conflicting memories and emotions, which can lead to **psychoses** or other mental instabilities if the Spirit roll fails.
    
- **Cohesion and Stability**: The more **Fragmented** the individual egos are before merging, the higher the risk. Ensuring the forks have undergone **mnemonic editing** beforehand can increase the chances of a successful merge.
    
- **Memory Overlap**: The merged character may experience disorientation from overlapping memories, especially if the forks were active at the same time. This disorientation could lead to temporary **penalties on rolls involving mental clarity** until the character has had time to adjust.
