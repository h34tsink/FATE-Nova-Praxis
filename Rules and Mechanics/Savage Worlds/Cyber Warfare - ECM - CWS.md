Cyber warfare attacks are performed by assaulting another vehicle’s computer systems with your own, by uploading viruses, overloading them with data, and more. These attacks and defensive measures employ a variation of the same technology used to create CISTs.

- A system attack requires attack action. To make these sorts of attacks you must have the Cyber-Warfare Suite Mod (pg. 241) and have someone to operate it, be it a PC, NPC, a member of the ship’s crew, or an AI.

- A ship’s Electronic Counter Measures (ECM) is used like Toughness to defend against cyber-attacks.

- The number listed in parenthesis is the ship’s Firewall, which acts like Armor when defending against cyber-attacks.  

---

The systems aboard the vehicle have been upgraded to feature **CIST technology**, allowing it to make electronic attacks against the systems of enemies within sensor range.

### **Mechanics**

- The attack is made using the attacker’s **Hacking (Software)** skill.
- On a hit, the attack deals **2d6 damage**. A hit with a raise deals an extra **1d6**.
- The vehicle may purchase this Mod multiple times, each time increasing the amount of damage dealt by **+1d6**

A vehicle that suffers damage from a cyber-warfare attack doesn’t suffer a normal Wound. Instead, the attacker may choose one of the following effects to apply to the target:

### **Effects**

- **Closed Gate**:
    
    - The target’s navigation officer must succeed on a **Knowledge (Software)** roll to activate its jump drive for the next **1d4 turns**.
        
- **Disrupt Maneuvering Systems**:
    
    - All rolls to pilot the craft suffer a **-2 penalty** for **1d4 turns**.
        
- **Gravity Fluctuation**:
    
    - The vehicle’s gravitics system (if any) surges or stalls, fluctuating rapidly for **1d4 turns**.
        
    - Everyone onboard suffers a **-2 penalty** on nearly all Skill rolls requiring movement, concentration, or balance (e.g., attacking).
        
- **Zero-G**:
    
    - The vehicle’s gravitics systems shut down. If in a zero-g environment, all internal compartments become zero-g environments for **2d4 turns**.
        
- **Disrupt Mobility**:
    
    - The vehicle’s **Acceleration** and **Top Speed** are halved for **1d4 turns**.
        
- **Overload**:
    
    - Overloads one of the vehicle’s power banks, causing it to explode. Treat this as a critical hit to the Crew as described in the _Savage Worlds Science Fiction Companion_.
        
- **Glitch ECM**:
    
    - Crashes or partially crashes the target’s ECM systems, reducing its ECM by **1d4** for **one hour**.
        
    - This effect is cumulative and can be applied multiple times.
        
    - The penalties can be repaired if a character spends a turn working on the issue, requiring a **Knowledge (Software)** roll with a penalty equal to half the total ECM penalty.
        
- **Glitch CWS**:
    
    - Disrupts the target’s Cyber-Warfare Suite systems, causing a **-2 penalty** on Cyber-Warfare attack rolls for **one hour**.
        
    - Subsequent applications add a cumulative **-1 penalty**.
        
    - These penalties can be repaired with a **Knowledge (Software)** roll, with a penalty equal to the total Cyber-Warfare penalty.
        
- **Glitch Targeting**:
    
    - Overloads the target’s weapons targeting systems, imposing a **-1 penalty** on all attack rolls for **one hour**.
        
    - Subsequent applications add a cumulative **-1 penalty**.
        
    - These penalties can be repaired with a **Knowledge (Software)** roll, with a penalty equal to the total targeting penalty.
