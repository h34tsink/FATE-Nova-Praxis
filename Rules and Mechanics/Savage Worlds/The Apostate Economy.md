### **Currency and Trade**
Apostate enclaves operate under a scarcity-based economy, relying heavily on physical commodities like gold to facilitate trade. Unlike the Coalition’s credit systems tied to digital accounts and CIDs, apostates favor tangible assets to maintain independence and avoid surveillance.

- **Gold as Currency**:
	- Gold Flakes (Glimmers / GF):
        For the smallest transactions, gold flakes are stored in clear plastic coins. Glimmers are valued for their portability and ease of use for microtransactions.
        **Conversion Scale: 10 Glimmers = 1 Shine.
        
    - Gold Coins (Shines / G):
        Small gold coins used for minor transactions. Shines are the most common currency for daily trade due to their balance of value and portability.
        **Conversion Scale: 10 Shines = 1 Lode.
        
    - Gold Bars (Lodes / GB):
        Compact gold bars for larger sums, often used in trade for bulk goods or high-value items. Lodes are typically reserved for large trades or stockpiling wealth.
        **Conversion Scale: 1 Lode = 100 Glimmers

### **Compilers and Scarcity**
Access to compilers—devices that can fabricate items from raw materials—is highly restricted for apostates due to Coalition controls.

- **Coalition Restrictions**:
  - Compilers are programmed to only function within official Coalition space.
  - They require a registered **Coalition Identification (CID)** to operate.
  - Templates for items are tied to individual CIDs, further preventing unauthorized use.
- **Workarounds**:
  - Apostates must use **false CIDs** and spoofing devices to trick compilers into functioning.
  - Even with these, templates are often outdated or limited, forcing apostates to trade for critical supplies.

Without reliable access to compilers, apostates rely on traditional production methods and resource gathering to survive, making their economy dependent on the physical exchange of goods and services.

### **Banks and Digital Transactions**
In the most secure apostate cities or habitats, **banks** provide limited electronic fund transfers. These banks issue **credit chips** tied to local systems, allowing for digital transactions without Coalition oversight.

- **Credit Chips**:
  - Secure but localized, they cannot be used outside specific enclaves.
  - Useful for large-scale trades within trusted networks.

### **The Gold War**
The use of gold as currency is illegal within Coalition society. **The Protectorate** actively works to dismantle the apostate economy by targeting gold production and distribution.

- **Protectorate Efforts**:
  - They hunt for gold molds and refining facilities in apostate territories.
  - Smugglers caught with gold face severe punishment.
  - These actions, dubbed the "Gold War," aim to weaken apostate independence by disrupting their currency.

### **Goods and Services in Apostate Enclaves**
Because of limited compiler access, apostate enclaves depend on scavenging, bartering, and local craftsmanship to sustain their communities. Commonly traded goods include:

- **Basic Necessities**:
  - Water, food, and breathable air filters.
  - Handcrafted tools and clothing.
- **Weapons and Defense**:
  - Firearms, modified mining equipment, and armor.
  - Rare, smuggled Coalition tech.
- **Illicit Tech**:
  - Hacking devices and false CID generators.
  - Repair kits for scavenged Coalition equipment.
- **Exotic Goods**:
  - Rare minerals or artifacts recovered from abandoned habitats.
  - Luxury items such as preserved foods or entertainment tech.

### **Role in Gameplay**
The Apostate Economy offers opportunities for:

- **Role-Playing**:
  - Negotiations in shadow markets.
  - Dealing with distrustful traders or rival factions.
- **Tactical Decisions**:
  - Balancing the need for resources against risk.
  - Smuggling goods or crafting alliances to secure rare items.
- **Moral Dilemmas**:
  - Helping an enclave fend off Protectorate raids.
  - Deciding whether to exploit or aid struggling communities.
