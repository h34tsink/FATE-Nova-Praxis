# 🧬 Scene Module: The Fork and the Flame
*First Fate Core Session – Nova Praxis Transition Scene*

---

## 🎭 Scene Purpose

Transition your players from **tactical mindset (Savage Worlds)** to **narrative-driven Fate play**.  
This scene introduces the Fate Core mechanics through emotional stakes, moral choices, and teamwork.

The PCs are trying to **reach and stabilize Valare**, a fragmented Fork caught between trauma and data corruption.  
They must merge her mind safely — but time and outside forces are against them.

---

## ⚙️ Scene Type: Extended Action – Negotiation / Merge Procedure

**Tone:** Sci-psychological tension, empathy vs efficiency  
**Duration:** 30–45 minutes (Standard → Extended)

**Scene Aspects:**
- “Traumatized Survivor”  
- “Fork on the Run”  
- “Hostile Environment”  
- “Data Ghosts in the Air”  

**Countdown Clock:** Each round = one “sync attempt.”  
4–5 attempts max before outside interference.

---

## 💬 Player Approach Archetypes

Encourage players to use narrative approaches rather than rigid skill checks.  
They can combine styles or swap mid-scene. Reward creativity and emotion.

| Approach | Description | Suggested Skills / Rolls | Potential Boost |
|-----------|--------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Empathy** | Emotional connection and reassurance | Empathy / Rapport | +2 if player expresses genuine emotional support |
| **Logic** | Rational persuasion, appealing to necessity | Reason / Will | +2 if they propose a safe, structured merge plan |
| **Technical** | Skillful control of neural sync, removing emotional blocks | Technology / Science | +2 for creative use of tech metaphors or devices |
| **Relationships** | Drawing on shared history or loyalty | Rapport / Provoke (as push-pull trust) | +2 for referencing shared memories or vows |

---

## 🎲 Extended Action Structure

Each “step” = an **Overcome** or **Create Advantage** roll toward stabilization.  
Use **Teamwork** for synergy and describe the collective emotional or technical impact.

**Target:** 10 shifts total (cumulative).  
**Time Pressure:** Each failed roll costs one “tick” on the countdown clock.  
When time runs out → outside interference or breakdown.

---

### 🧩 Example Roll Flow

**Round 1 – Empathy:**  
> “We know you’re scared, but we’re here for you.”  
Roll Empathy +2 → Fair (+2). Success → 2 shifts.

**Round 2 – Technical:**  
> “We can buffer the worst memories, so you don’t have to relive everything at once.”  
Roll Technology +3 → Good (+3). Success → +3 shifts (5 total).

**Round 3 – Relationships:**  
> “You trusted us before. Trust us now. We’ll stay with you, no matter what.”  
Roll Rapport +1 → Mediocre (+0). Fail → countdown advances.

**Round 4 – Logic:**  
> “Without your memories, we can’t protect anyone. Merging is the only way.”  
Invoke Aspect *‘Rational Compassion’* for +2 bonus → Success → +3 shifts (8 total).

**Round 5 – Teamwork:**  
> Empathy + Technical collaboration. Success → merge completes.  
> Failure → Valare fragments and flees through the mesh, leaving haunting echoes.

---

## 🧠 GM Techniques: Introducing Fate Subtly

### 1️⃣ Frame the Fiction First  
> “We’ll handle this like a team effort — every action you take adds momentum or tension. Focus on what your character does or feels.”

### 2️⃣ Show, Don’t Lecture  
Narrate before rolling. Players see that emotional description matters.

### 3️⃣ Use Aspects as World Texture  
> “The lab’s neural field hums — the Scene Aspect *‘Hostile Environment’* makes every failure sting harder.”  
Then ask: *“Do you want to invoke anything to push back?”*

### 4️⃣ Soft-Introduce Compels  
> “Your Aspect *‘Haunted by the Past’* might make this harder — want a Fate Point to lean into that doubt?”  
They’ll learn the rhythm naturally.

## 🎬 GM Notes Summary

- Focus each roll on **emotion + consequence.**  
- Every player choice should either *build trust* or *threaten it.*  
- Introduce **Fate Points**, **Aspects**, and **Teamwork** naturally through play.  
- Reward bold storytelling with compels and invokes, not just dice results.  
- Keep pacing tight and cinematic — fade or cut when emotion crests.
