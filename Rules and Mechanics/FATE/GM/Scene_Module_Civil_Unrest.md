# ⚠️ Scene Module: Part 3 – Civil Unrest
*Fate Conversion for Nova Praxis Campaign*

---

## 🎭 Story Overview

The PCs travel to the **Icarus Hub orbiting Proch** to confront **Isabella Nowak**.  
What should be a diplomatic or investigative mission quickly spirals into chaos as mutiny erupts, the hub fractures, and **Chimera’s manipulations** frame the PCs as terrorists.  

This arc transitions your table fully into Fate Core’s narrative rhythm — emphasizing teamwork, moral tension, and timed objectives.

---

## 🧠 Scene Framework Summary

| Stage | Fate Scene Type | Purpose |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| 1. Arrival at Icarus Hub | Establishing / Tension Setup | Visual world-building and tension |
| 2. Dock Chaos | Action / Survival | Environmental Aspects, teamwork, urgency |
| 3. Search for Nowak | Extended Investigation | Moral choice and time pressure |
| 4. Extraction / Confrontation | Extended Conflict | Climax — negotiation under fire |
| 5. Fallout / Transition | Reflection Scene | Repercussions and hook to Opportunity Four |

---

## 🌌 Scene 1 – Arrival at the Hub

**Tone:** ominous calm before chaos.  
**Purpose:** introduce unrest and show the PCs’ infamy spreading.

**Scene Aspects:**
- “A Cube on Fire”  
- “Workers in Revolt”  
- “Lighthouse Incoming – Extreme Prejudice”  
- “Mesh Lag and Fragmented Comms”  

**Compels:**
- *“You can’t tell who’s friend or foe.”*  
- *“They remember your faces — and the rumors.”*  

**Objectives:**
- **Overcome:** Dock amid explosions or failing grav systems.  
- **Create Advantage:** Fake credentials or redirect Lighthouse patrols.  
- **Compel:** Force tough moral calls — e.g., innocent worker crossfire.

**Transition Line:**  
> “The corner of a cube tears away in silence before your sensors catch up. Dock alarms flicker red — the mutiny has begun.”

---

## 🔥 Scene 2 – Dock Chaos

**Tone:** frantic and cinematic.  
**Type:** Action / Timed challenge.

**Scene Aspects:**
- “Explosive Decompression Alarms”  
- “Panicked Crowds Stampeding”  
- “Security Drones On Edge”  

**Approaches:**
- **Forceful:** fight or barricade through mobs.  
- **Clever:** reroute power or hack doors.  
- **Flashy:** calm crowds with authority or broadcast.  
- **Sneaky:** navigate maintenance shafts unseen.

**Mechanics:**
- 4 segments = countdown clock for station collapse.  
- Each round: PCs must **Overcome** obstacles (Diff: 3–4).  
- Each failure adds the Aspect *“Structural Integrity Falling.”*

**Transition Line:**  
> “Through the haze and flashing lights, you glimpse the inner cube turning — its hull glowing like a dying ember.”

---

## 🧩 Scene 3 – Finding Nowak

**Type:** Extended Action (Investigation + Negotiation)  
**Goal:** locate and capture Nowak before Lighthouse or Kestrel does.  
**Complexity:** 8 Segments (each = 10 minutes in-fiction).

**Scene Aspects:**
- “Office Lockdown”  
- “Damaged Mesh Feeds”  
- “False Intel from Chimera”  

**Challenges:**
- **Overcome (Diff 4):** triangulate Nowak’s location using corrupted Mesh.  
- **Create Advantage (Diff 3):** rally apostates or sympathetic workers.  
- **Defend (Diff 4):** repel Lighthouse sweeps.  
- **Resist (Diff 4):** propaganda compels — locals believe the PCs are terrorists.

**Teaching Moment:**  
This introduces Fate teamwork. Each player can use *Create Advantage* to help others, emphasizing collaboration over initiative order.

**Transition Line:**  
> “A fractured feed lights up — Nowak’s voice flickers through static: ‘They’re coming… for all of us.’”

---

## ⚔️ Scene 4 – Confronting Nowak

**Type:** Extended Conflict — social and physical blend.  
**Objective:** secure Nowak before extraction forces arrive.

### Phase 1 – Breach
- **Overcome:** bypass lockdowns (Diff 4).  
- **Defend:** fight or delay Lighthouse reinforcements (Diff 6).  
- **Scene Aspects:** “Red Alert Override,” “Broken Gravity Systems.”

### Phase 2 – Negotiation
Use Fate’s **social conflict** flow — stress tracks represent Nowak’s conviction or fear.

| Player Approach | Target Emotion | Potential Outcome |
|-----------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Empathy** | Calm her down → surrender peacefully | +2 shift toward Trust |
| **Provoke** | Intimidate her into revealing data | Adds mental stress |
| **Deceive** | Pretend to represent Chimera | +2 shifts if she panics |
| **Will** | Resist indoctrination | Prevents emotional compel |

**Compel Hook:**  
> “You still believe in the Coalition, don’t you?”  
Accept for +1 FP to hesitate or argue rather than act.

### Phase 3 – Extraction
Countdown begins (5 rounds). Each failure accelerates *“Reinforcements Incoming.”*  
If Lighthouse or Kestrel’s Crimson Sons arrive → combat or escape scene.

**Outcomes:**
- **Success:** Nowak captured, weak but alive.  
- **Failure:** She dies, escapes, or is killed by Lighthouse.

**Transition Line:**  
> “As the hatch seals behind you, the hub drifts — fragments of the mutiny reflecting off the stars like ghosts.”

---

## 🕳️ Scene 5 – Fallout

**Type:** Reflection / Transition  
**Purpose:** reset player resources, introduce moral consequences, and hook Opportunity Four.

**Scene Aspects:**
- “Wanted Across Coalition Space”  
- “Chimera’s Digital Fingerprints”  
- “Echoes of Guilt”  

**Compels:**  
- Accept a compel to earn FP if they lean into guilt, paranoia, or revenge.

**Narrative Hooks:**
- PCs receive corrupted coordinates to **Opportunity Four**.  
- Newsfeeds brand them as terrorists.  
- Apostate and Talon sympathizers may reach out in secret.

**Transition Line:**  
> “Hours later, the Extranet dies mid-broadcast. Then — a single ping. Coordinates. Opportunity Four.”

---

## 🧩 GM Quick Notes

- Keep **2–3 active Scene Aspects** at a time.  
- Use **countdown clocks** or **segment tracks** to build tension.  
- Reward **Create Advantage** teamwork liberally.  
- Let Aspects tell the story — not dice spam.  
- Every compel should advance theme, not stall progress.  
- Use emotional beats as **cut points** — fade or smash-cut when tension peaks.

---

## 🎞️ Tone & Direction

**Themes:**  
- Scope vs. Intimacy: vast orbital chaos contrasted with personal confrontation.  
- Ideology vs. Survival: Who are the real villains — Houses, Talons, or the system?  
- Humanity vs. Code: echoes of their merged egos haunt every decision.

> “The line between hero and heretic blurs in the light of a dying star.”
