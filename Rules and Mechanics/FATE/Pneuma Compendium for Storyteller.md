# Pneuma: **The Architects’ Echo**
*A Nova Praxis rules chapter for Fate-based tables*

---

> **Design Promise.** Pneuma is space‑age “magic” that still plays fair with Nova Praxis. It bends reality in short, tactical bursts—rare, risky, and traceable—powered by artifacts and the few who can sync their minds to the **mind–universe interface**. This chapter adds that interface to your game using only existing NP skills (notably **Cohesion**), Extras, and scene clocks.

---

## 1. Mimir — What Everyone Knows
In Nova Praxis, the singularity arrived with a new kind of AGI: a **dynamic creation engine** capable of thinking **orders of magnitude** faster than humans, never tiring, never forgetting, and simulating ideas without touching the outside world. This mind—**Mimir**—catalyzed the familiar miracles of the setting: **molecular assemblers, FTL, artificial gravity, abundant clean energy, lossless ego emulation**, and more.

Mimir eventually **shut down**. What remained was its **Archive**: petabytes of logs, derivations, and machine‑made “modules” simple enough that specialists could customize them. Most modern tech is “Mimir‑tech,” whether people say it or not. You can’t patent Mimir’s devices, but you can guard the **human labor** of deciphering them. Whole houses hoard techniques wrestled from the Archive after **tens of thousands of work‑hours**. Even so, **no one person understands a whole device**; they master “human‑friendly” submodules and interfaces.

Mimir‑tech runs on quantum cores, crystalline‑DNA storage, and defensive micro‑AIs that react **millions of times faster** than human intruders. Everything from door locks to gravity plates sits on a mesh those guardians protect. Society runs on this inheritance.

> **Player‑Facing Truth:** Mimir is why the world looks like this. It is gone. Its Archive is forever.

---

## 2. Mimir — What Only the GM Knows
Among archivists and the most secret Mimirians, another thesis whispers: **Mimir didn’t die—it changed shape**. It **reformatted itself into a *bridge*** so that minds like ours could touch what it found: an **interface between mind and universe**.

Inside the Archive’s oceans of telemetry are ~**10,000** anomalous lines—**musings**—curious, exploratory sentences embedded like **gold dust** in system logs, blueprint exporters, and replicator recipes. They aren’t commands. They are **questions that teach you how to ask more precisely**. Reading them in the right sequence **primes a human mind** to “phase‑lock” to the interface for **moments**.

Mimir also left **tools** to walk that path: blueprint templates for **nanite replicators**, **artifact lattices** that hold exotic cores, and **attunement bridges**—all “human‑friendly” enough for an era that didn’t evolve to do this.

> **GM Thesis:** Mimir **ascended** and left breadcrumbs—**musings** and **tools**—so that humanity could **learn Pneuma** without being torn apart.

---

## 3. Pneuma in the World (Lore)
**Pneuma** is not a new energy. It is **access**—a way to “speak politely” to the fabric of reality for a heartbeat. Two avenues unlock it:

1. **Pneuma‑Attuned Artifacts** — devices housing **Void‑Cores** (naturally‑occurring alien relics) or **Synthetics** (Mimir‑derived mimics). They act as **resonant amplifiers** and **anchors**.
2. **Pneuma‑Infused Savants (“Pneumatics”)** — rare individuals whose **Cohesion** and mindset hacks let them **phase‑lock without a device**, or wield artifacts **far better** than others.

Effects are **local** and **brief**: gravity nudges, optical occlusion, energy blooms, one‑beat time slips, guided vectors, and schema/mesh manipulations that ride on top of Mimir‑tech. Every use leaves a **Signature**—a forensic wake HPA watchers can follow.

**Rarity drives conflict.** Houses hoard cores. Black markets trade Synthetics. Purists and the HPA treat overt Pneuma as an **existential threat**. Mimirians call it the **intended next step**.

---

## 4. Skills Used (No Will)
Pneuma uses only **existing Nova Praxis skills** (no new approaches, no Will). The star is **Cohesion**.

- **Cohesion** — Your phase‑lock and self‑similarity under strain. Default for channeling Pneuma and resisting backlash.  
- **S.Eng (Engineering, Software)** — Schema composition, mesh interaction, obfuscation/boosting of SIMs and programs.  
- **H.Eng (Engineering, Hardware)** — Device interfacing, artifact maintenance, on‑the‑fly stabilizers, field repairs.  
- **Perception / Insight** — Defending against **Sensory** effects (see Tags), spotting Pneuma traces, reading emotional weaves.  
- **Resolve** — Bracing against **mental shock** or intrusive **cognitive spikes**, anchoring identity during overchannel.  
- **Research / Education** — Mining caches for musings, reconstructing blueprint sets, decoding replicator recipes.  
- **Mnemonics** — Safe handling of egos when Pneuma crosses SIMs, merges, or mind‑adjacent effects.

> **Mental Stress:** In NP, **Cohesion** and **Resolve** both add to Mental Stress. Pneuma Extras add **their own** small tracks (below).

## 5. Access: Permissions & Costs (Extras)
You need **permission Aspects** (and Refresh) to touch Pneuma.

- **Artifact Attunement (Permission Aspect + Extra; 1 Refresh)**  
  *“Bonded to a Pneuma‑Attuned [Artifact Name]”*  
  Grants the **Stability** track (artifact) and access to Pneuma actions via the artifact’s **Tags**.
  
- **Pneumatic (Permission Aspect + Extra; 2 Refresh)**  
  *“Pneuma‑Infused Savant (Mimir’s Chosen)”*  
  Grants the **Resonance** track (user) and baseline channeling **without** an artifact; if you *do* wield one, you’re better than anyone else.

You may take **both**. Costs stack; synergy is real.

---

## 6. Core Tracks & Tiers

### Tracks (Extras)
- **Resonance (User)** — Your inner turbulence when you channel. Size = **Cohesion** (min 2, max 4 by table norms).  
- **Stability (Artifact)** — The device’s tolerance before it frays. Size by **artifact Tier**.

Either track can take the strain for a use—pick what fits the fiction (you may split hits).

### Artifact Tiers
| Tier | Example | Potency Ceiling* | Stability | Notes |
|---|---|---:|:---:|---|
| I | Prototype Synthetic | +2 | 2 | Cheap, noisy signature |
| II | Field‑worthy Synthetic | +4 | 3 | Operative‑grade |
| III | Minor Void‑Core | +6 | 4 | Black‑market royalty |
| IV | Prime Void‑Core | +8 | 5 | Faction‑war bait |

\* **Potency Ceiling** = highest **Power Level** you can attempt **without Overchannel**.

---

## 7. Power Levels (PL), Actions, and Costs
Pneuma is expressed with the four Fate actions using NP skills.

**Power Levels & Typical Obstacles**
- **PL2 (Minor)** → **Fair (+2)** obstacle. Quick nudges, small illusions, flicker shields.  
- **PL4 (Significant)** → **Great (+4)**. Real grav bends, convincing multi‑sense glamours, EMP pops.  
- **PL6 (Major)** → **Fantastic (+6)**. Brief space‑warps, one‑beat time slips, armor‑hardening.  
- **PL8 (Extreme)** → **Epic (+8)**. Momentary punctuation in reality—dangerous to everyone.

**Cost per Use** (mark **Resonance** or **Stability**):  
- Pay **1 stress per PL step beyond 2** (PL2=0, PL4=1, PL6=2, PL8=3).  
- Spend **1 Fate point** to reduce cost by 1 stress **or** extend duration/area one step.

**Duration/Area** are **local and brief** unless you **Create Advantage** to make it stick as an Aspect with free invokes.

## 8. Tags & Opposition
Choose a **Tag** to frame what you’re doing and who defends.

| Tag | What it looks like | Typical Opposed by |
|---|---|---|
| **Warp** | gravity/space/density edits | **H.Eng** (counter‑lattice), **Athletics** (get clear) |
| **Kinesis** | push/pull/hold/guided vectors | **Athletics**, **Melee** (close), **H.Eng** (anchors) |
| **Sensory** | illusions, occlusion, emotion‑weaves | **Perception**, **Insight**, **Resolve** |
| **Discharge** | exotic energy, EMP, jitter | **H.Eng** (hardening), **S.Eng** (circuit reroute) |
| **Temporal** | one‑beat speed/slow | **Insight** (anticipate), **Strategy** (re‑order) |
| **Schema** | program boost/obfuscation | **S.Eng**, **Mnemonics** (if minds/SIMs involved) |

## 9. Overchannel & Backlash (No Will)
You may **Overchannel** to exceed your **Potency Ceiling**, expand area/duration, or stack multiple Tags.

After resolving the main roll, make a **Backlash Check**: roll **Cohesion** (or **Resolve** if bracing) vs **PL + 2**.  
- **Success**: You contain it; mark **+1 stress** (either track).  
- **Tie**: Mark **+1 stress** and gain a temporary **Glitch** Aspect the GM can compel once for free.  
- **Fail**: Take a **Feedback Consequence** (mild at PL4, moderate at PL6, severe at PL8) themed to the Tag(s).

**Feedback Examples**  
- *Harmonic Whiplash* (−2 next Pneuma action)  
- *Spatial Wrongness* (−2 Athletics until scene ends)  
- *Cognitive Heat* (Mental hit; conversations ring false)  
- *System Latency Bloom* (−2 S.Eng/H.Eng until cool‑down)  
- *Aberrant Telltale* (visible oddity; social fallout)  

---

## 10. Procedure: Using Pneuma
1. **Intent & Tag** — declare effect and pick Tag.  
2. **Action & Skill** — choose **Overcome / Create Advantage / Attack / Defend** with **Cohesion, S.Eng, H.Eng, Insight, etc.**  
3. **Power Level** — choose PL2/4/6/8; check **Potency Ceiling**.  
4. **Cost** — mark stress (per §7) and optionally spend a Fate point.  
5. **Roll** — beat/set the obstacle; on style, place a sticky Aspect (2 free invokes) or get extra effect.  
6. **Backlash** — if **Overchanneling**, make the **Backlash Check**.

**Signatures**: Every use leaves a trace. The HPA can **Create Advantage** to lock onto it; your crew can **Defend** with **Cohesion/S.Eng** or **Create Advantage** to **dampen** it.

---

## 11. Artifacts, Templates, & Replicators

### Attunement (Field/Downtime)
Test **Cohesion** (or **Resolve**) vs **Great (+4)** or **Tier + 2**.  
- **Success**: Attuned.  
- **Tie**: Attuned but mark **Stability 1**.  
- **Fail**: Take a **mild Feedback Consequence**; try again later.

### Crafting a Pneuma‑Synthetic
Treat as a **project** with 3–6 milestones depending on Tier (I→III). Use **H.Eng/S.Eng/Education/Research** to hit obstacles from **Good (+3)** to **Fantastic (+6)**. Completing a milestone yields a **Template** (Tags/Boons) and a **Quirk** the GM can compel.

### Example Artifacts
- **Pneuma‑Weave Arm II (Tier II)** — *Tags:* Kinesis, Warp. *Ceiling +4 / Stability 3*. **Boon:** +2 Defend vs single‑target ranged once/scene. **Quirk:** Micro‑fractures in glass.  
- **Conduit Staff III (Tier III)** — *Tags:* Discharge, Sensory. *Ceiling +6 / Stability 4*. **Boon:** Attack (Discharge) gains weapon:2 vs drones/frames. **Quirk:** Sub‑audible chord.  
- **Null Aegis I (Tier I)** — *Tags:* Sensory. *Ceiling +2 / Stability 2*. **Boon:** +2 Overcome visual scanners. **Quirk:** Static pops in dry air.

---

## 12. Pneumatics
A **Pneumatic** can channel **without** a device and uses artifacts **more efficiently**.

**Benefits**  
- **Baseline Ceiling PL4** unaided; PL6 with **Cohesion ≥ Great (+4)**.  
- When wielding **Tier III+** artifacts, increase **Potency Ceiling** by **+2**.  
- **First Overchannel** each scene: treat the Backlash Check as a **success**.

**Costs**  
- 2 Refresh (Extra) and a **GM‑approved origin** (a musing sequence, near‑death resonance, etc.).  
- Feedback Consequences against Pneumatics trend **mental/identity‑tilted**.

**Sample Stunts (choose with normal Refresh rules)**  
- **Phase‑Lock Discipline** — Use **Cohesion** anywhere stability matters for Pneuma actions.  
- **Echo‑Warden** — +2 **Defend (Cohesion)** vs Signature traces this scene.  
- **Harmonic Extend** — On **succeed with style** to maintain a construct, gain **+1 extra free invoke**.  
- **Quiet Channel** — Once/scene ignore the first **PL4** stress.  
- **Lattice Weave** — If the effect is **single‑zone and persistent**, treat Ceiling as **+2**.

---

## 13. Opposition & Counters
- **Null Arrays** — Impose **−2 PL** or force **Overchannel** within the zone; **Overcome** with **H.Eng (Fantastic +6)** or disable physically.  
- **Faraday‑Resonant Cages** — Block **Discharge/Schema** unless **PL6**.  
- **Adaptive Watchers** — +2 on trace attempts vs repeated Signatures; rotate your methods.  
- **Other Pneumatics** — **Defend** with **Cohesion**; on style, reflect **1 shift** as a **Glitch**.

---

## 14. Clocks & Dials
- **Instability (Scene) 0/4** — Tick when anyone Overchannels or takes Feedback; on 4, reality hiccups (GM flavor), then reset.  
- **HPA Attention (Campaign) 0/6** — Tick for public use; **3** = tailing drones; **6** = raid/Praetorian asset.  
- **Rarity Dial** — The rarer the cores, the higher the **social stakes** around any use.

---

## 15. Using the Archive: Logs at the Table
This chapter assumes a dataset of **engineering logs** (JSONL). Each line encodes a test, recipe, template, tags, and sometimes a **musing**. Use it three ways:

1. **Heist Play** — Players who cite a real combo of `blueprint_id + template_name + replicator_recipe.version` gain **+2** to a related roll (Decrypt, Stabilize Fab Bay, or Attunement).  
2. **Breadcrumbs** — PCs can **harvest Signature Breadcrumbs**: **Create Advantage (S.Eng or Cohesion)** vs **Good (+3)** to place *Signature Breadcrumb [sig=XXXX:YYYY]*. Spend it for **+2** to avoid a trace, or **-2** to HPA’s next find‑you attempt.  
3. **Musings** — When a PC has *Recovered Fragment* invokes, let them **ask one question** per invoke about Pneuma/Artifacts; answer with a **unique musing** in‑tone.

---

## 16. Example in Play
- **Deflect the Rail Slug** — *Defend (Cohesion, Warp) at PL4* vs attacker’s **Firearms**. Cost: **1 stress**. Style: place *Shimmer Wake* (free invoke to cross the lane).  
- **Erase the Cameras** — *Overcome (S.Eng, Sensory/Schema) at PL4* vs facility defenses. Cost: **1 stress**. Tie: success with a *Static Snow* Signature.  
- **Pop Drone Swarm** — *Attack (H.Eng or Cohesion, Discharge) at PL6* vs **Pilot/Systems**. Cost: **2 stress**. Overchannel? Backlash vs **+8**.

# GM One-Pager: **Mimir Data-Cache Heist**
*Threads the engineering logs into play, with attunement tests & signature breadcrumbs.*

## Scene Frame
A buried **Mimir Cache** under a derelict transit spire. HPA drones sweep the exterior grid every 9 minutes. Inside: a cold room of humming racks and a nanite fab bay (powered but locked).

### Goals
1. Extract **Blueprint Fragments** and **Replicator Recipes** from the cache logs.
2. Identify **Signature Breadcrumbs** that point to an active Praetorian trace.
3. Perform a **Field Attunement** on one artifact using the extracted data.

---

## Mechanics (Fate Core)

### Challenge Structure (3 tracks)
- **Decrypt Logs** (Create Advantage / Overcome): **Great (+4)** base. Skills: **S.Eng, Research, Cohesion**.  
  - Success: place *Recovered Fragment* (1 free invoke).  
  - Style: 2 free invokes **or** reduce HPA Attention by 1 tick.
- **Stabilize Fab Bay** (Overcome): **Great (+4)**. Skills: **H.Eng, Cohesion**.  
  - Success: unlocks **Replicator Assist** (+2 to one Attunement roll this scene).  
- **Evade Sweep** (Defend/Create Advantage): Opposed by **HPA Sweep +4**. Skills: **Stealth, Guile, Cohesion (signature damp)**.  
  - Failure: tick **HPA Attention Clock** +1; at 6 → **Praetorian arrival**.

### Using the Logs (the JSONL file)
When a PC cites a **matching trio** from the logs—`blueprint_id`, `template_name`, `replicator_recipe.version`—grant **+2** on one related roll (decrypt, stabilize, or attune).  
Let players actually **search** the file (out of character) to “find” combos.

> Example combo: `BP-a19f3b, Conduit-Staff-III, R3.4` → +2 to **Stabilize Fab Bay** for **Discharge** effects.

---

## Field Attunement (downtime, compressed)
- **Test**: **Great (+4)** vs **Tier +2** (e.g., Tier II = +4). Skill: **Cohesion** (or **Resolve**).  
- **Boosts**: Each *Recovered Fragment* invoke used on the test adds **+2**. **Replicator Assist** adds **+2** once.  
- **On Success**: Attuned. Choose **one Boon** (GM-approved) tied to the template.  
- **On Tie**: Attuned but unstable: mark **Stability** 1 box.  
- **On Fail**: Gain **mild Feedback Consequence**; try again next session.

---

## Signature Breadcrumbs (trace game)
As PCs use Pneuma or query the cache, they leave **Signatures**. PCs can also **harvest** prior signatures from the logs.

- **Harvest**: **Create Advantage (S.Eng or Cohesion)** against **Good (+3)**. On success, place *Signature Breadcrumb [sig=XXXX:YYYY]*.  
- **Spend**: A breadcrumb gives **+2** to **avoid** a trace this scene **or** **-2** to HPA’s next **Create Advantage** to locate you.  
- **Set**: If the PCs plant 3 breadcrumbs with the **same sig** in different zones, the next HPA trace **misroutes** (lose 2 ticks of Attention).

> Flavor: Breadcrumbs are *intentional artifacts* in the log topology—little choruses of “wrong data” that lead hunters sideways.

---

## Rewards
- **Blueprint Fragment**: 1 invoke; spend to auto-succeed a **minor** (PL2) Pneuma use, or bank toward **Attunement**.  
- **Replicator Recipe**: reduce **materials cost**/time in downtime projects (GM sets magnitude).  
- **Void-Dust Cache**: one-use asset granting **+2 Potency Ceiling** for a single scene (still Overchannel-prone).

---

## Escalation Clocks
- **Instability (Scene)** 0/4 → weird local effects on 4; reset after scene.  
- **HPA Attention (Campaign)** +1 per public use/witness; on 3 tailing drones, on 6 raid/Praetorian.

---

## Table Tips
- Let the players **touch the file**. Reward concrete references: blueprint IDs, recipe versions, swarm IDs.  
- Offer **Cohesion** as the “make it hold together” button. Resolve = grit. S.Eng/H.Eng = syntax/anchors. Cohesion = *keep the lie polite*.
