# Pneuma: Unified Rules (Draft)
*A Nova Praxis rules chapter — unified mechanics for lite and full play*

> **Design Promise.** Pneuma is space-age "magic" that plays fair with Nova Praxis. It bends reality in short, tactical bursts — rare, risky, and traceable. This chapter uses existing NP patterns: Stunts, Skills, Aspects, and the "once per scene free / FP for more" economy. Two depth levels (Focused and Full) share the same terminology, costs, and skill.

---

## 1. What Pneuma Is

**Pneuma** is access — a way to speak to the fabric of reality for a heartbeat. It doesn't grant omniscience or miracles. It creates **alignment**: the right person arrives, the relevant detail becomes visible, the wrong door becomes the only door.

Two avenues unlock it:

1. **Pneuma-Attuned Artifacts** — devices housing [[Glossary/Randallite|randallite]] cores — either natural (alien-origin or stellar-precipitated) or [[Glossary/Randallite#Technical / Operational|synthetic]] (Mimir-derived mimics). The randallite acts as a resonant amplifier and dimensional anchor.
2. **Pneumatics** — rare individuals whose mindset and training let them phase-lock to the mind-universe interface without a device, or wield artifacts far better than others.

Every use leaves a **Signature** — a forensic wake that watchers can follow.

**Hard Limit:** Pneuma **cannot create information from nothing**. It can only rearrange access to facts, people, objects, routes, attention, timing — things that already exist in the fiction, even if off-screen.

---

## 2. The Pneuma Skill

Pneuma introduces one new skill: **Pneuma**.

**Pneuma** — Your ability to phase-lock with the mind-universe interface, channel effects, and contain backlash.

- **Adds to:** Nothing by default (Pneuma is not a broad utility skill)
- **Used for:** Channeling Pneuma effects (Overcome, Create Advantage, Attack, Defend when using Pneuma), resisting Backlash, dampening Signatures
- **Cannot be used for:** Anything outside of Pneuma channeling and related defenses

> **Why a dedicated skill?** Cohesion already covers identity stability, resleeving trauma, and mental defense. SINC exists for Savants. Pneuma deserves the same treatment — a gated skill that doesn't inflate existing must-have stats.

**Skill acquisition:** You gain Pneuma at Rank 1 when you purchase either the **Artifact Attunement** or **Pneumatic** Stunt. Ranks beyond 1 follow normal Skill advancement (Skilled Stunt or Milestone swaps).

**Supporting skills** still matter by Tag (see Section 6):
- **H.Eng** — Warp, Kinesis, Discharge (hardware/physics effects)
- **S.Eng** — Schema, Discharge (software/mesh effects)
- **Cohesion** — Sensory, Temporal (mind-adjacent effects)
- **Perception/Insight** — Defending against Sensory effects
- **Resolve** — Bracing against mental backlash (alternate backlash defense)

## 3. Access: Stunts (Entry Cost)

Pneuma access follows the Savant pattern: **Stunt purchase + mandatory disadvantage Aspect**.

### Artifact Attunement (Stunt)
*Prerequisite: Possession of a Pneuma-Attuned Artifact*

You are bonded to a specific artifact and can channel Pneuma through it.

- Grants the **Pneuma** skill at Rank 1 (if you don't already have it)
- Grants access to the artifact's **Tags** (see Section 6)
- Your artifact has a **Stability Rating** (see Section 5)
- **Mandatory Aspect:** You must take an Aspect reflecting the bond and its drawbacks:
  *"Bonded to [Artifact Name]"* — compellable for dependency, theft risk, signature traces, artifact hunger, social suspicion

### Pneumatic (Stunt)
*Prerequisite: GM-approved origin (musing sequence, near-death resonance, Mimir contact, etc.)*

You can channel Pneuma without a device. If you also wield an artifact, you're better than anyone else.

- Grants the **Pneuma** skill at Rank 1 (if you don't already have it)
- Baseline access to **any two Tags** of your choice (without artifact)
- When wielding a Tier III+ artifact, increase its **Potency Ceiling by +2**
- **First Overchannel** each scene: treat the Backlash Check as one step better (fail → tie, tie → success)
- **Mandatory Aspect:** You must take an Aspect reflecting the cost:
  *"Pneuma-Infused: Reality Notices Me"* — compellable for unwanted attention, pattern obsession, identity drift, HPA interest

> **Stacking:** You may take both Stunts. Costs stack (two Stunt slots, two disadvantage Aspects). The synergy is real.

---

## 4. Stress: Physical or Mental

Pneuma strain hits the body or mind — player's choice each time, justified by fiction.

- **Physical stress** — nosebleeds, tremors, nerve pain, exhaustion, spatial wrongness
- **Mental stress** — pattern overload, fixation, synaptic ringing, identity wobble

Pneuma does **not** create a new stress track. It uses your existing **Physical Stress** (Athletics + Endurance) and **Mental Stress** (Cohesion + Resolve) tracks.

> **Why no dedicated track?** NP already has three tracks (Physical, Mental, System). Adding a fourth fragments the consequence economy and reduces dramatic tension. When Pneuma costs you your last Mental box, that matters more than filling a side track nobody else touches.

## 5. Artifact Tiers & Stability

Each artifact has a **Tier** that determines its power ceiling and durability.

| Tier | Example | Potency Ceiling | Stability | Notes |
|---|---|---:|:---:|---|
| I | Synth-rand prototype | PL2 | 2 | Dirty lattice, noisy signature |
| II | Field-grade synthetic | PL4 | 3 | Operative-grade, clean enough |
| III | Natural randallite core | PL6 | 4 | Black-market royalty |
| IV | Prime randallite core | PL8 | 5 | Faction-war bait |

**Stability** is a simple durability rating on the artifact itself (like vehicle stress). When an artifact takes strain:
- Mark a Stability box
- Stability clears after a scene of rest (or H.Eng maintenance, Diff 2)
- If all Stability boxes are filled, the artifact gains a **Consequence Aspect** (e.g., *"Lattice Fracture"*, *"Harmonic Drift"*) and cannot be used until repaired

**Potency Ceiling** = highest Power Level you can attempt without Overchannel. A Pneumatic wielding the artifact adds +2 to this ceiling.

## 6. Power Levels, Tags, and Actions

### Power Levels (PL)

| PL | Scale | Obstacle | Stress Cost | Example |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| PL2 | Minor | Fair (+2) | 0 | Quick nudge, flicker shield, faint illusion |
| PL4 | Significant | Great (+4) | 1 | Real grav bend, convincing glamour, EMP pop |
| PL6 | Major | Fantastic (+6) | 2 | Brief space-warp, one-beat time slip, armor-hardening |
| PL8 | Extreme | Epic (+8) | 3 | Momentary punctuation in reality — dangerous to everyone |

**Stress cost** is marked on Physical or Mental (your choice, per fiction). This cost is paid when you attempt the action, regardless of success.

**Spending FP on Pneuma:** You may spend **1 Fate Point** to either:
- Reduce stress cost by 1 (minimum 0), or
- Extend duration or area one step (local → zone, instant → scene)

### Tags

Choose a Tag to frame your effect. The Tag determines what the fiction looks like and who defends.

| Tag | What It Looks Like | Typical Skill for Channeling | Typical Opposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warp** | Gravity/space/density edits | Pneuma or H.Eng | H.Eng, Athletics |
| **Kinesis** | Push/pull/hold/guided vectors | Pneuma or H.Eng | Athletics, Melee |
| **Sensory** | Illusions, occlusion, emotion-weaves | Pneuma or Cohesion | Perception, Insight, Resolve |
| **Discharge** | Exotic energy, EMP, jitter | Pneuma or H.Eng/S.Eng | H.Eng, S.Eng |
| **Temporal** | One-beat speed/slow | Pneuma or Cohesion | Insight, Strategy |
| **Schema** | Program boost/obfuscation, mesh manipulation | Pneuma or S.Eng | S.Eng, Mnemonics |

> **Skill choice:** You may channel with **Pneuma** (always valid) or the Tag's associated skill (if it fits the fiction and the GM agrees). The Tag's associated skill is often better for specialized applications; Pneuma is the generalist fallback.

### Actions

Pneuma uses the four standard Fate actions:
- **Overcome** — bypass barriers, suppress traces, break through resistance
- **Create Advantage** — place Aspects representing Pneuma effects (the workhorse action)
- **Attack** — directly harm via energy, force, spatial shearing
- **Defend** — deflect, absorb, or redirect incoming harm via Pneuma

## 7. Channeling Modes

Modes are **stances** that shape how you channel. Choose a mode when you activate Pneuma. Modes work within the PL/Tag framework — they modify what your actions do, not replace them.

### Resonance (Reveal)
*You read the convergence. Patterns emerge. The relevant detail surfaces.*

- When you **Create Advantage** to assess or reveal, you may ask for the Aspect **and** one non-mechanical actionable fact (a name, access point, schedule window, weakness)
- On **Spin**, reveal one additional related Aspect or upgrade the revealed one to be more specific
- **Once per scene**, you may reduce the time for an Assessment in conflict by 1 round (minimum 1) without penalty. Additional uses in the same scene cost **1 FP** each.

**Resonance Stall:** GM creates a Temporary Effect Aspect: *"False Signal"* or *"Pattern Overload"* (free invoke for GM).

### Convergence (Arrange)
*You shape the alignment. Positioning, timing, and opportunity bend toward your intent.*

- When you **Create Advantage** via Maneuver, the resulting Temporary Aspect gains **+1 extra free invoke** (2 total)
- If the Maneuver targets a zone, you may place the Aspect at scene scope if the fiction supports it (GM approval)
- **Once per scene**, a Pneuma-created Aspect with 0 Shifts persists until invoked/compelled once (instead of vanishing). Additional uses cost **1 FP** each.

**Convergence Stall:** GM places a scene/zone Effect Aspect: *"Pressure Spike"*, *"Wrong Place, Right Now"*, or *"Thread Snagged"* (free invoke for GM).

### Pressure (Force)
*You force convergence. Reality aligns — and you're the low-friction channel it uses.*

**Once per scene**, you may do one of the following for free. Each additional use in the same scene costs **1 FP**:

**A. Twist an Invocation** — When anyone invokes an Aspect in the scene, you may change it from +2 ↔ reroll, or convert it into a Block (Potency 2).

**B. Declaration** — Introduce a plausible convergent element: a door unlocked because maintenance is mid-shift, a courier arriving now, a guard distracted by a real alert. GM veto applies.

**C. Hard Compel** — Offer a Compel using a Pneuma-related Aspect that forces a meaningful complication toward convergence. Standard Compel rules apply (target may refuse for 1 FP).

**Pressure Cost:** After using A, B, or C, you must also mark **1 stress** (Physical or Mental). This is in addition to any FP spent for extra uses.

**Pressure Stall:** GM gets a free Declaration-style complication: *"Convergence Backlash"* — something breaks, someone arrives, you're exposed.

## 8. Procedure (Quick Reference)

1. **Declare mode** — Resonance, Convergence, or Pressure
2. **Declare intent and Tag** — what you're doing and which Tag frames it
3. **Choose action and skill** — Overcome / Create Advantage / Attack / Defend with Pneuma or Tag-associated skill
4. **Choose Power Level** — PL2/4/6/8; check Potency Ceiling
5. **Pay stress cost** — mark Physical or Mental stress per PL (0/1/2/3)
6. **Roll** — beat the PL obstacle; on Spin, place a convergence-flavored Effect Aspect
7. **Backlash** — if Overchanneling, make the Backlash Check (Section 9)
8. **Signature** — every use leaves a trace (Section 11)

---

## 9. Overchannel & Backlash

You may **Overchannel** to exceed your Potency Ceiling, expand area/duration, or stack multiple Tags.

After resolving the main roll, make a **Backlash Check**: roll **Pneuma** (or **Resolve** to brace) vs **PL**.

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Success** | You contain it. Mark +1 stress (Physical or Mental). |
| **Tie** | Mark +1 stress and gain a temporary **Glitch** Aspect the GM can compel once for free. |
| **Fail** | Take a **Feedback Consequence** (see below). |
| **Stall (fail by 3+)** | Take a Feedback Consequence one severity step worse than normal. |

### Feedback Consequence Severity
| Overchanneled PL | Default Consequence |
|---|---|
| PL4 (beyond Ceiling) | Mild |
| PL6 (beyond Ceiling) | Moderate |
| PL8 (beyond Ceiling) | Severe |
| Any PL + Stall | One step worse (Mild → Moderate, etc.) |

> **Design note:** Backlash target is **PL** (not PL+2). At PL6 this means difficulty 6 — hard but achievable for a focused character. The old PL+2 formula made PL6+ backlash functionally impossible to pass without stacked invokes.

### Feedback Consequence Examples
- *Harmonic Whiplash* — -2 next Pneuma action
- *Spatial Wrongness* — -2 Athletics until scene ends
- *Cognitive Heat* — Mental stress; conversations ring false
- *System Latency Bloom* — -2 S.Eng/H.Eng until cooldown
- *Aberrant Telltale* — visible oddity; social fallout

> For the full flavor pack, see [[Rules and Mechanics/Pneuma Rules/Pneuma Backlash Consequences]].

## 10. Pneuma in Conflicts & Extended Actions

### Stress Reroute
**Once per scene**, while Pneuma is active, when you would take Physical stress you may take it as Mental (or vice versa) if you explain how Pneuma shifts the impact. Additional reroutes in the same scene cost **1 FP** each.

If rerouting prevents you from being Taken Out right now, the GM immediately creates a Temporary Effect Aspect on you: *"White-Knuckle Focus"* or *"Shaken"* (free invoke for GM).

### Extended Actions
**Once per Extended Action**, you may add +1 bonus Shift to a successful roll's contribution after the roll is made. On Spin, also reduce the Mark by 1.

**Cost:** Each time you use the +1 progress, mark 1 stress (Physical or Mental) or accept a Pneuma-flavored Compel. Additional uses in the same Extended Action cost **1 FP** each.

**Extended Stall (catastrophic):** The GM ends the attempt with a hard consequence that still points at relevance — you can't continue, but you learn why, who, or where next.

## 11. Signatures & Clocks

### Signature Traces
Every Pneuma use leaves a forensic wake. The HPA (or other watchers) can **Create Advantage** to lock onto it. Your crew can **Defend** with **Pneuma** or **S.Eng**, or **Create Advantage** to dampen the signature.

Repeated use of the same Tag/method grants watchers **+2** on trace attempts. Rotate your methods.

### Campaign Clocks

**Instability (Scene Clock) 0/4**
Tick when anyone Overchannels or takes Feedback. On 4, reality hiccups (GM flavor), then reset.

**HPA Attention (Campaign Clock) 0/6**
Tick for public or overt Pneuma use.
- **3** = Tailing drones, surveillance uptick
- **6** = Raid, Praetorian asset deployed

## 12. Artifacts: Attunement & Crafting

### Attunement
Test **Pneuma** (or **Resolve**) vs **Tier + 2**.
- **Success:** Attuned.
- **Tie:** Attuned, but mark 1 Stability on the artifact.
- **Fail:** Take a mild Feedback Consequence; try again after downtime.

### Fabricating Synthetic Randallite
Treat as a project with 3-6 milestones depending on target Tier (I → III). Uses H.Eng, S.Eng, Education, or Research against obstacles from Good (+3) to Fantastic (+6). Completing a milestone yields a Template (Tags/Boons) and a Quirk the GM can compel. Access to Mimir Archive fabrication blueprints reduces milestone count by 1. Natural randallite cannot be fabricated — only found.

### Example Artifacts
- **Pneuma-Weave Arm II (Tier II, synthetic core)** — Tags: Kinesis, Warp. Ceiling PL4 / Stability 3. Boon: +2 Defend vs single-target ranged, once per scene. Quirk: Micro-fractures in nearby glass.
- **Conduit Staff III (Tier III, natural randallite)** — Tags: Discharge, Sensory. Ceiling PL6 / Stability 4. Boon: Attack (Discharge) gains Weapon:2 vs drones/frames. Quirk: Sub-audible chord.
- **Null Aegis I (Tier I, synth-rand prototype)** — Tags: Sensory. Ceiling PL2 / Stability 2. Boon: +2 Overcome visual scanners, once per scene. Quirk: Static pops in dry air.

## 13. Pneumatics (Advanced)

A Pneumatic who also takes Artifact Attunement gains these additional benefits:

- **Baseline Ceiling PL4** unaided; PL6 with Pneuma skill at Great (+4) or higher
- When wielding Tier III+ artifacts (natural [[Glossary/Randallite|randallite]]), Potency Ceiling increases by +2
- First Overchannel each scene: treat the Backlash Check result as one step better

### Pneumatic Stunts (purchased with normal Stunt rules)
- **Phase-Lock Discipline** — Use Pneuma in place of Cohesion for any mental defense while channeling.
- **Echo-Warden** — +2 Defend (Pneuma) vs Signature trace attempts this scene. Once per scene free; FP for additional uses.
- **Harmonic Extend** — On succeed with style to maintain a construct, gain +1 extra free invoke. Once per scene free; FP for additional uses.
- **Quiet Channel** — Once per scene, ignore the stress cost for a single PL4 use. Additional uses cost 1 FP each.
- **Lattice Weave** — If the effect is single-zone and persistent, treat Potency Ceiling as +2. Once per scene free; FP for additional uses.
- **Higher-Q Resonance** — Resonance mode Assessments reveal 1 extra detail even without Spin, but Stall always creates a GM Effect Aspect.
- **Convergence Engine** — Once per session, treat a Pneuma-created Temporary Aspect as Lasting until the objective resolves.
- **Pressure Relief Valve** — Once per session, ignore the Pressure stress cost. The GM gains a banked Pneuma Compel (cashable later).

## 14. Opposition & Counters

- **Null Arrays** — Impose -2 PL or force Overchannel within the zone. Overcome with H.Eng (Fantastic +6) or disable physically.
- **Faraday-Resonant Cages** — Block Discharge/Schema unless PL6+.
- **Adaptive Watchers** — +2 on trace attempts vs repeated Signatures; rotate your methods.
- **Other Pneumatics** — Defend with Pneuma; on Spin, reflect 1 shift as a Glitch Aspect.

## 15. Spin & Stall Summary (Pneuma-Flavored)

**Spin while Pneuma is active:** You may name the Effect Aspect as a convergence artifact:
- *"Aligned Timing"*, *"Everything Connects"*, *"The Thread Exposed"*

**Stall while Pneuma is active:** The GM creates an Effect Aspect representing pressure misfiring:
- *"False Signal"*, *"Pressure Spike"*, *"Thread Snagged"*, *"Convergence Backlash"*

> In contests/conflicts, Spin overrides Stall (same as base NP rules).

## 16. Depth Levels: Focused vs Full

These rules support two levels of table complexity. Both use the same skill, terminology, and cost structures.

### Focused Mode (Lighter Tables)
Use Sections 1-4, pick **one mode** (Resonance, Convergence, or Pressure) as your character's permanent approach, and use the mode benefits from Section 7. Skip PL/Tags — treat all Pneuma actions as a flat **+2 bonus or free invoke** economy (once per scene free, FP for more). Skip Overchannel entirely.

**When to use:** Tables that want Pneuma as flavor without subsystem weight. Good for one-shots or groups unfamiliar with NP's crunchier side.

### Full Mode (Crunchy Tables)
Use all sections. PL, Tags, Overchannel, Signatures, Clocks, Artifacts — the works.

**When to use:** Campaign play where Pneuma is a significant story element. Groups comfortable with NP's Savant-level mechanical depth.

### Mixing at the Table
A Focused-mode character and a Full-mode character can coexist. The Focused character simply doesn't engage with PL/Tag/Overchannel mechanics — their Pneuma works like a well-defined Stunt. The Full character gets more tactical options and more risk.

---

## 17. GM Guidance

- Pneuma should **accelerate relevance**, not erase obstacles.
- Always ask: "What already exists that Pneuma can rearrange access to?"
- When in doubt, let Pneuma succeed and charge **pressure** via: stress, consequences, exposure, new threats, time compression, collateral, social fallout.
- Compel those mandatory disadvantage Aspects. They're the real cost of Pneuma, session over session.
- Track the HPA clock visibly. Let players see it tick. Let them feel watched.

## 18. Example Uses

- **Resonance Assessment:** "This facility feels wrong." → Create Advantage (Pneuma, Sensory) at PL2 vs Fair (+2). Cost: 0 stress. Reveal *"Mostly Home to Apostates"* + learn the shift-change window.
- **Convergence Maneuver:** Create *"Off-Balance"* on a foe. Create Advantage (Pneuma, Kinesis) at PL4 vs Great (+4). Cost: 1 stress. Convergence grants 2 free invokes.
- **Pressure Declaration:** "The courier arrives now." Free use (once per scene). Cost: 1 stress (Pressure cost). GM agrees it's plausible. The courier walks in.
- **Full Attack:** Pop a drone swarm. Attack (H.Eng, Discharge) at PL6 vs Pilot/Systems. Cost: 2 stress. Overchannel? Backlash vs Fantastic (+6).
- **Defend:** Deflect a rail slug. Defend (Pneuma, Warp) at PL4 vs attacker's Firearms. Cost: 1 stress. On Spin, place *"Shimmer Wake"* (free invoke to cross the lane).

## Appendix: Quick Comparison to Savant Programs

| Feature | Savant Programs | Pneuma |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | Savant Stunt + disadvantage Aspect | Artifact Attunement or Pneumatic Stunt + disadvantage Aspect |
| Dedicated Skill | SINC | Pneuma |
| Stress Track | System Stress | Physical or Mental (player's choice) |
| Failure Tax | System Stress on failed execution | Stress cost on attempt + Feedback Consequence on Overchannel fail |
| Scene Economy | Per-program activation | Once per scene free / FP for additional uses |
| Campaign Pressure | Social (hacking is illegal) | HPA Attention clock + Signature traces |
| Advancement | Additional Programs as Stunts | Additional Pneumatic Stunts + higher-Tier artifacts |
