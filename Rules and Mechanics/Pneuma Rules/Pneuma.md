# Pneuma Stunt: Entropic Convergence Pressure (ECP)
*A Nova Praxis / FATE stunt write-up: lore first, then table-ready mechanics.*

> [!note] Module Position
> This file is an **optional focused stunt module** for tables that want a lighter Pneuma layer.
> The canonical full subsystem is [[Rules and Mechanics/Pneuma Rules/Pneuma_Architects_Echo_Rules]].

---

## Lore: What Pneuma Is
**Pneuma** is the name given (by people who needed a label that didn’t sound like “the universe is bullying me”) to a rare phenomenon where **probability behaves like a fluid under pressure**.

When you “lock” onto a goal—*find the fork, escape the Centurions, stabilize the APEX, turn a crowd*—the local possibility-space starts to **shed entropy**: loose ends stop being loose, coincidences start clustering, and the world subtly (or rudely) begins **converging** toward a single, self-consistent outcome.

In practice, Pneuma doesn’t grant omniscience or miracles. It creates **alignment**:
- the *right* person arrives,
- the *relevant* detail becomes visible,
- the *wrong* door is suddenly the *only* door,
- the *detour* becomes the *key*.

The price is the same every time: **Pressure.**  
Reality converges… and you’re the low-friction channel it uses.

**Common “tells”**
- repeating symbols, recurring names/phrases, déjà vu with details
- “random” interruptions that are too perfectly timed
- improbable meetings / interruptions / system failures at key moments
- an unmistakable feeling of being *towed* toward relevance

## Mechanics Overview
**Pneuma** is a **Stunt** that behaves like a **Savant Program**: you “run” a Pneuma mode to shape play using **Aspects, Assessments, Maneuvers, Invokes/Compels, Conflicts, and Extended Actions**.

### The Three Modes (pick one when you activate Pneuma)
- **Resonance**: reveal what matters (Assessments, pattern-sight)
- **Convergence**: arrange what matters (Maneuvers, positioning, setup)
- **Pressure**: force what matters (Invokes/Compels/Blocks/Declarations—at a cost)

---

## Stunt: Pneuma — Entropic Convergence Pressure
**Once per scene**, you may declare you are **Running Pneuma** and choose a mode: **Resonance, Convergence, or Pressure**.  
When you do, create a Temporary Aspect on yourself:

**“On the Convergence Gradient”** (Temporary)  
- Gains **1 free invoke** (2 free invokes if the roll that triggered activation generated **Spin**).  
- Lasts until the end of the scene or until you take a **Stall** while Pneuma is active (see Stall effects).

**Activation trigger (pick one):**
- When you begin an **Assessment**
- When you attempt a **Maneuver**
- When you start an **Extended Action**
- When you accept a **Compel** that pulls you toward the “thread”
- When a conflict begins and you commit to a clear objective (“get to the vault,” “take that sniper,” “hold the airlock”)

> **Hard Limit (balance rule):** Pneuma **cannot create information from nothing**. It can only **rearrange access** to facts, people, objects, routes, attention, timing—things that already exist in the fiction (even if off-screen).

## Mode Benefits

### 1) Resonance (Assessments + Meaning)
When Pneuma is active in **Resonance**:
- **Assessments in conflict**: reduce the normal minimum time by **1 round** (to a minimum of **1 round**) without taking the usual penalty for shortening time.
- When you succeed on an **Assessment**, you may ask the GM for the revealed Aspect **or** take the revealed Aspect and also learn one **non-mechanical fact** that makes it immediately actionable (a name, access point, schedule window, weakness, etc.).
- If you generate **Spin** on the Assessment, you may reveal **one additional related Aspect** (or upgrade the revealed one to be more “potent”/specific).

**Resonance Stall:** If you Stall on an Assessment while Resonance is active, the GM creates an Effect Aspect:
**“False Signal”** or **“Pattern Overload”** (Temporary, free invoke for GM).

---

### 2) Convergence (Maneuvers + Setup)
When Pneuma is active in **Convergence**:
- When you succeed at a **Maneuver** that creates a Temporary Aspect, that Aspect gains **+1 extra free invoke** (so: 2 free invokes total on that created Aspect, before Fate Points are needed).
- If your Maneuver targets a **zone or scene**, you may place the Aspect at the broader scope (zone → scene) **if it makes sense** and the GM agrees the fiction supports that reach.
- If you create an Aspect with **0 Shifts**, Convergence lets it persist long enough to matter: it will not vanish until **after** it is invoked/compelled once (instead of “maybe disappearing immediately”).

**Convergence Stall:** On Stall, the GM may immediately place a scene/zone Effect Aspect such as:
**“Pressure Spike”**, **“Wrong Place, Right Now”**, or **“Thread Snagged”** (Temporary, free invoke for GM).

---

### 3) Pressure (Invokes/Compels/Blocks/Declarations)
When Pneuma is active in **Pressure**, you can force narrative alignment—**but it hurts**.

Once per scene, you may do **one** of the following:

**A. Twist an Invocation**  
When *anyone* invokes an Aspect in the scene, you may pay **1 FP** *or* mark **1 stress** (Physical or Mental) to change the invoke’s benefit from:
- +2 ↔ reroll  
or
- convert it into a **Block** instead (Potency 2 by default, GM may raise if fiction is nasty)

**B. “Make It Relevant” Declaration**  
You may invoke **On the Convergence Gradient** (free if available, otherwise FP as normal) to make a **Declaration** that introduces a *plausible* convergent element:
- a door is unlocked because maintenance is mid-shift
- a courier arrives right now
- a guard is distracted by a real alert
- a critical tool is present because “of course it is”
GM can veto anything that breaks the scenario’s spine.

**C. Compel the Thread (Hard Compel)**  
You may offer a Compel using a Pneuma-created/related Aspect (including **Pressure Spike**) that **forces a meaningful complication** toward convergence.  
If the target refuses, it costs them **1 FP** as normal—*but* you must pay **1 FP** to offer it (player-to-player compel rules) unless the GM is the one accepting on behalf of an NPC.

**Pressure Cost:** After using A/B/C, you must either:
- mark **1 Mental stress**, or
- take a **Mild Consequence** (your choice of Physical or Mental), or
- accept an immediate GM Compel tied to Pneuma (you gain the FP).

**Pressure Stall:** If you Stall while Pressure is active, the GM gets a free Declaration-style complication:
**“Convergence Backlash”** (something breaks, someone arrives, you’re exposed, your plan collapses into a worse-but-relevant path).

## Pneuma + Conflicts: Stress Track Interaction
While Pneuma is active (any mode), once per scene you may **reroute** stress:
- When you would take **Physical** stress, you may take it as **Mental** instead (or vice versa),
**if you explain how Pneuma shifts the impact** (pain becomes panic; panic becomes adrenaline, etc.).

If this reroute prevents you from being Taken Out **right now**, the GM immediately creates a Temporary Effect Aspect on you:
**“White-Knuckle Focus”** or **“Shaken”** (free invoke for GM).

---

## Pneuma + Extended Actions
When Pneuma is active during an Extended Action:
- Once per Extended Action, you may add **+1 bonus Shift** to a successful roll’s contribution **after** the roll is made (not to Effort—this is “extra progress”).
- If that roll generated **Spin**, also reduce the **Mark** by **1** (in addition to the normal “Spin reduces mark by 1” rule, if your table uses that—if not, treat this as the only mark reduction).

**Cost:** each time you use this +1 progress, mark **1 stress** (Physical or Mental) or accept a Pneuma-flavored Compel.

**Extended Stall (catastrophic):** Pneuma makes failure *dramatic*—the GM should end the attempt with a hard consequence that still points at relevance (you can’t continue, but you learn why / who / where next).

---

## Spin & Stall Summary (Pneuma-flavored)
- **Spin while Pneuma is active:** you may create an Effect Aspect as usual, but name it as a *convergence artifact*:
  - **“Aligned Timing”**, **“Everything Connects”**, **“The Thread Exposed”**
- **Stall while Pneuma is active:** the GM should create an Effect Aspect that represents *pressure misfiring*:
  - **“False Signal”**, **“Pressure Spike”**, **“Thread Snagged”**, **“Convergence Backlash”**

> In contests/conflicts, if Spin and Stall would both appear, **Spin overrides Stall** (same as base rules).

## Example Uses (fast table cues)
- **Resonance Assessment:** “This facility feels… wrong.” → reveal **“Mostly Home to Apostates”** + learn the **shift-change window**.
- **Convergence Maneuver:** create **“Off-Balance”** on a foe, but it comes with **2 free invokes** because Pneuma “finds the angle.”
- **Pressure Twist:** someone invokes **“Foggy”** for +2 to hide; you mark 1 Mental stress to convert it into a **Block: Can’t See Two Meters Ahead** (Potency 2) that affects the whole zone.

---

## GM Guidance (keeps Pneuma powerful but not a problem)
- Pneuma should **accelerate relevance**, not erase obstacles.
- Always ask: “What already exists that Pneuma can rearrange access to?”
- When in doubt, let Pneuma succeed… and charge **pressure** via:
  - stress reroute, mild consequence, exposure, new threats, time compression, collateral damage, social fallout

## Optional Add-Ons (if you want advancement hooks)
**Upgrade Stunt: “Higher-Q Resonance”**  
Resonance Assessments reveal **1 extra detail** even without Spin, but Stall always creates a GM Effect Aspect.

**Upgrade Stunt: “Convergence Engine”**  
Once per session, treat a Pneuma-created Temporary Aspect as **Lasting** until the objective resolves.

**Upgrade Stunt: “Pressure Relief Valve”**  
Once per session, you may ignore the Pressure Cost—then the GM gets a future “banked” Pneuma Compel (they can cash it in later).
