# Mass Battles
## Overview
Mass Battles represent large conflicts, from small warbands to full divisions or planetary assaults. These rules enable the Game Master to narrate battles where heroes plan and participate in the action.
### When to Use
- **Applicable Situations**: Large conflicts where the outcome is uncertain and player characters play a pivotal role.
- **Narrative Importance**: Both victory and failure can be equally interesting.
## Mechanics
### Setup
- **Force Tokens**: Represent troops, vehicles, ships, etc. The stronger or larger army starts with 10 tokens, and the opposing army gets a proportional number.
- **Adjustments**: Factor in elite troops, better equipment, etc. to determine token numbers.
### Battle Process
- **Planning**: Player characters discuss and decide their strategy.
- **Commander Rolls**: Opposed Battle skill rolls, modified as appropriate.
- **Round Duration**: Standard is two hours, but can vary based on the scenario.
### Morale Checks
- **Conditions**: After losing Force Tokens, the army leader rolls Spirit with modifiers.
- **Outcomes**:
  - **Success**: The army continues fighting.
  - **Failure**: Orderly retreat.
  - **Critical Failure**: Chaotic rout.
## Player Characters in Mass Battles
- **Support Rolls**: Before the commander's roll, players can make Support rolls to affect the Battle roll.
- **Consequences**:
  - **Success**: +1 to commander, hero takes Fatigue.
  - **Raise**: Option to choose Battle Effects or give +2 to commander, emerges unharmed.
  - **Failure**: Hero takes a Wound, no bonus to commander.
  - **Critical Failure**: Rolls on Battle Effects and suffers d4 + 1 Wounds.
### Ammo & Power Points
- **Usage**: Each round in the fray expends ammunition or Power Points.
- **Arcane Types**: Use 3d6 Power Points per round.
- **Ranged Weapons**: Fire at standard Rate of Fire, 2d6 times.
### Aftermath
- **Conclusion**: When a side routs, retreats, or runs out of tokens, the battle ends.
- **Extras Fate**: Determine the fate of named Extras or NPCs as needed.
### Morale Modifiers
- **-1**: For each Force Token lost.
- **+2**: For undead/fearless troops, or troops in fortifications or without retreat options.
## Example
- *Gabe in Battle*: With a minigun RoF of 5, he uses 7 × 40 bullets (from a 2d6 roll), totaling 280 rounds in a round of mass battle.
