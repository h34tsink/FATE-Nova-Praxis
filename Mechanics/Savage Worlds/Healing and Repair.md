## Recovering from Wounds

Characters in the Nova Praxis setting generally heal like normal humans, but advanced technology exists that makes it possible to overcome even the most horrific of wounds, including death itself.

Characters who are injured may heal naturally or have their wounds quickly repaired with a **medi-stimulant injection** (pg. 199). More severe wounds, such as lost limbs, might require **Augmentation** or **cloned replacements**.

## Repairing [[Cybersleeve|Cybersleeves]] and Drones

The nano-cybernetic musculature that comprises a **[[Cybersleeve]]’s muscular system** consists of a self-healing fibrous mesh. The outer layers are made of similar material, all capable of healing itself naturally over time. As such, **Cybersleeve** do not need regular maintenance to repair surface abrasions and other “flesh wounds” — they heal on their own.

**[[Cybersleeve|Cybersleeves]]** use the normal rules for natural healing. More severe injuries might require **cyber repair injections** (pg. 197) or dedicated repair at the hands of a trained technician.

## Repairing SIMs

**[[SIM|SIMs]]**, lacking physical bodies, have little to fear from guns and blades in the real world. However, their **virtual equivalents** can be just as deadly. Most **[[Virtuality|virtualities]]** make it impossible for a character to suffer wounds while in a virtual environment. Some, however, do not feature those safety precautions.

In a virtuality that enables damage to the avatar, a **[[SIM]]** can find themselves in significant danger. Injuries sustained in a virtuality mimic real-world injuries of the same type in every way. SIMs can die of organ failure or bleed out while waiting for help. **Death in a virtuality destroys the ego**, though it may be restored from backup.

Virtualities may contain **virtual medical gear**, such as **medi-stimulant injections**. In these virtual environments, medical aid can be administered as if the SIM were in the flesh, and they can be expected to recover similarly.

## Healing Fragmentation Psychosis

Characters who change states, restore from backups, or resleeve too often can suffer from **Fragmentation** (pg. 251). Fragmentation is also a real danger when [[Merging]] and forking an ego.

If a character gains a **psychosis** from Fragmentation, they must live with that psychosis for at least a week.

Once per month, a character may attempt to overcome a single psychosis they have had for at least a week. To do so, they may make a **Spirit (modified by Cohesion)** roll. The character suffers their **Fragmentation penalty** on this roll, as well as an additional **-1 penalty** when trying to overcome psychoses represented by **Major Hindrances**.

If successful, the character reduces their **Fragmentation penalty by 1** and removes one of their psychoses that they have had for at least a week.

On a **critical failure**, the character has begun to spiral out of control. They suffer another **Fragmentation level** and gain a new **psychosis**.

A character may not reduce their Fragmentation penalty or the number of psychoses they have by more than **one per month** unless they also undergo **mnemonic editing**.

# Mnemonic Editing

**Mnemonic editing** is a powerful but risky method of restoring a damaged ego. The process takes several hours, during which a person trained in Mnemonics can attempt to repair a damaged ego similarly to debugging computer code. This process involves making changes to the highly complex code that constitutes the mind—a potentially dangerous procedure.

Specific memories cannot be targeted for erasure, nor can personality traits simply be removed, but areas indicating psychological trauma can be isolated and even dampened somewhat. While the memory will remain intact, the resulting traumatic reaction will be lessened. In effect, this is an artificial way of forcing a person to come to terms with an event.

In game terms, while the ego is **dormant**, it can be edited by a person trained in mnemonic editing. To do this, the ego must be hosted on a system outfitted with **hostware**

The character performing the mnemonic editing rolls their **Knowledge (Mnemonics)** skill. This roll suffers from the recipient’s **Fragmentation Penalty**.

- If successful on the **Knowledge (Mnemonics)** roll, the target’s **Fragmentation penalty is reduced by 1**, and one of their psychoses is removed.

- If the editing attempt **fails**, the recipient must make a **Spirit roll**, modified by **Cohesion** and their current **Fragmentation penalty**. If they succeed, nothing happens—the attempt failed, but no further damage was done. If they fail, they suffer **Fragmentation** again.

- On a **critical failure**, the patient suffers additional **Fragmentation**.

Repeated attempts at mnemonic editing in a short amount of time become increasingly risky. Each attempt after the first, within the same month, suffers a **-2 penalty**. This is cumulative, so if you try **three times in a month**, the third attempt will suffer a **-4 penalty**!

# Modifiers
## Cohesion
Your **Cohesion** is based on the type of sleeve you are transitioning into, or whether or not you are becoming a **SIM**.

- **Cybersleeve or SIM**: **Cohesion -3**
- **Biosleeve**: **Cohesion -1**
## Additional Modifiers
- If you have suffered **Fragmentation**, you suffer a cumulative **-1 penalty** per psychosis you have.
### Situational Modifiers
- **First Time Resleeving or State Change**: **+0** — This is the first time you have ever changed states or resleeved.
    
- **Resleeving After Death**: **-2** — You are resleeving from your mnemonic core after being killed, and you remember dying.
    
- **No Mnemonics Engineer**: **-2** — You do not have a trained mnemonics engineer to guide the process and/or are alone, relying on the resurrection chamber to automate the process.
    
- **Guided Process (Knowledge (Mnemonics) d4+)**: **+0** — The process is guided by another person with Knowledge (Mnemonics) d4 or higher.
    
- **Guided Process (Knowledge (Mnemonics) d8+)**: **+1** — The process is guided by another person with Knowledge (Mnemonics) d8 or higher. This might require an additional cost of **5** for the specialist.
    
- **Guided Process (Knowledge (Mnemonics) d12+)**: **+2** — The process is guided by another person with Knowledge (Mnemonics) d12 or higher. This might require an additional cost of **7** for the specialist.
    
- **Resleeving into Identical Sleeve**: **+2** — You resleeve into a clone of your last biosleeve or the exact same model of cybersleeve you previously inhabited.
    
- **Resleeving into Original Body**: **+3** — You resleeve back into your original body (now a biosleeve).
