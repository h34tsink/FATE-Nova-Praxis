Mission Frameworks are used when PCs need to execute a series of complex actions to achieve a specific goal. They are especially useful for showcasing PCs’ Skills in a series of mini-scenes or when the party needs to split up.
## Key Concepts:
### Regular Gameplay vs. Frameworks
- **Regular Gameplay**: Conversations and conflicts play out in real-time or slightly slower.
- **Frameworks**: Larger scale action where a single Trait roll can determine several minutes or hours of in-game activity.
### Appropriate Uses for Frameworks
- Sneaking into a secured warehouse.
- Pulling off a complex heist.
- Engaging in a high-speed chase.
- Repairing a spacecraft, etc.
## Framework Setup
### Establish the Goal
- The goal should be clear, specific, and defined in a single sentence.
### Set the Time Between Segments
- Replace "Rounds" with "Segments".
- Each Segment is considerably longer, from a few minutes to an hour.
### Determine Framework Characteristics
- Write down 3-4 facts about the Framework to guide and inspire Complications and Advantages.
### Determine Framework Complexity
- Complexity rating indicates the difficulty. Simple Frameworks have a Complexity of 4, meaning 4 tasks for 4 characters.
### Determine Framework’s Complications
- Start with Complications equal to Complexity.
- Complications have varying difficulties:
    - Easy: 4
    - Challenging: 6
    - Hard: 8
    - Very Hard: 10
    - Seemingly Impossible: 12
- Some Complications are dependent on others.
### Success or Failure
- PCs make Skill rolls or actions to eliminate Complications.
- Framework ends successfully when Complexity is 0, and all critical Complications are overcome.
- Failure can occur if the [[Situation]] worsens significantly.
## Running the Framework
### Overcoming Complications
- PCs choose Traits relevant to the Complications and roll against the Framework’s difficulty.
- Bonuses and penalties may apply depending on the Trait's suitability and repetition.
- Outcomes vary from reducing Complexity to worsening the [[Situation]].
### Create an Advantage
- Instead of direct Complication resolution, PCs can create Advantages.
- Advantages grant Bennies to later attempts at overcoming Complications.
### Using Advantages
- Advantages are recorded and assigned Bennies, which can be used in future Complication resolutions.
### Dealing with Disadvantages
- Disadvantages are factors that impose penalties on overcoming Complications.
### Getting Help
- Additional characters can assist, offering bonuses to the Trait roll.
### Framework Failure
- If Complexity increases significantly, the operation fails spectacularly.
### Making it your own
- GMs are encouraged to adapt these rules to fit their narrative.
### Combat in a Framework
- Combat is abstracted to larger-scale outcomes.
- Success in combat Complications can still result in damage to PCs.
### Favors in Framework
- Favors can be used to overcome Complications or create Advantages, with consequences for failure.
### Frameworks, Savants, and the SINC Skill
- The SINC Skill and Savant Programs can be used to overcome Complications or create Advantages.
### Advanced Frameworks
- GMs can introduce regular gameplay scenes within Frameworks for specific Complications or triggered events.
