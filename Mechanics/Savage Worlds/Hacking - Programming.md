### Hacking

Hacking plays a critical role in many missions, allowing characters to control systems, break into networks, or provide support to their allies. 

- **Unprotected Systems**: A single roll of the Hacking skill can control simple systems (e.g., turning lights on/off).
- **Secure Systems**: Complex hacks require a device, Hacking skill, and connection to the target network. If the network is online, it can be accessed remotely. Isolated networks require physical access, often necessitating infiltration to connect locally. Complex hacks are resolved as a **Dramatic Task**.

| **Security** | **Penalty** | **Task Type** |
| ------------ |:-----------:| ------------- |
| Light        |     −2      | Challenging   |
| Medium       |     −4      | Difficult     |
| Heavy        |     −4      | Complex       |
| Ultra        |     −6      | Complex       |

- **Failure**: Consequences depend on the target system. Outcomes include lockouts, shutdowns, tracing the hacker’s location, or dispatching a response team.

#### Supporting with Hacking

Using an unprotected or connected system (such as public infrastructure, personal devices, or basic control panels) often requires just a single Hacking roll. This roll can be used to assist an ally or create challenges for foes.

#### Netrunning

Hacks involve entering "cyberspace" via a direct connection using a **data jack** and **cyberdeck**. The hacker interacts with the virtual world through **Action Cards**, which also represent the countermeasures in place.

- **Virtual Hazards**:
    - **Spades (Tracer)**: The system traces the hacker’s physical location.
    - **Hearts (Overload)**: The hacker takes 2d6 damage (possibly nonlethal).
    - **Diamonds (Worm)**: Data is corrupted, losing d6 Task Tokens.
    - **Clubs (System Dump)**: The system shuts down, ejecting the hacker, who is **Stunned**.

#### Cyberdecks

Cyberdecks interface the hacker’s brainwaves with virtual systems, allowing up to 4 additional users to connect and offer **+1 Support**. These users can toggle between virtual and real-world views as a limited free action.

#### Programs

Cyberdecks can hold up to 4 **program slots**, which can assist hacking efforts. Programs cost $100, and some delete themselves after use. Programs may be created using **Hacking** and can aid in specific tasks.

- **BUDDY (1)**: Allows up to 4 connected allies to make Support rolls.
- **GUARDIAN (2)**: Blocks up to 4 damage from Overload.
- **HELPER (2)**: Adds +2 to Hacking totals.
- **REROUTE (1)**: Reroll a failed Hacking roll (including Critical Failures). Deletes itself if failed again.
- **SCRAMBLER (1)**: Spoofs Tracer countermeasures with a false location.
- **SPAMMER (2)**: Overloads the network, automatically failing the countermeasure for an Action Card.
- **VIRUS (1)**: Extends a Dramatic Task by 1 round.

#### Creating Software

Characters trained in **Knowledge (Software)** can create programs designed to aid specific tasks. Crafting software typically takes a few minutes to a few hours.

- **Task-Specific Programs**: Creating software for a specific task takes about 1 minute. Roll **Hacking** and declare the task. Success provides a bonus **Benny** usable for that task. Each raise provides an additional Benny, usable by anyone who uses the program.

Examples of tasks:

- **Security Response Time Simulation**: Helps predict security reactions.
- **Psych Profile Analysis**: Provides bonuses to detect lies.
- **Structural Analysis Simulation**: Aids in demolition tasks.

#### Viruses

Viruses can perform various invasive tasks but are difficult to deploy due to advanced security AIs. A virus must be crafted, loaded into the hacker’s cyberdeck, and deployed during hacking operations.

##### Virus Crafting

Crafting a virus takes 1d4 hours and requires a **Hacking** roll with a −2 penalty (higher penalties for more secure systems). Viruses must be loaded into the hacker's cyberdeck for use. When creating a virus, the hacker chooses from the following effects:

- **System Error** (Slots 2)
- **System Invasion** (Slots 2)
- **Security Skim** (Slots 2)
- **Fog of War** (Slots 2)
- **Eminent Domain** (Slots 2)

Each virus requires a **Hacking** roll with a −2 penalty to deploy. On a success, the virus performs one of the selected effects. With a **raise**, the hacker may add an additional use of the virus, following the same rules as crafting for **Eminent Domain**.

- **Raises**: Each raise during virus creation can provide additional effects:
    - Delayed execution of the virus.
    - Multiple effects (must be executed over at least 2 rounds).
    - Gain a +2 bonus on the virus deployment roll.
    - Choose between two different effects at the time of execution.

Viruses act as powerful tools for advanced hacking operations, but once detected, they are eradicated by the system’s security AI.

### Step-by-Step Guide for Performing a Hacking Attempt

**Hacking** often involves breaking into a secure system or accessing sensitive data. Here's how to perform a hacking attempt, including the basic steps and special rules for more complex situations like **netrunning**.
#### 1. **Preparation**

To begin a hacking attempt, the following must be in place:

- **Hacking Skill**: Ensure the character attempting the hack has the **Hacking skill**.
- **Device**: The hacker needs a computing device (like a cyberdeck, laptop, or terminal).
- **Connection**: There must be a connection to the target network. The network can either be:
    - **Online**: Accessible from any stable connection (e.g., remotely).
    - **Isolated**: Only accessible from within an enemy facility or building, requiring physical infiltration to connect to a local node or terminal.
#### 2. **Establish the Connection**

If the network is isolated, the hacker and their team must first **infiltrate** the facility or compound and locate a terminal or node to establish the connection. This step may involve sneaking, fighting, or persuading the way in. If the network is online, skip to the next step.
#### 3. **Resolve as a Dramatic Task**

Once connected, hacking a secure system is treated as a **Dramatic Task** in **SWADE**. Here's how to resolve it:

- **Determine the Difficulty**: The security of the system determines the penalties and difficulty of the task:
    
    - **Light Security**: −2 penalty, **Challenging Task**.
    - **Medium Security**: −4 penalty, **Difficult Task**.
    - **Heavy Security**: −4 penalty, **Complex Task**.
    - **Ultra Security**: −6 penalty, **Complex Task**.
    
- **Roll Hacking Skill**: The hacker rolls **Hacking** (with the security penalty applied) over several rounds. The number of successful rolls required and the time it takes depend on the Dramatic Task's difficulty.

| **Security** | **Penalty** | **Task Type** |
| ------------ | ----------- | ------------- |
| Light        | −2          | Challenging   |
| Medium       | −4          | Difficult     |
| Heavy        | −4          | Complex       |
| Ultra        | −6          | Complex       |
#### 4. **Track Task Tokens**

The goal is to accumulate **Task Tokens** during the Dramatic Task:

- **Success**: Each successful roll grants one Task Token.
- **Failure**: Failing a roll may result in the system reacting negatively (explained below).
#### 5. **Handle Failure**

If the hacker fails to gain at least one **Task Token** in a round, or the Dramatic Task is completely failed, the consequences depend on the system’s response:

- **Lockdown**: The system might lock down and prevent further access for several hours.
- **Shutdown**: The system might shut down entirely, making it impossible to continue the task.
- **Trace**: The system may trace the hacker’s physical location, potentially sending response teams to deal with them.
#### 6. **Netrunning**

In certain settings, hacking involves entering a **cyberspace** simulation (known as **netrunning**) to interact directly with the target network. **Netrunning** follows the same steps as hacking but includes additional elements:

- **Connection**: Netrunning requires a **data jack** and a **cyberdeck** to connect the hacker's mind to the virtual world. Once connected, the hacker perceives the network as a virtual environment, such as a world of digital structures or a more fantastical setting.
    
- **Action Cards**: Each round, the hacker draws an **Action Card** representing both their initiative and the **countermeasures** the system deploys against them. If the hacker fails to gain at least one **Task Token** in a round, additional consequences occur based on the card’s suit:
    
    - **Spades (Tracer)**: The system traces the hacker's physical location to dispatch security forces.
    - **Hearts (Overload)**: The system sends a current down the connection, causing 2d6 damage to the hacker.
    - **Diamonds (Worm)**: The system sends corrupted data, deleting any stolen information and causing the hacker to lose d6 Task Tokens.
    - **Clubs (System Dump)**: The system shuts down, ejecting the hacker and leaving them **Stunned**.
#### 7. **Special Programs and Assistance**

- **Programs**: Hackers can use **Programs** installed on their cyberdeck to aid the task. For example, programs like **REROUTE** allow rerolling failed hacking attempts, while **BUDDY** enables allies to make **Support** rolls.
- **Support**: In certain cases, hackers can use their skill to support allies by interacting with systems such as disabling alarms, opening doors, or manipulating security cameras. Each action is resolved with a single Hacking roll, applying a **Support** bonus to the ally’s action.
#### 8. **Complete the Hacking Attempt**

The hacking attempt is complete when the hacker has accumulated enough **Task Tokens** to successfully break into the system or when failure results in one of the system's negative reactions. Success allows the hacker to achieve their goal, such as retrieving sensitive data, disabling security systems, or taking control of critical infrastructure.
