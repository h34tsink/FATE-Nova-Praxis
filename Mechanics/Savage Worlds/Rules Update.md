## Savage Worlds Skills Update

### Core Skills
- **Athletics, Common Knowledge, Notice, Persuasion, Stealth**: Every hero starts with a d4 in these five basic abilities.
### Changes and Additions
- **Charisma**: Removed from the game.
- **Climbing**: Folded into Athletics.
- **Common Knowledge**: Now its own skill, not a Smarts roll.
- **Investigation -> Research**: Name change for clarity.
- **Focus**: New skill for Gifted Arcane [[Background]].
- **Knowledge**: Broken down into specific skills: Academics, Battle, Electronics, Hacking, Language, Occult, Science.
- **Lockpicking -> Thievery**: Now includes pickpocketing, sleight of hand, safecracking, and other roguish activities.
- **Performance**: New skill tied to Spirit, covers singing, acting, playing instruments, also for deception, bluffing, or disguise.
- **Repair**: Clarified use.
- **Streetwise**: Now an Edge.
- **Swimming, Throwing**: Handled by Athletics.
- **Tracking -> Survival**: Integrated into Survival.
- **Stealth**: Successful Sneak Attack can render victim Vulnerable or give attacker The Drop on a raise.
- **Modifiers**: Comprehensive modifiers for skills like Stealth and Tracking (now part of Survival) are GM's discretion, ranging from +4 to -4.
- **Familiarization Penalty**: Typically ranges from -2 to -4.
- **Persuasion**: Can be used to Support allies (unopposed roll) or against resistant targets (opposed vs. target's Spirit). Also functions as a macro skill for networking.
- **Reaction Table**: Updated and expanded with more attitude levels.

## Gear Rules Updates

### Armor
- **Stacking**: Worn Armor stacks with natural Armor at full value.
- **Additional Layer**: Stacks with one other layer; lesser armor adds half its value to total, increasing Minimum Strength penalty of heavier armor by a die type.
### Minimum Strength
- **Requirement**: Items may have a Minimum Strength to use without penalty.
- **Armor/Worn Gear**: Each die type difference between Strength and item's Minimum Strength causes a −1 penalty to Pace, Agility, and Agility-related skill rolls.
- **Melee/Thrown Weapons**: Damage die limited by user's Strength die. If Strength is less than Minimum Strength, no benefits from weapon's positive abilities, only penalties.
- **Ranged Weapons**: −1 attack penalty per die step difference between Strength and weapon's minimum.
### Parry
- **Bonus**: Weapon bonus added to character's Parry score.
- **Dual Wielding**: Penalties to Parry stack, bonuses do not (unless with Ambidextrous Edge).
### Range
- **[[Distances]]**: Lists weapon’s Short, Medium, and Long Range. Extreme Range is up to 4 × Long Range.
### Rate of Fire
- **Shots Per Action**: Indicates the number of shots fired in a single action.
### Semi-Auto
- **Designation Removal**: Removed from the game. Characters can take same action up to three times per turn with multi-action penalty.
### Three Round Burst
- **Military Weapons**: Fires three rounds with one trigger pull. RoF is 1, but fires three bullets and adds +1 to Shooting and damage rolls.
### Two Hands
- **Usage**: Two-handed weapon can be used one-handed at a −4 penalty. Full Strength for damage but loses other advantages.
### Encumbrance
- **Effects**: Encumbered characters have reduced Pace, running rolls, Agility and linked skills, and Vigor rolls for resisting Fatigue.
- **Limits**: Can move at Pace 1 for rounds equal to Vigor at three times the lifted weight; thereafter, must succeed Vigor roll or take Fatigue.
- **Maximum Weight**: Four times the listed weight.

## Vehicle Stats

### General Statistics
- **Size**: Relative scale of the vehicle to a human. Vehicles take three Wounds before wrecking; Large, Huge, and Gargantuan vehicles take additional Wounds.
- **Handling**: Added or subtracted from operator's maneuvering rolls. Ranges from -4 to +4.
- **Top Speed**: Expressed in MPH. For tabletop integration, convert MPH to Pace by multiplying by 1.5.
- **Toughness**: Base durability including Armor. For real-world vehicles with Heavy Armor, start with +4 and adjust based on armor thickness and materials.
- **Passengers**: Number of crew and additional passengers.
- **Cost**: Average price of the vehicle.

### Vehicle Notes
- **Air Bags & Safety Harnesses**: Halve collision damage for passengers.
- **Amphibious**: Can enter water without issues.
- **Anti-Missile Counter Measures (AMCM)**: Add +2 to evasion rolls against missiles.
- **Four-Wheel Drive (4WD)**: Difficult Ground treated as 1.5'' instead of 2''.
- **Heavy Armor**: Only Heavy Weapons can damage; halve damage from collisions with non-Heavy Armor objects.
- **Hover**: Can ignore low terrain obstacles and water.
- **Infrared Night Vision**: Halves Illumination penalties against heat-emitting targets.
- **Linked Weapons**: Dual or quad linked for increased hit and damage.
- **Night Vision**: Eliminates Dim and Dark Illumination penalties with ambient light.
- **Reaction Fire**: Special abilities in Chases.
- **Sloped Armor**: −2 Shooting penalty for direct-fire ballistic attacks.
- **Spacecraft**: Designed for outer space; Atmospheric ones can enter/exit planetary atmospheres.
- **Stabilizer**: Reduces Unstable Platform penalty.
- **Stealth Paint**: -4 penalty for Electronics rolls to spot/lock onto the vehicle.
- **Tracked**: Ignores movement penalties for Difficult Ground.
- **Weapons**: Location and type of vehicle weapons.

### Weapon Mounts
- **Fixed**: Fires in a specific direction.
- **Pintle Mount**: 180° arc, must align with turret if mounted on it.
- **Turret**: 360° arc of fire.

### Converting from Previous Editions
- **Acceleration (Acc)**: Now factored into Handling and Top Speed.
- **Climb**: Factored into each aircraft’s Handling.
- **Pace**: For real vehicles, use actual top speed in MPH. For fictional vehicles, multiply old Top Speed by 3 to get real-world MPH.
