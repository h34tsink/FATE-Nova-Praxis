# Nova Praxis (FATE) — Play Rules Quick Reference (Concise)

## Core Mechanic (Effort → Shifts)
- Roll **4dF** (FUDGE dice: each die is **-1 / 0 / +1**), add **Skill** (+ other modifiers) = **Effort**.
- **Shifts** = Effort − Difficulty (or − opponent’s Effort in opposed rolls).
- **Success**: Effort ≥ Difficulty.
- **Failure**: Effort < Difficulty.

### Spin / [[Stall]]
- **Spin**: succeed by **3+ Shifts** (Effort ≥ Difficulty + 3).
- **Stall**: fail by **3+ Shifts** (Effort ≤ Difficulty − 3).
- In opposed rolls (contests/conflict), **Spin overrides Stall** (same exchange can’t “be both”).

### Don’t have FUDGE dice?
- Use **d6 − d6** (one positive, one negative). Range becomes **-5..+5** (a bit swingier).

---

## Taking Action

### 1) Test (vs GM difficulty)
Use when the world pushes back, not a specific opponent.
1. GM describes situation, picks Skill, sets **Difficulty**.
2. Roll → Effort.
3. Compare → Shifts (and Spin/Stall if applicable).

Examples: research a fact, search a scene, notice a pattern.

### 2) Contest (vs another character)
Use when you’re directly opposed and it’s a single moment of resolution.
- Both roll appropriate Skills.
- **Higher Effort wins**.
- **Tie**: both “succeed,” but outcome depends on context (often reroll or status quo).

Examples: Guile vs Insight (lie), Stealth vs Perception (sneak).

### 3) Conflict (when it won’t resolve cleanly)
Use when the opposition needs **exchanges/rounds** (fights, chases, tense stand-offs).
- Happens in **rounds**; each character gets a **turn** each round.

**Attack sequence**
- Defender rolls **defense Skill** first; their Effort becomes the attacker’s **Difficulty**.
- Attacker rolls **attack Skill**.
- If attacker succeeds: **Stress dealt** = **Shifts + Weapon Rating − Armor Rating** (min 0).
- Defender can reduce remaining stress by taking **Consequences**.

**Taken Out**
- When your **Stress Track** is fully checked and you can’t (or won’t) take enough Consequences to cover it, you’re **Taken Out**.

---

## Time & Actions in Conflict
On your turn you normally get:
- **1 Simple Action** (the main thing you do)
- **1 Free Action** (quick, small thing)
- **Instant Actions** can happen anytime **if allowed**, usually **max 1/turn**  
  - **Defense tests** are a special Instant Action and **don’t count** against that limit.

**Free Action example:** move to an adjacent zone.

---

## Extended Actions (big tasks, not a conflict)
Use when it takes time + multiple rolls (repairs, engineering, long persuasion effort).
- Set **Difficulty** like a normal test.
- Compute **Mark** = Difficulty × **3**.
- Make repeated tests over time; **accumulate Shifts** across rolls.
- Success when total accumulated Shifts ≥ Mark.

**Spin / Stall in Extended Actions**
- **Spin**: reduce Mark by **1**.
- **Stall**: catastrophic failure—**can’t continue** (GM says how bad).

---

## Doing Multiple Things

### Supplemental Action (doing “another thing” alongside your Simple Action)
- If you do a tested Simple Action while also doing something else: **-1** to the primary test.
- If unclear which is primary: primary = the thing that needs the roll / has higher stakes; supplemental = the “extra.”

GM may increase the penalty if it’s especially messy.

### Combining Skills (two Skills genuinely matter to the one action)
Roll using the **primary Skill**, modified by the secondary Skill:
- Secondary higher than primary: **+1**
- Secondary lower than primary: **-1**
- Same rating: **+0**

> Combining Skills is **not** “two full actions at once.” If that’s what you want, it takes multiple turns.

---

## Degrees of Success (quick feel)
- **0 Shifts**: minimal success (you barely pull it off; minor cost/complication possible).
- **1–2 Shifts**: solid, reliable success.
- **3–4 Shifts**: significant success (**Spin**).
- **5+ Shifts**: potent success (often extra useful side benefits).

# Aspects (the engine)

## What Aspects do
Aspects are true statements (phrases/quotes) about:
- characters, gear, zones, scenes, organizations—**anything**
- they can help you **or** complicate your life (and fuel Fate Points)

## Fate Points & Refresh
- Spend **Fate Points (FP)** to **invoke** Aspects for benefits.
- Gain FP when you **accept compels** that cause trouble.
- At session start you **Refresh** to your **Refresh Rating** (default often **5**).
- Fate Points **don’t carry over** between sessions (use ’em).

---

## Invoking an Aspect (spend FP)
Pick an Aspect that *actually* helps right now, pay **1 FP**, and get **one**:
- **+2** to a roll
- **Reroll** the dice
- Create a **Block**
- Make a **Declaration** (narrative control; GM approval)

### Invoking for Free (first hit is free)
Any Aspect created as a direct result of actions can be invoked/compelled **for free once**:
- Maneuvers (created Aspects)
- Consequences inflicted
- Effect Aspects from Spin
- If Spin helped create it, it may allow **extra free use** (see Maneuvers).

---

## Compelling an Aspect (earn FP)
A compel is “your Aspect makes your life harder *right now*.”
If you accept the compel, you gain **1 FP** and suffer one:
- **-2** on a roll
- **Forced reroll**
- Suffer a **Block**
- Suffer a **Declaration**

You can decline by paying **1 FP** (that FP leaves play).

### Active vs Passive Compels
- **Active**: GM (or another player) offers it directly.
- **Passive**: the Aspect naturally causes trouble; GM awards FP when it *actually impacts you*.

### Soft Compels
- A “minor” compel you can reject **for free** (group-friendly variant).
- **Never** use soft compels for **Persistent** Aspects.

---

## Scope Limit (don’t stack infinite Aspects)
Rule of thumb: **1 Aspect per scope per test**.
Common scopes include: Character, Consequence, Stunt, Augmentation, Scene, Zone, Equipment, Temporary, Other Character.
- **Persistent Aspects** are special: they don’t count against scope limits for free +2/-2 or reroll use.

---

## “Therefore Rule” (anti-abuse sanity check)
To invoke/compel, justify with **one** “therefore”:
- “I have X Aspect, **therefore** it helps me do Y.”
If you need multiple chained assumptions, it’s probably not valid.

---

# Persistent, Temporary, Lasting

## Persistent Aspects (P)
Used when it’s huge/inescapable (or Severe/Extreme consequences).
- For **+2/-2 or reroll**, you can invoke/compel a Persistent Aspect **without paying FP** (just state it).
- For **Blocks or Declarations**, you still pay FP as normal.
- You **don’t gain FP** when a Persistent Aspect is compelled.
- Refusing a Persistent compel always costs **1 FP**.

## Temporary vs Lasting Aspects
- **Temporary**: usually lasts a round or the scene; often removable.
- **Lasting**: sticks around until resolved (relationships, debts, reputations, “Never show your face on Luna again!”).
- Temporary Aspects may be removed with a Simple Action (often a quick test around **Diff 2**, GM call).

---

# Creating / Discovering / Declaring Aspects

## Maneuvers (Create an Aspect)
A Maneuver is a Simple Action taken **specifically** to create a Temporary Aspect.
- Resolve as a **test** (vs difficulty) or **contest** (vs target’s opposing skill).
- If you get **Spin** while creating it, the created Aspect may get **two free uses** (instead of one).

**Duration (quick rule)**
- Success with **0 Shifts**: fleeting/weak; often disappears after first invoke/compel.
- Success with **1+ Shifts**: lasts the **scene** or until actively removed (GM call).

## Assessments (Reveal an Aspect)
You observe/analyze to discover an existing Aspect.
- Usually a **test** (objects/places) or **contest** (people: often Insight vs Guile).
- Baseline difficulty often **2** if nothing else fits.

**In conflict**
- Typically takes **3 rounds** minimum.
- You can shorten it by taking a penalty equal to rounds removed (down to 1 round).

**Outside conflict**
- Takes seconds/minutes.
- If you spend **~1 hour**: **+2**; **~4 hours**: **+3** (GM call).
- Spin may reveal a stronger truth or an additional Aspect.

## Declarations (Add a story element / new Aspect)
You can Declare via:
1) **Invoking/compelling an Aspect** instead of taking +2/reroll, **or**
2) Making a **Skill test** based on relevant knowledge to Declare.

- **GM veto always applies** (must fit the story + setting).
- Best used to add drama, reveal useful truths, or move a dragging scene forward.

**Keep Declarations narrative**
- Avoid “rules edits” in the moment.
- If your Declaration starts involving **numbers/fractions**, it’s a warning sign you’re doing mechanics instead of story.

# Stress & Consequences (damage, trauma, fallout)

## Stress Track
- Stress from an attack fills boxes **left to right**.
- If you can’t absorb stress (stress track full) → **Taken Out**.

## Consequences (reduce stress)
You may take Consequences to reduce incoming stress. They are Aspects describing harm:
- Mild / Moderate / Severe / Extreme (labels vary by table, but severity increases impact).
- Consequences can be invoked/compelled; first use is typically **free** (created by action).
- **Severe(P)** and **Extreme(P)** are **Persistent**, granting free +2/-2 or reroll use.

---

## One-page flow (mental model)
1. **Pick action type**: Test / Contest / Conflict / Extended
2. **Roll Skill → Effort**
3. **Compute Shifts**
4. Apply **Spin/Stall** if 3+ margin
5. Use **Aspects + FP** (invoke/compel; respect scope + therefore)
6. In conflict: apply **Stress**, then **Consequences**, then resolve **Taken Out** if needed
