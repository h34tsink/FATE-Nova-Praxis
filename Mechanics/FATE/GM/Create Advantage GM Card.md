# 🎲 Create Advantage – GM Reference Card
*Fate Core Quick Guide for Scene Discovery & Tactical Play*

---

## 🎯 Purpose
Use **Create an Advantage** when a player wants to:
- Reveal a hidden truth or factor in the scene.  
- Invent a new tactical, emotional, or environmental edge.  
- Set up future rolls with free invokes.  

It’s the *creative engine* of Fate — how characters make the world work for them.

---

## ⚙️ Flow of Play (GM Procedure)

1. **Player declares intent**  
   → “I’m looking for something to help us.”  
   → “I want to calm them down.”  
   → “I hack the mesh for leverage.”

2. **Clarify the intent if needed**  
   - Ask: “Are you trying to discover info, change the scene, or distract the enemy?”

3. **Pick the appropriate skill or approach**  
   - **Clever:** analyze, plan, manipulate systems  
   - **Careful:** prepare traps, reinforce positions  
   - **Flashy:** draw attention, inspire allies  
   - **Forceful:** intimidate, exert physical control  
   - **Sneaky:** misdirect, infiltrate  
   - **Quick:** react or reposition before others  

4. **Roll and interpret the result** (see table below).  
5. **Name the Aspect together.**  
   - Player-created → they phrase it.  
   - Hidden Aspect → you reveal it cinematically.  
6. **Place the Aspect on the table** and track its invokes.

---

## 📊 Results Table

| Outcome | Result | Example |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Failure (-1 or less)** | Aspect not created, or success at a cost | “You find the comms relay, but trigger an alert.” |
| **Tie (0)** | Aspect exists, no free invokes | “‘Riot Distraction’ created, but you’ll need to spend FP to use it.” |
| **Success (+1–+2)** | Aspect created or revealed + **1 free invoke** | “‘Power Conduits Overloaded’ — 1 tag.” |
| **Success with Style (+3+)** | Aspect created or revealed + **2 free invokes** | “‘Weak Ceiling Girders’ — 2 tags.” |

## 🧠 GM Tips

- Don’t require precision — **intent matters more than details.**  
- If players seem uncertain, **offer three prompts:**  
  > “Do you want to find something, change something, or distract someone?”  
- Always connect the result back to **fictional change.**  
- Keep the number of Aspects manageable (2–4 active per scene).  
- Encourage players to name their Aspects with flavor — it boosts immersion.  
- NPCs can also Create Advantages! (Example: *‘Drones Synchronize Fire’*.)

## 💬 Example in Action

**Scene:** Riot at Dock 27  
**Hidden Aspect (GM):** *‘Lighthouse Reinforcements En Route’*  

**Player:** “I scan comms for movement chatter.”  
**Roll:** Success!  
**GM:** “You intercept encrypted chatter — Reinforcements En Route. You get one free invoke if you act on that intel before they land.”  

✅ Aspect revealed  
✅ Fiction advanced  
✅ Everyone’s engaged

## ❤️ Summary

| Question | Quick Answer |
|-----------|---------------|
| Do players need to know the Aspect name before rolling? | No. They can be vague — you interpret it. |
| Can they invent Aspects? | Yes, that’s the point! |
| Can they reveal hidden ones? | Absolutely — via Create Advantage. |
| How many free invokes? | 1 on success, 2 on success with style. |
| What if they fail? | Complicate the fiction — alarms, time loss, suspicion, etc. |
