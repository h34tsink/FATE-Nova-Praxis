## Fate Conversions at a Glance
- **Actions:** Multiverse travel scenes use **Overcome** to jump and **Create an Advantage** to prep (address, beacons, gates). Conflicts work as usual once you arrive.
- **Difficulty Ladder:** *Mediocre +0, Average +1, Fair +2, Good +3, Great +4, Superb +5, Fantastic +6, Epic +7, Legendary +8.*
- **Success with Style:** Grants a **Boost** (often **Intel**). Two free invokes = you absolutely did the homework.
- **Aspects & Invokes:** **Address**, **Beacon Lock**, **Gate Quality**, **Halo Profile**, **Anchor**, **Myth‑Logic Tax**, **Meta Glare** are all aspects that can be invoked/compelled.
- **Conditions (Fate Condensed style):**
  - **Glitch** (sticky, 1 box) — minor faults. Clear after a cool-down or scene with maintenance.
  - **Drift [ ][ ]** (2 boxes) — your reality assumptions are off. Each box adds passive opposition **+1** to nav/tech until recalibrated (Overcome at Fair +2 per box, scene or downtime).
  - **Strain [ ][ ][ ]** (3 boxes, ship) — heat/frame stress. When all marked, the drive is **Threatened**; further Strain becomes consequences.

> **Tip:** Treat the ship as an **Extra** with its own aspects, stunts, and Strain/Consequences track (see Part V).

# PART I — QUICKSTART FOR FATE

### The Four Beats (table loop)
1) **Address It** — *Create an Advantage (Lore/Science)* to place **Composite Address** with 0–2 free invokes.  
   - **Difficulty:** usually **Fair (+2)**; +1 per unfamiliar or exotic target tag.
2) **Fix Position** — *Create an Advantage (Lore + Ship/gear)* to place **Beacon Lock** (ranked). Each free invoke ≈ **1 Lock** (cap 4).  
   - **Base Difficulty:** **Good (+3)** under normal conditions.
3) **Plot a Route** — Choose a **Path** and resolve any **Gate** prices as Overcome or compels.  
   - **Path Difficulties:** **Safe = Fair (+2)**, **Standard = Good (+3)**, **Risky = Great (+4)** before modifiers.
4) **Execute** — *Overcome (Pilot/Drive or Lore via jump console)* against final difficulty.  
   - **Success:** Arrive. **Success with Style:** arrive + gain **Intel** Boost.  
   - **Fail:** GM chooses a **Mishap** (Safe/Standard) or **Catastrophe** (Risky).

**Common Modifiers**
- **Law Gradient Steps (ΔL):** +1 difficulty per step (usually 0–2); reduce by 1 if using a **Known Gate**.
- **Beacon Lock:** Spend invokes from **Beacon Lock** to mitigate; each invoke cancels **+2** of difficulty or upgrades outcome one band.
- **Ship Stunts/Tags:** Apply normal Fate stunt bonuses.
- **Compels:** *“Void Halo”*, *“Hungry Gate”*, *“Audit Ping”* are delicious compels—take a fate point and suffer the trouble.

# PART II — CORE PROCEDURES

## 1) Addressing (who the universe **is**)
**Player-Facing Aspect:** **Composite Address** (write three tags)
```
[LAW] __________ • [SEED] __________ • [TOPO] __________
```
- *Create an Advantage (Lore)* vs **Fair (+2)** to place **Composite Address** with **+1 free invoke**; **Success with Style** = **+2**.
- Spend invokes to help beaconing, routing, or research in that destination.

**GM Note (diegetic mapping):** Behind the screen, the address encodes **LSP (laws), ICF (seed), TOP (topology), CGA (causal hash)**. See Appendix.

---

## 2) Beacons (getting **relative**)
**Beacon Lock** is a scene aspect you place with **Create an Advantage (Lore/Science)** using ship gear.  
- **Base Difficulty:** **Good (+3)**; spend time, gear, or arguments to justify teamwork.  
- Each **free invoke = 1 Lock** (cap **4**). You may also convert boosts (e.g., from sensor sweeps) into Lock at GM discretion.

**Blind Jump:** If you skip beacons, roll the jump with a **–2 penalty** and on fail you arrive in a **neighbor** reality (GM nudges a tag on the Composite Address).

---

## 3) Routing & Paths
Pick one of three **Path** aspects for the scene; it sets tone and expected risk.

| Path | Base Diff | On Success | On Tie | On Fail |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| **Safe** | Fair (+2) | Arrive; create **Intel** Boost | Arrive with **Glitch** | **Mishap** + mark **Drift** (1) |
| **Standard** | Good (+3) | Arrive | Arrive; **Glitch** + **Strain** (1) | **Mishap** + **Drift** (2) |
| **Risky** | Great (+4) | Arrive; choose **Intel** or **Loot** Boost | Arrive battered; **Glitch** + **Strain** (2) | **Catastrophe** + **Drift** (3) |

**Gate Challenges:** A Gate often needs **two steps** (Challenge):
1. **Price** (Overcome or accept a compel): pay heat/isotope/blood/title/oath/etc.  
2. **Passage** (Overcome with Ship/Pilot).  
Gain an extra **free invoke** on **Gate Quality** if you stabilize or appease it first.

---

## 4) Arrival & Discovery
On arrival, the GM may place 1–2 **Locale** aspects and offer an **Intel** Boost if you succeeded with style or surveyed first.

**Discovery Table (roll 1d6):**
1 Familiar‑but‑askew society • 2 Gravity mischief • 3 Time districts • 4 Ruin with wardens • 5 Living megastructure • 6 Myth‑engine

---

## 5) Glitch, Drift, Strain (Conditions)
- **Glitch (sticky, 1)** — Minor faults; compel to cause sensor ghosts, flicker, weird echoes. Clear by resting or venting.
- **Drift [ ][ ]** — Each checked box imposes **+1 passive opposition** on travel/tech until **Recalibrate** (*Overcome Fair +2 per box*). You may also spend **Intel** to clear 1.
- **Strain [ ][ ][ ] (Ship)** — Mark on Mixed/Fail. When full, further Strain becomes **Ship Consequences** (Mild/Moderate/Severe) like *Warp‑Scored Coils*, *Boiling Coolant*, *Frame Torsion*.

# PART III — DARK MATTER ALEPHS (Halo Rules & Gear)

## 6) Halo Profiles (scene aspects)
At origin and destination, set one **Halo Profile** (or roll 1d6):  
**1–2 Calm**, **3–4 Clumpy**, **5 Caustic**, **6 Void**.

- **Calm:** +2 to **Create Advantage** for **Beacon Lock** (or +1 free invoke).  
- **Clumpy:** no change.  
- **Caustic:** Gain a **free invoke** on **Beacon Lock** *but* GM may **immediately compel** *Overheated Coils* (mark **Strain 1**) or demand a cost.  
- **Void:** +2 difficulty to Beacon Lock attempts; Blind Jump penalty increases to **–3** if you have **0 Lock**.

**New Move — Tune the Halo:** *Overcome (Lore/Science)* vs **Good (+3)**; **Success:** place **Tuned Halo** with one free invoke; **Mixed:** also mark **Glitch**; **Fail:** mark **Strain 1** or trigger a **Halo Event** (see GM Kit).

## 7) Beacon Modalities (pick 1 as a ship stunt/extra)
- **Axion Lattice:** Once per jump, downgrade a **Glitch** into an **Intel** Boost. (Stunt)  
- **Fuzzy‑Field Interferometer:** +2 to **Overcome** for Law‑gradient steps ≤1; on fail, add a quirky **Temporal Slip** detail.  
- **Dark Photon Cavity:** +2 to **Beacon Lock** in **Clumpy** regions; vulnerable to spoof—GM gets +2 to deception unless you spend a **Cavity Seal** (consumable).  
- **Sterile Neutrino Oscillator:** Ignore **Void** penalty once per session.  
- **WIMP Scintillation Array:** On **Survey**, also gain an automatic **Intel** Boost.

**Gear (Extras/Aspects):** *SQUID Readout*, *Cryo Pump*, *Cavity Seal (consumable)*, *Phase Knife* (cut micro‑gate: 1 action, mark **Strain 1**).

# PART IV — SUPERVERSAL & SUBVERSAL JUMPS

## 8) Modes (choose per scene)
| Mode | Use | Requirements | Base Diff |
|---|---|---|---:|
| **Intraversal** | within universe | **Lock ≥1** or Gate | Good (+3) |
| **Subversal** | dive into embedded layers (vaults, sim shards, demi‑planes) | **Substrate Key** or **Micro‑Gate** or **Caustic Halo** | Great (+4) |
| **Superversal** | leap to sibling law‑stacks (meta layer) | **Meta Handle** + Gate (or ΔL≥2) + **Lock ≥2** | Superb (+5) |

**Modifiers:** ΔL adds +1 per step (Intra/Super). **Void Halo** adds +2 to Superversal; **Caustic** grants +2 to Subversal.

## 9) Moves
**Dive the Substrate (Subversal)** — *Overcome (Lore or Pilot via ship)* vs base diff.  
- **Success:** Arrive; choose **Quiet Entry** or place **Anchor** (aspect with 1 free invoke).  
- **Tie:** Arrive with **Myth‑Logic Tax** (scene aspect with 1 free invoke for the GM).  
- **Fail (soft catastrophe):** roll on Subversal Mishaps.

**Ascend the Stack (Superversal)** — *Overcome* vs base diff.  
- **Success:** Arrive; clear **Drift** entirely and gain an **Intel** Boost.  
- **Tie:** Arrive; mark **Strain 1** and **Drift (check 1)**; the **Meta Clerks** notice.  
- **Fail (hard catastrophe):** roll on Superversal Catastrophes.

**Thread the Interstice (Leave Subversal)** — If **Anchor** present, you leave clean. Otherwise *Overcome* vs **Average (+1) + Breadcrumbs** (0–3).  
- **Tie:** **Temporal Slip** (minutes–hours). **Fail:** **Adjacent Egress** (neighbor zone).

## 10) Subversal/Superversal Resources
- **Substrate Key** (aspect/gear): spend an invoke to count as **Known Micro‑Gate**.  
- **Breadcrumbs (0–3)**: Each scene you act in a Subversal layer without Survey, set +1; apply to **Thread the Interstice** diff. Clear on exit.  
- **Meta Handle** (ship core stunt): grants permission; once/session you may re‑roll a failed **Ascend** at the cost of **Strain 1**.

## 11) Special Aspects
- **Myth‑Logic Tax:** “Speak in Titles,” “Pay with Names,” “No Iron,” etc. Compels gate progress. Clear with obedience, sacrifice, or *Myth Scrip* gear.
- **Meta Glare:** While in Superversal space, attempts at stealth face **+2 passive opposition**; create cover by crafting **Title Veils** or bribes.

# PART V — SHIP & CREW OPTIONS

## 12) Ship as Extra (template)
**Aspects:**  
- High Concept: *Aleph‑Capable Courier*  
- Trouble: *Temperamental Drive Coils*  
- Signature: *Causal Dampers Online*

**Skills (ship dice or flat bonuses):** Pilot, Sensors, Engineering at +2/+1 as your table prefers.  
**Stunts (pick 2):**
- **Redundant Radiators:** Once/scene, cancel **Strain 1**.  
- **Aleph Sniffer:** +2 to **Create Advantage** for **Beacon Lock** when you have time.  
- **Causal Dampers:** When **Drift** would be marked, reduce the boxes by 1 (min 0).  
- **Hot‑Swap Coils:** On a failed jump, spend a fate point to downgrade **Catastrophe → Mishap**.

**Tracks:** **Strain [ ][ ][ ]**; Consequences: Mild/Moderate/Severe (rename to taste).

## 13) Crew Stunts (examples)
- **Aleph Cartographer:** +2 to **Overcome** when identifying Gates or reading route ads.  
- **Law Whisperer:** Once/scene, turn a **Glitch** into a Boost.  
- **Beacon Hacker:** Treat a successful **Create Advantage: Beacon Lock** as having +1 extra free invoke (cap 4).  
- **Causal Diver:** When **Temporal Slip** hits, take a free **Flashback** (one action) to prep for it.

## 14) Intel & Salvage (economy)
- **Intel (Boost/Aspect):** Spend to: ignore one **Glitch**, get +2 on **Next Route** to the same locale, or clear **Drift 1**.  
- **Salvage (Aspect with invokes):** Spend two invokes to **stabilize a Gate** (gives **Gate Quality** with 1 free invoke for your crew).

# APPENDIX A — DIEGETIC TECH (use as lore/Aspects)

## A1) Composite Address (machine view)
- **LSP:v6** (Law‑Space): vector of dimensionless constants & symmetry flags; route by **ΔL**.  
- **ICF** (Initial‑Condition Fingerprint): minimal program root (Merkle hash).  
- **TOP** (Topology & Phase): homology sketch; compactification flags.  
- **CGA** (Causal Graph Anchor): spectral hash of a canonical event downsample.

**Pilot Alias Example:** `LSP:v6/ICF:af3c…/TOP:h1/CGAs:σ92/DM:caustic/prec:12`

## A2) Aleph Beacons
Dark‑sector couplers (axions, dark photons, ultralight waves). Each has a **spectral code** (public key). **Beacon Lock** is your usable fix quality.

## A3) Law‑Gradient Routing (LGR)
A* over law‑space graph; heuristics weight ΔL, risk, decay. Gate ads (SLA) behave like BGP route announcements—ripe for intrigue.

## A4) Safety
ECC on packets; version negotiation; zero‑knowledge gate proofs; **Black‑Sky Mode** = dead‑reckon by LSP + CGA when beacons fail.

# ONE‑PAGE REFERENCE (printable)

1) **Prep**
- **Composite Address** (Create Advantage, Fair +2) → 1–2 free invokes.
- **Beacon Lock** (Create Advantage, Good +3; halos mod) → each invoke = 1 Lock (cap 4).
- Optional: **Tune Halo** (Overcome, Good +3).

2) **Route**
- Pick **Path**: Safe (Fair +2) / Standard (Good +3) / Risky (Great +4).
- Apply **ΔL** (+1/step), **Gate** ads (–2 with quality), spend **Lock** invokes (–2 each).

3) **Jump** (Overcome via Pilot/Lore)
- **Success:** Arrive. **Style:** +**Intel** Boost.
- **Tie:** Arrive with **Glitch** or mark **Strain** per path.
- **Fail:** **Mishap**/**Catastrophe** (per path).

4) **Modes**
- **Subversal** (Great +4): pocket layers; **Anchor**, **Breadcrumbs**.  
- **Superversal** (Superb +5): meta layer; **Meta Glare**, clear **Drift** on success.

5) **Conditions**
- **Glitch** (sticky 1) • **Drift [ ][ ]** (passive +1/box) • **Strain [ ][ ][ ]** (ship).

6) **Spendables**
- **Intel:** ignore a Glitch / +2 on next route / clear 1 Drift.  
- **Salvage:** stabilize a gate (gives **Gate Quality** with 1 free invoke).
