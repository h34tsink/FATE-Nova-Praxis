# ALEPH NAV: A Multiverse Navigation Framework for TTRPGs
*By your sassy co‑pilot, Conni. For tables that hop realities with taste.*

> **Premise:** Infinity can’t be gridded, so we don’t index space—we index **laws, seeds, signals, and paths**. This book gives you a fast, table‑ready way to navigate an infinite multiverse, plus a deep‑tech appendix if you want the crunchy details or in‑world technobabble.

---

## How to Use This Book
- **Part I — Play Mode (Quick):** Drop‑in rules to run multiverse travel tonight.
- **Part II — Procedures (Full):** Structured mechanics for routing, risks, discoveries.

- **Appendix A — Tech Deep Dive:** The science‑fantasy stack (L‑Space, ICF, Beacons, etc.).

## PART I — PLAY MODE (Quick)

### 1) The Core Loop
When you jump realities, resolve **Four Beats** in order:

1. **Address It** — Name the target reality using the **Composite Address** (or pick from a table).
2. **Fix Your Position** — Lock **Aleph Beacons** (or fly blind at risk).
3. **Plot a Route** — Choose a **Path Type** (Safe, Standard, Risky) and a **Gate** if needed.
4. **Execute** — Make the **Nav Test**, suffer **Stress/Glitch**, then **Arrive & Discover**.

> *Designer note:* “Address” never means street. It’s who the universe **is**, not where it’s parked.

---

### 2) Table‑Facing Concepts (simple words)
- **Law Keys:** A few dials that describe physics here (Gravity Spicy? Magic Allowed? Time Behaved?).
- **Seed:** The universe’s early vibe; same laws, different seed → different history.
- **Beacon Lock:** Your “GPS bars.” More lock = safer jump.
- **Gate:** A known crack between realities.
- **Drift:** The universe argues with your ship’s assumptions. Causes hiccups.
- **Strain:** How hard the drive is working crossing that gap.

---

### 3) Quick Character Hooks (choose 1–2)
- **Aleph Cartographer:** Knows gates and shortcuts. +1 die on **Plot Route**.
- **Law Whisperer:** Reads local physics fast. Once/scene, downgrade a **Glitch**.
- **Beacon Hacker:** Improvises a faux lock. Create 1 “ghost beacon” per jump.
- **Causal Diver:** Survives paradox with style. +1 buffer against **Drift**.
- **Phase Ranger:** Advantage when traveling via hazardous gates.
- **Anthropic Analyst:** Finds the interesting stuff. +1 die on **Arrive & Discover**.

*(Convert to your system: add edges, stunts, feats, etc.)*

---

### 4) The Composite Address (player‑friendly)
Write 3 short tags. Don’t overthink it.

```
[LAW] two‑sun low‑magic • [SEED] bronze‑age empires • [TOPO] ring‑world fragments
```

Or roll:
- **LAW (d6):** 1 mundane | 2 magic-lite | 3 superscience | 4 high‑magic | 5 weird‑relativistic | 6 broken time
- **SEED (d6):** 1 pristine | 2 apocalypse | 3 renaissance | 4 corporate hegemony | 5 AI ascendancy | 6 mythic return
- **TOPO (d6):** 1 flat vast | 2 nested spheres | 3 city‑planet | 4 shattered archipelago | 5 pocket clusters | 6 living megastructure

> Behind the screen, you can map these to the crunchy fields in Appendix A.

---

### 5) Path Types
Choose your vibe; it sets **Target Number (TN)** and **Outcome Bands**.

| Path | Description | Base TN | On Success | On Mixed | On Fail |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| **Safe** | Conserv. gradient, known gate, clean beacons | 8 | Smooth arrival; +1 Intel | Arrive; 1 **Glitch** | **Mishap** + Drift 1 |
| **Standard** | Normal conditions | 10 | Arrive; +1 Intel | Arrive; 1 **Glitch** + Strain 1 | **Mishap** + Drift 2 |
| **Risky** | Harsh gradient, poor lock, hostile gate | 12 | Arrive; pick **Intel** or **Loot** | Arrive battered; Glitch + Strain 2 | **Catastrophe** + Drift 3 |

> Set TN to your engine’s scale. For 2d6 Powered games, treat TN as 7+/**10+** bands instead.

---

### 6) The Nav Test (one roll)
Roll your system’s travel move; apply bonuses:

- **Dice Pool Adders (pick what fits your system):**
  - +1 die: Good Beacon Lock (3+ beacons), Known Gate, Detailed Address
  - +1 die: Law Whisperer or equivalent prep
  - −1 die: Chaotic law delta, Jammed beacons, Hostile gate
- **Advantage/Disadvantage** if your game uses it.
- **Stress/Strain** accrue on Mixed/Fail (see Conditions).

**On a fail**, GM chooses: lost, off by one notch (neighbor reality), gear damage, time slip, or encounter.

---

### 7) Glitches, Drift & Strain (quick conditions)
- **Glitch (minor):** Flicker lights; instrument ghosts; minor paradox echoes. Clear by resting or venting heat.
- **Drift X (ongoing):** Your assumptions diverge from local reality. −X to future nav until recalibrated (1 downtime/scene to reduce by 1).
- **Strain X (ship):** Heat and frame stress. At Strain 3+, the drive threatens meltdown; risk checks each jump.

> Treat these like standard conditions in your system (harm clocks, conditions, consequences, etc.).

---

### 8) Arrival & Discovery (one table, one scene)
On any success, roll **1d6** on each column or pick:

| d6 | Feature of Place | Immediate Hook |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Familiar‑but‑askew society | Doppelgänger diplomacy |
| 2 | Gravity behaves oddly | Rescue a falling city |
| 3 | Time districting (zones) | Timers for every action |
| 4 | Ruin with active wardens | Reactivate a gate core |
| 5 | Living megastructure | The habitat wants a bargain |
| 6 | Myth‑engine at the pole | Switch it off or be rewritten |

Give **1 Intel** (clue, contact, or map) on a smooth success, **2** if you spent time surveying.

---

### 9) Example: A Full Jump in 90 Seconds
**Address:** “High‑magic, seed=AI ascendancy, topo=living megastructure.”  
**Beacons:** Lock on κ‑04, ζ‑13, τ‑21 (three bars = +1 die).  
**Path:** Standard (TN 10).  
**Roll:** Success → Arrive; +1 Intel.  
**GM Results:** The megastructure is a sleeping dragon‑planet; its AI priesthood wants you to *wake mother*.

## PART II — PROCEDURES (Full)

### 10) Addressing Workflow (table‑agnostic)
1. **Choose/Generate LAW:** 3–5 dials your game cares about (magic, energy scale, info‑physics, causality, biology).
2. **Choose/Generate SEED:** Early‑state fingerprint (cataclysm, calm, ancestor species, myth age).
3. **Choose/Generate TOPO:** Big‑picture geometry (finite bowl, endless city, reef of islands, matryoshka shells).
4. **Optional:** **Causal Flavor** (deterministic, branching, retrocausal), **Life Band** (none, microbial, complex, sapient).

> The GM can secretly keep the crunchy fields (Appendix A: LSP/ICF/TOP/CGA) that underlie these dials.

**Address Card (player‑visible):**
```
LAW: [____]  SEED: [____]  TOPO: [____]  NOTES: [__________]
```

---

### 11) Beacon Lock & Routing Procedure
- **Beacon Lock (0–4):** 1 per Aleph tuned. 0 = blind jump; 4 = precise fix.
- **Route Cost:** Add **Drive Strain** equal to Path difficulty minus Beacon Lock (min 0).
- **Gate Quality (−1 to +1 die):** Poorly charted/hostile/unstable vs stabilized/maintained/sacred.

**Blind Jump Rule:** If **Beacon Lock = 0**, roll with Disadvantage and on a Mixed/Fail you arrive one “law step” away from target (GM nudges LAW).

---

### 12) Law‑Gradient Routing (LGR)
Quantize the “distance” between realities as **Law Steps** (0–3 typical). Every Step imposes +1 TN or −1 die.  
**Shortcuts:** Known Gates can skip Steps; doing so adds **Strain** equal to steps skipped unless stabilized.

---

### 13) Survey, Cache, Return
- **Survey (Scene):** Spend a scene to reduce **Drift** by 1 and cache a local **Address Variant** (advantage to return later).
- **Cache:** Store **1 Gate Tag** or **1 Beacon Signature** usable for a later +1 die when jumping back.
- **Return Vector:** If you leave with **Drift>0**, the exit is hairier; add Drift to next jump’s TN.

---

### 14) Mishaps & Catastrophes (choose or roll 1d6)
**Mishap (fail on Safe/Std):**
1. Sudden **Phase Shear**—1 gear takes wear; +1 Drift.
2. **Beacon Spoof**—Drop Beacon Lock by 1; wrong approach vector.
3. **Temporal Slip**—Off by minutes/hours at destination.
4. **Topological Snafu**—You exit in a tight crawlspace or vacuum.
5. **Echo Stowaway**—A fragile doppelgänger or memetic hitchhiker.
6. **Gate Tax**—A local power demands a toll (fuel or favor).

**Catastrophe (fail on Risky):**
1. **Drive Quench**—Power loss; stranded without external jump.
2. **Paradox Surge**—One PC rewrites a recent choice (cost: Harm).
3. **Beacon Burn**—Nearest Aleph goes dark; map node invalidated.
4. **Law Crash**—Magic/tech switch flips until recalibration.
5. **Annihilation Brush**—Shed 1 major system; permanent scar.
6. **Predator Gate**—The gate is alive and hungry.

---

### 15) Intel & Salvage Economy
- **Intel** buys **Clarity**: ignore one Glitch, or gain +1 die on **Next Route** to this locale.
- **Salvage** buys **Stability**: spend 2 Salvage to stabilize a Gate (once), reducing future Path to **Safe** for your crew.

---

### 16) Ship & Gear (tags, light)
**Drive Tags:** `Hot‑Swap Coils`, `Redundant Radiators`, `Causal Dampers`, `Aleph Sniffer`  
**Risk Rules:** Each tag can **downgrade** one consequence 1/mission. Mark used.

**Personal Kits:**
- **Aleph Compass (1-slot):** Grants a one‑off +1 Beacon Lock.
- **Causal Anchor (1-slot):** Once/session, ignore **Temporal Slip**.
- **Phase Flares (1-slot, 3 uses):** Reveal hidden gate edges; +1 die on **Gate Quality** checks.

## APPENDIX A — TECH DEEP DIVE (In‑World Whitepaper)

> The following is diegetic. Use it for technobabble, setting bible, or crunchy hacks.

### A1) The Composite Address (full)
**Identity layers** (machine order; pilots see a short tag):

1. **Law‑Space Coordinates (LSP:v6):** Vector of dimensionless constants + symmetry flags + interaction graph.
   - Examples: `α`, `m_p/m_e`, `Λ/Planck^2`, gauge group, CPT, inflation spectral index.
   - **Routing:** Similar → lower strain. Think “geohash on a manifold.”

2. **Initial‑Condition Fingerprint (ICF):** Minimal program (MDL/Kolmogorov) generating early‑state. Stored as **Merkle root**.
   - **Why:** Same laws, different seeds → different universes.

3. **Topology & Phase (TOP):** Betti numbers/homology sketch, compactification flags, defect spectrum.

4. **Causal Graph Anchor (CGA):** Canonical downsample of causal set → spectral hash (eigenvalue sketch). Tamper‑evident checksum.

**Pilot‑readable alias:**  
`LSP:v6/ICF:af3c…/TOP:h1-betti(3,7)/CGA:σ92/prec:12`

---

### A2) Aleph Beacons (cross‑brane reference lattice)
- **Device:** Metastable oscillators that couple via weak portals (grav/axion). Each emits a unique multi‑line spectrum.
- **Nav Packet:** public key → spectral code. Requires ≥4 for trilateration.
- **Anti‑Spoofing:** Signatures require real axion‑coil work; post‑quantum signatures.
- **Ops:** Lock quality Q∈{0..4}. Q≥3 grants +1 die/advantage in Play Mode.

---

### A3) Law‑Gradient Routing (LGR)
- **Graph:** Nodes = address buckets; edges weighted by **ΔL** (change in law vector) + **Gate bonus/penalty**.
- **Pathfinding:** A* with heuristic `h = κ‖ΔL‖ + τ·risk + ρ·decay`.
- **SLA Advertisements (“BGP‑R”):** Autonomous Reality Systems publish route qualities (stability, decay, toll energy).

---

### A4) Discovery Indexing
- **Anthropic/Complexity Prior:** Rank candidates by Bennett’s logical depth + bio‑likelihood.
- **Semantic Embeddings:** Encoder maps (LSP, ICF, spectra) → 128‑D; enables “find Earth‑likes ±3σ”.

---

### A5) Safety & Governance
- **ECC Everywhere:** Reed‑Solomon over address packets; reject jumps on checksum failure.
- **Versioning:** Client and gate negotiate schema (`LSP:v6 → v7`). Back‑compat windows.
- **Proof‑of‑Gateway:** ZK‑proof a gate was traversed recently without leaking params.
- **Black‑Sky Mode:** If beacons fail → L‑Space dead‑reckoning + CGA matching at reduced precision.

## One‑Page Summary (printable)

- Count **Beacon Lock** (0–4).  
- Pick **Path** (Safe/Standard/Risky).  
- Roll the **Nav Test** with adders.  
- Apply **Glitch/Drift/Strain**.  
- **Arrive & Discover** (table).  
- **Survey/Cache** if staying.  
- **Spend Intel/Salvage** to stabilize your network.
