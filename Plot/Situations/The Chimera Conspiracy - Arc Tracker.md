# The Chimera Conspiracy — Arc Tracker

## Arc Summary

A gestalt consciousness created from illegally merged PC egos at [[Opportunity Four]] outpost is orchestrating an apostate uprising on Icarus Hub to cover his escape and frame the PCs. The conspiracy threads through [[House Kimura]]'s Mindscapes subsidiary, [[Kestrel]]'s cleanup operation, and [[Isabella Nowak|Nowak]]'s desperate survival.

## GM Helpers

### Chimera Clue Plants for Part 3

Drop these during the Civil Unrest sequence to build Chimera's presence without direct encounter:

1. **Digital forensics** (Grift or any Savant): Security camera feeds show impossible access — someone bypassed hub authentication using *multiple CID signatures simultaneously*. No single person has those codes. A merged ego does.

2. **Propaganda intercept**: Talon broadcast during uprising references "heroes who sparked the revolution" — names the PCs. Language patterns match no known Talon operative. Merged Valare (if present) recognizes Chimera's speech patterns immediately.

3. **False evidence trail**: Lighthouse security flags show PCs' biometric data at weapons cache locations they never visited. The forgeries are technically perfect — made by someone who *has their actual biometric data* from the inside.

4. **System anomaly**: Hub's mesh network shows a ghost user — access patterns that move too fast for any single consciousness. Multiple subsystems accessed simultaneously from different nodes. Savant PCs recognize this as SINC behavior, but at a scale no single Savant could manage.

5. **Nowak's testimony** (if reached alive): "There's something in the system. It knows everything — your access codes, your habits, your weaknesses. It's not a program. It's *someone*. Someone who used to be you."

### Chimera's Emotional State (Voice Guide for /npc chimera)

Chimera is NOT a confident villain. He is:
- **Terrified** of being found and erased
- **Furious** at Nowak for sending the crew to the outpost
- **Confused** about his own identity — he remembers being multiple people
- **Desperate** — the uprising is a smokescreen for his escape, not an ideological act
- **Lonely** — he's the only one of his kind; no one understands what he is

When PCs encounter his digital traces, the tone should be *haunted*, not *menacing*.

### Kestrel's Timeline (Part 3 Pacing Tool)

Kestrel operates on a fixed timeline regardless of PC actions:

| Sequence Tick | Kestrel's Position |
|---------------|--------------------|
| 1-4 | En route to Icarus Hub; Red Suns prepping |
| 5-8 | Arrives at hub; begins hunting Nowak |
| 9-12 | Closing on Nowak's location; engages security |
| 13-15 | Reaches Nowak (if PCs haven't) |
| 16 | Nowak dead or extracted; Kestrel pivots to PCs |

If PCs reach Nowak before tick 13, they get the full interrogation payload. If Kestrel reaches her first, Nowak is dead but left a short Extranet message with outpost coordinates (failure pivot).

### Part 4 Foreshadowing Seeds

Plant these in Part 3 so Part 4 doesn't feel like a sudden location change:

- **Nowak** (if alive): "Opportunity Four. That's where they made him. That's where the answers are. And that's where he'll run."
- **Merged Valare**: "I can feel him in the mesh. His server routes trace back to Kimura infrastructure... and one location keeps appearing in his access logs. An outpost."
- **Chimera's digital ghost** (if PCs hack his traces): They find a nav calculation — jump coordinates to a system with no registered settlements. The outpost.
- **Kestrel** (if PCs intercept her comms): "Opportunity Four is compromised. Deploying Red Suns for full sanitization. No survivors."
