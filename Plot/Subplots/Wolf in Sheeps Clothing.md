# Wolf in Sheep's Clothing

*A murder mystery on [[Locations/Sol System/Ansari Station|Ansari Station]] that feeds directly into [[Plot/Subplots/The Ashford Mandate|The Ashford Mandate]].*

---

## The Situation

[[Locations/Sol System/Ansari Station|Ansari Station]] has been harried by a string of murders. Five victims over three months — all seemingly unconnected. Different occupations, different social circles, different causes of death. Station security has classified them as unrelated incidents. They are wrong.

The victims were killed to cover up the theft of **Jinzhan quarantine override codes** — codes that [[Factions/Houses/House Jinzhan|House Jinzhan]] has publicly denied exist. These codes can grant total control of the ships and kill-satellites maintaining the [[Glossary/Technophage|Earth quarantine]]. In the right hands, they could temporarily disable the entire defense grid — long enough for a ship to reach the surface and return.

Jinzhan knows about the murders. Jinzhan knows about the theft. Jinzhan is covering it up, because admitting the codes exist means admitting there is a backdoor into the most critical defense system in human history.

[[Characters/Named NPCs/Carmen Ashford|Carmen Ashford]] wants these codes so she can temporarily disable the Earth defense systems and land a ship on the planet to acquire live samples of the [[Glossary/Technophage|Technophage]].

---

Each victim had access to a piece of the override code architecture — no single person held the complete set. Carmen's procurement cell had to acquire fragments from multiple sources and kill each one to prevent a trail.

## The Killer

Carmen's procurement cell on Ansari is run by **Sable Mori** — a former Jinzhan intelligence operative who defected after seeing classified data about the quarantine's actual effectiveness (or lack thereof). Mori is a true believer in Carmen's vision. She is methodical, patient, and deeply uncomfortable with what she's doing.

## Jinzhan's Cover-Up

Jinzhan knows. Their internal security division — **Section Nine** — has identified the pattern and connected at least three of the five deaths. But they cannot go public because:

1. **The codes aren't supposed to exist.** Publicly, the quarantine grid has no master override. Admitting override codes are real means admitting the entire Earth defense system has a single point of failure.
2. **Political fallout.** [[Factions/Houses/House Dalianis|Dalianis]] manufactures most of the quarantine fleet hardware. If Dalianis learns that Jinzhan built backdoor overrides into ships Dalianis paid for, the alliance fractures.
3. **The Protectorate would intervene.** Override codes for the quarantine grid would be classified as a Coalition-level security threat. The [[Glossary/Protectorate|Protectorate]] would seize control of the investigation, and Jinzhan would lose autonomy over their own defense systems.

So Jinzhan is running a quiet internal investigation through Section Nine while publicly denying everything. They have one operative on Ansari — **Agent Kessler** — working the case alone, without backup, with orders to recover the codes and eliminate anyone who knows about them.

## How the PCs Get Involved

### Hook A — Briar's Network

[[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar]] knew "Comet" (victim #4) — they ran in the same Apostate data-broker circles on Ansari. When Comet turns up dead, Briar asks the PCs to look into it. She doesn't believe the "natural causes" ruling. She's right, and the thread leads to Carmen's procurement cell.

### Hook B — Dalianis Intelligence

[[Characters/Players/Aeddarius Crucial/Aeddarius Crucial - FATE Character|Aeddarius]] receives a quiet Dalianis intelligence briefing flagging unusual Jinzhan activity on Ansari — Section Nine deploying an operative without notifying Coalition joint security. Dalianis wants to know what Jinzhan is hiding. Aeddarius is positioned to investigate.

### Hook C — The Missing Analyst

Dr. Ren Aubrey (victim #5) had a family member or colleague who reaches out to the PCs for help finding him. A straightforward missing-person job that turns into something much worse when the trail leads to the other four deaths.

### Hook D — Grift Stumbles Into It

[[Characters/Players/Dustin (Grift) Halloway/Dustin Halloway - FATE Character|Grift]] is on Ansari for an unrelated job and notices anomalies in station security logs — records that have been altered, surveillance gaps that are too clean to be accidental. His *Curiosity Kills More Than Cats* takes over.

## Resolution Paths

### Path 1 — Stop the Procurement

The PCs identify Sable Mori, confront or capture her, and recover the override codes before Carmen can use them. Jinzhan's secret stays contained — but Kessler may try to silence the PCs to protect the cover-up. The PCs now know the codes exist, which makes them targets.

### Path 2 — The Codes Are Already Gone

By the time the PCs arrive, Mori has already transmitted the codes to Carmen. The murders were the cover for a job that's already done. The PCs can still catch Mori, but the damage is done — Carmen now has the means to reach Earth. The question becomes: do the PCs race to stop her before she uses them?

### Path 3 — Expose Jinzhan

The PCs leak the existence of the override codes to the [[Glossary/Protectorate|Protectorate]] or to [[Factions/Houses/House Dalianis|Dalianis]]. This triggers a political crisis — Jinzhan's quarantine authority is challenged, the Dalianis-Jinzhan alliance fractures, and the entire Earth defense grid comes under Coalition review. Carmen's window may close, but the political fallout creates chaos that other factions exploit.

### Path 4 — Let It Happen

The PCs learn about Carmen's plan (from Mori, from the codes, from the trail) and decide to let it play out — either because they're curious, because they've been paid not to interfere, or because they think Carmen might be right. This accelerates [[Plot/Subplots/The Ashford Mandate|The Ashford Mandate]] directly.

## Tone

Murder mystery crossed with espionage thriller. The bodies are the hook, the codes are the MacGuffin, and the real story is the conspiracy — Jinzhan hiding a backdoor to humanity's last defense, and a woman who wants to use it to walk into hell and bring back a piece of it.
