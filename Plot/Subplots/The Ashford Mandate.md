# The Ashford Mandate

*An epic-scale background situation — interweave across sessions or run as a concentrated arc.*

---

## The Premise

**Carmen Ashford** is a brilliant engineer and former [[Glossary/Technophage|Technophage]] researcher who had a catastrophic lab accident during an experiment. She spent three days in a coma. When she woke up, she was different.

She claims she experienced a vision — not religious, not mystical, but *architectural*. She saw the heat death of the universe as an engineering problem, and she saw the solution. Every step. Every material requirement. Every timeline. She woke up with a blueprint burned into her mind that she believes will save humanity from extinction on a cosmic scale.

She is either a prophet, a genius, or a brain-damaged woman chasing a coma hallucination. The terrifying part: her math checks out.

---

## Carmen's Plan

### Phase 1 — The Swarm

Build auto-replicating nano swarms capable of constructing von Neumann self-replicating probes. This requires reverse-engineering [[Glossary/Technophage|Technophage]] technology — the same grey goo catastrophe that nearly destroyed Earth and forced the [[Glossary/Exodus|Exodus]].

**The problem:** Self-replicating nanomachines are the most illegal technology in known space. The Technophage is humanity's deepest trauma. Anyone caught developing replicating nanotech faces summary execution by the [[Glossary/Protectorate|Protectorate]]. Carmen needs a live Technophage sample to reverse-engineer — and the only source is quarantined Earth, defended by [[Factions/Houses/House Jinzhan|Jinzhan]] ships and kill-satellites. To reach the surface, she first needs to acquire Jinzhan quarantine override codes — the subject of [[Plot/Subplots/Wolf in Sheeps Clothing|Wolf in Sheep's Clothing]].

### Phase 2 — The Ring

Deploy the probes to construct **solar collector rings** around Sol — massive orbital structures that harvest energy directly from the sun at a scale that dwarfs anything the Coalition has built. The power output would be sufficient to fuel interstellar-scale engineering.

**The problem:** Building the rings requires raw material on a planetary scale. Carmen has identified the source: **Mercury**. Specifically, half its mass — consumed by the swarm, refined into ring components, and assembled in solar orbit. Mercury has settlements. People live there. The [[Locations/Mercurian Tunnel System|Mercurian Tunnel System]] is home to thousands of Apostates, including [[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar "Silvermist" Blackwood]].

### Phase 3 — The Seeds

Once the rings are operational and generating power at stellar scale, deploy a second wave of probes outward — seeding new star systems with the same infrastructure. Eventually: construct new planets. Terraform them. Give humanity a path to survive the slow death of the universe.

**The timeline:** Centuries. Carmen knows she won't live to see it finish, even with resleeving. She doesn't care. The plan is bigger than one lifetime.

### Personality

Carmen is not a cackling villain. She's patient, empathetic, and genuinely believes she's doing the most important work in human history. She will explain her plan calmly to anyone who asks, because she believes that anyone rational will agree with her once they see the numbers.

She is also willing to kill everyone on Mercury to do it.

That's not cruelty — it's triage. In her framework, the lives on Mercury weigh against the survival of every human being who will ever exist. The math is clear. She's sorry about it. She'll lose sleep over it. She'll do it anyway.

**Voice:** Warm, precise, academic. She talks about planetary-scale destruction the way a surgeon talks about amputation — regrettable, necessary, performed with care. She never raises her voice. She never threatens. She simply explains what's going to happen and why it has to.

## The True Believers

Carmen doesn't work alone. Her vision has attracted a small but devoted following — engineers, scientists, and idealists who've seen her numbers and been convinced.

**The Ashford Circle** is not a cult, though it functions like one. Members are recruited through academic channels, private research networks, and the deep Extranet. They believe they are the most important people alive — the team that will save the species. Some of them are right. Some of them are dangerous.

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Carmen Ashford** | Architect. The vision and the math. |
| **Procurement Cell** | Black-market operatives acquiring Technophage samples and restricted materials. 3-5 members, compartmentalized, don't know the full plan. |
| **Research Cell** | Engineers and nanobiologists working on replicator design in a hidden lab. 4-6 members, fully briefed, true believers. |
| **Logistics Cell** | Shell companies, funding networks, transport. 2-3 members, partially briefed. |
| **The Convert** | A highly placed Coalition official or House operative who has seen the math and quietly redirects resources. Identity unknown to most Circle members. |

## How the PCs Encounter This

Carmen's plan intersects with the campaign through **escalating contact points** — each one raising the stakes and forcing harder questions.

### Tier 1 — Rumors and Acquisitions

The PCs hear about someone trying to acquire a Technophage sample on the black market. This is terrifying — the Technophage is the bogeyman of the Nova Praxis era. Anyone chasing a live sample is either insane or building a weapon.

**Hooks:**
- [[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar]] hears about unusual procurement requests moving through Apostate channels — restricted nano-fabrication equipment, containment systems rated for grey goo, and a sealed bio-sample with a classification she's never seen
- A Protectorate alert crosses [[Characters/Players/Aeddarius Crucial/Aeddarius Crucial - FATE Character|Aeddarius]]' Dalianis intelligence feed — someone is probing Coalition archives for Technophage containment protocols
- [[Characters/Players/Kallius/Kallius - FATE Character|Kallius]] encounters a mnemonic fragment from a scientist whose memories were partially wiped — the fragment contains equations he doesn't understand but that reference "solar-scale energy harvesting"

**Tone:** Conspiracy thriller. Something bad is being built. Nobody knows what yet.

### Tier 2 — The Override Codes (Wolf in Sheep's Clothing)

Before Carmen can acquire a Technophage sample, she needs to get past the [[Glossary/Technophage|Earth quarantine]] — [[Factions/Houses/House Jinzhan|Jinzhan]] ships and kill-satellites that will destroy anything approaching the surface. Her procurement cell on [[Locations/Sol System/Ansari Station|Ansari Station]] has been killing Jinzhan personnel to steal fragments of override codes that Jinzhan publicly denies exist.

This is the full [[Plot/Subplots/Wolf in Sheeps Clothing|Wolf in Sheep's Clothing]] subplot — a murder mystery that feeds directly into Carmen's plan. The PCs can encounter it through Briar's network, Dalianis intelligence, a missing-person job, or Grift's curiosity. See that document for full details.

**Decision point:** Do the PCs stop the procurement, expose Jinzhan's cover-up, or let the codes reach Carmen?

### Tier 3 — The Sample

If Carmen acquires the override codes (or already has them by the time the PCs get involved), she uses them to temporarily disable the quarantine grid and land a ship on Earth's surface. She retrieves live Technophage samples and returns to her hidden research facility in the asteroid belt.

The PCs may intercept the Earth run, investigate the facility after the fact, or be hired to track the source of a Technophage sample that has appeared on the black market (Carmen's research cell acquiring additional material).

If they investigate, the trail leads to shell companies, dead drops, and eventually the research facility itself. Inside: Carmen's research cell, working on replicator prototypes. The work is elegant, controlled, and horrifying.

**Tone:** Horror. The Technophage is alive in a lab, being studied by people who think they can control it. The PCs have seen what happens when someone thinks they can control the Technophage.

### Tier 4 — The Revelation

The PCs meet Carmen. She explains everything — calmly, rationally, with supporting data. She shows them the projections. She shows them the timeline. She shows them what happens if she doesn't act: the slow death of every star, every civilization, every possibility. She's not asking for permission. She's offering them a chance to be part of the solution.

And then she shows them Mercury.

**Decision point:** Carmen's plan requires consuming half of Mercury's mass. The Mercurian Tunnel System — home to Apostate communities, to Briar Blackwood, to thousands of people — will be destroyed. Carmen has relocation plans. They are detailed and humane and completely inadequate for the scale of displacement involved.

**Tone:** Moral reckoning. Carmen is not wrong about the science. She may not be wrong about the necessity. But she's asking them to accept the destruction of a world to save a species that won't benefit for centuries. Is that trade worth making? Who gets to decide?

### Tier 5 — The Clock

If the PCs don't stop Carmen, the swarm launches. If they do stop her, the question lingers: was she right? Was the vision real? Did they just doom humanity to save Mercury?

**No clean answer.** This is the point. The PCs can destroy the research, kill Carmen, negotiate a compromise (smaller scale, different material source), or let her proceed. Every choice has consequences that echo for the rest of the campaign.

## Intersections with Other Arcs

### The Standard Deviation

Carmen's nano-replicator research and [[Plot/Subplots/The Standard Deviation|Holt's]] ego-engineering experiments are both products of the same pre-Singularity scientific culture — the generation that believed human extinction was an engineering problem with an engineering solution. They don't know each other, but they'd understand each other. If Carmen discovers the Standard Deviation archive and the 194 remaining sleepers, she might try to recruit engineered egos as operators for her swarm deployment.

### Briar Blackwood

Carmen's plan directly threatens Briar's home. When Briar finds out — and she will — it becomes the most personal fight of her life. This transforms Briar from job broker to active ally with her own agenda: stop the Mercury harvest at any cost. That's a massive shift in her relationship with the PCs, especially Grift.

### Chimera

A gestalt consciousness with faction-level intelligence and no moral anchor, learning that someone is building self-replicating probes capable of constructing planets. Chimera might try to acquire the technology. Chimera might try to acquire Carmen. Or Chimera might try to become the swarm's operating intelligence — a mind that can think at stellar scale, piloting a machine that builds worlds.

---

## The Question

Carmen Ashford is offering to save humanity from the heat death of the universe at the cost of one small planet and the illegal resurrection of the most dangerous technology ever created.

Is she right?

The campaign doesn't need to answer that. The players do.
