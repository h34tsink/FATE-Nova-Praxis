The Setting in 60 Seconds

Nova Praxis is a transhuman sci-fi RPG set in a future where humanity fled Earth after a nanotech plague — the Technophage — consumed it. Survivors live across a star-spanning Coalition ruled by six corporate Houses locked in a cold war called the Shadow War.

The biggest revolution: Apotheosis. Your mind — your ego — can be backed up, transferred to a new body (a sleeve), or run as pure software. Death is no longer permanent. But this raises hard questions about identity, humanity, and who gets to decide what "human" means.

Pure

Born human, still in your original body. No Apotheosis. No backup. When you die, you're gone. Greater willpower, +2 Refresh.

Sleeved

Your ego has been digitized. You inhabit a body — biosleeve or cybersleeve — that can be swapped. Backed up. Resleeved.

SIM

Pure software. No body. You live in virtual space, perceive the physical world through mesh sensors, and act through drones.

Step 1

Aspects

You get 5 Aspects — one per letter A through E. Each is a phrase (3–8 words) that defines who your character is. Good Aspects create both advantages AND complications.

Golden Rule: Every Aspect should be two-edged — something that makes you great AND something that can bite you. "Legionnaire, Born and Raised" gives combat bonuses and can be compelled when your military loyalty creates problems.

The Aspect Alphabet

Examples: "I'll make captain one day!" • Obsessed with Becoming a Sensie Star • Walking the Long Road to Ascension
B
Examples: "I'd lay down my life for the Coalition" • Suspicious of Technology and the Horrors it Can Unleash

How Aspects Work at the Table

Invoking (You spend 1 Fate Point)

+2 bonus to a roll, OR reroll entirely. You say how the Aspect applies — the GM can't stop you unless it makes no sense.

Compelling (You earn 1 Fate Point)

The GM offers you a complication tied to an Aspect. Accept it → you get 1 FP and the complication happens. Refuse it → spend 1 FP to avoid it.

Scope Rule: You can only invoke one of each type of Aspect per roll (one character Aspect, one scene Aspect, one gear Aspect, etc.). Having three Aspects that all do the same thing wastes Aspects — spread them out.

Step 2

Choose Your State

This is one of the most identity-defining choices. Your State determines how your physical skills work, what free Stunts you get, and how you experience the world.

Once you go Apotheosized, you can never go back to Pure. Pure → Sleeved or SIM is a one-way door. Sleeved ↔ SIM can change during play via a resurrection chamber.
Pure

Original biological body. No backup. Heightened willpower and humanity.

+ Refresh +2
+ 8 Physical Ranks
+ Free: Carpe Diem Stunt
+ Bonus Aspect (body trait)
+ +1 to any non-Physical Skill
– Death is permanent (no backup)
Sleeved

Apotheosized ego in a biosleeve or cybersleeve. Backed up. Resleeving is possible but traumatic.

+ Free sleeve (Cost ≤ higher of Assets/Rep +4)
+ Physical Skills from sleeve
+ Can resleeve on death
+ Access to Augmentations
– Resleeving is a mental attack (Cohesion check)
SIM

Pure software. Lives in virtual space. Perceives physical world through mesh sensors.

+ 7 Physical Ranks (virtual)
+ Free: SIM State Stunt
+ Free: One SIM choice Stunt
+ Full access to virtual worlds
– Needs drones for physical interaction
– Lag penalties at range

Steps 5–6

House Allegiance & Rep

Your House gives you market discounts, a social identity, and hooks into the Shadow War. Apostates trade all of that for freedom and a bonus from black-market contacts.

Rep-Rating = H.Net Skill + 2, plus a free bump equal to your Diplomacy rank. This determines what goods you can acquire through legitimate Coalition channels and how favors work.

Starting Gear (Step 7)

Starting Gear Formula: First item = Cost equal to higher of (Assets or Rep) + 2. Each next item is Cost −1 lower than the previous. You can have unlimited Cost 0–1 items. Restricted items count against your limit of Assets + 1 (unless you took Licensed Stunt).

Quick Reference

Core rules you'll use every session.

Core Mechanic

4dF + Skill Rank + Modifiers = Effort
If Effort ≥ Difficulty → Success
Difference = Shifts
Spin: 3+ Shifts = create a free Aspect
Stall: Failed by 3+ = spectacular failure
