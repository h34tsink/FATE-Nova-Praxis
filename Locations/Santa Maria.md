# Santa Maria

**Summary:** A mobile [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] flotilla of a few dozen ships that drifts the seams of [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] space — part safe haven for fugitives, part staging ground for salvage and piracy. Its centerpiece is the *Gatekeeper*, a seized Coalition carrier capable of deploying its own jump gate, which gives the flotilla something almost no other Apostate community has: the ability to travel faster than light on its own terms.

## Lore

### The Flotilla

Santa Maria isn't a place. It's a fleet — somewhere between a refugee convoy and a pirate court. A few dozen vessels of wildly mixed origin sail in loose formation: grounded freighters welded into long-haul habitats, salvaged Coalition cutters running false transponders, jury-rigged science ships, and at least one Houseworld luxury liner that nobody asks too many questions about. Together they form a small, mobile city that can disperse on a few minutes' notice.

Life aboard is cramped, hot, and politically uneasy. There is no single captain — each ship carries its own crew, its own factions, its own grievances. What binds them is the *Gatekeeper* and the route it provides. Lose the carrier, and the flotilla becomes a scattered handful of stranded ships at sub-light speeds.

### The Gatekeeper

The flagship is a former Coalition carrier seized in an action nobody on the flotilla likes to discuss in front of strangers. She mounts a deployable jump-gate apparatus — a hard-won prize that lets the flotilla open its own short-range jumps without depending on Coalition jump rings. This is the single fact that makes Santa Maria more than a refugee fleet. It is the reason the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] would very much like to find them, and the reason they almost never do.

The Gatekeeper is the flotilla's heart, courtroom, hospital, and arsenal. Disputes are settled in her hangar bays. Newcomers are interviewed there. Decisions about where the flotilla jumps next are made in her bridge by an informal council of ship captains.

### Role in the Apostate Movement

Santa Maria fills a niche that [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]] cannot:

- **Refuge for the burned.** Apostates whose names have surfaced on Coalition watchlists, defaulters who cannot reach [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]], and dissidents fleeing House crackdowns can find a berth here without the long journey to [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|HD413]].
- **Staging point for raids.** Salvage runs into derelict orbits, hit-and-run actions against [[Glossary/Compilers|Compiler]] convoys, and quiet operations against unattended Coalition outposts.
- **Mobile market.** Black-market trade in [[Glossary/CID|CID]]-locked compiler stock, weapons, sleeves, and information passes through the flotilla's holds.
- **Cultural seedbed.** The bare-knuckle pit fights and survival doctrine that produced forms like *Cascade of Endless Edges* (see [[Rules and Mechanics/Blade-Forms]]) trace as much to Santa Maria's hangars as to [[Locations/New Montreal|New Montreal]]'s underground.

### Tone and Texture

Recycled air. Tape-patched bulkheads. Children who have never set foot on a planet. A soft lattice of running lights from a dozen ships holding station against a starfield. Loud laughter in one cargo bay, a knife fight in the next, a quiet meeting of captains in a third. Everything portable. Everything ready to scatter.

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Mobile, Outlaw, Always One Jump Ahead*
- *The Gatekeeper Is Our Lifeline*
- *Everyone Here Burned a Bridge to Get Here*
- *Trade in Favors, Pay in Risk*

### Travel and Access

- **Finding the flotilla:** Requires a personal introduction or a verified Apostate contact. Cold approaches are intercepted, scanned, and usually turned away. Coalition transponders draw immediate dispersal.
- **Departing the flotilla:** Easy in theory, harder in practice — the flotilla rarely jumps to anywhere a Coalition citizen would willingly visit.
- **Jump capability:** The Gatekeeper can deploy its jump apparatus on a few hours' notice. Range is shorter than a fixed Coalition jump ring, but the flotilla doesn't need infrastructure on the other end.

### On-Board Hazards

- **Failing systems:** Recycled atmosphere, power brownouts, and patched hull plates. Notice or Engineering rolls to anticipate failures before they bite.
- **Crowd politics:** Every ship has its own informal authority. A favor owed on one deck is a debt collected on another. Rapport rolls to navigate captain-to-captain etiquette.
- **No CID infrastructure:** [[Glossary/Compilers|Compilers]] do not work for non-citizens. Repairs and resupply are scavenged or traded for in [[Glossary/Gold|gold]] and favors.

### Digital Environment

The flotilla runs a closed mesh — no Extranet handshake, no Coalition routing. SINC and Hacking checks against outside systems are at -2 unless routed through a smuggled relay. Internal mesh is parochial, paranoid, and well-monitored by whoever currently holds the *Gatekeeper*'s comms deck.

## Continuity

- **Assumptions:** Santa Maria has not appeared on-screen in any played session as of the current campaign. It exists in canon as a known Apostate institution and as the cultural origin point for the *Cascade of Endless Edges* blade form. Treat it as available for plot use without retconning prior session content.
- **Conflicts to resolve:** None at this time.
