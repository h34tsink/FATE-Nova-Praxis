## Basic Data

**Location:** Exoplanet; distant from Sol
**Gravity:** 1.1G (slightly higher than Earth)
**Conditions:** Tropical; water-rich; abundant agricultural production
**Estimated Population:** 800,000
**Primary Control:** House Jinzhan

## Overview

Shouye exists as House Jinzhan's most strategically important asset—the agricultural world that supplies food to the Coalition. The planet maintains a distinctly Chinese cultural character inherited from original settlement patterns. Massive agricultural operations feed millions across Coalition space, making Shouye critical to the survival of off-world civilization.

## Physical Environment

### Climate & Geography
- **Tropical climate:** Warm year-round; abundant rainfall
- **Vegetation:** Dense forests covering most land; cleared agricultural zones
- **Water:** Extensive river systems; multiple large lakes
- **Soil:** Extremely fertile; ideal for agriculture
- **Seasons:** Minimal seasonal variation; production continues year-round

### Fauna & Hazards

**Tiger Wasps:**
- **Description:** Large predatory insect; approximately 50 cm wingspan
- **Danger:** Venomous; capable of killing humans
- **Habitat:** Forest zones; attracted to agricultural areas
- **Activity:** Nocturnal hunters; diurnal hibernation
- **Threat:** Constant hazard to outdoor work; protective measures required
- **Defense:** Protective suits, insecticides, early warning systems
- **Cultural:** Hunting tiger wasps considered coming-of-age ritual

**Other Predators:**
- **Reptiles:** Large carnivorous reptiles in forest regions
- **Insects:** Various insect species; some dangerous
- **Amphibians:** Water-dwelling creatures in rivers/swamps
- **Unknown:** Rumors of larger creatures in unexplored regions
- **Ecosystem:** Complex web of predators and prey

## Colonial History & Population

### Settlement Pattern
- **Timeline:** Colonized as part of early Coalition expansion
- **Origin:** Primarily Chinese colonists; cultural pattern established early
- **Development:** Focused entirely on agriculture from beginning
- **Population:** Remained relatively small until last century
- **Expansion:** Rapid growth as Coalition demands increased

### Current Settlements

**Primary Cities:**
- **Songhai:** Capital and largest settlement (est. 300,000)
- **Chenzhou:** Secondary agricultural hub (est. 200,000)
- **Guangnan:** Tertiary agricultural center (est. 150,000)
- **Smaller towns:** Estimated 150,000 distributed in smaller centers

**Characteristics:**
- **Architecture:** Mix of Chinese and modern styles
- **Culture:** Chinese traditions dominant; language mix of Mandarin and Standard
- **Organization:** House Jinzhan administration; appointed governors
- **Connection:** Tight links to House Jinzhan political structure

### Population Character
- **Agricultural workers:** Vast majority employed in farming/processing
- **House personnel:** Administrative and management staff
- **Military:** Small defensive garrison
- **Service sectors:** Merchants, laborers, entertainers, support staff
- **Hierarchy:** Clear class structure; House Jinzhan at top

## Agriculture & Food Production

### Primary Crops
- **Rice:** Staple crop; most of production
- **Wheat:** Secondary grain crop
- **Vegetables:** Diverse vegetable production for nutritional variety
- **Fruits:** Fruit orchards; some exotic fruits
- **Spices:** Herbal and spice cultivation
- **Supplement:** Genetically modified crops developed by House Jinzhan

### Agricultural System
- **Scale:** Massive operation; feeds millions across Coalition
- **Organization:** Divided into regional agricultural zones
- **Workforce:** Mostly colonists; some contracted workers
- **Technology:** Advanced agricultural technology and automation
- **Output:** Surplus exported throughout Coalition; critical trade commodity

### Food Processing
- **Facilities:** Extensive processing centers in major cities
- **Products:** Dried food, preserved goods, processed meals
- **Export:** Packaged food shipped throughout Coalition space
- **Storage:** Massive warehouses for long-term storage
- **Distribution:** House Jinzhan manages all food distribution

## Control & Government

### House Jinzhan Authority
- **Governor:** House-appointed military or administrative official
- **Administration:** Hierarchical; reports to House Jinzhan leadership
- **Police:** House security force maintains order
- **Military:** Small defensive garrison; insufficient for independence
- **Policy:** All major decisions require House approval

### Population Relationship
- **Status:** Effectively serfs; bound to agricultural work
- **Mobility:** Restricted; difficult to leave colony permanently
- **Conditions:** Adequate but controlled; standard of living regulated
- **Discontent:** Periodic unrest; rebellions suppressed militarily
- **Wages:** Paid in Jinzhan credits or food rations; limited currency value

### Recent Tensions
- **Dissatisfaction:** Growing unhappiness with House Jinzhan control
- **Rebellion:** Periodic uprisings crushed by military
- **Escape:** Some workers attempting to reach apostate colonies
- **Resistance:** Underground resistance groups operating secretly
- **Suppression:** House Jinzhan responding with increased military presence

## Notable Features

### The Chinese Remnant
- **Historical:** Original colonists preserved Chinese cultural traditions
- **Language:** Mandarin Chinese widely spoken; Standard as second language
- **Religion:** Mix of Buddhism, Taoism, and modern secular beliefs
- **Holidays:** Traditional Chinese festivals celebrated annually
- **Architecture:** Temples and traditional buildings throughout settlements
- **Identity:** Colonists maintain strong Chinese cultural identity

### Strategic Importance
- **Food monopoly:** Shouye produces most Coalition food supply
- **Leverage:** House Jinzhan uses food control for political power
- **Vulnerability:** Coalition entirely dependent on Shouye production
- **Target:** Apostate groups have targeted food supplies
- **Collapse risk:** Failure of agriculture would devastate Coalition

## Campaign Hooks

- **Food crisis:** Sabotaging or protecting Shouye food production
- **Rebellion:** Supporting or suppressing colonial uprising
- **Espionage:** Gathering intelligence on Shouye operations
- **Rescue:** Extracting refugees from House Jinzhan control
- **Tiger hunting:** Expedition into dangerous fauna regions
- **Agricultural theft:** Stealing rare crops or genetic material
- **Black market:** Smuggling food to opposition groups
- **Cultural:** Learning about preserved Chinese traditions
- **Resistance:** Working with underground rebel movements
