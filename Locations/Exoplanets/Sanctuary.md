## Basic Data

**Location:** HD 413 system; significant distance from Sol
**Gravity:** 0.92G (near Earth-normal)
**Conditions:** Temperate; water-rich; abundant resources
**Estimated Population:** 2+ million apostates (official Census: 1.8 million)

## Overview

Sanctuary represents the apostate movement's greatest achievement—a thriving colony completely independent from Coalition control. Established generations ago, Sanctuary has grown into a sophisticated civilization with underground cities, advanced manufacturing, and expanding territorial ambitions. The planet serves as both refuge and staging ground for apostate operations throughout known space.

## Physical Environment

### Planetary Conditions
- **Atmosphere:** Breathable; 18% oxygen, 81% nitrogen (standard for humans)
- **Gravity:** 0.92G; comfortable for long-term habitation
- **Water:** Abundant; global ocean with large continents
- **Climate:** Temperate; seasonal variations mild; generally favorable
- **Flora/Fauna:** Apparently Earth-origin; heavily terraformed centuries ago

### Geographic Features
- **Continents:** Multiple major landmasses; most development on primary continent
- **Oceans:** Rich with engineered aquatic life; some harvested for food
- **Geology:** Geologically stable; some geothermal activity
- **Resources:** Rich deposits of metals and useful minerals
- **Landscape:** Mixed forests, plains, and grasslands

## Major Population Centers

### New Montreal: The Primary City
- **Location:** Primary continent; largest settlement
- **Population:** Estimated 1.2+ million
- **Designation:** Capital city; center of apostate government
- **Character:** Built underground with surface parks and facilities
- **Architecture:** Blend of natural caverns and constructed structures

**Underground City:**
- **Depth:** Extends hundreds of meters below surface
- **Levels:** Multi-layered structure with distinct districts
- **Environment:** Controlled atmosphere; artificial lighting
- **Size:** Sprawling; could accommodate 2-3 million easily
- **History:** Established generations ago; continuously expanded

**District Organization:**
- **Commercial:** Markets, trade, financial systems
- **Residential:** Housing for millions; mix of luxury and standard
- **Industrial:** Manufacturing, processing, construction facilities
- **Agricultural:** Hydroponic farms providing food for population
- **Cultural:** Schools, libraries, theaters, museums, entertainment
- **Administrative:** Government offices and decision-making centers
- **Security:** Defense headquarters and monitoring stations

**Surface Facilities:**
- **Parks:** Large parks providing outdoor recreation and escape
- **Farms:** Agricultural fields supplementing hydroponic production
- **Processing:** Resource extraction and initial processing
- **Defense:** Anti-ship and anti-asteroid defense batteries
- **Transportation:** Landing facilities for ships and shuttles

### Valhalla: The Expansion Settlement
- **Location:** Secondary continent (smaller landmass)
- **Population:** Estimated 500,000
- **Status:** Primary expansion project; rapidly growing
- **Designation:** Future second major city; long-term settlement goal
- **Development:** Aggressive construction and infrastructure development

**Current State:**
- **Phase:** Still in major construction phase
- **Focus:** Building infrastructure for much larger population
- **Speed:** Unusual rate of development; accelerating
- **Purpose:** Unknown to outside observers; speculated to be strategic positioning

**Speculation:**
- **Military base:** Theory that Valhalla is becoming military stronghold
- **Independent capability:** Possibly ensuring second city survives if primary is destroyed
- **Expansion:** Perhaps planning for much larger population influx
- **Politics:** Possible factional movement; some groups establishing separate power base

### Other Settlements
- **Quantity:** 10-20 smaller settlements distributed across continents
- **Population:** Estimated 300,000 combined
- **Character:** Mix of agricultural, industrial, and residential communities
- **Organization:** Connected to primary cities via underground transport

## Government & Ideology

### Apostate Philosophy
- **Core principle:** Freedom from Coalition restrictions; personal autonomy
- **Governance:** Consensus-based; minimize centralized authority
- **Augmentation:** No restrictions; individuals free to modify as desired
- **Consciousness:** Free uploading and copying without HPA restrictions
- **Economy:** Mixed; blend of collective and individual enterprise

### Government Structure
- **Legislature:** Council of representatives from major settlements
- **Council:** 30-50 members; decisions require consensus or super-majority
- **Executive:** Minimal; administrative functions handled by departments
- **Enforcement:** Distributed; communities handle own justice systems
- **Flexibility:** Structure evolving; constant debates about proper governance

### Recent Changes
- **Centralization:** Slow trend toward stronger central authority
- **Defense:** Growing military presence and expansion planning
- **Expansion:** Aggressive growth into Valhalla; rapid development
- **Militarization:** Increased military training and equipment production
- **Secrecy:** Less transparent about internal planning than historical norm
- **Concern:** Some apostate factions worried about authoritarian drift

## Economy & Production

### Manufacturing
- **Scale:** Significant industrial capacity; could supply thousands of worlds
- **Products:** Weapons, ships, equipment, technology, consumer goods
- **Trade:** Supplies apostate colonies throughout known space
- **Employment:** Thousands of workers in manufacturing sector
- **Quality:** High-quality goods; competitive with House production

### Agriculture
- **Hydroponic farms:** Supplement planet-based agriculture
- **Surface farming:** Extensive agricultural lands in suitable climate zones
- **Aquaculture:** Engineered ocean species provide protein
- **Export:** Surplus food exported to apostate and fringe settlements
- **Technology:** Advanced agricultural technology developed in-house

### Services
- **Financial:** Banking and investment services for apostate community
- **Transportation:** Shipping and logistics networks
- **Communication:** Mesh infrastructure connecting apostate worlds
- **Technology:** Research and development across many fields
- **Culture:** Entertainment and hospitality services

## Military Capability

### Defense Forces
- **Scale:** Estimated 100,000+ personnel (military and support)
- **Organization:** Combined military force; all branches integrated
- **Technology:** Advanced weapons and ships; competitive with Coalition
- **Strategy:** Defensive focus; designed to repel Coalition invasion
- **Expansion:** Growing; unusual rate of militarization

### Recent Developments
- **Ship construction:** Rapid warship construction program
- **Weapon development:** Advanced weapons and defense systems
- **Training:** Intensified military training programs
- **Fortifications:** Building orbital defense systems
- **Readiness:** Alert level increased; preparing for potential conflict

### Strategic Concern
- **Coalition perspective:** Sanctuary's militarization represents potential threat
- **Intent unclear:** Unknown whether expansion is defensive or offensive
- **Threat assessment:** Coalition beginning formal threat evaluation
- **Monitoring:** Increased intelligence focus on Sanctuary developments
- **Diplomatic:** Coalition beginning to consider formal acknowledgment of Sanctuary
