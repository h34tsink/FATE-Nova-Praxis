## Basic Data

**Location:** Kimura colonial world; extreme distance from Sol
**Gravity:** Similar to Earth (1.0G)
**Conditions:** Extreme seasonal variations; polar regions extend to mid-latitudes
**Estimated Population:** 1.2 million (officially); unknown aberrant population

## Overview

Chengdan represents House Kimura's premier colonial achievement—and its darkest secret. The planet was colonized 40+ years ago with the intention of creating a transhuman haven where extreme augmentation and genetic modification could be freely pursued. Instead, colonists have apparently undergone extreme evolutionary changes, producing aberrant posthumans whose very existence challenges Coalition assumptions about consciousness and humanity.

## Physical Environment

### Extreme Seasonality
- **Orbital dynamics:** Highly elliptical orbit creates extreme seasonal variations
- **Peak:** Summer temperatures reach tropical extremes in equatorial region
- **Trough:** Winter temperatures drop to lethal levels across most of planet
- **Habitable zone:** Small tropical belt near equator remains habitable year-round
- **Population:** All settlements clustered in equatorial region for survivability

### Seasonal Cycle
- **Winter:** 6-month period of extreme cold; settlements largely isolated
- **Transition:** 2-month periods of temperate weather
- **Summer:** 3-month period of intense heat; settlements under stress
- **Survival:** Society evolved around seasonal rhythms and preparations

### Landscape
- **Topography:** Mix of highlands and lowlands; no oceans visible
- **Flora:** Apparently terraformed with Terran-origin plants; heavily mutated
- **Fauna:** Mix of brought-in animals and strange native species
- **Geology:** Active volcanic regions providing geothermal energy

## Colonial History

### Initial Settlement
- **Timeline:** 40+ years ago
- **Purpose:** House Kimura-sponsored transhuman colony
- **Philosophy:** Push boundaries of human augmentation and modification
- **Population sent:** 50,000 initial colonists (augmentation enthusiasts and volunteers)
- **Oversight:** Minimal Coalition monitoring; House Kimura controlled development

### Development
- **Rapid growth:** Population increased dramatically due to rapid reproduction or cloning
- **Augmentation:** Extreme genetic modification and cybernetic enhancement pursued
- **Success:** Colony thriving economically and technologically
- **Communication:** Regular contact with House Kimura; reports optimistic

### The Change
- **Timeline:** Last 5-10 years
- **Observation:** Something changed fundamentally in colony population
- **Appearance:** New genetic forms emerging; posthuman transformation evident
- **Reports:** Recent colonist reports describe children born with strange characteristics
- **Concern:** Even House Kimura beginning to question colony trajectory

## The Aberrants

### Physical Descriptions
- **Variation:** Multiple distinct posthuman forms observed
- **Changes:** Go far beyond typical augmentation/modification
- **Structure:** Some reports describe non-humanoid body plans
- **Capabilities:** Appear to have unusual abilities (speed, strength, sensory enhancement)
- **Coherence:** Unclear if different forms represent different species or variations

### Reported Forms
- **The Swift:** Described as lean, four-limbed creatures with exceptional running speed
- **The Builders:** Large, strong forms optimized for construction and manipulation
- **The Seers:** Forms with dramatically enhanced sensory apparatus (multiple eyes, etc.)
- **Others:** Various reports of additional forms; unclear how many exist
- **Communication:** All appear to retain human-level or higher intelligence

### Origins
- **Cause:** Unknown; multiple theories
- **Natural evolution:** Rapid speciation from human stock due to extreme selection pressure
- **Engineered:** Deliberate genetic modification program reaching its intended form
- **Mutation:** Unexpected consequence of Chengdan environment on augmented humans
- **External:** Alien influence or contamination from Chengdan native life
- **Conspiracy:** Some theorize intentional replacement of human colonists

### Current Population
- **Human remnant:** Small percentage of original human colonists remain
- **Hybrid:** Population includes both human and aberrant forms
- **Ratio:** Unknown; aberrants apparently outnumber humans significantly
- **Integration:** Degree of genetic mixing between forms unclear
- **Reproduction:** Different forms apparently capable of reproducing

## Colonial Settlements

### Primary Settlement: New Kimura
- **Population:** Estimated 500,000+
- **Location:** Largest equatorial settlement
- **Control:** Mixed; original House Kimura administration and native aberrant council
- **Status:** Functioning normally; appears cooperative
- **Trade:** Continues normal commerce with outer Coalition
- **Mystery:** Minimal information about internal governance changes

### Secondary Settlements
- **Quantity:** 5-7 other significant settlements
- **Distribution:** Spread along equatorial zone
- **Population:** Estimated 400,000 combined
- **Status:** Appearing cooperative; unusual architectural changes observed
- **Development:** Some reports of rapid construction and infrastructure changes

### Architecture Changes
- **Observations:** Recent colony reports describe structural modifications
- **Changes:** New buildings with non-standard designs; unusual materials
- **Purpose:** Function unclear; some structures serve no obvious purpose
- **Theory:** Possible adaptation to new posthuman physiology
- **Significance:** Changes suggest planning for fundamentally different living requirements

## Governance & Control

### Official Structure
- **House Kimura administration:** Nominally in charge; influence apparently declining
- **Colonial council:** New governing body formed; composition unclear
- **Integration:** Apparent cooperation between House representatives and native forms
- **Decision-making:** Process unclear; House Kimura reports increasing resistance to oversight
- **Autonomy:** Colony increasingly making independent decisions

### Reality
- **Balance shifting:** Aberrant forms apparently gaining influence over governance
- **House concern:** House Kimura leadership uncertain about colony trajectory
- **Non-intervention:** Coalition ordered House Kimura to allow colony autonomy
- **Monitoring:** Minimal Coalition oversight; relying on House reports
- **Uncertainty:** No one outside colony has clear picture of actual power structure
