# Exoplanets Overview

Humanity's expansion beyond Sol System through Jump Gate technology has established colonies on six major exoplanets. Each world presents unique challenges and opportunities for the Coalition's expansion.

## Major Colonies

### [[Proch]]
**First Colony - 600,000 population**
- Original human expansion target
- Active dinosaur populations  
- Federation tunnel networks discovered
- Keppler (250k) and Giordino (150k) major cities
- Hunting culture and paleontology research

### [[Sanctuary]]
**Apostate Haven - 2M+ population**
- Primary apostate-controlled world
- New Montreal underground city (1.2M)
- Valhalla expansion settlement (500k)
- Growing independence movement
- Increasingly militarized

### [[Chengdan]]
**House Kimura Colony - 1.2M population**
- Extreme seasonal variations
- Aberrant posthuman emergence (The Swift, The Builders, The Seers)
- New Kimura settlement
- Mysterious evolutionary phenomena

### [[Shouye]]
**Agricultural World - 800,000 population**
- House Jinzhan food monopoly
- Chinese cultural dominance
- Tiger wasp predators
- Songhai capital city (300k)
- Agricultural serfdom concerns

### [[Locations/Exo Planets/Cygnus Alpha]]
**Mining Colony - 400,000 population**
- Federation tunnel networks
- Hidden apostate communities
- Copper Station and Federation Base
- Archaeological significance

### [[Sagitta]]
**Expansion Colony - 300,000 population**
- Massive chasm settlements
- Mysterious magnetic anomaly
- Chasm City vertical construction (150k)
- Classified research station
- Strategic importance unclear

## Common Themes

**Federation Ruins**: Multiple worlds show evidence of the mysterious precursor civilization
- Tunnel networks (Proch, Cygnus Alpha)
- Abandoned technology
- Xenoarchaeological research ongoing

**Apostate Presence**: Several worlds harbor significant apostate populations
- Sanctuary (primary haven)
- Cygnus Alpha (hidden communities)
- Growing independence movements

**House Control**: Coalition Houses maintain monopolies
- Jinzhan: Shouye food production
- Kimura: Chengdan technology and posthuman research
- Multiple houses compete for mining/resource rights

**Unique Hazards**: Each world presents distinct challenges
- Proch: Dinosaurs and megafauna
- Shouye: Tiger wasps
- Chengdan: Evolutionary acceleration
- Sagitta: Magnetic anomalies

## Strategic Considerations

**Coalition Expansion**: Exoplanet colonies critical for:
- Resource independence from Sol System
- Political legitimacy (controlling territory)
- Testing grounds for new technology
- Population pressure relief

**Threats**:
- Apostate independence movements (Sanctuary)
- Posthuman emergence (Chengdan)
- Federation technology mysteries
- Distance from Coalition authority
