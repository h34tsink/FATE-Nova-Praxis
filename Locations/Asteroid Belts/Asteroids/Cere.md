
## **Overview**

The asteroid Cere is a rugged, isolated settlement orbiting the outskirts of a mining-rich belt. Known for its role as a water source for Mars and Luna, Cere houses two primary facilities: **Trident**, an ice-cutting and shipping hub, and **Anapos Station**, an orbital platform for processing and distributing the ice. However, beneath these official operations lies a shadowy world of freelancers, smugglers, and disenfranchised workers who have turned parts of Cere into a haven for those avoiding Coalition scrutiny.

### **Key Features**

- **Population**: Approximately 2,000 inhabitants, mostly independent miners, scavengers, and members of fringe groups like the Talons of Freedom.
- **Atmosphere**: Cere relies on a patchwork life-support system in its subterranean habitat modules. Air quality is functional but often stale, with frequent pressure adjustments due to aging infrastructure.
- **Governance**: Officially managed by corporate overseers at Trident and Anapos, unofficial areas of Cere are run by a loose council of influential figures, including faction representatives and key traders. Disputes are often settled privately or violently.
- **Economy**: Bartering and resource exchange dominate the economy. Ice trade, smuggled goods, refurbished mining equipment, and illegal tech are common currencies.

### **Points of Interest**

- **Trident**:
  - A small town built around the ice-cutting operations, Trident provides housing and services for workers. While corporate oversight is strict in this area, underground dealings thrive beneath the surface.
  - Leads to Valare’s fork may be found here through dockworkers or technicians with connections to the shadow network.

- **Anapos Station**:
  - This orbital facility processes ice for shipment to Mars and Luna. It is highly secure and less accessible to outsiders but offers valuable resources and trade connections for those with the right credentials.

- **Main Docking Bay**:
  - The primary entry point for ships. It’s a cavernous, heavily shielded facility capable of housing up to five mid-sized vessels.
  - Security is maintained by a mix of automated turrets and local enforcers. Smugglers and refugees frequently arrive here, making it a hub for gossip and leads.

- **The Core Markets**:
  - A bustling bazaar in the central habitat module, the Core Markets are a maze of stalls selling mining equipment, contraband, and exotic wares.
  - An ideal place to gather information, purchase supplies, or hire guides.

- **The Refugee Quarters**:
  - Located in the lower levels, these quarters are crowded but secure spaces where displaced individuals and those on the run find temporary shelter.
  - Many of the inhabitants are sympathetic to the Talons’ cause, making it a good place to look for allies or Valare’s fork.

- **Deepshaft Tavern**:
  - A popular bar for Cere’s inhabitants, located near the old mining shafts. It’s a dimly lit, smoky establishment where deals are made and tensions often boil over into brawls.
  - The barkeep, a grizzled miner named **Kal Paddock**, knows everything that happens on Cere—for the right price.

- **Abandoned Mining Shafts**:
  - These shafts extend deep into Cere’s interior, providing ample hiding spots for those wishing to remain out of sight.
  - Dangerous and poorly mapped, they’re home to collapsing tunnels, toxic gas pockets, and predatory scavengers.

- **[[Locations/Shadow Market]]**:
- A notorious haven for those living off the grid, the [[Locations/Shadow Market]] operates in the darker corners of Cere. Here, players might find information about Valare’s fork or those who facilitated her escape.
