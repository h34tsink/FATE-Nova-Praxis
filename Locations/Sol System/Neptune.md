## Basic Data

**Classification:** Ice Giant
**Moons:** 14+ known moons
**Distance from Sol:** Extreme; outer boundary of Coalition space
**Primary Settlement:** Naesock Processing Hub (200,000+)
**Strategic Status:** Highest classification; weapons research facility

## Overview

Neptune represents the outer boundary of Coalition-controlled space. The ice giant orbits at such distance that communication with inner Coalition involves significant time delays. Naesock Processing Hub, supposedly a generic research facility, actually houses classified weapons development and advanced technology research. The facility's isolation makes it ideal for research the Coalition doesn't want public scrutiny of.

## Physical Characteristics

### Atmosphere & Conditions
- **Composition:** Water ice, methane ice, ammonia ice
- **Temperature:** Extremely cold; among coldest planets
- **Winds:** Supersonic winds (fastest in solar system)
- **Appearance:** Deep blue color from atmospheric methane
- **Radiation:** Lower than inner planets due to distance
- **Accessibility:** No accessible habitable zones

### Orbital Mechanics
- **Distance:** Extreme; at edge of practical Coalition space
- **Travel time:** Weeks/months from Mars; communication delayed
- **Isolation:** Effectively isolated from Coalition control
- **Significance:** Distance provides concealment for operations
- **Vulnerability:** Difficult to defend against external threats

### Moons
- **Quantity:** 14+ known moons
- **Primary moon:** Triton (largest; geologically active)
- **Others:** Mostly small, unremarkable bodies
- **Population:** Minimal; mostly automated facilities
- **Resources:** Unexploited; limited strategic value
- **Development:** Minimal except for Naesock orbit

## Naesock Processing Hub

### Official Description
- **Designation:** Research and processing facility
- **Operator:** House Kimura
- **Population:** 200,000+ (military, scientists, support)
- **Purpose:** "Advanced processing and research"
- **Classification:** Highly classified
- **Access:** Extremely restricted; military personnel only

### Actual Operations (Suspected/Rumored)

**Research Programs:**
- **Weapons development:** Creating new military technology
- **Advanced processing:** Unknown materials from deep space
- **Bioweapons:** Possible biological weapons research
- **Energy systems:** Experimental propulsion or power generation
- **Classified military:** Programs too sensitive for Mars oversight

**Security Measures:**
- **Military base:** Fortress-like security
- **Personnel screening:** Extensive background checks
- **Information compartmentalization:** Staff know only their section
- **Isolation:** Minimal external communication
- **Weapons:** Orbital defense systems protecting facility

**Personnel:**
- **Scientists:** Best researchers recruited from Coalition
- **Military:** Significant military command presence
- **Support:** Engineers, technicians, logistical staff
- **Security:** Extensive security and counterintelligence personnel
- **Administration:** Coalition appointed oversight

### Hidden Talons Connection

**Weapons Supply:**
- **Relationship:** Station commander reportedly supports Talons of Freedom
- **Supply:** Provides weapons and military technology to insurgents
- **Access:** Talons use station for weapons acquisition
- **Risk:** Coalition discovery would eliminate insurgent supply line
- **Detection:** Unknown if Coalition leadership aware of connection

**Strategic Implications:**
- **Insurgent support:** Talons obtaining advanced Coalition weapons
- **Military threat:** Insurgents better armed than expected
- **Loyalty question:** Naesock command loyalty to Coalition questionable
- **Political:** Possible factional support for Talons
- **Investigation:** This relationship could be investigation target

## Triton Moon

### Geographic Features
- **Status:** Geologically active ice moon
- **Volcanoes:** Active cryovolcanoes (ice geysers)
- **Geology:** Unusual for outer system moon
- **Rotation:** Retrograde; orbits "backwards" relative to Neptune
- **Atmosphere:** Thin nitrogen atmosphere

### Minimal Operations
- **Population:** Only automated facilities
- **Purpose:** Monitoring and research
- **Unique:** Only geologically active ice moon
- **Scientific interest:** Natural laboratory for geology
- **Resources:** Ice and unusual mineral deposits
