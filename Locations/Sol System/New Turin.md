## Basic Data

**Location:** Mare Acidalium region, Mars
**Population:** ~1.2 million
**Primary Control:** [[Factions/Hidden Agendas/House Turin|House Turin]]
**Status:** Technically subordinate to Coalition; effectively independent
**Character:** Russian organized crime culture, open illegality, frontier lawlessness

## Overview

New Turin is the Coalition's open secret. The city operates as an independent criminal state within Mars — technically subject to Coalition law, practically beyond its reach. [[Factions/Hidden Agendas/House Turin|House Turin]] runs New Turin like a Bratva territory: personal loyalty, controlled violence, and a commercial ecosystem built on what the Coalition prohibits elsewhere.

It hosts the largest black market in Coalition space and serves as the operational headquarters for the Rings of Olympus. The Coalition tolerates it because dismantling it would require a military campaign with uncertain outcome and certain disruption to shadow-economy supply chains that the Coalition itself benefits from.

## Criminal Economy

- **Primary industries:** Illegal weapons manufacturing, drug production, mercenary recruitment
- **Black market:** Largest in Coalition space — handles stolen goods, forbidden tech, contraband sleeves
- **Operational base:** HQ for [[Factions/Hidden Agendas/Rings of Olympus|Rings of Olympus]] and House Turin's off-book operations
- **Connections:** Smuggling routes to Apostate colonies, fringe worlds, and Cere
- **Security:** Heavily guarded against Coalition inspection; perimeter has early-warning systems

## Physical Layout

- **Old tunnels:** Original mining infrastructure repurposed as the criminal network's backbone
- **Outer perimeter:** Legitimate-seeming mining and manufacturing operations as cover
- **Inner city:** Bratva hierarchy visible and unashamed — leadership doesn't hide
- **The Red Hand:** Largest casino in New Turin; doubles as criminal headquarters
- **Slavic Quarter:** Original Russian-heritage settlement; most traditional area in the city
- **Manufacturing district:** Openly operating illegal weapons factories
- **Docks:** Off-books ship repair, smuggling intake, and contraband storage

## The Bratva Control

House Turin runs New Turin through a pure chain of loyalty and credible violence. Viktor Kozlov sets the terms. Everyone else pays tribute or gets cleared out. Outsiders are welcome if they bring profit; threatening outsiders are removed quickly and quietly.

The culture is unambiguously old-school Russian organized crime adapted to transhuman technology. Resleeving creates complications — identity continuity is tracked through biometric chains and personal markers, not just appearance.

## Campaign Hooks

- Acquiring illegal tech or weapons without Coalition scrutiny
- Undercover work infiltrating Bratva hierarchy
- Extracting someone House Turin has taken an interest in
- Disrupting a smuggling route that traces back to a faction the party opposes
- Navigating a deal between Turin and another House that both sides want kept quiet
