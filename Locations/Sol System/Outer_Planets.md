## Overview

Beyond Mars, the outer Coalition territory grows increasingly lawless and mysterious. The Asteroid Belt serves as primary location for the Tartarus prison and numerous pirate sanctuaries. The outer planets—Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune—host specialized research stations, mining colonies, and apostate enclaves far from Coalition oversight.

## Asteroid Belt

### Tartarus: The Prison
- **Location:** Primary asteroid in belt
- **Function:** Primary Coalition prison; holds most dangerous criminals
- **Capacity:** Thousands of prisoners (exact numbers classified)
- **Type:** Combination of traditional confinement and consciousness isolation
- **Security:** Extremely high; heavily guarded military installation
- **Escape rate:** Near zero; practically inescapable

**Security Measures:**
- **Physical:** Asteroid fortified with military-grade defenses
- **Network:** Cut off from broader mesh; isolated systems only
- **Personnel:** Guards outnumber prisoners 2:1
- **Execution:** Facility authorized to execute condemned prisoners
- **Isolation:** Some prisoners held in consciousness suspension

**Notable Sections:**
- **General population:** Standard criminals (murder, theft, corruption)
- **Political prisoners:** Those deemed threats to Coalition
- **Death row:** Condemned prisoners awaiting execution
- **Research wing:** Prisoners used for controversial experiments
- **Black site:** Rumored secret section for disappeared persons

### Scattered Pirate Bases
- **Distribution:** Multiple small asteroid bases throughout belt
- **Organization:** Loose pirate confederacy; no unified command
- **Activity:** Raiding Coalition shipping, weapons trafficking, slavery
- **Territory:** Considered neutral zone; difficult to police
- **Recruitment:** Refuge for mercenaries and independent operators

## Jupiter System

### Europa Research Station
- **Location:** Orbiting moon Europa
- **Population:** Estimated 100,000 researchers and support staff
- **Operator:** House Silva (official); controversial House Jinzhan involvement
- **Function:** Life research center; searching for extraterrestrial biology
- **Status:** Classified research; minimal public information

**Research Focus:**
- **Objective:** Understand/manipulate alien biology from beneath Europa's ice
- **Creatures:** Strange organisms discovered in ocean beneath ice
- **Illnesses:** Researchers experiencing unexplained diseases
- **Secrecy:** House Silva tightly controlling information about discoveries
- **Mystery:** What is being found in the ice?

**Anomalies:**
- **Disease outbreak:** Unusual illnesses affecting research staff
- **Behavioral changes:** Some researchers exhibiting strange behavior before illness
- **Mortality rate:** Deaths increasing; official cause: "contamination"
- **Conspiracy theories:** Speculation about biological weapons or alien infection
- **Cover-up:** Coalition denying or minimizing health crisis

## Saturn System

### Titan City: Saturn's Mining Hub
- **Location:** Orbiting moon Titan
- **Population:** Estimated 1.5 million
- **Primary function:** Methane mining and hydrocarbon processing
- **Control:** Shared by multiple Houses (no single dominant)
- **Character:** Diverse, tough, frontier atmosphere

**Resources:**
- **Primary:** Methane extraction from Titan's atmosphere and surface
- **Processing:** On-site refineries converting methane to usable fuel
- **Distribution:** Supplies most Coalition fuel needs
- **Economy:** Entirely dependent on hydrocarbon production
- **Trade:** Major commercial hub for outer system traffic

**Population Character:**
- **Workers:** Miners, processors, and support staff
- **Operators:** House representatives managing mining operations
- **Traders:** Commercial merchants buying/selling hydrocarbon goods
- **Transients:** Ships passing through for trade and refueling
- **Gambling & entertainment:** Significant nightlife economy

**Infrastructure:**
- **Space elevator:** Connects surface to orbital station
- **Refineries:** Massive processing facilities
- **Ports:** Orbital docking facilities for ships
- **Settlements:** Mix of temporary and permanent structures
- **Diversity:** Less organized than Mars/Luna; more frontier feel

### Notable Feature: Space Elevator
- **Connection:** Surface to orbital station
- **Scale:** Critical infrastructure for entire colony
- **Vulnerability:** Single point of failure; target for sabotage
- **History:** Recently rebuilt after Consolidation Wars damage
- **Control:** Coalition-managed but nominally neutral

## Uranus System

### The Colony: Apostate Haven
- **Location:** High orbit near Uranus
- **Population:** Estimated 500,000 apostates (hiding from Coalition)
- **Status:** De facto apostate-controlled territory
- **Control:** No Coalition authority; self-governing
- **Character:** Refuge for stateless and hunted individuals

**Physical Characteristics:**
- **Ships:** Primarily mobile; community of connected large vessels
- **Prefabs:** Portable habitat modules easily deployable
- **Movement:** Entire colony capable of relocating if threatened
- **Defense:** Limited but mobile defensive systems
- **Stealth:** Designed to avoid Coalition detection

**Social Structure:**
- **Leadership:** Council of elder apostates; consensus-based
- **Philosophy:** Freedom from Coalition restrictions; mutual aid
- **Values:** Individual autonomy; no forced augmentation policies
- **Tension:** Constant internal debates about government and rules
- **Security:** Modest defensive capabilities; relies on distance and stealth

**Threats:**
- **Coalition:** Occasional pressure to relocate or submit
- **Children of Dionysus:** Apocalyptic faction spreading chaos and terror
- **Supplies:** Dependent on trade with fringe settlements
- **Sustainability:** Difficult to maintain permanent population
- **Future:** Survival depends on remaining hidden

## Neptune System

### Naesock Processing Hub
- **Location:** Far outer system; orbiting Neptune
- **Population:** Estimated 200,000
- **Function:** Advanced processing and research facility
- **Control:** House Kimura
- **Secrecy:** Minimal public information about operations

**Suspected Operations:**
- **Refining:** Processing unusual materials from deep space
- **Research:** Advanced technology development (likely classified)
- **Manufacturing:** Rumored to produce military hardware
- **Trade:** Handling goods too sensitive for open markets
- **Purpose:** Unknown; heavily guarded and secretive

**Notable Feature:**
- **Talons of Freedom presence:** US military insurgents using station for weapons acquisition
- **Smuggling:** Black market access to House Kimura military technology
- **Alliance:** Station commander reportedly supports Talons operations
- **Risk:** Coalition discovery would eliminate insurgent supply line

## Campaign Hooks

### Asteroid Belt
- **Prison break:** Extracting or stopping escape from Tartarus
- **Pirate hunt:** Tracking specific pirate faction through belt
- **Smuggling:** Running contraband through asteroid pirate territory
- **Alliance:** Joining or opposing pirate confederation

### Outer Planets
- **Research infiltration:** Espionage against House research programs
- **Europa mystery:** Investigating strange illnesses and alien organisms
- **Apostate contact:** Making contact with Colony or supporting them
- **Naesock infiltration:** Stealing technology or supporting Talons
- **Mining operations:** Corporate espionage or labor disputes
