## Basic Data

**Gravity:** 0.37G
**Length of Day:** 58.65 Earth Days
**Estimated Population:** 260,000
**Primary Control:** House Cipriani

## Overview

Mercury serves as the energy powerhouse of Coalition space, housing nearly all antimatter production in the galaxy. House Cipriani's monopoly over this critical resource solidifies the House's position as the primary provider of power across human civilization.

## Major Settlements & Locations

### Specular Spread
- **Function:** Solar panel field and production facilities
- **Operator:** House Cipriani subsidiary (Specular Spread)
- **Purpose:** Convert sunlight to power for antimatter processing
- **Scale:** Massive solar panel fields covering much of Mercury's surface
- **Importance:** Critical component of energy production chain

### Base 10
- **Function:** Antimatter production facilities
- **Operator:** House Cipriani subsidiary (Base 10)
- **Purpose:** Process and create antimatter for Coalition power needs
- **Technology:** Hundreds of antimatter processing plants
- **Strategic value:** Sole source of antimatter in Coalition space

### Tellerus Fields
- **Historical significance:** Site of worst battle between Earth remnants and Coalition
- **Event:** Battle that solidified Coalition's position of dominance
- **Scale:** Largest organized military engagement Coalition has fought
- **Outcome:** Remnant forces broken and scattered
- **Legacy:** Believed large numbers of remnant supporters still hide in tunnel networks

### Mercurial Gateway
- **Function:** Jump gate facility
- **Type:** One of two jump rings in Sol system
- **Primary use:** Shipping processed antimatter to other systems
- **Alternative route:** Access to Eye of Sol near Earth
- **Strategic position:** Crucial transit point for antimatter distribution

### Mercurian Tunnel System
- **Extent:** Connects most of Mercury's habitats and facilities
- **Age:** Ancient; filled with unexplored sections
- **Hidden threat:** Believed to shelter survivors of Tellerus Fields
- **Strategic challenge:** Difficult to fully survey or control
- **Operational concern:** Pirate staging areas and hidden bases

### Balor
- **Function:** Large surface habitat
- **Purpose:** Vacation spot and getaway for workers
- **Luxury factor:** Recreation destination for Mercury facility workers
- **Strategic value:** Doubles as fallback position in case of emergency

## Strategic Significance

### Energy Monopoly
Mercury's antimatter production gives House Cipriani extraordinary leverage:
- **Power dependency** - All Coalition civilization depends on Mercury antimatter
- **Economic advantage** - Monopoly control ensures Cipriani wealth
- **Political leverage** - Can withhold power as coercive tool
- **Colonial necessity** - Outer system colonies cannot function without Mercury antimatter

### Military Vulnerability
- **High value target** - Most valuable target in Coalition space
- **Concentrated resource** - Single location produces all antimatter
- **Defense heavy** - Requires massive military presence to protect
- **Piracy risk** - Constant threat from pirates and insurgents

### Hidden Threats

**Tunnel Insurgency**
- **Tellerus remnants** - Survivors believed hiding in tunnel networks
- **Organized activity** - Evidence suggests organized rather than scattered resistance
- **Pirate coordination** - May coordinate with space pirates for supply raids
- **Coalition concern** - Why remnants haven't attacked despite apparent capability?

**Piracy Escalation**
- **Recent increase** - Piracy has grown worse in recent years
- **Hit-and-run tactics** - Attacks target shipping vessels for antimatter cargo
- **Supply theft** - Organized efforts to capture and divert antimatter shipments
- **Coalition response gap** - Protectorate struggling to respond quickly enough

## Campaign Hooks

- **Tunnel exploration** - Investigation of Tellerus Fields survivors or remnant bases
- **Pirate intercept** - Protecting antimatter shipment from raiders
- **Political intrigue** - House conflicts over Mercury control and power allocation
- **Facility sabotage** - Protecting critical infrastructure from terrorism
- **Antimatter theft** - High-stakes heist or security operation
