## Basic Data

**Location:** Syrtis region, Mars
**Population:** ~200,000 (primarily Apostates and stateless individuals)
**Status:** Former Coalition mining center; Coalition-acknowledged but unenforced
**Control:** De facto Apostate enclave — no recognized authority
**Character:** Lawless, decentralized, refuge for those who can't or won't exist in Coalition records

## Overview

Minos used to matter. It was an important mining settlement during Mars's early terraforming expansion — busy, Coalition-managed, economically viable. Then the ore ran out, the Coalition relocated operations, and Minos was functionally abandoned.

What the Coalition left behind, the Apostates and stateless filled. Minos is now Mars's largest ungoverned space — a ghost city that's very much alive, operating entirely outside HPA surveillance, Coalition bureaucracy, and House jurisdiction. The Coalition officially acknowledges its existence. It doesn't enforce law there. The arrangement suits both sides: the Coalition avoids a suppression campaign it doesn't want, and Minos residents stay off the grid they've rejected.

## Physical Character

The city is a mix of maintained structures and slow-decay ruins. Buildings that have use get repaired; those that don't get stripped for parts. There's no urban planning, no maintenance schedule, no central authority deciding what's priority. The city's layout follows the old mining grid, with improvised additions filling gaps.

Power and atmosphere run from a patchwork of maintained generators and life-support systems. Failure in one section is normal and handled locally.

## Population

- **Apostates:** The ideological core — people who've rejected HPA and Coalition oversight, living body-free or in unsanctioned sleeves
- **Refugees:** Displaced by House conflicts, crackdowns, economic collapse. Minos asks no questions
- **Criminals and fugitives:** People who need to not be found. Minos provides that, for a price
- **Salvagers:** Independent operators working the surrounding derelict zones
- **Idealists:** Those building something — commune governance experiments, underground research, off-grid culture

## Economy

Salvage, black market manufacturing, and illegal services are the main industries. No credits required — barter and favors circulate freely. Information about who's in the city is considered private property; selling it to Coalition interests is the closest thing Minos has to a social crime.

## Strategic Role

Minos is where the Apostate movement on Mars has roots. It's not an organized base — there's no commander, no unified cell structure — but the ideological density and off-grid infrastructure make it a natural node for contacts, equipment, and people who need to move without appearing in Coalition records.

## Campaign Hooks

- First contact with the Apostate network on Mars without going through official channels
- Locating someone who's gone stateless and disappeared into Minos
- A salvage claim that intersects with something Minos's inhabitants want to keep buried
- Coalition pressure building — the unofficial tolerance may be about to end
