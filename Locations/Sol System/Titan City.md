## Basic Data

**Location:** Saturn's moon Titan
**Population:** 1.5+ million
**Primary Control:** Shared by multiple Houses (no single dominant)
**Primary Function:** Methane extraction and hydrocarbon processing
**Character:** Major frontier settlement; diverse, working-class, rough atmosphere

## Overview

Titan City is the Coalition's fuel engine. Nearly all of Coalition space depends on the methane and hydrocarbon products refined here. The city's population — miners, processors, traders, and transients — exists in service of that single economic function. Everything else, including Titan City's unusual political independence, flows from that.

Because no single House controls Titan City, the city operates with relative autonomy from Coalition governance. Multiple Houses share interests here, which produces a fractious local politics but also means no one faction can shut the fuel supply off unilaterally. That ambiguity is the city's greatest protection.

## Methane Production System

### Extraction

- **Atmospheric harvesting:** Processors remove methane from Titan's upper atmosphere
- **Surface collection:** Methane harvested from surface deposits and subsurface methane seas
- **Scale:** Thousands of extraction points operating continuously
- **Technology:** Advanced equipment; failure at this scale is catastrophic

### Processing & Distribution

- **Refineries:** State-of-the-art complexes; several are the largest structures on Titan
- **Products:** Various hydrocarbon products for different Coalition energy needs
- **Storage:** Massive tank farms holding refined fuel reserves
- **Distribution:** Continuous shipments to Mars, Luna, and outer system stations
- **Pricing leverage:** Titan City essentially sets Coalition fuel prices

## Infrastructure

- **Space elevator:** Critical connection between surface and orbital loading stations; vulnerability point
- **Refineries:** Massive, continuous-operation facilities dominating the city's eastern sectors
- **Orbital ports:** Docking facilities for fuel transfer to outbound ships
- **Transit network:** Road and rail connecting extraction fields to processing to docks
- **Entertainment district:** Significant gambling and nightlife economy — frontier workers spend credits locally

## Political Dynamics

- **Multi-House division:** [[Factions/Houses/House Silva|House Silva]], [[Factions/Houses/House Dalianis|House Dalianis]], and others share control, creating permanent tension
- **Labor power:** Working class is the majority; labor unrest is a recurring threat that all Houses take seriously
- **Coalition concern:** The Coalition is quietly developing backup fuel sources — depending entirely on Titan is a strategic vulnerability
- **Local independence:** Distance from Mars means slow Coalition response; the city enforces its own norms day-to-day

## Campaign Hooks

- Protecting or sabotaging the space elevator during a fuel crisis
- Labor dispute or strike action exploited by a rival House
- Corporate espionage targeting refinery technology
- Navigating multi-House political conflict over a distribution contract
- A shipment that contains more than fuel
