## Basic Data

**Gravity:** 0.38G
**Length of Day:** 24.6 hours
**Estimated Population:** 21.2 million
**Primary Control:** Coalition Government

## Overview

Mars stands as humanity's greatest achievement in terraforming and colonization. As the seat of the Coalition government and home to the Senate, Mars represents both the heights of human civilization and the fault lines where power, ideology, and survival intersect. The planet hosts eight major settlements, countless smaller habitats, and the heart of the off-world military apparatus.

## Major Settlements

### Olympus: The Capital
- **Location:** Olympus Mons (volcanic region)
- **Population:** Estimated 6+ million (largest urban center)
- **Administration:** Coalition Senate seat; primary governmental center
- **Geography:** Eight wards spreading across the volcanic shield
- **Control:** Technically neutral; effectively dominated by House Silva and House Dalianis
- **Character:** Impressive architecture; underground city extends deep into volcanic tunnels

**Government & Administration:**
- **Senate building:** Rostra (massive fortress-like structure)
- **Monitors:** 50+ permanently stationed in city
- **Oversight level:** Extreme; most monitored location in Coalition space
- **Security:** Coalition military presence heavier than some outer worlds
- **Bureaucracy:** Government administration spanning all eight wards

**Wards:**
- **Ward 1 (Senatorial):** Senate and government administration buildings
- **Ward 2-8:** Residential, commercial, industrial, and support infrastructure
- **Underground:** Extensive tunnel systems extending down into volcanic substrate
- **Population spread:** Millions distributed across eight distinct sectors

**Social Character:**
- **Politics:** Center of Coalition factional maneuvering
- **Elite concentration:** Highest proportion of Apotheosized elites
- **Class divisions:** Stark contrast between Ward 1 elites and outer ward workers
- **Crime:** Sophisticated, political; tied to Shadow War machinations

**Unique Features:**
- **Archives:** Coalition historical records and knowledge repositories
- **Museums:** Exhibits on Consolidation Wars, early Mars colonization
- **Research:** Governmental and academic institutions concentrated here
- **Industry:** Administrative and financial services (not manufacturing)

### New Turin: House Turin's Dark Kingdom
- **Location:** Mare Acidalium region
- **Population:** Estimated 1.2 million
- **Control:** House Turin (Russian Mafia/Bratva)
- **Status:** Technically subordinate to Coalition; effectively independent
- **Character:** Lawless frontier; Russian criminal culture dominates

**Criminal Economy:**
- **Primary industries:** Illegal weapons manufacturing, drug production, mercenary recruitment
- **Black market:** Largest in Coalition space; handles stolen goods and forbidden tech
- **Operational base:** HQ for Rings of Olympus and House Turin operations
- **Connections:** Smuggling routes to apostate colonies and fringe worlds
- **Security:** Heavily guarded against Coalition inspection

**Physical Layout:**
- **Old tunnels:** Original mining infrastructure repurposed for operations
- **Modern additions:** New construction for black market facilities
- **Outer perimeter:** Legitimate business facade (mining, manufacturing cover)
- **Inner city:** Brutal hierarchy; Mafia leadership openly visible

**The Bratva Control:**
- **Boss:** Viktor Kozlov (House Turin leader)
- **Operations:** Weapons, drugs, assassinations, kidnapping, slavery
- **Rule:** Personal loyalty and violence
- **Outsiders:** Tolerated if profitable; eliminated if threatening
- **Culture:** Old-school Russian organized crime with modern technology

**Notable Establishments:**
- **The Red Hand:** Largest casino and criminal headquarters
- **Slavic Quarter:** Original Russian settlement; most traditional area
- **Manufacturing district:** Openly criminal weapons factories
- **Docks:** Smuggling routes; off-books ship repair facilities

### Remus: The Water Gate
- **Location:** Amazonis Planitia (northern plain)
- **Population:** Estimated 800,000
- **Primary function:** Water distribution beanstalk terminal
- **Control:** House Cipriani
- **Strategic importance:** Critical infrastructure; points of control for Mars water supply

**The Beanstalk System:**
- **Function:** Moves Martian water to off-world locations
- **Capacity:** Supplies Luna, orbital stations, and outer planets
- **Infrastructure:** Massive terminal complexes on surface
- **Control point:** Remus serves as primary distribution hub
- **Economic importance:** Cipriani monopoly over water distribution

**Population Character:**
- **Workforce:** Mostly contract workers and temporary residents
- **Class:** Working to middle-class; no major elite presence
- **Stability:** Stable and relatively peaceful (water distribution is essential)
- **Economy:** Entirely dependent on beanstalk operations
- **Loyalty:** Generally pro-Coalition (efficient government service)

### Helios: The Floating City
- **Location:** High altitude above Tharsis bulge
- **Population:** Estimated 600,000
- **Setting:** Floating city platforms in thin upper atmosphere
- **Control:** Mixed House influence (primarily Silva and Dalianis)
- **Character:** Beautiful; refuge for philosophers, artists, and scholars

**Unique Features:**
- **Floating platforms:** Suspended by antigrav or atmospheric buoyancy systems
- **Atmosphere:** Relatively breathable with filtration (easier than surface)
- **Culture:** Artistic and intellectual hub; university systems
- **Recreation:** Spectacular views; zero-gravity research opportunities
- **Population:** Highly educated; research scientists and academics

**Functions:**
- **Universities:** Several major academic institutions
- **Research:** Non-military scientific research and development
- **Philosophy:** Center for ideological discussion (Apostate sympathies common)
- **Arts:** Theater, music, visual arts communities

### Charon: Genesis Project Operations
- **Location:** Hellas Planitia basin
- **Population:** Estimated 400,000
- **Primary function:** Terraforming research and development
- **Control:** House Jinzhan (agricultural biotech)
- **Project name:** Genesis Project (long-term Mars terraforming)

**Genesis Project Focus:**
- **Objective:** Make Mars fully terraformable through advanced biotech
- **Methods:** Genetic engineering, atmospheric processors, microbial engineering
- **Forests:** Genesis forests planted across large tracts (experimental)
- **Results:** Slow but measurable progress over decades
- **Timeline:** Centuries required for full completion

**Notable Features:**
- **Research labs:** Massive biotech research complexes
- **Forest zones:** Experimental planted areas with genetically modified trees
- **Atmosphere processors:** Giant machines processing Martian air
- **Testing grounds:** Terraforming technologies deployed on test plots

### Minos: The Ghost City
- **Location:** Syrtis region
- **Population:** Estimated 200,000 (primarily apostates in hiding)
- **Status:** Former mining center; largely abandoned by Coalition
- **Control:** De facto apostate enclave
- **Character:** Lawless; minimal Coalition authority

**History:**
- **Original:** Important mining settlement during early terraforming
- **Decline:** Mining exhausted; Coalition relocated operations
- **Current:** Repurposed as illegal apostate settlement
- **Status:** Coalition officially acknowledges but doesn't enforce law
- **Economy:** Black market, salvage, and illegal manufacturing

**Apostate Operations:**
- **Population:** Mix of refugees and criminals seeking freedom from HPA
- **Leadership:** Decentralized; no single authority
- **Infrastructure:** Mix of maintained structures and abandoned ruins
- **Economy:** Salvage, manufacturing, and illegal services
- **Security:** Minimal; residents defend own interests

### Phobos: The Factory Moon
- **Inhabitants:** Phobos orbits Mars close to surface
- **Population:** Estimated 300,000
- **Operators:** House Dalianis and House Cipriani (co-management)
- **Function:** Manufacturing facilities and Resurrection Chamber network

**Manufacturing Base:**
- **Scale:** Significant warframe and weapons manufacturing
- **Specialization:** House Dalianis military contracts
- **Workforce:** Contracted workers; living conditions poor
- **Output:** Critical to Mars military operations

**Thetis Revival Labs:**
- **Function:** Advanced resurrection facilities for military personnel
- **Capacity:** Can resleve entire military units after battle
- **Technology:** Cutting-edge; uses both traditional and experimental methods
- **Access:** Restricted to Coalition and House members only
- **Security:** Highest level; heavily guarded against theft/infiltration

### Deimos: Military Moons
- **Inhabitants:** Smaller moon with two major installations
- **Population:** Estimated 150,000 combined
- **Control:** Mixed Coalition military oversight

**Kratos Installation:**
- **Function:** Military training facility; largest in Coalition space
- **Programs:** Pilot training, special forces, combat training
- **Facilities:** Simulation centers, obstacle courses, live-fire ranges
- **Population:** Constantly rotating military personnel
- **Strategic:** Trains all elite Coalition combat forces

**Voltair Installation:**
- **Function:** Religious military garrison
- **Population:** Primarily Catholic (surprising Coalition presence)
- **Character:** Strict discipline; religious observance integrated with military
- **Oddity:** Only significant Catholic military population in Coalition space
- **Purpose:** Unknown; possibly faith-based special operations unit

## Critical Locations

### The Asteroid Belt (Near Mars)
- **Position:** Orbiting in space near Mars
- **Function:** Mining, piracy, and prison facilities
- **Key locations:** Tartarus (prison), scattered pirate bases
- **Strategic:** Potential threat to Mars supply lines

## Campaign Hooks

- **Olympus intrigue:** Shadow War operations in government
- **New Turin infiltration:** Undercover work in Russian mob territory
- **Water politics:** Cistern control and distribution conspiracy
- **Genesis Project:** Infiltrating or aiding terraforming research
- **Minos apostate network:** Contact with stateless population
- **Phobos resurrection theft:** Stealing back-ups or destroying evidence
- **Military training:** Undertaking elite Kratos programs
- **Floating city mystery:** Investigating intellectual dissidents
