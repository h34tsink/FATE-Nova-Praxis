## Basic Data

**Location:** Amazonis Planitia, northern Mars
**Population:** ~800,000
**Primary Function:** Water distribution beanstalk terminal
**Primary Control:** [[Factions/Houses/House Cipriani|House Cipriani]]
**Character:** Working-class infrastructure city; essential, stable, politically quiet on the surface

## Overview

Remus exists because water moves through it. The city grew up around the terminus of the Martian water distribution beanstalk — massive infrastructure that moves Martian water reserves to Luna, orbital stations, and the outer planets. Without Remus functioning, the supply chain sustaining half of Coalition space begins to crack.

[[Factions/Houses/House Cipriani|House Cipriani]] controls Remus absolutely, and through it exercises quiet leverage over every settlement dependent on Martian water. This control is rarely discussed openly — Cipriani prefers to maintain the fiction that water distribution is a neutral Coalition service rather than a House monopoly.

## The Beanstalk System

- **Function:** Orbital transfer of processed Martian water to orbital stations and beyond
- **Capacity:** Primary supply for Luna, multiple orbital stations, and outer planet outposts
- **Terminal infrastructure:** Massive ground-side complexes handling intake, pressurization, and loading
- **Control point:** Remus is the primary distribution hub — the valve Cipriani controls
- **Economic significance:** Water monopoly rivals [[Locations/Sol System/Saturn|Titan City]]'s fuel monopoly in strategic weight

## Population & Character

- **Workforce dominant:** Mostly contract workers, engineers, and support staff — few elites
- **Class:** Solidly working-to-middle class; minimal House presence beyond management tier
- **Stability:** Among the more peaceful Martian settlements — essential services create social contract
- **Loyalty:** Generally pro-Coalition; the city runs smoothly and people know why
- **Culture:** Functional, transient, without much local identity — Remus is a job, not a home for most

## Strategic Importance

Remus is the chokepoint that makes House Cipriani's broader water leverage real. Control Remus, control the beanstalk. Control the beanstalk, and you have a quiet hand on the throat of every settlement that can't synthesize its own water.

A disruption at Remus — whether political, mechanical, or violent — ripples outward within days across the Coalition's supply chain.

## Campaign Hooks

- Sabotage or protection of the beanstalk terminal during escalating House tensions
- Labor dispute among contract workers exploited by a rival House
- Cipriani internal politics — management succession, covert investigation of a distribution diversion
- A water shipment that contains something that shouldn't be there
