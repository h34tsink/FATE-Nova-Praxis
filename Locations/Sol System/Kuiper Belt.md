## Basic Data

**Location:** Beyond Neptune's orbit, ~30–50 AU from Sol
**Character:** Extreme frontier — sparse habitation, high autonomy, minimal Coalition enforcement
**Primary Settlement:** [[Locations/Sol System/Pluto]] (largest permanent population in the region)
**Economic Role:** Rare mineral extraction, deep-space observation, fringe logistics

## Overview

The Kuiper Belt marks the practical edge of Coalition civilization. Beyond Neptune, the density of habitation drops sharply, Coalition patrols grow infrequent, and the settlements that exist here operate with a degree of independence that would be impossible closer to Mars. That independence cuts both ways — Kuiper Belt stations are also largely on their own when things go wrong.

The belt itself is a vast disk of icy bodies, dwarf planets, and debris stretching from Neptune outward. Most of it is uninhabited and unmapped in detail. The settlements that exist here are small, self-sufficient out of necessity, and accustomed to long communication delays with the inner system.

## Notable Locations

- **[[Locations/Sol System/Pluto]]** — Largest permanent settlement in the Kuiper Belt; three communities totaling ~12,000; scientific research and rare mineral extraction
- **Charon Crossing** — Transit station between Pluto and its large moon Charon; small administrative and resupply hub
- **Scattered stations** — Small independent outposts for mining operations, deep-space relay, and fringe commerce; most are unlisted

## Strategic Character

The Coalition maintains nominal jurisdiction over Kuiper Belt settlements but exercises it rarely. The practical authority is local — station commanders, mining consortium bosses, and whoever can enforce their position on a given rock.

This makes the belt attractive for:

- Off-grid operations that need distance from Coalition surveillance
- Long-term research projects that can't get Coalition funding or approval
- Individuals and groups who want to exist at the edge of known space without disappearing entirely

## Environmental Conditions

- **Distance:** Communication lag to Mars is measured in hours, not minutes
- **Supply:** Self-sufficiency is mandatory; resupply missions are infrequent
- **Temperature:** Extreme cold; all habitation is sealed and heated
- **Isolation:** Emergencies must be handled locally — Coalition response time is measured in weeks

## Campaign Hooks

- A station in the belt that's gone silent — communication failure or something worse
- Fringe research project that the Coalition doesn't know about and wouldn't approve
- Long-haul smuggling run using Kuiper Belt waypoints to avoid inner-system scrutiny
- Something found in the debris field that shouldn't be there
