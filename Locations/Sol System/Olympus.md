## Basic Data

**Location:** Olympus Mons, Mars
**Gravity:** 0.38G
**Population:** 6+ million
**Status:** Coalition capital; most politically important location in known space

## Overview

Olympus stands as humanity's greatest political and social achievement—and its most dangerous power nexus. Built into and beneath Olympus Mons, the capital city sprawls across multiple levels and wards, hosting the Coalition government, countless corporate interests, and military apparatus. More Monitors dwell here than anywhere else in Coalition space, and the city serves as the primary battleground for the Shadow War between Houses.

## The Eight Wards

### Ward 1: The Senatorial District
- **Heart:** Rostra fortress and government administration
- **Function:** Coalition Senate chambers, judiciary, bureaucracy
- **Population:** Estimated 500,000 (officials, staff, elite residents)
- **Architecture:** Imposing fortress-like structures; opulent interiors
- **Access:** Heavily restricted; guards at every entry point
- **Atmosphere:** Formal, political, dangerous

**Rostra Fortress:**
- **Scale:** Massive multi-level underground fortress
- **Levels:** Extends hundreds of meters beneath surface
- **Senate:** Primary legislative chamber with 300+ seats
- **Security:** Extremely high; multiple redundant systems
- **Defenses:** Can be sealed and defended against internal threats

**Government Offices:**
- **Administration:** Departments and ministries
- **Finance:** Budget and economic planning
- **Military:** Overall strategy and operations
- **Security:** Internal security apparatus (including Monitors)

### Wards 2-8: The Districts
- **Combined population:** 5.5+ million
- **Mixed functions:** Residential, commercial, industrial, support services
- **Character:** Massive underground city; sprawling complexity
- **Organization:** Each ward maintains semi-autonomous governance
- **Infrastructure:** Interconnected by tunnels, elevators, transportation systems

**Ward 2 (Commerce & Trade):**
- **Function:** Primary commercial district
- **Facilities:** Markets, banks, trading houses, merchant guilds
- **Population:** 600,000+
- **Business:** Coalition-wide commerce headquarters
- **Culture:** Money and deals dominate

**Ward 3 (Residential - Upper Class):**
- **Housing:** Luxury residences for elite classes
- **Population:** 400,000
- **Service:** Support staff, servants, workers
- **Security:** Gated and monitored communities
- **Amenities:** High-end shopping, dining, entertainment

**Ward 4 (Academic & Research):**
- **Universities:** Coalition universities and research institutions
- **Population:** 500,000+
- **Focus:** Science, engineering, medicine, law
- **Library:** Massive archives of Coalition knowledge
- **Employment:** Academic and research positions

**Ward 5 (Industrial & Manufacturing):**
- **Production:** Secondary industrial center (weapons, equipment, parts)
- **Population:** 700,000
- **Working conditions:** Difficult but better than outer Mars
- **Contracts:** Primarily Coalition government work
- **Character:** Heavily unionized; better-paid than outer cities

**Ward 6 (Military Services):**
- **Barracks:** Military housing and training facilities
- **Population:** 800,000+
- **Focus:** Officer training, advanced military education
- **Facilities:** Simulation centers, training grounds
- **Elite:** Home of Coalition's best military personnel

**Ward 7 (Entertainment & Recreation):**
- **Gambling:** Major casinos and gambling establishments
- **Nightlife:** Bars, clubs, restaurants, entertainment venues
- **Population:** 500,000
- **Economy:** Service industry; entertainment and hospitality
- **Shadow War:** Hidden battleground for espionage and intrigue

**Ward 8 (Residential - Working Class):**
- **Housing:** Standard residential units for workers and middle-class
- **Population:** 1.5+ million
- **Jobs:** Support services, manufacturing, trade workers
- **Conditions:** Adequate but crowded; minimal frills
- **Community:** Lower crime than elite areas; more communal culture

## Underground Infrastructure

### The Tunnel System
- **Extent:** Massive interconnected tunnel network extends deep below city
- **Depth:** Tunnels extend hundreds of meters into Olympus Mons substrate
- **Original:** Created by volcanic activity; shaped and expanded by humans
- **Navigation:** Complex; easy to get lost for newcomers
- **Secrets:** Rumored to contain hidden chambers and old facilities

### Surface Access Points
- **Limited:** Only controlled access to surface
- **Airlocks:** Multiple redundant airlock systems for atmospheric sealing
- **Restrictions:** Surface exposure extremely limited; dangerous and monitored
- **Emergency:** Redundant escape routes exist in case of siege

## Political Reality

### The Shadow War in Olympus
- **Theaters:** Olympus is primary Shadow War battleground
- **Espionage:** Constant intelligence operations between Houses
- **Assassination:** Regular political and commercial murders
- **Cover:** Government oversight provides plausible deniability
- **Impact:** Political decisions shaped by factional interests rather than merit

### Monitor Presence
- **Quantity:** 50+ Monitors permanently stationed
- **Purpose:** Official: Enforce Human Personhood Accord; Actual: Monitor factional interests
- **Reality:** Cannot fully police city; often complicit in Shadow War
- **Authority:** Technically above House interests; often serve them
- **Credibility:** Public uncertain whether Monitors serve justice or power

### House Influence
- **Distribution:** No single House dominates; coalition of interests
- **Dominants:** House Silva and House Dalianis wield most influence
- **Competition:** Constant maneuvering for advantage
- **Openness:** Shadow War relatively open; political assassinations normal
- **Vulnerability:** Factional conflict creates security gaps for outsiders

## Critical Facilities

### Archives
- **Function:** Coalition historical records and knowledge repositories
- **Access:** Restricted; requires authorization
- **Contents:** Government records, scientific knowledge, historical documents
- **Security:** Heavily guarded; important intelligence repository
- **Rumors:** Alleged to contain classified technical specifications

### Universities
- **Institutions:** Multiple Coalition universities and research centers
- **Focus:** Science, engineering, medicine, law, military strategy
- **Faculty:** Best minds in known space
- **Students:** Elite training ground for future government/military leaders
- **Research:** Significant ongoing projects in various fields

### Museums
- **Exhibits:** Consolidation Wars, early Mars colonization, Coalition history
- **Purpose:** Public relations and citizen indoctrination
- **Tourist:** Major attraction; helps legitimize Coalition government
- **Content:** Curated to emphasize Coalition success and necessity

## Campaign Hooks

- **Shadow War operation:** Working for one House against another
- **Military training:** Undertaking advanced Coalition combat training
- **Underground exploration:** Discovering secrets in tunnel system
- **Academic intrigue:** University politics and research theft
- **Rostra infiltration:** Stealing government documents or secrets
- **Ward intrigue:** Navigating complex social and political networks
