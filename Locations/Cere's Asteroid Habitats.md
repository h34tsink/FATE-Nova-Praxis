# Cere's Asteroid Habitats

The physical structure underlying [[Locations/Cere|Cere Mining Colony]] — a cluster of small captured asteroids connected by pressurized tunnels, docking spars, and modular habitat pods. Not a designed station; an accretion of need and improvisation built over decades of mining operations.

## Lore

### Structure

The cluster consists of five to seven primary asteroid bodies (the count depends on whether you consider the smallest fragments separate bodies or debris). Each has been partially excavated for mining, then repurposed as habitation and infrastructure expanded. Connecting structures range from proper pressurized corridors to emergency-sealed cargo tubes that were never meant to be permanent.

Key structural features:
- **Docking spars:** External arms extending from the largest asteroid bodies. Berths for small-to-mid-sized vessels. No capital ship capacity. Most seals are functional; some require manual cycling.
- **Habitat pods:** Modular living units bolted to asteroid surfaces or wedged into excavated cavities. Range from standard-issue Colony prefabs to improvised shelters built from shipping containers and mining equipment.
- **Mining shafts:** The original infrastructure. Deep excavations following ore veins into the asteroid cores. Active shafts still produce; abandoned shafts form the colony's hidden geography.
- **Connecting tunnels:** The circulatory system. Pressurized corridors of varying quality linking the asteroid bodies. Some are stable and maintained. Others are one bad seal from vacuum.

### Hidden Infrastructure

What makes Cere valuable to the [[Glossary/Talons of Freedom|Talons]] and other off-grid groups is the gap between official and actual geography. The colony's layout was never fully mapped — mining operations created passages that were sealed, forgotten, and rediscovered. This means:

- **Hidden docking bays:** Berths concealed within asteroid shadow zones or accessed through mining tunnels. Ships can arrive and depart without appearing on the main docking logs.
- **Unmapped passages:** Connecting routes between asteroid bodies that bypass monitored corridors. Known to long-term residents, invisible to visitors.
- **Dead zones:** Areas with no mesh coverage, no atmosphere monitoring, and no power grid connection. Perfect for hiding. Dangerous for everyone.
- **Signal isolation:** The asteroid rock itself blocks most transmissions. This makes certain areas naturally shielded from digital surveillance — and from rescue calls.

### Talon Connection

The Talons of Freedom don't control Cere. They don't need to. The colony's structure and culture provide natural cover for their operations:

- Sympathizers in the population protect Talon-adjacent visitors without being asked
- Hidden docking routes allow discreet arrivals and departures
- The unmapped passages serve as emergency escape routes
- Cere's general hostility to [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] authority means nobody cooperates with inspection teams

This isn't a stronghold — it's a place that's structurally inconvenient to surveil and culturally inclined to mind its own business.

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Hidden Refuge in Hostile Rock*
- *Talon-Friendly, Nobody's Loyal*
- *What the Maps Don't Show Will Keep You Alive*
- *The Rock Itself Is Armor Against Surveillance*

### Navigation

Moving through the habitats requires local knowledge or time. Without a guide:
- **Main corridors:** No roll needed, but you're visible and logged
- **Secondary passages:** Investigate or Contacts to find. Moderate difficulty. Avoids most surveillance
- **Hidden routes:** Requires a local contact (Kal, Seren, or equivalent) or an exceptional Investigate roll. Hard difficulty. Off-grid movement
