# New Montreal

**Summary:** The largest known [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] settlement in human space — an underground city of more than 1.2 million carved into the bedrock of [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]] in the HD413 system. New Montreal is the closest the Apostate movement has to a permanent capital: an industrial, cultural, and political center that runs without [[Glossary/CID|CID]], without [[Glossary/Compilers|Coalition compilers]], and without anyone's permission.

## Lore

### Origin

The original colony was a survival shelter — a few thousand refugees who had ridden the [[Locations/Santa Maria|Santa Maria flotilla]] out of Sol space and finally found a planet they could land on. Sanctuary was hot, dry, and inhospitable on the surface, so they went down. Hundreds of meters into the rock, where temperature was stable and Coalition surveillance couldn't find them, they began to dig.

Generations later, that emergency shelter is a city.

### The Underground City

New Montreal extends through a multi-layered lattice of natural caverns and constructed chambers. The deepest residential levels are kilometers below the surface; the upper levels merge with covered surface parks and sealed atrium structures. The city has its own districts the way a planetside city has its own districts — not by neighborhood but by depth.

**Commercial layer.** Markets, [[Glossary/Gold|gold]]-denominated trade, fixers, brokers, and the public face of New Montreal's economy. Where outsiders go.

**Residential layers.** Housing for over a million people. Quality varies wildly — some chambers are spacious and well-lit, with engineered atriums and hydroponic gardens; others are cramped, hot, and stacked. The longer a family has been on Sanctuary, the higher up they typically live.

**Industrial deep.** Manufacturing, ship-component fabrication, weapon production, and the heavy machinery that keeps the city alive. Loud, hot, and dangerous. New Montreal builds its own ships, its own weapons, and its own consumer goods at a scale that competes with House production.

**Agricultural ring.** Hydroponic farms layered through the city's middle levels, supplemented by surface farms in shielded enclosures. New Montreal feeds itself, with surplus exported to other [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] settlements.

**Administrative core.** The council chambers, the negotiation halls, the disputed offices of an ever-evolving consensus government.

**The pits.** The bare-knuckle fighting circuits of New Montreal are infamous across [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] space. The pits are where the *Cascade of Endless Edges* blade form was refined — the city's pit fighters claim Mala Voss as a folk hero, though whether she actually existed is the kind of question the pits don't answer. (See [[Rules and Mechanics/Blade-Forms]].)

### Government

Consensus-based, decentralized, and chronically argumentative. A council of 30-50 representatives from the city's districts and the surrounding [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]] settlements meets in the administrative core to make collective decisions. There is no permanent executive. There is no police force in the Coalition sense. Disputes are handled at the district level by elected mediators.

The system works — with friction. Recent years have seen a slow drift toward stronger central authority, driven partly by external pressure (the Coalition is no longer ignoring [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]] the way it used to) and partly by the rapid expansion of [[Locations/Exoplanets/Valhalla|Valhalla]] on the secondary continent. Some New Montreal factions are openly worried about an authoritarian drift. Others think the drift is overdue.

### Culture

New Montreal is loud, multilingual, hot in the deeper levels, and culturally distinct from anywhere in Coalition space. There is no [[Glossary/CID|CID]] here. There are no [[Glossary/Compilers|compilers]]. People work, trade, build, fight, argue, and bury their dead in ways the Coalition would find primitive and the people of New Montreal would call free. Augmentation is unrestricted. Uploading and forking are unrestricted. Every taboo the Coalition built into the [[Glossary/HPA|HPA]] is openly violated here, often as a matter of pride.

The texture is industrial-organic: stone and steel, biolume and tungsten, hydroponic green and pit-fight red. Everyone smells faintly of recycled air. Children grow up knowing the ceiling has weight.

### Strategic Importance

New Montreal is the largest single concentration of Apostates anywhere. If the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] decides to deal with the Apostate movement militarily, this is the target. The city's defenders know it. Surface batteries, deep-shelter contingencies, and a rapidly expanding ship-construction program at [[Locations/Exoplanets/Valhalla|Valhalla]] are all responses to the same calculation: the Coalition will come eventually, and New Montreal needs to be ready.

### Travel and Access

- **Reaching New Montreal:** Requires a [[Locations/Exoplanets/Sanctuary|Sanctuary]] landing slot, which requires an Apostate sponsor or a smuggler willing to risk a covert insertion. The Coalition does not maintain a jump ring connection to HD413.
- **Moving inside the city:** Trams, lifts, and walking corridors. Outsiders need a guide on first arrival or get hopelessly lost.
- **Leaving the city:** Easier than arriving, but the city tracks who departs and where they go. New Montreal has long memory.

### On the Ground

- **No Coalition infrastructure:** Compilers do not work without [[Glossary/CID|CID]]. Trade in [[Glossary/Gold|gold]], favors, and physical goods. SINC and Hacking checks against Coalition systems are at -2 from inside the city — the mesh is parochial.
- **Pit culture:** A character with combat training can find a pit fight in any district. Reputation in the pits opens doors. Failing in the pits closes them.
- **Council politics:** Rapport and Lore to navigate the consensus-government negotiation halls. Outsiders have no formal standing but can be granted hearings.
- **Surveillance pressure:** Coalition intelligence has known and unknown assets in New Montreal. Anyone arriving with a Coalition background draws attention from both sides.
