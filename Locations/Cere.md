# Cere

A failing mining colony carved into a cluster of captured asteroids at the edge of viable habitation. Cere is the kind of place the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] built when resources mattered more than people, and then forgot about when the margins dropped. What's left is a patchwork settlement of miners, refugees, fixers, and people who don't want to be found — held together by duct tape, recycled atmosphere, and mutual distrust.

## Lore

### Geography and Structure

Cere isn't a single station — it's a cluster of small asteroids connected by pressurized tunnels, docking spars, and jury-rigged habitat pods. The original mining infrastructure forms the skeleton: excavated shafts, ore processing bays, and transport corridors. Civilian habitation grew around it like scar tissue, filling gaps with repurposed cargo containers, sealed-off shaft junctions, and improvised living spaces.

The colony has no central authority worth the name. Docking operations are managed by local fixers. Interior corridors follow mining logic, not urban planning — you navigate by shaft numbers and junction markers, not street names. Power and atmosphere are distributed through aging systems that fail in sections on a regular basis.

### Environment

- **Air quality:** Recycled and metallic. Scrubbers run past their service life. Bad air pockets form in dead-end shafts and sealed sections. The taste never leaves your mouth.
- **Gravity:** Variable. The asteroid cluster doesn't spin uniformly. Some corridors feel heavy, others light. The transitions are disorienting for newcomers.
- **Lighting:** Industrial yellow or nothing. Flickering is normal. Extended blackouts in abandoned sections are common.
- **Temperature:** Ranges from cold (near exterior walls and docking bays) to oppressively warm (near reactor clusters and ore furnaces).

### Population

Cere's population is transient and stratified:

- **Miners:** The original workforce. Hard-edged, territorial, distrustful of outsiders. Some have been here for decades.
- **Refugees:** Displaced by [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] crackdowns, House conflicts, or economic collapse. Cluster in the refugee quarters. Scared, resourceful, and invisible to official records.
- **Fixers and brokers:** The informal economy. Docking fees, information trades, supply chains, and favors. [[GM AI/Entity Cards/R2/Kal Paddock (R2 Minor Contact)|Kal Paddock]] is the most visible of these.
- **Talon sympathizers:** The [[Glossary/Talons of Freedom|Talons of Freedom]] have deep roots here. Not an official presence — more a shared attitude and a network of favors.
- **Transients:** Smugglers, salvagers, and people passing through who don't want questions asked.

### Economy

Cere's mining output has declined for years. What keeps the colony alive is its position as a waypoint for off-grid traffic and its willingness to look the other way. Credits work, but favors are the real currency. Information is the most valuable commodity — who arrived, who left, who's asking questions, and who paid to stop them.

## Key Locations

- **[[Locations/Cere/Deepshaft Tavern]]** — Social hub, information exchange, and Kal Paddock's base of operations
- **Core Markets** — The commercial center; vendors, comm brokers, supply traders, and a few operations without signs
- **Refugee Quarters** — Where the displaced hide and wait. Dense, quiet, and protective of its own
- **Docking Intake** — The entry point. Fee collectors, manifest scanners, and the first layer of assessment
- **Mining Shafts (active)** — Still-operational tunnels. Dangerous work, territorial crews, industrial hazards
- **Abandoned Mining Access** — Sealed-off and forgotten shafts. No surveillance, no mesh, no help. Where people go to disappear
- **Shadow Relay Node** — Repurposed comms infrastructure in a disused maintenance corridor. Tapped by multiple parties

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Failing Infrastructure, Recycled Everything*
- *Nobody's Glad You're Here*
- *Information Is Currency, Silence Is Armor*
- *Patchwork Comms — Old Signals, New Enemies*

### Environmental Hazards

- **Bad air pockets:** Overcome (Athletics or Notice) to detect before entering. Failure = stress or *Choking* aspect
- **Unstable sections:** Shifting corridors, collapsing tunnel supports. Athletics to navigate, or spend time finding a stable route
- **Power failures:** Sections go dark without warning. Notice to anticipate, or operate blind
- **Variable gravity:** -1 to physical actions in transitional zones unless acclimated

### Digital Environment

Cere's mesh network is patchy, old, and compromised. Multiple parties tap the local comms. SINC rolls at -1 in most areas, -2 in abandoned sections. Network queries leave traces that are hard to scrub.
