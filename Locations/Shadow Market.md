# Shadow Market

Not a place — a network. The Shadow Market is a distributed economy of information brokers, smugglers, and traders operating outside [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] law. It has no headquarters, no leadership structure, and no membership list. It exists wherever two people agree to trade something the Coalition doesn't want traded, using channels the Coalition doesn't control.

## Lore

### What It Is

The Shadow Market is the informal name for the intersecting networks of black-market operators, data brokers, smugglers, and fixers who facilitate commerce and information exchange beyond Coalition oversight. It's not a faction in the political sense — nobody swears allegiance to the Shadow Market. It's a *practice*: a set of protocols, trust chains, and communication channels that allow transactions to happen invisibly.

Key characteristics:
- **Distributed:** No central hub. Operations happen on stations, colonies, ships, and through encrypted mesh channels. Where there's demand and someone willing to supply, a Shadow Market transaction can happen.
- **Trust-chain access:** You don't find the Shadow Market. Someone who's already in it introduces you. Access is gated by reputation and referral. Cold approaches fail or get you robbed.
- **Favor-based economy:** Credits work, but the real currency is obligation. A favor owed to a Shadow Market operator is a debt that compounds — and can be called in at the worst possible time.
- **Competitive, not cooperative:** Runners and brokers compete with each other. There's no code of honor — just mutual interest in keeping the channels open. Betrayal happens, but operators who burn too many contacts stop getting access.

### What It Trades

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Information** | Shipping manifests, personnel records, faction intelligence, location data, identity packages |
| **Contraband** | Restricted augmentations, unlicensed sleeves, controlled substances, weapons above civilian grade |
| **Services** | Identity forgery, mnemonic editing referrals, transport off-grid, message relay, digital scrubbing |
| **Access** | Docking clearances, security bypasses, introductions to faction contacts, safe passage through controlled zones |

### How It Operates

Shadow Market transactions follow a pattern:

1. **Contact:** A runner or broker makes themselves available in a location — a bar, a market stall, a dead-drop point. They don't advertise. You know they're there because someone told you, or because you know the signals.
2. **Assessment:** The operator sizes up the buyer. Can they pay? Are they a risk? Are they being followed? This happens before any deal is discussed.
3. **Offer:** The operator names a price — usually in favors or future obligations, not just credits. The price reflects the risk to the operator, not just the value of the goods.
4. **Delivery:** The transaction completes through a method that minimizes exposure for both parties. Dead drops, intermediaries, encrypted data transfers, or face-to-face in a controlled space.
5. **Obligation:** If the price included a favor, it remains outstanding until called in. The operator decides when and how. Defaulting on a Shadow Market obligation burns your access network-wide.

### Presence on Cere

[[Locations/Cere|Cere]] is a natural Shadow Market node. The colony's failing official infrastructure, transient population, and general hostility to Coalition oversight create ideal conditions:

- Runners operate openly in the Core Markets, identifiable only to those who know what to look for
- Dead-drop points exist throughout the colony — data caches, physical stashes, message boards disguised as maintenance logs
- The [[Locations/Cere/Deepshaft Tavern]] is a common contact point, though [[GM AI/Entity Cards/R2/Kal Paddock (R2 Minor Contact)|Kal Paddock]] enforces his "no faction business on the floor" rule
- The colony's patchy mesh network makes digital transactions harder to trace

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Web of Favors, Chain of Debts*
- *You Don't Find Us — We Decide You're Worth Finding*
- *The Price Is Never Just Credits*
- *Competitive, Not Cooperative*

### Accessing the Shadow Market

- **With a referral (Contacts roll, moderate):** Someone in the PCs' network can point them to a runner. The roll determines how clean the introduction is — success means a straightforward meeting, success with cost means the contact wants something first.
- **Cold approach (Investigate or Deceive, hard):** Finding a runner without a referral means reading signals, following supply chains, or bluffing your way in. High risk of being sized up as a mark instead of a client.
- **Through reputation:** If the PCs have a history of reliable dealings, operators may approach them. This is the best-case scenario — it means the network has decided you're worth the risk.

### The Favor Economy

When PCs accept a Shadow Market deal priced in favors, create an aspect: *Owes the Shadow Market a Favor*. This aspect can be:
- **Compelled** when the operator calls in the debt — usually at the worst possible time
- **Invoked** by the PCs if they need to demonstrate credibility within the network ("I'm already in — check my record")
- **Cleared** by fulfilling the obligation, which may itself create new complications

### Shadow Market Runners (Generic NPC)

When a runner appears in a scene, use these defaults:
- **Rank:** R2 (Minor NPC) unless the situation demands more
- **Tone:** Transactional, guarded, assessing
- **Motivation:** Profit and network preservation
- **Red line:** Anything that exposes the channel to Coalition attention

## Inventory — What the Shadow Market Trades

Availability depends on the node. A station with Talon connections stocks different goods than a port with House Kimura contractors passing through. The [[Glossary/Talons of Freedom|Talons of Freedom]] are one of the largest suppliers of black-market weapons and armaments network-wide.

**Cost** reflects the book value. Shadow Market markup varies — expect +1 to +3 Cost depending on scarcity, risk, and how badly the buyer needs it. Favors can offset credits.
