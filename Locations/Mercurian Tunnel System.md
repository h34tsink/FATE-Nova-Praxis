# Mercurian Tunnel System

**Summary:** An ancient, half-mapped lattice of pressurized tunnels and excavated chambers running beneath the surface of [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|Mercury]]. Originally cut for mining and infrastructure between [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|House Cipriani]]'s antimatter facilities, the tunnels grew far beyond their planned extent and became a refuge for the survivors of the Tellerus Fields, an [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] underground city of thousands, and a persistent thorn in the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]]'s side.

## Lore

### Origin

When [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|Mercury]] became the Coalition's energy heart, [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|House Cipriani]] cut tunnels for everything: ore extraction, thermal regulation, transit between solar fields and antimatter plants, hardened shelter against radiation events. Generations of expansion turned the tunnels into a planet-spanning lattice. Maps were kept by departments that no longer exist. Sections were sealed off after accidents and never reopened. Whole branches were dug by contractors who didn't share their schematics.

By the time [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|House Cipriani]] tried to consolidate control of the underground, it was already too late. The tunnel system had outgrown its makers.

### The Tellerus Survivors

The Battle of Tellerus Fields was the worst engagement of the post-Coalition wars — a remnant Earth force broken on Mercury's surface. Coalition records say the remnant army was destroyed. The tunnels say otherwise. Survivors went underground and didn't come back up. Generations later, their descendants are still down there, blended into the broader Apostate population that drifted to Mercury for the same reason: nobody can find you in tunnels nobody has mapped.

### The Underground

Today the tunnels host a sprawling underground community — thousands of [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostates]], remnant descendants, defaulters, fugitives, and people who simply prefer the dark. There is no central authority. Settlements form in hardened junctions, pressurized chambers, and the abandoned support shafts of disused antimatter plants. Some communities are small, secretive, and hostile to outsiders. Others run open markets that cater to smugglers willing to make the trip down.

Notable residents include [[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar "Silvermist" Blackwood]], who runs her acquisitions and grey-market business out of a tunnel safehouse and treats the system as her personal fortress.

### Texture

- **Air:** Recycled by patched scrubbers and stolen Cipriani filtration units. Quality varies by junction. Some chambers are clean enough for unaugmented humans; others require rebreathers.
- **Light:** Industrial work lamps, scavenged biolume, and long stretches of total dark between settlements.
- **Heat:** Mercury's surface is murderous in either direction. The tunnels are stable but warm — radiating heat from the antimatter plants above bleeds down into the upper levels.
- **Sound:** Rock movement, machinery vibration, and the omnipresent hum of [[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|Specular Spread]]'s power infrastructure echoing through the strata.
- **Navigation:** By junction code, by memory, by paid local guide. Outsiders without a guide get lost or worse.

### Strategic Significance

[[Locations/Sol System/Mercury|House Cipriani]] knows the tunnels exist. They cannot afford the manpower to fully clear them, and the Coalition cannot afford to alienate the population that mans the antimatter plants. The tunnel system is therefore tolerated, surveilled where possible, and periodically raided when a target becomes too visible. The Apostates know this. They keep their visibility low and their staging operations distributed.

The tunnels are also believed to be a coordination point for [[Factions/Hidden Agendas/Piracy and Insurgency|piracy operations]] against antimatter shipping — the recent escalation in pirate activity around Mercurial space is widely suspected to originate from cells operating out of the deeper tunnels.

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Nobody Has the Whole Map*
- *The Coalition Can't Afford to Look Too Hard*
- *Generations Underground*
- *Every Tunnel Leads Somewhere — Eventually*

### Environmental Hazards

- **Unmapped sections:** Investigate or Lore to know whether a tunnel goes anywhere useful. Failure = lost, wasted time, or worse.
- **Atmospheric drift:** Bad air pockets in sealed sections. Notice to detect; failure = stress or *Choking*.
- **Cave-ins:** Aging structural supports. Athletics to escape; Engineering to shore up.
- **Heat bleed:** Sections directly under antimatter plants run hot. Physique to endure long traversals; otherwise *Heatstroke* aspect.
- **Radiation pockets:** Where shielding has failed near old facilities. Notice to spot the warning signs; otherwise long-term consequence.

### Social Hazards

- **Wrong door:** Some communities shoot first. Rapport or Stealth to approach; failure = hostile encounter.
- **Owed favors:** Information costs favors. Anyone running through the tunnels accumulates a debt log.
- **Coalition raids:** Periodic and unpredictable. Notice to anticipate; Stealth to avoid being swept up.

### Digital Environment

The tunnels have a patched, parochial mesh — local nodes only, no Extranet bridge in most sections. SINC and Hacking checks at -2. A few chambers maintain contraband relays that can punch out to wider networks, usually run by fixers like [[Characters/Named NPCs/Briar Blackwood|Briar Blackwood]].
