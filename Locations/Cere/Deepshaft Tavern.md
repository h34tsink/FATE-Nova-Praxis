# Deepshaft Tavern

The closest thing [[Locations/Cere|Cere]] has to a public square — a low-ceilinged bar carved into an old mineshaft junction where information, favors, and cheap alcohol all flow through the same economy. Run by [[GM AI/Entity Cards/R2/Kal Paddock (R2 Minor Contact)|Kal Paddock]], who treats the space as neutral ground but doesn't pretend it's safe.

## Lore

### The Space

The Deepshaft occupies a wide junction where three mining shafts converge. The ceiling is low — reinforced rock, scarred by drilling. The bar itself is fabricated from salvaged mining equipment: a conveyor belt repurposed as a counter, ore cart seats bolted to the floor, lighting rigs pulled from shaft headframes and rewired to flicker in amber.

The layout serves Kal's purposes:
- **Main room:** Open seating, communal tables, the bar. This is where miners decompress and newcomers get sized up. Conversations carry — the acoustics are deliberate. *Everyone's Listening* isn't just an aspect; it's architecture.
- **Corner booths:** Slightly recessed alcoves cut into the rock walls. Not private — but angled so you can watch the room without being easily overheard. Kal holds one by default. Regulars hold others by reputation.
- **Back corridor:** A narrow passage behind the bar leading to storage, a small kitchen, and a locked room that Kal uses for business that doesn't happen in public. Access is by invitation.
- **Shaft access:** One of the three original mining shafts is still partially open behind a sealed hatch in the back. Kal controls the hatch. It's an emergency exit, a smuggling route, and leverage — all depending on who's asking.

### Atmosphere

The air smells like recycled breath, cheap alcohol, and machine oil. The lighting flickers — some of it intentional (Kal adjusts the dimming to signal mood or warn regulars), some of it just Cere's failing power grid. Background noise is constant: clink of glasses, murmur of conversation, the occasional hiss of atmospheric regulators cycling.

The clientele is mixed:
- Miners between shifts, drinking to forget the work
- Fixers and brokers making deals over small tables
- Refugees who've figured out that the Deepshaft is safer than most corners of Cere
- Transients who don't want questions and pay enough to avoid them
- Talon sympathizers who use the space as an informal meeting point without ever saying so

### Rules of the House

Kal doesn't post rules. He enforces them:
1. **Pay for what you take.** Drinks, information, time at a table. Credits or favors.
2. **Keep it quiet.** Loud problems become Kal's problems, and Kal solves his problems by making yours worse.
3. **No faction business on the floor.** You can be Talon, Coalition, Shadow Market, whatever — but you do your recruiting and your threats somewhere else.
4. **The back room is earned.** You don't ask for it. If Kal wants to talk privately, he'll move you there.

### Information Economy

The Deepshaft's real function is information exchange. Kal sees everyone who comes through Cere's docking intake and hears most of what happens on the colony floor. He doesn't sell information freely — he trades it, with careful attention to who benefits and what it costs him.

Other regulars also trade information, but on a smaller scale. The corner booths are where secondary deals happen: a miner who saw something, a dock runner with a shipping manifest, a refugee who overheard a conversation. The Deepshaft is a marketplace where the product is *knowing things*.

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Everyone's Listening*
- *Cheap Drinks, Expensive Answers*
- *Neutral Ground (Kal's Rules Apply)*

### Skills in Play

- **Contacts:** Finding the right person to ask. The Deepshaft is where you go to start a contact chain, not to end one.
- **Rapport:** Earning enough trust for a real answer. Most people here default to suspicion.
- **Resources:** Buying information, drinks, or access to the back corridor. Credits open doors; favors open better ones.
- **Notice:** Reading the room — who's watching, who flinched, who's pretending not to listen.
- **Deceive:** Asking questions without revealing why. The Deepshaft punishes obvious intent.

### Complications

- Other investigators (rivals, faction operatives) may be present and asking the same questions
- Loud or clumsy questioning triggers the *Everyone's Listening* aspect — information spreads to parties the PCs didn't intend
- Kal may warn competitors if the PCs are too obvious, not out of malice but to manage risk on his turf
