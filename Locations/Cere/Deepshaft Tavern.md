# Deepshaft Tavern

**Location:** [[Cere|Cere Mining Colony]]  
**Operator:** [[Kal Paddock]]  
**Type:** Information Hub / Refuge Shelter  
**Atmosphere:** Tense, distrustful, but hospitable to those who pay  

## Overview

The Deepshaft Tavern is the beating heart of Cere's underground social networks. It's where miners decompress, refugees hide from authorities, information brokers make deals, and the locals gather to exchange rumors. The walls are reinforced against tunnel collapses, the comm is relatively clean, and Kal keeps it neutral enough that most factions leave it alone—but not so neutral that serious business doesn't happen here.

The bar itself is made from salvaged mining equipment, the lights flicker in ways that might be intentional (for security), and the air smells like recycled breath, cheap alcohol, and desperation.

## Notable NPCs

- **[[Kal Paddock]]** — Owner and information broker; his word is law here

## Session 9 Scene 1 Role

The Deepshaft Tavern is the first stop for investigating **[[Valare|Valare's fork]]**. Kal has seen her, heard about her, and knows which questions trigger attention.

**Possible Scene Beats:**

- PCs enter and ask openly about Valare (alerts everyone, including [[Valare|Valare's programs]])
- PCs approach Kal quietly for information (costs a favor or resources)
- A [[Talons of Freedom|Talon]] sympathizer tests the PCs' intentions
- [[Shadow Market]] runners are also here, asking questions

## GM Notes

**Atmosphere Cues:**

- *Everyone's Listening* — Conversations carry weight; privacy is bought, not given
- *Information Has a Price* — Kal doesn't give anything away
- *Hidden Connections* — The tavern is where allegiances shift

**Complications:**

- Other investigators (rivals, factions) may be present
- Kal might warn rivals if the PCs are too obvious
- Local authorities might receive tips about suspicious activity

**Sample Interactions:**

- "Kal, you seem to know people. Ever heard of Valare?"
- "I need a place to lay low for a night."
- "What's the going rate for information about new arrivals?"
