# Parallax Station

**Summary:** A mid-sized independent station with deep [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] connections — the location where the stolen high-tech walker traced back in [[Sessions/Session 1/Session 1 - Game Summary|Session 1]] was being staged for the planned attack on [[Locations/Sol System/Europa Research Station|Europa Station]]. Operated, or at least dominated, by the network around Alaric "The Broker" Baines.

## Lore

### What It Is

Parallax is one of the small, semi-legitimate stations that drift in the regulatory grey zones of [[Locations/Sol System/_Overview|Sol space]] — the kind of place that exists on the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]] registry under a vague service designation, files its paperwork, pays its docking fees, and quietly runs a parallel economy in its lower decks. The official manifest is unremarkable. The actual traffic is anything but.

The station was the staging ground for the [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] cell that stole the prototype walker from a high-reputation Coalition citizen and intended to use it in a coordinated strike on [[Locations/Sol System/Europa Research Station|Europa Station]]. The walker was held in a hangar bay on the station when [[Characters/Players/Dr. Lorem (Doc) Ipsum/Dr. Lorem Ipsum - FATE Character|Doc]], [[Characters/Players/Kallius/Kallius - FATE Character|Kallius]], [[Characters/Players/Grace Pryzbylski/Grace Pryzbylski - FATE Character|Grace]], and [[Characters/Players/Dustin (Grift) Halloway/Dustin Halloway - FATE Character|Grift]] tracked it down and broke through the station's guards to reach it.

### The Broker's Network

Alaric "The Broker" Baines runs the kind of operation that doesn't show up on a station map. Information, smuggled hardware, sleeve trades, illegal augmentation work, and the occasional ego transfer for clients who don't want a paper trail. Parallax is one of his hubs — not the only one, but a significant one, with the docking access and the bay capacity to handle large stolen assets like a military-grade walker.

Baines's network on Parallax cooperates with [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] cells when their interests align, and the Session 1 walker theft was exactly that kind of alignment: Apostate operatives needed a place to stage and modify the stolen mech, and Baines needed the favor and the cut.

### Texture

- **Outer rings.** Civilian docking, public concourse, the legitimate-looking face of the station. Coffee bars, sleeve technicians, the occasional Coalition inspector going through motions.
- **Middle decks.** Cargo handling, warehouse leasing, light manufacturing. The legitimate-looking middle of the station.
- **Lower bays.** The hangars where things happen that aren't on any manifest. Heavy equipment, smuggled hardware, modified vehicles, and crew that doesn't take kindly to questions.
- **Side corridors.** Baines's people work in the spaces between official spaces — repurposed maintenance shafts, unlogged office space, and a couple of meeting rooms that no station schematic shows.

The station feels worn but functional. Brighter than [[Locations/Cere|Cere]], more legitimate than the [[Locations/Shadow Market|Shadow Market]], and considerably more dangerous than its docking fees suggest.

### Strategic Position

Parallax matters because it's *useful*. It has the docking capacity to handle large cargo. It's positioned where multiple traffic patterns intersect. It has lax enough oversight that contraband moves through it without much friction. And it has Baines, which means that anything that arrives at Parallax can be sold, modified, hidden, or moved on within hours.

For [[Factions/Ideologies/Apostates|Apostate]] cells operating in [[Locations/Sol System/_Overview|Sol space]], that combination is rare and valuable.

## Mechanics

### Aspects

- *Legitimate on the Manifest, Crooked in the Bays*
- *Baines's People Run the Side Corridors*
- *Everything Has a Price — and a Cut*
- *No Questions in the Lower Bays*

### Layout (zones)

- **Public Docking & Concourse** — Open access, light surveillance, civilian crowd
- **Middle Decks (Warehousing)** — Cargo flow, light security, leased space
- **Lower Bays (Hangars)** — Heavy equipment, restricted access, armed guards
- **Side Corridors (Baines's Network)** — Off-the-books offices and meeting rooms
- **Station Operations** — Control center, life support, official security

### On the Ground

- **Approaching the lower bays:** Stealth or forged credentials. Failure = guard encounter and an alert that propagates through the side corridors.
- **Negotiating with Baines's people:** Rapport at -1 if the PCs are clearly Coalition-aligned; favors and gold work better than threats.
- **Station guards:** Mid-tier private security plus Apostate operatives in the affected bays. Combat-capable, well-equipped, willing to escalate.
- **Mesh environment:** Standard Coalition mesh on the public decks; compromised and tapped in the side corridors. SINC checks at -1 in the lower bays.
