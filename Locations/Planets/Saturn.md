### Saturn Overview
- **Gravity:** 1.06G
- **Day Length:** 10.2 hours
- **Estimated Population:** 12.5 million

#### Notable Settlements/Locations

##### Titan City
- **Location:** On Saturn’s largest moon, Titan.
- **Role:** The largest city beyond the asteroid belt and a central hub for mining operations in Saturn’s rings.
- **Structure:** Comprised of concentric rings, each pressurized and environmentally controlled, with a central space elevator.
- **Economy:** Focuses on processing and shipping materials from Saturn’s rings and water extraction from Titan itself.
- **Societal Hierarchy:** The city is divided by socio-economic status, with high-Rep members and House dignitaries occupying the upper tiers, while the lower levels are home to the working class and apostates.

##### The Rings
- **Nature:** Composed of dust and water ice, Saturn’s rings are a major site for a multi-House mining operation.
- **Process:** Materials are scooped up, sorted, and processed, serving as a crucial resource for the Coalition.

#### Saturn's Role in the Coalition
- **Limited Surface Interest:** Saturn’s surface is of little strategic value due to its hostile environment.
- **Resource Hub:** Saturn’s rings and Titan are key locations for resource extraction, particularly water and raw materials, essential for sustaining Coalition operations.

#### Sociopolitical Dynamics on Titan City
- **Diverse Population:** Hosting a mix of members from various Houses, Titan City reflects the Coalition's diversity in a single urban setting.
- **Economic Engine:** The city’s primary focus on mining and processing operations makes it an economic powerhouse for the outer planets.
- **Class Disparity:** The stark contrast between the affluent upper tiers and the impoverished lower levels highlights ongoing social and economic challenges within the Coalition.
- **Security Concerns:** The presence of Lighthouse, a security firm known for its harsh tactics, underscores the complex balance between law enforcement and civil liberties in a high-stakes environment.
