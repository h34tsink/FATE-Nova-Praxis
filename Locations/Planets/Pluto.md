### Pluto Overview
- **Gravity:** 0.06G
- **Day Length:** 6.39 [[Earth]] days
- **Estimated Population:** 12,000

#### Notable Settlements/Locations

##### Persephone Station
- **Location:** A space station in orbit around Pluto.
- **Purpose:** Acts as a research and observation outpost, focusing on the Kuiper Belt and beyond.
- **Facilities:** Equipped with advanced telescopes and deep space sensors, the station is a hub for astronomers and scientists studying the outer reaches of the solar system.

##### Charon Crossing
- **Nature:** A settlement on Pluto's largest moon, Charon.
- **Function:** Primarily a mining colony extracting rare minerals from Charon's surface.
- **Community:** Populated by a mix of miners, engineers, and their families, Charon Crossing is a tight-knit community that has adapted to the extreme conditions of the outer solar system.

##### Erebos Depths
- **Characteristics:** An underground habitat on Pluto, named after the Greek god of darkness.
- **Innovation:** Utilizes geothermal energy from Pluto's interior for heating and power, showcasing human ingenuity in utilizing local resources in extreme environments.

#### Pluto's Role in the Coalition
- **Frontier Outpost:** Pluto serves as a frontier for scientific exploration and mining, representing humanity's reach into the furthest corners of the solar system.
- **Scientific Research:** With its unique position in the Kuiper Belt, Pluto is an ideal location for studying comets, distant celestial bodies, and the heliosphere.

#### Sociopolitical Dynamics
- **Isolated Community:** The small population on Pluto and its moon Charon forms an isolated but resilient community, capable of enduring the challenges of life on the edge of the solar system.
- **Strategic Importance:** Despite its remoteness, Pluto's settlements like Persephone Station and Charon Crossing play a strategic role in advancing human knowledge and resource extraction in space.
