# Nova Praxis Character Creation Guide

A comprehensive step-by-step guide for creating characters in Nova Praxis FATE.

---

## 📋 Character Creation Overview

Nova Praxis uses a structured character creation process that builds your transhuman character step-by-step. The process adapts based on your choices - certain steps appear only if relevant to your character type.

### Core Creation Steps

1. **Character Concept** - Define your identity through Aspects
2. **Character State** - Choose your form of existence (Pure/SIM/Sleeved)
3. **House Affiliation** - Select Coalition House or Apostate status  
4. **Skills** - Distribute skill ranks
5. **Reputation** - Calculate social standing *(House members only)*
6. **Stunts** - Select special abilities
7. **Savant Programs** - Configure nanobot programs *(Savant only)*
8. **Sleeve** - Design your artificial body *(Sleeved only)*
9. **Gear** - Select starting equipment
10. **Finalize** - Review and complete

## Step 1: Select Aspects

> [!info]- Purpose
> Aspects are used to describe important aspects of your character, and should describe parts of your character that you wish to be relevant to the campaign. 

### What Are Aspects?

An Aspect, whether a phrase or a quote, should always consist of **at least three to eight words**. You don't want it to be overly simple, but at the same time, you don't want it to be overly wordy. After all, you'll be calling out your Aspects in play—a mouthful of an Aspect may grow tiresome quickly.

Characters get **5 Aspects**, each of which should be a balance of both positive and negative whenever possible. They should work as both a way for your character to excel at his niche, and suffer in ways you find interesting and character-defining.

### The Aspect Alphabet

> [!tip]- Recommended for New Players
> While not required, it is recommended (especially for new players) to use the **Aspect Alphabet technique**. The Aspect Alphabet uses each of the first five letters of the alphabet to define a specific type of Aspect. This technique helps produce believable and well-rounded characters that have goals, methods of reaching those goals, and reasons for not having already reached them.

> [!example]- **A** is for **Ambition**
> **What are your goals? What drives you to put your life, reputation, and sanity on the line?**
> 
> **Examples:**
> - "I'll make captain one day!"
> - Obsessed with Becoming a Sensie Star
> - Walking the Long Road to Ascension

> [!example]- **B** is for **Belief**
> **What do you believe in? What philosophies guide your actions and make you the person you are?**
> 
> **Examples:**
> - "A SIM isn't human. It's just delusional software."
> - "I'd lay down my life for the Coalition."
> - Suspicious of Technology and the Horrors it Can Unleash

> [!example]- **C** is for **Connection**
> **What person, place, or event still plays a large role in your life? Is there someone you can always depend on? Was there an event in your life that made you who you are?**
> 
> **Examples:**
> - Grew up on the Streets of Olympus
> - Trained by Targe Gamarrol, Praetorian of House Tsarya
> - "I saw the technophage with my own eyes!"

> [!example]- **D** is for **Disadvantage**
> **Why haven't you achieved your goals already? What weaknesses, addictions, compulsions, or challenges stand in your way?**
> 
> **Examples:**
> - Soft Spot for the Damsel in Distress
> - "Never mind the apostates. They're expendable."
> - Crippling Fear of Space Travel

> [!example]- **E** is for **Expertise**
> **What are you good at? How do you go about overcoming obstacles? In a broad sense, what methods do you employ to succeed in life?**
> 
> **Examples:**
> - Smuggler with a Heart of Gold
> - "I've never met a system I couldn't crack."
> - Silver-Tongued Devil Always Gets His Way

### Important: Aspect Scope Rules

> [!warning]- Don't Duplicate Aspects
> Remember the rules for scopes: **more than one of the same type of Aspect cannot be invoked at the same time**. 
> 
> Keep that in mind when selecting Aspects. Having multiple Aspects that basically say the same thing about your character isn't useful. If, for example, you have three Aspects that could reasonably be invoked to help your character on Guile tests to bargain for good prices, you may still only invoke one.
> 
> Because of this, it's better to be sure your Aspects don't all share the same focus.

### Creating Good Aspects

**✅ Good Aspects are:**
- 3-8 words long (not too simple, not too wordy)
- Double-edged (can help or hinder)
- Specific but flexible
- Tied to the setting
- Relevant to the campaign

**❌ Avoid:**
- Single-word aspects
- Pure benefits with no drawbacks
- Multiple aspects that serve the same purpose
- Vague or generic phrases
- Aspects that never come up

## Step 2: Character State

> [!info]- Purpose
> Choose how your consciousness exists in the post-Apotheosis world. This fundamental choice affects your physical capabilities, available options, and gameplay style.

### 🧑 Pure (Original Body)

> [!state]- Pure State
> **Description:** You still inhabit your original, un-Apotheosized body.
> 
> **Benefits:**
> - **Physical Skill Ranks:** 8 ranks to distribute among Athletics, Endurance, Perception (max 5 per skill)
> - **Refresh Bonus:** +2 Fate Points
> - **Free Stunt:** Carpe Diem
> - **Skill Bonus:** +1 to one non-physical skill (max rank 5)
> - **Additional Aspect:** Physical Trait (describes your natural body)
> 
> **Drawbacks:**
> - Cannot resleeve if killed
> - Lose ALL Pure benefits if you later get Apotheosis augmentation
> - More vulnerable than sleeved characters
> 
> **Best For:** Characters focused on humanity, natural abilities, or Purist ideologies

### 💻 SIM (Substrate-Independent Mind)

> [!state]- SIM State
> **Description:** Digital consciousness residing as pure software in a host computer.
> 
> **Benefits:**
> - **Physical Skill Ranks:** 7 ranks for virtual interaction (Athletics, Endurance, Perception in VR)
> - **Free Stunt:** SIM State
> - **Skill Bonuses:** +1 SINC, +1 Software Engineering (max rank 5 each)
> - **Choice Stunt:** Select one free from: Digitally Evolved, Digital Spider, Drone Jock, Infusion, Skilled SINC, System Slip
> - **Home Environment:** Custom virtual space under your complete control
> - **Digital Advantages:** Immune to physical threats, can copy yourself, instant mesh travel
> 
> **Drawbacks:**
> - **Interaction Limitation:** Can only view one mesh at a time (5 min to transition)
> - **Lag Penalties:** Distance-based penalties on actions:
>   - Within 1000 km: no penalty
>   - Same star system: -2 penalty
>   - Different star system: -4 penalty
> - **Physical Presence:** Requires drones for physical world interaction
> - **Visibility:** Must appear as semi-transparent ARO when ghosting (HPA requirement)
> 
> **Best For:** Hackers, digital spies, AI-focused characters, those embracing posthuman evolution

### 🤖 Sleeved (Artificial Body)

> [!state]- Sleeved State
> **Description:** Apotheosized consciousness inhabiting a biosleeve or cybersleeve.
> 
> **Benefits:**
> - **Starting Sleeve:** Get biosleeve or cybersleeve with Cost ≤ MAX(Assets, Rep) + 4
> - **Resleeving:** Can transfer to new bodies if killed
> - **Full Physicality:** Complete physical presence and interaction
> - **Customization:** Choose physical capabilities and augmentations
> - **Upgrade Path:** Can switch or upgrade sleeves later
> 
> **Drawbacks:**
> - Physical skills determined by sleeve, not by choice
> - Sleeve can be damaged/destroyed (though you survive)
> - Maintenance and replacement costs
> - Psychological trauma from resleeving
> 
> **Best For:** Combat characters, those wanting physical customization, transhumans

## Step 3: House Affiliation

> [!info]- Purpose
> Choose your allegiance to one of the six Coalition Houses, or embrace life as an Apostate outside the system.

### The Six Houses

> [!house]- House Cipriani
> **Focus:** Energy, Transportation, Manufacturing
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Atmospheric Flying Vehicles
> - Energy Systems
> - CISTs
> - Transportation
> 
> **Ideology:** Purist
> **Best For:** Pilots, engineers, energy specialists

> [!house]- House Dalianis
> **Focus:** Starships, Military Hardware, Exploration
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Starships
> - Rail/Coil Guns
> - Combat Drones
> 
> **Ideology:** Pragmatic Purist
> **Best For:** Military characters, explorers, starship crew

> [!house]- House Jinzhan
> **Focus:** Construction, Security, Food Production
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Construction
> - Security Technology
> - Food
> - Explosives
> - Ship & Vehicle Weaponry
> 
> **Ideology:** Purist (Isolationist)
> **Best For:** Security specialists, engineers, traditionalists

> [!house]- House Kimura
> **Focus:** Technology, Computers, Cybernetics
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Agents (AI Assistants)
> - Computers & Software
> - Cybersleeves
> - Cybernetic Augmentations
> - Virtuality Software
> - Recon Warframes
> 
> **Ideology:** Transhuman
> **Best For:** Hackers, technologists, cyber-enhanced characters

> [!house]- House Silva
> **Focus:** Biotechnology, Medical, Genetics
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Biosleeves
> - Biotech Augmentations
> - PAc Weaponry
> 
> **Ideology:** Transhuman
> **Best For:** Medics, biologists, genetic specialists

> [!house]- House Tsarya
> **Focus:** Armor, Ground Vehicles, Military Manufacturing
> 
> **Market Discounts:**
> - Vehicular & Personal Armor
> - Ground Vehicles
> - Assault Warframes
> 
> **Ideology:** Pragmatic Purist
> **Best For:** Soldiers, armor specialists, mercenaries

### 🕶️ Apostate

> [!warning]- Apostate Status
> **Description:** You live outside the Coalition system with no House allegiance.
> 
> **Benefits:**
> - Complete independence
> - Access to black markets
> - +2 bonus on Assets tests for Favor requests from fellow apostates
> - Free Stunt: Non-Citizen
> 
> **Drawbacks:**
> - **No Rep Rating:** Cannot use Reputation for resources or social influence
> - Hunted by House security
> - Limited access to legal markets
> - Must be self-reliant
> 
> **Special:** Skips Reputation step entirely

## Step 4: Skills

> [!info]- Purpose
> Distribute skill ranks across your character's abilities using one of three distribution patterns.

### Skill Distribution Patterns

> [!example]- Specialist (14 skills)
> **Focus:** High expertise in few areas
> 
> | Rank | Count | Total Ranks |
> |------|-------|-------------|
> | +5   | 1     | 5           |
> | +4   | 2     | 8           |
> | +3   | 2     | 6           |
> | +2   | 3     | 6           |
> | +1   | 6     | 6           |
> | **Total** | **14** | **31 ranks** |

> [!example]- Expert (16 skills) - *Most Common*
> **Focus:** Balanced capabilities with good breadth
> 
> | Rank | Count | Total Ranks |
> |------|-------|-------------|
> | +4   | 3     | 12          |
> | +3   | 3     | 9           |
> | +2   | 4     | 8           |
> | +1   | 6     | 6           |
> | **Total** | **16** | **35 ranks** |

> [!example]- Generalist (18 skills)
> **Focus:** Broad knowledge across many areas
> 
> | Rank | Count | Total Ranks |
> |------|-------|-------------|
> | +3   | 7     | 21          |
> | +2   | 8     | 16          |
> | +1   | 3     | 3           |
> | **Total** | **18** | **40 ranks** |

### Available Skills

**Physical Skills** *(Separate pool based on State)*:
- **Athletics** - Agility, climbing, running, physical movement
- **Endurance** - Stamina, toughness, resisting damage
- **Perception** - Awareness, noticing details, senses

**Standard Skills** *(Use distribution pattern above)*:
- **Assets** - Wealth, resources, purchasing power
- **Cohesion** - Mental stability, sense of self, resurrection recovery
- **Demolitions** - Explosives, destruction
- **Diplomacy** - Negotiation, persuasion, social grace
- **Education** - General knowledge, academic subjects
- **Engineering, Hardware (H.Eng)** - Mechanics, electronics, repairs
- **Engineering, Software (S.Eng)** - Programming, hacking, coding
- **Firearms** - Guns, ranged combat
- **Guile** - Deception, lies, disguise
- **Insight** - Reading people, detecting lies
- **Intimidation** - Threats, coercion
- **Melee** - Hand-to-hand combat, close weapons
- **Mnemonics** - Digital interfaces, computer interaction
- **Networking, House (H.Net)** - Coalition contacts *(House members)*
- **Networking, Apostate (A.Net)** - Underground contacts *(Apostates)*
- **Pilot** - Vehicles, spacecraft operation
- **Research** - Investigation, finding information
- **Resolve** - Willpower, mental fortitude
- **Stealth** - Sneaking, hiding, remaining unnoticed
- **Strategy** - Planning, tactics, analyzing situations

**Special Skill**:
- **SINC** - Savant Interface Neural Control *(Requires Savant stunt)*

### Physical Skill Allocation

> [!important]- Physical Skills Are SEPARATE
> Physical skills (Athletics, Endurance, Perception) use a **separate pool** from your main skill distribution:
> 
> - **Pure:** 8 ranks (max 5 per skill)
> - **SIM:** 7 ranks (max 5 per skill) - for virtual interaction
> - **Sleeved:** Physical skills come FROM your sleeve, not allocated here
> 
> These ranks are IN ADDITION to your chosen distribution pattern.

## Step 5: Reputation *(House Members Only)*

> [!info]- Purpose
> Calculate your social standing and access to Coalition resources based on your networking and social skills.

### Rep Calculation Formula

> [!formula]- Reputation Rating
> ```
> Rep = (House Networking + 2)
> ```
> 
> **Example 1:** House Networking 3
> - Rep = (3 + 2) = **5**
> 
> **Example 2:** House Networking 5
> - Rep = (5 + 2) = **7**
> 


### What Rep Represents

- **Social Influence:** Your standing within Coalition society
- **Resource Access:** Ability to call in favors, borrow equipment
- **House Status:** Your rank and respect within your House
- **Coalition Database:** Your IRD (Interplanetary Reputation Database) rating

### Rep Uses in Play

- Request assistance from House contacts
- Access restricted areas or information
- Leverage social pressure
- Determine initial reactions from NPCs

> [!warning]- Apostates Have No Rep
> Apostates are not in the Coalition system and have **no Reputation rating**. This step is skipped entirely for Apostate characters.

## Step 6: Stunts

> [!info]- Purpose
> Select special abilities, techniques, and advantages that make your character unique and mechanically distinct.

### Starting Stunts

- **Base:** 3 free stunts for all characters
- **State Bonuses:** Additional automatic stunts from your State choice
- **Additional Stunts:** Can buy more by reducing Refresh (1 Refresh = 1 Stunt)

### Stunt Categories

**Combat Stunts** - Fighting techniques and weapon mastery
**Defense Stunts** - Armor expertise and damage mitigation
**Mental Stunts** - Enhanced memory, willpower, perception
**Social Stunts** - Persuasion, deception, leadership
**Technical Stunts** - Hacking, engineering, research
**Physical Stunts** - Athletics, stealth, endurance
**Special Stunts** - Unique abilities like Savant

### Notable Stunts

> [!note]- Savant
> **Prerequisite:** None
> **Effect:** Grants access to SINC skill and Savant Programs
> 
> This stunt unlocks Step 8 (Savant Programs) where you configure nanobot programs that manipulate reality. Savants can use swarms of nanobots for telekinesis, matter manipulation, energy projection, and more.

> [!note]- Carpe Diem
> **Prerequisite:** Pure State
> **Effect:** Enhanced Fate Point usage
> **Automatic:** Pure characters get this free

> [!note]- SIM State
> **Prerequisite:** SIM State
> **Effect:** Digital existence benefits
> **Automatic:** SIM characters get this free

## Step 7: Savant Programs *(Savant Only)*

> [!warning]- Conditional Step
> This step **only appears** if you selected the **Savant** stunt in Step 6.

> [!info]- Purpose
> Configure nanobot programs that allow you to manipulate matter and energy through your SINC interface.

### Program Categories

**Movement Programs** - Telekinesis, propulsion, flight
**Perception Programs** - Enhanced senses, detection
**Defense Programs** - Shields, barriers, protection
**Utility Programs** - Matter manipulation, fabrication
**Weapon Programs** - Offensive energy projection

### How Savant Programs Work

- Activated using **SINC** skill
- Powered by ambient nanobots in environment
- Limited by nanobot concentration/availability
- More powerful effects require higher SINC ranks
- Can acquire additional programs during play

### Starting Programs

Number of starting programs based on SINC skill rank at character creation.

## Step 8: Sleeve *(Sleeved Only)*

> [!warning]- Conditional Step
> This step **only appears** if you selected **Sleeved** as your Character State in Step 2.

> [!info]- Purpose
> Design your artificial body (biosleeve or cybersleeve) including physical capabilities and augmentations.

### Sleeve Cost Limit

> [!formula]- Maximum Sleeve Cost
> ```
> Max Sleeve Cost = MAX(Assets Skill Rank, Rep Rating) + 4
> ```
> 
> **Example:** Assets 3, Rep 7
> - Max Cost = 7 + 4 = **11**
> 
> You can afford any sleeve costing 11 or less.

### Sleeve Types

> [!sleeve]- Biosleeves (Organic)
> **Description:** Vat-grown biological bodies with genetic enhancements
> 
> **Variants:**
> - **Alpha** (Cost 6): Basic, Unattractive, 8 physical ranks, 2 aug points
> - **Beta** (Cost 7): Standard, 9 physical ranks, 3 aug points
> - **Gamma** (Cost 9): Enhanced, 10 physical ranks, 4 aug points
> - **Delta** (Cost 11): Premium, 11 physical ranks, 6 aug points
> - **Epsilon** (Cost 14): Elite, 12 physical ranks, 8 aug points
> 
> **Pros:** Fully organic, socially accepted, compatible with bioware
> **Cons:** More expensive than cybersleeves

> [!sleeve]- Cybersleeves (Synthetic)
> **Description:** Fully mechanical bodies with synthetic components
> 
> **Variants:**
> - **Alpha** (Cost 5): Basic, 7 physical ranks, 3 aug points, AR 1
> - **Beta** (Cost 6): Standard, 8 physical ranks, 4 aug points, AR 1
> - **Gamma** (Cost 8): Enhanced, 9 physical ranks, 5 aug points, AR 2
> - **Delta** (Cost 10): Advanced, 10 physical ranks, 7 aug points, AR 2
> - **Epsilon** (Cost 13): Elite, 11 physical ranks, 9 aug points, AR 2
> 
> **Pros:** Built-in armor, cheaper than biosleeves, compatible with cyberware
> **Cons:** Inhuman appearance (social penalties), Purist prejudice

### Physical Skills From Sleeve

Your sleeve provides a **pool of physical skill ranks** to distribute:
- Allocate ranks among Athletics, Endurance, and Perception
- Maximum 5 ranks per skill
- These replace the normal physical skill allocation

### Augmentation Points

Your sleeve provides **augmentation points** to spend on modifications:
- Each augmentation costs Augmentation Value (AV)
- Cannot exceed your sleeve's augmentation point budget
- Some augmentations are included free with certain sleeves
- See Augmentations for full list

## Step 9: Starting Gear

> [!info]- Purpose
> Select equipment, weapons, armor, and tools your character begins play with.

### Gear Budget

Based on your **Assets** skill and **Rep Rating** (for House members):

- Higher Assets = More personal wealth and equipment
- Higher Rep = Better access to House resources
- Apostates rely primarily on Assets (no Rep bonus)

### Gear Categories

**Weapons** - See Firearms and Melee Weapons
**Armor** - See Armor
**Equipment** - See Equipment
**Explosives** - See Explosives

### Gear Selection Tips

✅ **Do:**
- Choose gear that fits your character concept
- Balance offense and defense
- Consider utility items
- Think about your typical missions

❌ **Avoid:**
- Spending everything on one item
- Ignoring basic necessities
- Taking gear you lack skills to use

## Step 10: Finalize Character

> [!info]- Purpose
> Review all your choices, verify calculations, and commit your character to the game.

### Final Checklist

> [!check]- Identity & Aspects
> - ✅ Character name set
> - ✅ Player name set  
> - ✅ Five Aspects defined
> - ✅ High Concept describes character
> - ✅ Trouble creates interesting complications

> [!check]- State & Allegiance
> - ✅ State chosen (Pure/SIM/Sleeved)
> - ✅ House or Apostate selected
> - ✅ State benefits applied
> - ✅ Automatic stunts/aspects added

> [!check]- Skills
> - ✅ All skill ranks assigned
> - ✅ Distribution pattern complete
> - ✅ Physical skills allocated (Pure/SIM) or from sleeve (Sleeved)
> - ✅ No unassigned ranks

> [!check]- Reputation & Resources
> - ✅ Rep calculated correctly (House members)
> - ✅ Rep = 0 confirmed (Apostates)
> - ✅ Assets skill determines starting wealth

> [!check]- Abilities
> - ✅ Minimum 3 stunts selected
> - ✅ Savant programs chosen (if Savant)
> - ✅ All prerequisites met
> - ✅ Refresh rating positive

> [!check]- Equipment & Sleeve
> - ✅ Sleeve selected and within cost (if Sleeved)
> - ✅ Augmentations chosen (if Sleeved)
> - ✅ Starting gear selected
> - ✅ Gear within budget

### Calculated Statistics

The following should be automatically calculated:

> [!formula]- Refresh
> ```
> Refresh = 3 (base) + State Bonuses - Extra Stunts
> ```
> Pure characters: 3 + 2 = 5 Refresh (before buying extra stunts)

> [!formula]- Physical Stress
> ```
> Physical Stress = 2 + Endurance Rank
> ```
> Only if you have a physical form (Pure or Sleeved)

> [!formula]- Mental Stress  
> ```
> Mental Stress = 2 + Resolve Rank
> ```
> All characters have Mental Stress

### Consequence Slots

All characters have three consequence slots:
- **Mild** (absorbs 2 stress)
- **Moderate** (absorbs 4 stress)
- **Severe** (absorbs 6 stress)
