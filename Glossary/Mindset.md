The networked mesh of nanomachines that replaces a person's cerebrum during [[Glossary/Apotheosis]]. A mindset is the substrate of a digitized consciousness — billions of nanomachines, each carrying out the function of the brain cell it replaced, woven together into a single distributed processor that *is* the person.

## What a Mindset Provides

- **Direct neural interface** — overlays [[Glossary/Augmented Reality|augmented reality]] data straight onto the user's senses without external hardware
- **Resident [[Glossary/Agent]]** — a low-level AI that handles calendars, messaging, social networks, and routine tasks
- **Real-time backup** to a [[Glossary/Mnemonic Core|mnemonic core]], the extractable nanocluster that allows a person to be re-uploaded into a new [[Glossary/Sleeve|sleeve]] after death
- **Native [[Glossary/Virtuality]] access** — no ego channel hardware required; the mindset can hijack its own sensory streams to enter virtual environments

Roughly 35% of humanity has a mindset. To outsiders, the user looks unchanged; internally, the cerebrum has been replaced cell by cell over the course of about a week, and the person who walks out is software — networked, backed up, and effectively immortal so long as the [[Factions/The Coalition/_Coalition Overview|Coalition]]'s resleeving infrastructure holds.
