In Nova Praxis usage, a **Chimera** is a single consciousness assembled by illegally merging multiple [[Glossary/Apotheosis|Apotheosized]] [[Glossary/Mindset|mindsets]] into one [[Glossary/Sleeve|sleeve]] or [[Glossary/SIM|SIM]] instance. The procedure is a catastrophic violation of the [[Glossary/Human Personhood Accord|Human Personhood Accord]] — every component ego is technically murdered to produce the merged whole — and its very existence is treated as a doomsday-class crime by Coalition law.

A successful Chimera inherits the skills, memories, and instincts of every contributing mindset, but is **none of the people it remembers being**. Most attempts fail: incompatible egos fragment, the sleeve burns out, or the merged mind collapses into psychosis. Stable fusion appears to require pre-selected, psychologically complementary subjects — which is exactly what the [[Glossary/Standard Deviation Project|Standard Deviation Project]] was designed to manufacture a century before the campaign began.

The campaign's principal Chimera is the gestalt formed from the player characters' egos by Dr. Haruto Nakamura's Mindscapes team at Opportunity Four — see the entity card at [[GM AI/Entity Cards/R5/Chimera (R5 Villain)]] for full game stats and the active arc tracker at [[Plot/Situations/The Chimera Conspiracy - Arc Tracker]].
