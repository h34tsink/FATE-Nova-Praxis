# Randallite

**Origin:** Lisa Randall (brane cosmology, Randall-Sundrum model)
**Class:** Exotic material — bulk-precipitated crystalline metal
**Summary:** The only known material that originates outside our brane. Randallite precipitates from the multiversal bulk under extreme energy conditions, condensing into a crystalline metallic lattice that acts as a natural resonant anchor for Pneuma effects.

---

## Physical Properties

Randallite is not an element on any periodic table. It occupies no electron shell, emits no standard spectral line, and does not bond with conventional matter in any predictable way. It is, by every measurable standard, *foreign* — matter that belongs to the higher-dimensional bulk and has been forced into our brane's physics by violence.

**Appearance:** Dense, semi-translucent crystalline metal. Color shifts depending on purity and origin — raw specimens tend toward deep indigo-black with internal fracture planes that catch light in non-Euclidean patterns. Polished samples look like smoked glass with metallic luster. Some describe a faint iridescence that doesn't track the light source.

**Texture:** Cool to the touch regardless of ambient temperature. Pneuma-sensitive individuals report a low tactile hum — not vibration, more like the sensation of touching something that is *also touching you back*.

**Density:** Anomalously high for its apparent volume. A fist-sized specimen weighs like lead but behaves structurally like a ceramic-metal hybrid.

**Stability:** Chemically inert under normal conditions. Does not oxidize, corrode, or degrade. Resistant to conventional machining — shaping requires Mimir-derived fabrication techniques or other randallite tools.

---

## Precipitation

Randallite does not form naturally in our universe under normal conditions. It **precipitates** — condenses from the multiversal bulk into physical matter — only when sufficient energy is concentrated in a sufficiently small region to momentarily thin the brane boundary.

**Known precipitation conditions:**
- **Stellar collapse** — neutron star formation, magnetar flares, and certain supernova geometries produce randallite microcrystals in the debris field
- **Mimir-era reactors** — several Archive blueprints describe reactor configurations that could induce controlled precipitation (none have been fully replicated)
- **Alien origin** — the largest known specimens are [[Factions/Aliens/Birrin/Birrin Species - Biology and Culture|Birrin]]-associated or recovered from pre-human sites; their precipitation methods are unknown
- **Pneuma cascade events** — uncontrolled, dangerous, and poorly documented; when Pneuma use reaches catastrophic intensity, trace amounts of randallite have been found at the epicenter

**Yield:** Vanishingly small. A stellar collapse might scatter micrograms across a debris field light-years wide. Controlled precipitation (if achievable) produces milligrams per attempt. The largest intact specimen in known human possession is roughly the size of a closed fist and is the subject of at least three active faction conflicts.

## Why It Matters

Randallite is the **physical anchor** for Pneuma technology. Its extra-dimensional origin means it exists partially outside our brane's causal rules — it resonates with the [[Glossary/Penrose Field|Penrose Field]], couples to volitional bias at quantum scales, and acts as a bridge between mind and the measurement-independence layer that Pneuma exploits.

Without randallite (or its synthetic mimics), Pneuma effects require a rare natural gift ([[Rules and Mechanics/Pneuma Rules/Pneuma Unified Rules (Draft)|Pneumatic]] savants). With it, a trained operator can channel effects that would otherwise require extraordinary innate ability.

**Two forms exist:**
- **Natural randallite** — alien-origin or stellar-precipitated. Higher purity, stronger resonance, rarer. Used in [[Rules and Mechanics/Pneuma Rules/Pneuma Unified Rules (Draft)|Tier III-IV artifacts]].
- **Synthetic randallite** — Mimir-derived fabrication mimics the lattice structure using achievable physics. Lower purity, noisier signature, more available. Used in Tier I-II artifacts. Often called simply "synthetic" or "synth-rand."

## Naming & Slang

Randallite has accumulated names the way any valuable, dangerous, and politically charged substance does.

### Formal / Academic
| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Randallite** | Standard academic and technical name. After Lisa Randall. |
| **Bulk precipitate** | Process-descriptive. Used in materials science papers and Archive annotations. |
| **Randall-Sundrum condensate** | Full formal name in theoretical physics contexts. Nobody says this out loud twice. |
| **Transversal matter** | Broad category term — any matter originating outside our brane. Randallite is the only confirmed example. |
| **Branite** | Older term, still used in some Mimir Archive entries. Fell out of favor when "Randallite" was standardized. Some engineers still prefer it. |

### Technical / Operational
| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Core** | The shaped randallite component inside an artifact. "Void-Core" is deprecated but still appears in older schematics. |
| **Lattice** | Refers to the internal crystalline structure. "Clean lattice" = high purity. "Dirty lattice" = degraded or synthetic. |
| **Substrate** | The raw material before shaping. "Bulk substrate" in procurement docs. |
| **Condensate** | Shorthand in lab settings. "Run the condensate through the analyzer." |
| **Kaluzite** | After Theodor Kaluza. Used interchangeably with randallite in some [[Factions/Houses/House Kimura|Kimura]] research divisions. Implies a focus on the dimensional-origin theory. |

### Street / Underworld
| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Rand** | Universal short form. "He's carrying rand." "How much rand?" |
| **Bulk** | Even shorter. "Got bulk?" Ambiguous enough to use in public. |
| **Glass** | After the visual appearance. "Thirty grams of black glass, uncut." |
| **Sundrum** | Slightly more educated slang. Implies the speaker knows what they're handling. "That's real Sundrum, not synth." |
| **Seed** | A raw, unprocessed randallite fragment. "Planting seeds" = embedding randallite in a device. |
| **Bones** | Processed randallite rods or structural elements inside artifacts. "Good bones in that staff." |
| **Echo** | Poetic slang. Randallite as a physical echo of [[Glossary/Mimir's Echo|Mimir's last work]]. "She's running on pure echo." |

### Institutional / Political
| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Controlled material** | HPA classification. Possession without license is a [[Glossary/Script Break|Script Break]]. |
| **Strategic reserve** | House terminology for hoarded stockpiles. |
| **Bulkite** | Dismissive bureaucratic term. Used in HPA paperwork to make it sound mundane. "Bulkite sample, 2.3g, confiscated." |
| **Catalyst** | Corporate euphemism. "Catalyst-enhanced device" = Pneuma artifact, in brochures that can't say Pneuma. |

## Cultural Associations

- **[[Factions/Religion/Mimirians|Mimirians]]** revere randallite as Mimir's intended gift — proof that the bulk is accessible and that humanity was meant to reach it. They prefer the term "condensate" or "echo."
- **[[Factions/Ideologies/Purists|Purists]]** consider randallite contamination — extradimensional pollution. They want it contained, cataloged, and locked away. "Bulkite" is their preferred diminutive.
- **[[Factions/Houses/House Kimura|Kimura]]** leads synthetic randallite fabrication. Their labs produce most Tier I-II synth-rand. They prefer "kaluzite" in internal docs.
- **Black markets** trade in "rand," "glass," and "seeds." Pricing is erratic, verification is dangerous (testing a sample means activating it), and fakes are common.
- **The [[Factions/Aliens/Birrin/The Quiet Pier|Quiet Pier]]** possesses randallite specimens of unknown age and origin. They do not explain where it came from. They do not sell it.

## GM Hooks

- A derelict freighter's manifest lists "bulk substrate, 400g" — enough to build multiple Tier III artifacts. Every faction wants it.
- A Kimura lab achieves controlled precipitation for 0.3 seconds. The resulting sample is 11 milligrams. The energy bill would power a station for a week. They want to do it again.
- Street dealer is selling "black glass" that's actually synth-rand with a dirty lattice — it works, but the signature is deafening. HPA is already triangulating.
- A PC finds a randallite seed in a place it shouldn't be — inside a pre-Mimir ruin, in a human grave, in their own sleeve's maintenance cavity.
