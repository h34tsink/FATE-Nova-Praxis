A [[Glossary/Mimir]]-developed augmentation procedure that interweaves trillions of nanomachines through living tissue, creating a hybrid mesh of biological and synthetic cells throughout the body. Where [[Glossary/Apotheosis]] transforms the **mind**, Synthesis transforms the **body** — the result is far more durable than baseline human flesh, with precise nanomachine-mediated monitoring and control of every system.

Roughly **7% of humanity** has undergone the procedure. Synthesized individuals are often held up as the ideal of *transhumanity*: the harmonic merger of organic and synthetic life, distinct from the more divisive posthumans who have abandoned the flesh entirely for purely synthetic [[Glossary/Sleeves|sleeves]] or digital existence. To [[Glossary/Pure|Purists]], Synthesis is the line where a person stops being human; to transhumanists, it is the proof that the line was never there.
