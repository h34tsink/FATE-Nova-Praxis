# Augmented Reality

A **virtual environment layered over the real world**, visible only to those with display hardware or a [[Glossary/Mindset|mindset]]. Where [[Glossary/Virtuality|virtuality]] submerges the user in a separate environment, augmented reality (AR) makes minor (or major) tweaks to the one they currently inhabit.

## How It Works

An AR system pulls three streams together:

1. A **map of the user's environment** — built from glasses, lenses, or entoptic implants scanning the surroundings
2. **Positional and directional data** — where the user is and what they're looking at
3. **Augmented reality objects (AROs)** — pulled from local AR servers and overlaid onto the user's senses

The result: a plain stone floor in an empty room can appear as a lush garden. A blank wall can hold a painting. A windowpane can run video. An ARO can be as small as a tag floating over an item for sale, or as large as a full environment overhaul.

## Display Hardware

- **AR glasses or contact lenses** — basic external hardware
- **Entoptic augmentations** — projectors mounted on the eye itself
- **Cybernetic eye replacements** — the most invasive option, with the highest fidelity
- **Mindset** — direct neural overlay; no external hardware required, the data is fed straight into the user's senses

## ARIDs

People and objects in Coalition space are commonly tagged with **augmented reality IDs (ARIDs)** that present information to anyone looking. A tagged person might broadcast a status message ("Leave me alone," "In a hurry") or their professional credentials. Items for sale display price and provenance. The [[Glossary/Mesh Web|Mesh]] is the security-grade version of the same concept.

## Mechanics

AR is the default user interface for almost everything in Coalition society. Keyboards, mice, touchscreens, and holographic displays are all relics. Computer interaction happens via direct brain interface or gesture-based AR.
