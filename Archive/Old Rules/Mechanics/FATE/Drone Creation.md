## I. Drone Overview and Control

Drones are frequently encountered in Coalition cities, habitats, and apostate enclaves [2]. They generally operate via a **complex narrow AI** [2, 3].

### Purpose and Usage

*   Drones are employed for **hazardous jobs** such as mining, search and rescue, or exploration [2].
*   They are also widely used for **security** (e.g., the Lonestar works alongside Centurion forces) [2, 4].
*   Most **military operations are carried out entirely by drones** [2].
*   Larger autonomous platforms, such as **warframes and starships**, may be treated as drones if they are equipped with an AI or operated remotely [3].

### Control Methods

| Control Method | Action Required | Effect | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Passive Control** | Free Action | Issuing simple commands to the drone's narrow AI based on its inherent Skills [3, 5]. | [5] |
| **Active Control (Remote)** | Simple Action | Direct control using a neural link or mindset. The drone gains a **+1 bonus** on its Skill tests [5]. | [5] |
| **Active Control (Puppeteer)** | Simple Action | Possessing the drone using the *Puppeteer* Savant Program grants a **+2 bonus** on all Skill tests [5, 6]. The controller views the world from the drone’s perspective [6-8]. | [5-8] |

**Signal and Security Considerations:**

*   **Lag** can impose penalties similar to those suffered by SIMs when controlling a drone over great distances [2, 9].
*   Signal jamming technology can render drones useless, allowing enemies to **capture and repurpose** them [2].
*   If an attack targets a remote controller (not using the *Puppeteer* Program), the attacker suffers a **-2 penalty** on the Savant Program execution test due to the weak signal link and security between the drone and the operator [10, 11].

## II. Drone Creation Rules

Drones are created similarly to cybersleeves [5]. The process for purchasing a drone is almost identical to purchasing a cybersleeve, with the following key differences [5, 12]:

1.  **Base Cost:** The Cost of a drone is **1 rank less** than a comparable sleeve [12].
2.  **Aspects:** Drones gain **two Aspects** (in addition to *Synthetic Biology* and *Inhuman Appearance*) that define their nature and intended purpose [12].
3.  **Hostware:** Drones **cannot support a hostware suite** [12].
4.  **Skills:** Drones distribute ranks equal to **twice the number of Physical Skill Ranks** a comparable cybersleeve receives [12].
    *   Skills available for ranking include standard Physical Skills (Athletics, Endurance, Perception), as well as **Demolitions, Insight, Firearms, Melee, Pilot, Resolve, Stealth, and Strategy** [12].
    *   A drone may never have more than 5 ranks in a single Skill [12].
5.  **Armor Rating (AR):** The drone's **AR is equal to its Endurance + 1** [12]. This AR value is used instead of the standard cybersleeve armor bonus [12].
6.  **Restricted Status:** If a drone has restricted Augmentations, it is itself **restricted** and requires a license for legal ownership [12].
