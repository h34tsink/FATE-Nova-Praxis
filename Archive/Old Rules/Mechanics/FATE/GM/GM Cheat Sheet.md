# 🎛️ FATE GM MASTER CHEAT SHEET
*For Nova Praxis & Narrative-Driven Play*

---

## 🧠 Core GM Loop

**1️⃣ Frame the Scene**
- Set tone, location, and stakes.
- Define **2–3 Scene Aspects** immediately.
- Ask: “What’s *changing* in this moment?”

**2️⃣ Spotlight the Characters**
- Who’s acting first?  
- What’s their intent — survive, connect, manipulate, destroy?  
- Frame each action as an *emotional or tactical beat.*

**3️⃣ Roll the Dice**
- Difficulty = narrative tension, not simulation.  
- Tie results to consequences, not “hits.”

**4️⃣ Update the World**
- Every roll should change the fiction:
  - Success = new Aspects or progress.  
  - Failure = cost, complication, or reveal.  
  - Style = momentum + 2 free invokes.  

**5️⃣ Fade or Cut**
- When the scene’s emotion peaks, or a new truth lands — cut.  
- Transition with a line of tone or imagery.

## 🎭 Scene Construction

| Step | Description | Example |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Establish** | Introduce location, tone, and Aspects. | “Dock 27 burns under zero-g, smoke curling like code.” |
| **Incite** | Introduce tension or dilemma. | “The workers see your weapons. Do you lower them?” |
| **Escalate** | Raise pressure — time, enemies, emotions. | “The mesh flickers — Lighthouse enforcers inbound.” |
| **Resolve** | What changed? Which Aspects shift? | “‘Workers in Revolt’ → ‘Mass Panic.’” |
| **Transition** | End on cinematic or emotional beat. | “The station turns. The fires look like constellations.” |

💡 *Rule of Thumb:*  
If the fiction isn’t changing, you’re between scenes — fade or cut.

## 🎬 Scene Aspects Reference

**Purpose:** describe narrative truths that affect everyone.

| Type | Visible? | Examples | How to Use |
|------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Obvious** | Yes | “Low Gravity,” “Gunfire in Every Direction” | Announce openly. Invocable anytime. |
| **Hidden** | No | “Security Feeds Looping,” “Backup Incoming” | Discover with *Create Advantage.* |
| **Dynamic** | Appears mid-play | “Fire Spreading,” “Oxygen Running Low” | Add or evolve as story shifts. |

🎲 **Players can:**
- **Invoke:** spend 1 FP or use free invoke for +2 or reroll.  
- **Compel:** earn FP by making it worse.  
- **Overcome:** remove or mitigate an Aspect.

⚙️ **GM Tips:**
- Keep 2–4 Scene Aspects in play.  
- Cross out or fade ones that no longer matter.  
- Encourage players to invent new Aspects narratively.

## 🎯 Create Advantage Flow

1. **Player Intent:** “I want to find, create, or exploit something useful.”  
2. **Clarify:** “Are you trying to reveal, change, or distract?”  
3. **Roll:** Against a difficulty that fits fiction.  
4. **On Success:**  
   - Reveal a hidden Aspect *or*  
   - Create a new one with **1 free invoke**.  
5. **On Success with Style:** 2 free invokes.  
6. **On Failure:** complication or cost.

| Skill Type | Example | Aspect |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Tech | “Loop cameras” | *Security Feeds Looping* |
| Social | “Calm the mob” | *Crowd Distracted* |
| Combat | “Suppress their advance” | *Pinned Down* |
| Physical | “Gain high ground” | *Commanding Position* |

## 💥 Compels

**Definition:** When an Aspect *creates trouble.*  

**Types:**
1. **Character Compel** – an Aspect of a PC complicates the moment.  
   - *“Your loyalty to the Apostates means you can’t betray them now.”*  
2. **Scene Compel** – the environment turns against them.  
   - *“The damaged Mesh flickers out mid-command.”*  

**Offer a Fate Point.**  
Player accepts → complication happens.  
Player refuses → they spend 1 FP to resist.

💡 *Tip:*  
Always use compels to *move the story forward*, not stall it.  
Every compel should create **new momentum** or **new tension.**

## 🤝 Teamwork & Cooperation

When multiple PCs help on the same action:

- Each helper rolls or narrates support.  
- Every success adds +1 to the main roller’s total (max +2).  
- Fictionally describe how they assist (emotionally or physically).  

**Synergy Example:**
> - Kael hacks the Mesh (*Create Advantage: Security Looping*).  
> - Lira uses Empathy to calm the mob (*Create Advantage: Distracted Crowd*).  
> - Imani sneaks past with +4 from both invokes.  
>  
> Smooth, cinematic, collaborative.

## 🧩 Stress & Consequences

| Track | Represents | Resets |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Physical Stress** | Injury, exhaustion, harm | After rest |
| **Mental Stress** | Fear, trauma, confusion | After rest / therapy |
| **Consequences** | Lasting harm that creates new Aspects | When healed or resolved |

| Severity | Example | Recovery |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Mild (2) | *“Bruised and Rattled”* | Scene end |
| Moderate (4) | *“Gunshot Wound”* | Session end |
| Severe (6) | *“Cracked Mindframe”* | Arc end |

Each consequence is a **new Aspect** — the GM can *invoke or compel* it just like any other.

## ⚙️ Conflict Summary (Physical / Social / Mental)

1. **Establish Goal & Stakes**  
   - What do each side want?
2. **Exchange Actions**  
   - Attack, Defend, Create Advantage, Overcome.  
3. **Apply Shifts & Stress.**
   - Shifts = difference between rolls.  
   - Absorb with Stress boxes or take Consequences.  
4. **Narrate Visually.**
   - “Her voice cracks. The riot pauses. Then screams begin again.”

💡 Use the same structure for *social* and *mental* conflicts.  
It’s not *what* they fight, it’s *what’s at stake.*

## ⏱️ Scene Pacing & Transitions

| Scene Type | Duration | Goal |
|-------------|-----------|------|
| **Micro** | 5–10 min | Link or reveal |
| **Standard** | 20–40 min | Advance plot, teach a rule |
| **Extended** | 45–90 min | Climax, showdown, or multi-step goal |

**Transition Types:**
- **Time Shift:** skip forward.  
- **Location Shift:** change scenery or focus.  
- **Emotional Shift:** carry a feeling forward.  
- **Aspect Shift:** state of the world changes → cut.

> *“Her reflection flickers into static — cut to the Icarus Hub burning in orbit.”*

## 🧭 Session Structure (Cinematic Flow)

> **Setup ➜ Escalation ➜ Consequence ➜ Fallout ➜ Transition ➜ Next Hook**

| Phase | GM Focus | Fate Tools |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Setup** | Establish tone, stakes, and Aspects | Obvious Scene Aspects, light compels |
| **Escalation** | Introduce moral and tactical dilemmas | Create Advantage, NPC invokes |
| **Consequence** | Reveal fallout of success or failure | Stress & Consequences |
| **Fallout** | Emotional or narrative resolution | Compels, downtime scenes |
| **Transition** | Bridge to next act | Narrative cut, new Scene Aspects |

## 💀 Advanced GM Moves

| Move | Description | Use When |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Twist the Aspect** | Flip an existing one into danger. | “Security System Disabled” → “Security System Rebooting.” |
| **Split the Scene** | Parallelize simultaneous actions. | Riot outside, infiltration inside. |
| **Flashback Invocation** | Let a player spend FP to retroactively justify preparation. | “I *knew* we’d need a signal scrambler.” |
| **Compel the Clock** | Use time as a pressure Aspect. | “Reinforcements 1 Segment Away.” |
| **Let NPCs Invoke** | Keep tension fair. | Enemies spend FP too. |

---

## ⚡ Fate Point Economy

- Start each session with **3 Fate Points per player**.  
- Compels feed the pool → Invokes spend it.  
- Keep the economy flowing:  
  - If things stall, offer a Compel.  
  - If players hoard, hit them with Aspects that *demand* use.

> **Rule of Fun:** every player should spend or earn at least 2–3 FP per scene.
