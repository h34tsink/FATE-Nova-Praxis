Emerging from the foundations of older faiths, new religions have evolved, integrating aspects of [[Astralists]] beliefs. These new doctrines tend to reject traditional notions of the soul as an ethereal, invisible essence within the body. Instead, they conceptualize the soul as a platonic ideal representing the unique consciousness of an individual. Adherents of these faiths assert that the soul is synonymous with one's identity and is unaffected by physical alterations or consciousness replication. They advocate that a copy of an individual is still the same person; both versions are equally authentic, sharing the same soul.
